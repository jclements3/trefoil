- A2 and T1 may freely share a pitch — this sits at the natural

- Never use a unison as a default solution to a voice leading problem
- Avoid unisons on consecutive beats within the same pair
- Unisons are most effective on strong beats and phrase endings
- A unison on a weak beat is acceptable only if both voices move

STEP 6 — VOICE LEADING RULES
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
1. No parallel fifths or octaves between any two adjacent voices
2. No voice crossing within a pair (S1 always above S2, etc.)
3. Voice crossing between pairs is permitted where musically effective
4. Prefer contrary or oblique motion when splitting a single SATB note
5. Prefer thirds and sixths for intervals between paired voices
6. Stepwise motion preferred in all voices; leaps of a sixth or more
   must resolve stepwise in the opposite direction
7. No augmented melodic intervals in any single voice line
8. Maintain the original harmonic rhythm unless a passing chord
   strengthens the voice leading
9. Do not open the arrangement at full range — reserve extreme
   high and low notes for climax moments only
