NOTATION: MOVING vs SUSTAINED
===============================================================
On the harp, fingers that hold a string are SUSTAINED (stem down),
and fingers that move to a new string are MOVING (stem up).

On each staff (RH and LH), use TWO layers:
  Layer 1 (stems up):   MOVING notes — fingers that change
  Layer 2 (stems down): SUSTAINED notes — fingers that hold

When the chord changes, some notes may stay the same (common tones
between chords). Those notes stay in the sustained layer. Only notes
that actually change go in the moving layer.

When ALL notes change (e.g., complete chord change), all notes
appear in the moving layer with stems up.

When NO notes change (same chord repeated), all notes appear in
the sustained layer with stems down — no re-articulation.

The Pedal voice is always in the LH sustained layer (stems down)
as the lowest note.

===============================================================

   - Moving fingers take the shortest path to the new chord tone
   - Prefer contrary motion between hands
   - No parallel 5ths or octaves between adjacent voices

4. TEXTURE VARIETY across the piece:
   - Open with lighter voicing (fewer fingers, wider spacing)
   - Build to fuller voicing (more fingers, closer position)
   - Climax: full 8 voices + pedal, widest range

   - Arpeggiate on some beats (especially strong beats)
