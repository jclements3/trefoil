TEXTURE PLAN
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
The arrangement opens with five voices (S1 S2 A1 A2 + B1) for bars
1–2, giving an SSAA-plus-bass texture that keeps the upper choir
acoustically prominent and reserves full ensemble weight. Tenor I and
Tenor II join at bar 3, expanding to seven voices for bars 3–4 and
adding a warm lower-middle layer before the midpoint. The Baritone
enters at bar 5, completing the eight-voice texture for the entire
second half of the piece.

Texture types employed:

1. SSAA antiphonal opening (bars 1–2): upper choir alone with B1
   bass foundation — the TTBB group is silent, creating a deliberately
   lighter, chorale-like opening as instructed (reduced texture build).

2. Full 8-voice homophony (bars 5–12): all voices together in block-
   chord style with the original harmonic rhythm maintained throughout.
   The repeated phrase (bars 5–6 = bars 7–8 in S1) is given identical
   treatment in all voices, as the strophic nature of the hymn supports
   straightforward repetition here.

3. Contrary-motion interior voices (throughout): A1 ascends while A2
   holds or moves obliquely; T1 follows the original tenor while T2
   moves in diatonic parallel thirds below, providing inner-voice
   melodic independence without obscuring the soprano melody.

4. Suspension figures at the final cadence (bars 11–12): A1 oscillates
   Bb3–Ab3 before settling to G3; A2 has a 4–3 neighbour figure
   (Eb3–D3–Eb3); T1 and T2 mirror this with Bb3–Ab3–Bb3 / G3–F3–G3,
   implying a 7–6 suspension against the bass.

Dynamic arc: piano at the five-voice opening, growing through the
seven-voice entry of the tenors to a full mezzo-forte at bar 5, and
building to forte at the climactic repeated phrase (bars 5–8, 7–8).
The final phrase (bars 9–12) returns to a richer, slightly broader
tempo at the cadence, ending piano-forte with the full ensemble on
the Eb major chord.

RULE COMPROMISES
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
1. Parallel fifths, A1–A2, bar 3, beats 2–3:
   A1 moves Bb3→C4 (up step); A2 moves Eb3→G3 (up third).
   Interval: P5 (beat 2) → P4 (beat 3) — parallel similar motion
   from P5 to P4. Strictly speaking this approaches a parallel
   perfect interval; however, the destination is a P4 (not a P5
   or P8), and both voices move by short diatonic steps in the
   same direction as part of an ascending homophonic texture. In
   8-part choral writing between inner voices this is a standard
   acceptable compromise. Noted for transparency.

2. Parallel similar motion to P4, S2–A1, bars 11–12 barline:
   S2 moves D4→C4 (down step); A1 moves Bb3→G3 (actually, A1
   bar 11 ends on B, = Bb3, bar 12 starts on G, = G3, a third
   down). The interval S2–A1 moves from M3 to P4 in similar
   (downward) motion. Not a parallel perfect 5th or octave;
   noted as a mild voice-leading looseness in the approach to
   the final cadence.

3. A2 low D3 (D,) at bars 8 and 12: touching the absolute bottom
   of A2's specified range. The instruction permits D3 as the
   lower limit. Used sparingly at cadence points only, where the
   downward resolution is musically natural.
