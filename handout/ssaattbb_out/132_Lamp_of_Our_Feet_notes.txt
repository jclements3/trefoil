TRANSPOSITION
-------------
Original key:  G major (1 sharp)
Chosen key:    G major (1 sharp) — no transposition required
Semitones:     0 (no change)
Direction:     N/A

Lever harp (Eb storage tuning) requires the following levers raised for G major:
  Raise: Eb → Bb → F# (i.e., all strings to natural except F which is raised to F#)
  Lever positions: Ab=down, Eb=down, Bb=down, F=UP (F#), C=down, G=down, D=down

G major (1 sharp) is comfortably within the 4-sharp maximum, requiring only one
lever raised from Eb storage. No range problems arose that would have required
choosing a different key.


HARMONIC SUBSTITUTIONS
-----------------------
No harmonic substitutions were required for outer-voice harmonic structure.
The original hymn harmonies (I, IV, V, ii, vi) are all diatonic in G major and
required no reharmonization of the chord progression itself.

The chromatic inner-voice accidentals (see Diatonic Violations section below)
were handled by replacing the individual note rather than reharmonizing the
entire beat, as the surrounding chord context remained valid diatonically.

Bar 6 (beat 3): The original alto C#4 implied a secondary leading tone toward D
  in a V/ii flavor. Replacing with D4 (the resolution pitch itself) makes the
  motion even more direct (D already implying a G cadential approach) without
  altering the I → V harmonic rhythm.

Bar 12 (beat 3): Same situation as bar 6 in the recapitulation. C#4 in the
  original alto replaced by C4 (diatonic neighbor), preserving the vi–V–I
  cadential color without chromaticism.

Bar 13 (beat 3): Identical case. C#4 → C4 (diatonic scale degree 4).


DIATONIC VIOLATIONS CAUGHT
---------------------------
All violations are from inner voices of the original SATB. S1 (Soprano I)
follows the original soprano exactly; that voice contained no accidentals.

1. Bar 6, beat 3 — Original Alto (S1V2 in source): C#4 (^C)
   Replacement in A1: D4 (upper neighbor — step above the original)
   Replacement in A2: G3 (chord tone of G major, unrelated motion)
   Reason: C# is not diatonic in G major; the harp cannot play it without a
   lever flip. D4 is the natural resolution of the leading-tone figure and
   preserves the descending melodic gesture into bar 7.

2. Bar 12, beat 3 — Original Alto (S1V2 in source): C#4 (^C)
   Replacement in A1: E4 (upper third of the bar's C4)
   Replacement in A2: B3 (chord tone)
   Reason: Same as bar 6. C# diatonic violation; E4 preserves ascending
   inner-voice motion toward the climax phrase.

3. Bar 13, beat 3 — Original Alto (S1V2 in source): C#4 (^C)
   Replacement in A1: D4 (direct resolution, same as bar 6)
   Replacement in A2: A3 (stepwise descent)
   Reason: Same chromatic figure; diatonic replacement maintains smooth
   voice leading through the final cadential approach.

4. Bar 8, beat 3 — Original Tenor (S2V1 in source): G#3 (^G,)
   Written in T1 as G (G3 concert, natural): T1 bar 8 = E F G (natural)
   Written in T2 as G (G3 concert): T2 bar 8 rests (antiphonal gap)
   Reason: G# is not diatonic in G major. The original G# was an ascending
   chromatic passing tone (E–F#–G#–A). The diatonic replacement G natural
   preserves the ascending scalar figure (E–F#–G–A) without chromaticism,
   and the gesture still resolves upward to A in beat 1 of bar 9.

5. Bass adjustments for range (not accidentals, but register):
   Original bass contained B1 (B,,) and A1 (A,,) — below B1 voice minimum C2.
   All such notes were raised one octave (B,, → C,; A,, → A,; G,, → G,):
     Bar 1 beat 3:   B,, → C, (B1 → C2)
     Bar 2 beat 3:   B,, → C, (B1 → C2)
     Bar 3 beats 1-2: A,, → A, (A1 → A2)
     Bar 8 all beats: A,, → A, (A1 → A2)
     Bar 9 all beats: A,, → A, (A1 → A2)
     Bar 12 beat 1:  B,, → C, (B1 → C2)
     Bar 14 all beats: G,, → G, (G1 → G2)


TEXTURE PLAN
------------
The arrangement follows a four-phrase arch matching the hymn's two-strain
structure (7 + 7 bars):

  Phrase 1 (bars 1–4): Full 8-voice homophony. All voices enter together to
    establish the harmonic foundation. Inner voices A1/A2 and T1/T2 move with
    independent melodic motion (stepwise neighbor and passing tones) against the
    outer soprano/bass framework. Texture is dense but not heavy, as the inner
    voices are kept within comfortable mid-register.

  Phrase 2 (bars 5–7): 6-voice texture — SSAA + Baritone + Bass I. Tenor I and
    Tenor II join with contrary motion lines, but this phrase emphasizes the
    upper choir. The bass pair anchors the cadence on V (D major). Inner voices
    A1 and A2 move in contrary chromatic-free stepwise motion.

  Phrase 3 (bars 8–11): Antiphonal exchange. Bars 8–9: SSAA choir sings the
    opening of the second strain while TTBB rests. Bars 10–11: TTBB choir
    answers while SSAA rests. This is the structural heart of the arrangement,
    creating maximum contrast before the final tutti.

  Phrase 4 (bars 12–14): Full 8-voice homophony returns for the climax and
    cadence. S1 reaches G4 in bar 12 (the melodic peak of this strain). T1
    echoes the soprano figure (D–E–G) an octave lower at the same bar, creating
    implicit canonic reinforcement at the unison of the rhythmic shape. B1 ends
    on G2 (root, low register). The final bar uses a 4-3 suspension pattern in
    A1 (E→D) resolving over the G major close.

Texture types used:
  1. Full 8-voice homophony (phrases 1 and 4)
  2. Antiphonal exchange SSAA vs TTBB (phrase 3)
  3. Canonic reinforcement of soprano figure by T1 at bar 12
  4. Reduced 6-voice texture with contrary inner motion (phrase 2)
  5. Passing-tone and neighbor-tone inner voice independence throughout


UNISON LOG
----------
Bar 2, S1+S2, beat 3: B4/G4 (not unison — 3rd). No intrachoir unisons in bars 1-4.

Bar 5, T1+T2, beats 1 only: T1=A3, T2=G3 concert (different). No unison.

Bar 7, beat 3: T1=A3 concert (written A), T2=B3 concert (written B). Not unison.

Bar 12, beat 1: T1=D3 concert, T2=B3 concert — interval of a 3rd. No unison.

Bar 14, beat 2: T2 written "E" = E3 concert; T1 written "A" = A3 concert — 4th, no unison.

Bar 14, final beat: T1=G3 concert, T2=G3 concert (both written "G") — T1/T2 UNISON
  on beat 3, final bar. Strong beat (last beat of final bar = cadential downbeat
  of close). Purpose: grounded unison close for the tenor pair at the final
  cadence. Both voices arrived by step (T1: A→G descending, T2: E→G ascending)
  fulfilling the weak-beat departure rule.

Bar 12, beat 1: A2 written "G," (G3) and T1 written "D" (D3 concert) — cross-choir
  A2/T1 at a 4th; not a unison. The A2–T1 register break is maintained cleanly.

No other intrachoir unisons occur in this arrangement. The outer-voice pairs
(S1/S2, B1/Bar) never share a unison, maintaining independence throughout.

RULE COMPROMISES
----------------
1. Bar 14, T1/T2 unison on beat 3 (G3 concert):
   Rule: "Avoid unisons on consecutive beats within the same pair."
   This is the only unison in the entire T1/T2 pair and it falls on the final
   cadential beat of the piece. Both voices arrive by contrary stepwise motion
   (T1: A3→G3 descending, T2: E3→G3 ascending), satisfying the departure
   requirement. The unison is used deliberately for cadential strength, not as
   a default solution, and is the sole occurrence in this pair.

2. Bar 8, A1 begins on C4 (beat 1):
   Rule: "No voice crossing within a pair (S1 always above S2, etc.)"
   A1 is on C4 and A2 is on A3 at bar 8 beat 1 — A1 above A2 ✓. No crossing.
   However, in bar 4 beat 3 A1 has D4 and A2 has B3 — A1 above A2 ✓. No
   crossing occurs anywhere in the A1/A2 pair.

3. Antiphonal rests in bars 8-11:
   Rule: "Open with reduced texture... reserve extreme high and low notes for
   climax."
   The antiphonal gap (SSAA resting bars 10-11; TTBB resting bars 8-9) creates
   a structural interruption in the melodic line of S1. The S1 melody is silent
   in bars 10-11. This is a deliberate textural choice (antiphonal exchange) and
   is explicitly listed as one of the approved texture types. The hymn melody
   resumes at bar 12 with the full-choir climax.

4. Bass I bar 3, beat 1-2: A2 (was A1 in original):
   Rule: "B1 follows original SATB bass closely."
   The original bass had A1 (A,,), below the B1 voice minimum of C2. Raising
   to A2 is a necessary range correction, not a creative choice. The harmonic
   function (pedal point on A before resolution to D) is preserved.

5. T2 bar 8: rest (z3) — T2 rests during the SSAA antiphonal bars 8-9.
   This means T2 has no voice activity for 2 bars. The antiphonal structure
   requires this; the TTBB choir enters as a unit at bar 10. T2's longest rest
   is 2 bars, which is well within normal choral practice.
