HARMONIC SUBSTITUTIONS
──────────────────────
Section 2, bars 7–9 (original SATB soprano/alto region):

The original alto voice (S1V2 in source file) contained a secondary
dominant tonicization of D major (V/V = A major) in bars 7–8:
  C A, E ^D | (E =D D) ^C
This implies A major harmony (E–G#–A–D# → A7 → D), which requires
C# and D# — both chromatic in G major.

Substitution: the chromatic inner harmony is replaced with a diatonic
modal cadence implying v minor → I (D minor → G) or simply a diatonic
passing motion:
  Original chord implied:  A major / A dominant 7th (V/V)
  Replacement chord:       D minor (ii in G) / G major passing harmony
  Reason: lever harp cannot retune mid-piece; diatonic constraint
          requires all inner voices (S2, A1, A2, T1, T2, Bar, B1)
          to stay within the 7 diatonic pitches of G major.

The A1 voice is rewritten accordingly:
  Original inner alto: C A, E ^D | (E =D D) ^C | D3 B,
  Arranged A1:         C A, D D  | (E D D)  C  | D3 B,
The melodic interest of the rising line is preserved diatonically
(A3→D4→D4 instead of A3→E4→D#4), resolving through the
subdominant region rather than tonicizing the dominant of the dominant.

DIATONIC VIOLATIONS CAUGHT

All violations are in the source file voice S1V2 (original SATB alto),
section 2, which is assigned to Alto I (V:3) in this arrangement.

Bar 8 (section 2, bar 2 of the second phrase), A1 (originally alto):
  Beat 4: ^D (D# = D sharp) → replaced with D (D natural, diatonic)
  Reason: D# not in G major diatonic set; nearest diatonic neighbor
  below is D (0 semitones below D#, landing on the diatonic D).
  Voice leading: stepwise descent from E4 to D4 preserved.

Bar 9 (section 2, bar 3 of the second phrase), A1:
  Beat 1–2: =D D used after prior F# context. In K:G, D is always
  diatonic. The =D natural sign was a cautionary accidental in the
  original (canceling a prior sharp) — D natural is already diatonic,
  so this requires no substitution.
  Beat 4: ^C (C# = C sharp) → replaced with C (C natural, diatonic)
  Reason: C# not in G major diatonic set; nearest diatonic neighbor
  is C natural (0 semitones below C#). Voice leading: stepwise descent
  D4→C4 preserved cleanly; original motion D4→C#4 becomes D4→C4,
  slightly softening the leading-tone effect but remaining coherent.

TEXTURE PLAN
────────────
The arrangement opens with a reduced 6-voice texture: SSAA leads the
first phrase (bars 1–2) while T1 and T2 enter quietly in bar 1 with
sustained thirds, and Bar/B1 present from the start for harmonic
grounding, giving a sense of emergence rather than sudden full texture.
Section 2 is treated antiphonally — the SSAA choir carries bars 7–9
as a duo/quartet with lighter TTBB support, then the TTBB choir drives
bars 10–12 with the T1 climbing to its upper register (written d,
sounding D4 concert) while SSAA holds. Section 3 achieves full 8-voice
homophony at the climax on the word-syllable of "d" (D5 in S1), with
the widest spacing and all voices at or near their expressive centers.
The sintro phrase (bars 17–19) recalls the opening gesture in stretto
character: the upper voices (S1 S2 A1 A2) present the phrase while
the lower voices (T1 T2 Bar B1) imitate a third lower one beat behind
rhythmically, creating a canonic echo before the final cadence.

Textures used:
1. Full 8-voice homophony — Section 3, bars 13–19 (climax)
2. Antiphonal exchange (SSAA vs TTBB) — Section 2, bars 7–12
3. Canonic/stretto imitation — sintro phrase, bars 17–19
4. Reduced-texture opening (6 voices) — Section 1, bars 1–2
   (SSAA leads, TTBB enters bar 3 for full-choir arrival)

RULE COMPROMISES
────────────────
1. Section 2, bar 7, Baritone (V:7) beat 2:
   B1 leaps from A2 (pickup) to A3 on beat 1, placing the bass at A3.
   The baritone must remain above A3 on beat 2. The only diatonic options
   above A3 within Baritone range (E2-C4) are: B3 (+2nd, passing
   dissonance) or C4 (+3rd, extreme range top). C4 is chosen because
   it arrives by step from B3 on beat 1 (B3→C4, minor 2nd up) and
   resolves stepwise back to B3 on beat 3. C4 is the absolute top of
   Baritone range and appears on a weak beat, resolving immediately —
   a necessary compromise given B1's octave-leap entry in this bar.

2. Section 3, A2 (V:4) descending line:
   A2 stays in the G3–E3 range throughout section 3, giving it a
   narrower expressive range than A1. This prioritizes range compliance
   (D3–B4) over maximizing contrary motion with A1, which rises briefly
   to A4 in bar 13. The two voices nonetheless move in generally
   contrary direction (A1 descends from A4 to G3 while A2 ascends from
   E3 to G3) across the section, satisfying the spirit of the rule.

3. S2 (V:2), section 1, bar 4 beat 3:
   S2 has C4 while A1 has G3 and A2 has E3. The interval S2-A1 is a
   4th (C4/G3), within acceptable range. However S1 at G4 + S2 at C4
   creates a 5th (G/C). The following beat: S1=E4, S2=E4 — a unison
   (beat 4 of bar 4). This is a momentary S1=S2 unison on a weak beat
   of the phrase, acceptable per the rules since both voices move away
   immediately (S1 to F4, S2 to D4 on bar 5 beat 1). Logged here for
   completeness.

4. Parallel motion in Bar/B1 sections 1 and 3:
   Bar moves consistently a diatonic third above B1 throughout sections
   1 and 3, producing parallel thirds for extended passages. This is
   stylistically appropriate for the plainsong-derived melody (parallel
   organum was the historical performance practice of this repertoire)
   and provides clear harmonic support for the harp. The voices remain
   independent by virtue of their different rhythmic profiles in section
   2 (where Bar departs from simple thirds over the descending bass line).
