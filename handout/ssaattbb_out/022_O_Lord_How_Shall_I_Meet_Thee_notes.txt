Words: Paul Gerhardt, 1653. Music: Johann Crüger, 1653.

down = Eb major). No lever raising is needed; the harp plays directly
from the default tuning.

Lever positions for Eb major:
  All levers DOWN (Eb storage tuning is Eb major)
  No levers need to be raised for this key.

All 8 voices remain comfortably within professional ranges in Eb major.
The nearest alternative keys (Bb major, 1 lever raised; Ab major, invalid)
were not needed.

TEXTURE PLAN
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
The arrangement opens with SSAA alone (Phrase A, first statement) in a
transparent 4-voice texture that establishes the hymn tune intimately
before the full ensemble enters. On the repeat of Phrase A the lower
four voices (TTBB) join homophonically, immediately doubling the range
downward and intensifying the sound. Phrase B introduces antiphonal
writing: the upper choir (SSAA) carries the melody while the lower choir
(TTBB) provides a countermelody a tenth below, creating a double-choir
call-and-response effect. Phrase C brings the full ensemble together in
8-voice homophony that builds steadily through a rising bass line and
soprano line (reaching Bb4 at the climax of bar 14) before the
penultimate bar deploys a cascading 4-3 suspension in the altos and
tenors to heighten the approach to the final cadence. The last chord
is a resonant spread Eb major triad across all 8 voices, fermata held.

Texture types used:
1. Solo choir (SSAA only) — Phrase A first statement
2. Full 8-voice homophony — Phrase A repeat and Phrase C
3. Antiphonal double choir (SSAA melodic / TTBB contrapuntal) — Phrase B
4. Suspension figure at cadence (4-3 in A1/T1 over Bb7) — bar 15

  phrase-end G2, B1 to D2). Acceptable per rules.

Bar 9, beat 1, V:7 (Baritone) and V:8 (Bass I): Ab2.
  Weak-beat entry unison on the downbeat of the phrase. Both voices move
  away by step on beat 2 (Bar to Bb2, B1 to G2). Purpose: grounding the

Final chord, V:5 (Tenor I) and V:6 (Tenor II): G3 and Eb3 respectively
  (no unison — these are different notes). V:3 (Alto I) and V:4 (Alto II)
  are G3 and Eb3 respectively (also no unison). The final chord is fully
  spread with no within-pair unisons.

No other within-pair unisons occur in the arrangement. The SSAA pair unison
allowance and TTBB pair unison allowance are used sparingly and only where
noted above.

RULE COMPROMISES
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
1. Bar 12 phrase-end, V:5 (T1): The phrase ends on D3 (sounding), and
   the Phrase C pickup begins on G3 (sounding) — a 4th upward, not a
   step. This occurs across a phrase boundary and the 4th is in the
   opposite direction from the preceding descent (bars 11-12 descend
   Eb3–D3–C3–Bb2–D3), which satisfies the spirit of the "resolve leaps
   stepwise in the opposite direction" rule even though the interval is
   not strictly a step. The phrase break provides sufficient separation.

2. Bar 7 (phrase A repeat), V:8 (B1) and V:7 (Baritone): Both voices
   arrive at F2 on beat 3 (unison). The rules permit Bar+B1 unisons
   occasionally; both voices move by step immediately after (Bar to F2
   phrase-end G2, B1 to D2). This is a weak-beat unison with stepwise
   contrary motion resolution, satisfying the unison guidance rules.

3. Bar 6 (phrase A repeat), V:8 (B1): reaches C2 on beat 3 (written C,
   in bass clef), the absolute minimum of the B1 range. It is approached
   and left by step (Eb2–C2–F2). This was chosen to provide a full
   descending bass line that implies the Fm harmony; an alternative note
   (e.g., Eb2 or F2) would have weakened the voice leading.

SPLITTING STRATEGY NOTES
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
S1 (V:1): Follows the original SATB soprano exactly throughout.
  No ornamentation was added since the tune is a direct Lutheran chorale
  with strong rhythmic identity. S1 carries no accidentals (none exist
  in the original).

S2 (V:2): Harmonizes a diatonic third below S1 in most bars, occasionally
  moving to a sixth below (bar 2, beat 3: S1=G4, S2=B3=Bb3, a 6th).
  Doubles S1 at the phrase cadences only where both arrive on Eb.

A1 (V:3): Recomposed as an independent melodic line using chord tones and
  passing motion. In Phrase A it doubles the original SATB alto line
  closely; in Phrases B and C it takes an independent descending path.

A2 (V:4): Moves generally in contrary motion to A1. In Phrase A bars
  1-3 it provides a lower third to A1. In Phrase B it doubles A2
  an octave below A2's Phrase A pitch area.

T1 (V:5): In Phrase A repeat, takes an inner passing line (Eb3-F3-G3-F3
  in bar 5) that differs from the soprano line, adding harmonic depth
  without duplication. In Phrases B and C it mirrors the soprano a
  tenth below.

T2 (V:6): Moves in contrary motion to T1 where possible. In Phrase B
  it descends while T1 descends more slowly, creating a gentle wedge
  close at the Phrase B cadence.

Baritone (V:7): Follows the original SATB bass line closely but an octave
  higher, harmonizing a third above B1. Stays in the Eb2-Bb2 register
  throughout for a warm baritone color.

Bass I (V:8): Follows the original SATB bass closely, staying in the
  C2-G2 register. The final chord places B1 on Eb2, grounding the
  ensemble on the root in a rich low position.
