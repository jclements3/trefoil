ARRANGER NOTES
Awake My Soul, An Offering Bring (OpenHymnal #279)
SSAATTBB arrangement for 8-voice ensemble and lever harp
Music: Thomas Ravenscroft, 'Melismata 22,' 1611. Words: Brian L. Penney, 2009.

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
TRANSPOSITION
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Original key: F major (1 flat).
Chosen key: F major (1 flat). No transposition required.
Semitones shifted: 0.

F major is within the valid range for lever harp in Eb storage tuning. Only one
lever adjustment is required: raise the Bb lever to produce Bb natural across
the instrument. All other levers remain down (at Eb storage pitch), giving the
diatonic set F G A Bb C D E.

Harp lever positions for F major:
  Ab lever: DOWN (string sounds Ab — unused in F major)
  Eb lever: DOWN (string sounds Eb — unused in F major)
  Bb lever: UP (string sounds Bb — the key's 4th degree, essential)
  F lever:  DOWN (F strings sound natural F from string tuning)
  C lever:  DOWN (C strings sound natural C from string tuning)
  G lever:  DOWN (G strings sound natural G from string tuning)
  D lever:  DOWN (D strings sound natural D from string tuning)

Note: The Eb storage tuning makes F natural (not Eb) the actual pitch of the
F strings at rest. The above "DOWN" positions reflect that F, C, G, D are
already at natural pitch in Eb storage; only Bb requires an active lever raise.

HARMONIC SUBSTITUTIONS
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

No full harmonic substitutions were required. The original harmony is largely
diatonic in F major. The chromatic element is the raised 6th degree (F#,
functioning as a transient leading tone to G in the context of a V of ii or a
Dorian inflection), which appears only in the melody (S1) and in one inner
voice. The melody instance is permitted freely. The inner voice instance was
resolved as a diatonic violation (see below) without requiring chord-level
reharmonization, since the surrounding chords (G minor / D minor functions)
remain available in diatonic F major.

DIATONIC VIOLATIONS CAUGHT
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Three instances of ^F (F#) appeared in the original inner voice S2V1 (which maps
to Tenor I in this arrangement). In each case F# was replaced with F natural, the
immediately available diatonic neighbor one semitone below. The surrounding voice
leading (stepwise motion in the bass, sustained harmony above) accommodates F
natural without creating an augmented melodic interval or a parallel fifth.

  Measure 2,  Tenor I, beat 4: ^F, (F#3) → F, (F3)
    Context: the line descends A3-Bb3-A3 and the original ^F, was a chromatic
    lower neighbor to G3. Replacing with F natural creates a clean diatonic
    descent A3-Bb3-A3-F3 implying a cadential motion to the dominant.

  Measure 6,  Tenor I, beat 3: ^F, (F#3) → F, (F3)
    Context: identical melodic gesture (B3-G3-^F3-D3 in original → B3-G3-F3-D3).
    The passage implies a passing motion through the V chord; F natural preserves
    the descent and keeps the harmony squarely on G minor / d minor without
    introducing secondary dominant color.

  Measure 10, Tenor I, beat 3: ^F, (F#3) → F, (F3)
    Context: literal repeat of measure 6. Same substitution, same reasoning.

No other accidentals appeared in inner voices.

TEXTURE PLAN
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

The piece is a short through-composed hymn tune (12 bars plus a one-beat pickup)
with an internal repeat (mm. 9-12 mirror mm. 5-8). The textural arc rises from
a lean, chorale-like opening to full 8-voice homophony at the final cadence.

Texture types employed:

1. FULL 8-VOICE HOMOPHONY — the dominant texture throughout, reflecting the
   congregational hymn character of the piece. All 8 voices move together in
   rhythmic unison, producing a broad, resonant sound that suits the antique
   modal flavor of the Ravenscroft original.

2. CALL AND RESPONSE (implied) — the repeated two-bar cadential cell (mm. 7-8
   and mm. 11-12, the "G ^F G" figure in S1) functions as a refrain-like
   response to the preceding 6-bar statement. The Baritone and Bass I hold a
   firm C4-to-Bb3 descent at this point while S1 sings the raised-6th figure,
   creating a clear outer-voice dialogue between treble and bass registers.

3. SUSTAINED INNER VOICES WITH MELODIC OUTER SHELL — in measures 5-6 and 9-10,
   A1 and T1 hold longer values (dotted and repeated notes) while S1 and B1
   carry the more rhythmically active material, approximating a solo/sustained-
   accompaniment texture within the homophonic frame.

Dynamic arc: begin at a moderate mezzo-forte with the full 8 voices blended but
not forced; build intensity across the middle section (mm. 5-8); withdraw
slightly at the repeat (m. 9) as if echoing; then gather to a confident,
resonant forte at the final cadence (mm. 11-12).

UNISON LOG
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

No within-pair unisons occur. All four voice pairs (S1/S2, A1/A2, T1/T2,
Bar/B1) maintain independent pitches on every beat of the arrangement.

Cross-choir unisons (A2/T1) do occur and are expected per the arranging brief:
  - A2 = D4 and T1 (concert) = D4 at several beats in mm. 3, 4, 9, and 10.
    These are strong-beat unisons at the register break, intentional for
    ensemble blend and tonal centering.

S1:
  Highest note: D5 (midi 74) at m1 beat 3 and m3 beat 3.
    This is a comfortable top for soprano, well within range.
  Lowest note: F#4 (midi 66), the raised 6th, at m2, m8, m12.
    Not a concern — this is the coloristic chromatic note in the melody.

S2:
  Highest note: A4 (midi 69) at scattered points (m1 beat 3, m3 beat 3).
    Comfortable for Soprano II; no concern.
  Lowest note: D4 (midi 62), throughout. Comfortable.

A1:
  Highest note: F4 (midi 65) — the most frequent pitch. No extreme notes.
  Lowest note: Bb3 (midi 58) at phrase endings (m7, m11). Comfortable.

A2:
  Highest note: D4 (midi 62) at several points. Comfortable for Alto II.
  Lowest note: G3 (midi 55) at m2 beat 2 and m4 beat 2. Low but acceptable;
    this is within the stated D3-Bb4 range.

T1 (concert pitch):
  Highest note: D4 (midi 62) at m1, m3 — the leading structural note.
    Comfortable for Tenor I (range top G4).
  Lowest note: F3 (midi 53) at m2 beat 4 (the substituted F natural). Fine.

T2 (concert pitch):
  Highest note: Bb3 (midi 58) at m2 beat 1, m4 beat 1 and 2. Comfortable.
  Lowest note: Bb2 (midi 46) at m1 beat 1 (pickup area).
    This is above the stated G2 minimum; no concern.

Baritone:
  Highest note: C4 (midi 60) at m3 beat 4, m4, m5, m7, and repeats.
    C4 is exactly at the Baritone top limit; these are structurally important
    beats (phrase peaks) so the upper register is appropriate.
  Lowest note: F3 (midi 53) at m2 beats 3-4, m6, m7 beat 3.
    Comfortable, well above the E2 minimum.

Bass I:
  Highest note: Bb3 (midi 58) at m3 beat 4, m5, m7. Comfortable.
  Lowest note: G2 (midi 43) throughout. The original source reached Bb1 and G1
    (organ pedal range); these have been raised one octave to G2 and Bb2 to
    bring them within professional bass vocal range (C2-A3). This does not
    affect the harmony since the bass line doubled at the octave above is still
    the lowest sounding vocal part.

RULE COMPROMISES
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

1. Dynamic arc / opening texture (Rule 7 — open with 4-6 voices):
   This piece is only 12 bars in a simple hymn-tune style. Opening with a
   reduced texture and then adding voices is impractical given the brevity and
   homophonic character of the original. The arrangement opens at full 8 voices
   from the pickup. The gain in congregational solidarity outweighs the textural
   arc ideal for this particular tune. A director wishing a reduced opening
   could assign mm. 1-4 to SSAA alone and bring in TTBB at m5.

2. Climax S1 pitch (Rule — S1 at G5-A5 for climax):
   The original soprano melody peaks at D5, with no phrase rising to G5 or A5.
   Inserting such a note would require a free cadenza or ornament not present in
   the source. The climax effect is achieved instead by full 8-voice density,
   the C4 peak in the Baritone, and the characteristic raised-6th (F#4) figure
   in S1 at the final cadence, which provides harmonic brightness without
   exceeding the tune's range.

3. Baritone upper note (C4, m3-m5 and repeats):
   C4 is the stated comfortable top for Baritone. Using it on multiple beats in
   consecutive measures is near the limit of constant comfort. However the C4
   notes fall on beats where B1 is at Bb3, making any lower Baritone note a
   voice crossing. The C4 is therefore structurally unavoidable unless the B1
   line is transposed down, which would place Bass I below its lower range limit.
   Conductors may wish to have the Baritone approach these C4 notes with a
   slight dynamic taper to avoid forcing.

4. Suspension at cadences:
   The original tune cadences with a simple stepwise approach (no prepared
   suspension written in source). This arrangement maintains the plain cadence
   to respect the source material's syllabic, congregational character. A
   4-3 suspension could be added by holding S2 on A4 at m8 beat 3 while
   S1 moves through F# to G4; this is left to the director's discretion.
