HARMONIC SUBSTITUTIONS
-----------------------
Bar 5, beat 3-4: Original alto implies a raised ^F# (F#) over a
  Gm–Am–Gm progression. The ^F# in S1V2 was treated as a secondary
  leading-tone tonicization. The accompanying harmony was kept as
  Gm → Am → Gm (diatonic) rather than introducing a D major chord
  (V of Gm), since D major is diatonic to F major and would have
  required no alteration — but the raised leading tone in the inner
  voice is the issue. The harmony remains Gm, and the ^F# is confined
  solely to S1.

Bar 9: Original inner voices contained chromatic passing material
  suggesting a brief C dominant 7th (with Bb in tenor). The progression
  was kept as Bb–F–Gm–C with all inner voices using only F-major
  diatonic tones. The C chord on beat 4 uses C–E–G–Bb (C dominant 7th)
  in the full ensemble, but the Bb is supplied by the diatonic scale
  of F major, so no substitution was required there.

Bar 10, beats 1-2: Original S1 has (^F G) — a half-step approach to G.
  This is treated as a chromatic passing tone in S1 only. The remaining
  seven voices harmonize with diatonic Gm → G (plain G minor), avoiding
  any secondary dominant implication. This keeps the harp doubling
  clean while S1's ^F# creates characteristic lever-harp color.


DIATONIC VIOLATIONS CAUGHT
---------------------------
The following accidentals appeared in the original SATB inner voices
and were replaced with the nearest diatonic neighbor in F major
(diatonic scale: F G A Bb C D E):

Bar 2, S1V2 (now distributed to S2/A1/A2):
  Original: ^F/ (F#, eighth note)
  Replaced with: G (whole step up, natural resolution)
  Reason: F# is non-diatonic in F major; G is the stepwise resolution
  already implied by the original melodic direction.

Bar 5, S1V2 (original alto, now S2/A1/A2):
  Original: (G ^F/E/) ^F2 — two instances of F#
  Replaced with: S2 → (G G/E/) G2; A1 → (G F/E/) F2
  Reason: Both F# replaced by G (natural neighbor above) or F (natural
  neighbor below). Voice leading to bar 6 is preserved.

Bar 7, S1V2 (original alto):
  Original: G G2 ^F (F# on beat 4)
  Replaced with: S2 → G G A G; A1 → E E D E
  Reason: ^F# on the final beat would resolve upward to G in bar 8;
  replacing with G (S2) or E (A1) preserves the resolution while
  remaining diatonic. S2 takes G as a unison with bar 8 arrival.

Bar 4, S2V2 (original bass):
  Original: F, A, G, _E, (Eb on beat 4)
  Replaced with: B1 → F, A, G, C, (C substituted for Eb)
  Reason: Eb is non-diatonic in F major. The beat 4 function is a
  passing chord leading to Dm/Gm; C (scale degree 5) substitutes
  smoothly and maintains the descending bass motion.

Bar 7, S2V1 (original tenor):
  Original: G, (_E D3/2) C/ — Eb passing tone
  Replaced with: T1 → G B A G (entirely recomposed as independent line)
  Reason: The Eb passing tone in the original tenor cannot be used in
  any voice except S1. The T1 line was recomposed using diatonic Gm
  arpeggiation (G–Bb–A–G) which implies the same harmony without the
  chromatic note.

Bar 9, S1V2 (original alto):
  Original: D G3/4F//_E3/4D// C — Eb in the run
  Replaced with: S2 → D G E E; A1 → Bb D C C
  Reason: Eb in the 32nd-note run was replaced by E natural (diatonic).

TEXTURE PLAN
------------
The arrangement opens with reduced texture (SSAA only, bars 1-1) to
establish the modal character of the hymn at piano dynamic, then adds
the tenor pair in bar 2 for a gradual swell to full 8-voice texture
by bar 3. The first section (bars 1-5) uses 4-voice + 6-voice + 8-voice
layering to build toward the fermata climax. The middle section (bars
6-10) alternates antiphonal exchange (SSAA vs TTBB call-and-response)
with full homophony, providing textural contrast at mezzo-forte. The
final section (bars 11-14) employs stretto imitation between the
soprano and tenor pairs (bar 11), call-and-response between S1 and B1
over sustained inner voices (bar 12), a broad sustained spread (bar 13)
reaching the widest register for the penultimate cadence, and a final
full 8-voice F major chord (bar 14) at forte.

Textures employed:
  1. Reduced entry (bars 1-2): SSAA + Tenor pair entering bar 2;
     bass pair entering bar 3 — gradual orchestration build.
  2. Full 8-voice homophony (bars 3-5, 10, 14): block chords with
     all voices together; primary texture at cadences.
  3. Antiphonal exchange (bars 4, 6, 9): SSAA choir answers TTBB
     choir on alternating beats or phrases.
  4. Stretto imitation (bar 11): Tenor pair imitates soprano pair
     at the unison (same written pitch, one octave lower sounding)
     at the sintro marking.
  5. Call and response between outer voices (bar 12): S1 and B1
     carry the melodic fragments while inner voices sustain harmony.

RULE COMPROMISES
----------------
Bar 3, Tenor pair: T1 and T2 share the same two pitches (G-Bb) in
  unison for both half notes. This is a deliberate textural effect at
  the first full-ensemble fermata point (bars 1-2 had them entering
  together), used to blend the tenor timbre into the texture. They
  separate to independent motion at bar 4.

Bar 9, Bar+B1: Both Baritone and Bass I carry the same line
  G,-E,-D,-E, in bar 9. This octave doubling between Bar and B1
  (a unison in pitch class) is used to anchor the imitative TTBB
  entry in bar 9. The voices are separated by register (Bar sounds
  at G3-E3-D3-E3, B1 at G2-E2-D2-E2), making this an octave in
  practice rather than a true unison. The bar is short and both
  voices diverge in bar 10.

Bar 2, T1+T2: T1 enters on D (half) then D D (quarters); T2 on
  Bb (half) then Bb Bb. Somewhat static for an independent melodic
  line, but serves to establish the inner harmony on a reduced-texture
  entry bar without overcrowding the texture. Both voices gain greater
  independence from bar 3 onward.
