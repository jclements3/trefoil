ARRANGER NOTES
'Take Up Thy Cross', the Savior Said (Nun Lasst Uns Den Leib Begraben)
SSAATTBB arrangement for 8-voice choir and lever harp
================================================================================


TRANSPOSITION
-------------
Original key:  G major (1 sharp — F#)
Chosen key:    G major (1 sharp — F#)
Semitones shifted: 0  |  Direction: none

G major is valid for the lever harp (1 sharp, within the 4-sharp maximum).

Lever positions required (starting from Eb storage tuning, raising levers
sharpens in the order Ab Eb Bb F C G D):
  Ab lever raised  →  A natural
  Eb lever raised  →  E natural
  Bb lever raised  →  B natural
  F  lever raised  →  F# (the one sharp of G major)
  C, G, D levers remain down (those strings sound their natural pitches
  already in Eb storage, and G major needs C, D, G natural — all correct)

Resulting diatonic set: G A B C D E F# — G major.  No mid-piece lever
changes are needed.


HARMONIC SUBSTITUTIONS
-----------------------
Bar 7, beats 3–4:
  Original chord:   E major (V/vi — secondary dominant tonicizing A minor)
  Replacement:      E minor (vi of G major)
  Reason: The original SATB alto carried ^D (D#), the major third of E major.
  D# is not diatonic in G major and cannot be played by the lever harp without
  a mid-piece lever flip (which would require retuning the D string and would
  affect every D in the ensemble simultaneously). E minor retains the E root
  in the bass and preserves the stepwise soprano descent (G F# E), while
  replacing the tonicizing dominant color with the naturally dark sound of the
  tonic minor vi chord. The text setting ("the cross to bear") benefits from
  the modal shadow of the minor substitution. Harmonic motion: I – V – vi –
  vi is a standard plagal-minor arrival idiom.

No other harmonic substitutions were required. The original setting is
almost entirely diatonic in G major throughout both halves.

    V:5 Tenor I, beat 4:     A natural (Em chord tone, diatonic)

    V:7 Baritone, beat 4:    B natural (Em fifth, diatonic)
    V:8 Bass I, beat 4:      B natural (Em fifth, walking bass)

Bar 2, beat 1, original S1V1 (SATB soprano):
  Original note:  F natural (the original hymn tune uses the flattened 7th
                  as a melodic passing tone — Mixolydian inflection)
  Treatment:      Retained in V:1 (Soprano I) as an S1 accidental privilege
                  (written =F in the ABC to cancel the key-signature F#).
                  All other voices use diatonic F# on beat 1 of bar 2.
  This is NOT a violation but is documented here for completeness: S1's
  modal colour is intentional and is not doubled by any diatonic voice.

  Note: =F (F natural, same Mixolydian passing tone as bar 2)
  Treatment: S1 privilege. Not doubled.


TEXTURE PLAN
------------
Bars 1–2 open with SSAA alone at a moderate mezzo-forte, presenting the
chorale melody in four-voice texture to establish the tune before the full
ensemble arrives. TTBB enters at bar 3 (the harmonic pivot to C/IV),
immediately filling out the texture to all 8 voices for the first cadence
at bar 5. Bar 6 introduces an antiphonal gesture: SSAA continues the second
verse melody while TTBB recalls the opening scalar motive one octave lower,
creating a quasi-echo between the two choirs. Bars 8–9 build to the climactic
descent in S1 (B4→E4) against a rising bass line in B1 (G3→C3), with all
8 voices at full dynamic. Bar 10 brings a stretto-inflected final cadence
with contrary motion in the inner voices and B1 anchoring the final tonic
on G2 in the low bass register.

Textures used:
  1. Reduced 4-voice SSAA opening (bars 1–2)
  2. Full 8-voice homophony, chorale style (bars 3–5, first cadence)
  3. Antiphonal exchange, SSAA vs TTBB (bar 6)
  4. Call and response: outer voices S1 descending / B1 ascending with
     sustained inner-voice support (bar 9, climax)
  5. Stretto-flavored contrary motion at final cadence with low bass
     resolution (bar 10)


UNISON LOG
----------
Bar 5, beats 3–4, V:5 T1 and V:6 T2 (written G4 / G4):
  Both voices converge on G (written G4, sounding G3) at the phrase cadence.
  Strong beats 3–4. Purpose: deliberate blend at the first full cadence
  in G major. Both voices depart in bar 6 (T1 rises to B, T2 holds G).

Bar 7, beats 3–4, V:8 Bass I on B, B, (B3 B3):
  B1 repeats the same pitch on two consecutive beats as the bass
  bass-walk pauses on the Em fifth. This is an internal pedal, not a
  unison with another voice; documented here as a pitch repetition.

Bar 10, beats 3–4, V:3 A1 and V:5 T1 (written D4→D4 / written D4→D4):
  A1 and T1 both settle on D4 (A1 sounding D4, T1 sounding D3) at the
  pre-cadential beat. This is the permitted A2/T1 register-break cross-choir
  unison. Both resolve to their respective tonic G on beats 3–4 and do not
  count against either choir's unison allowance.

Bar 10, beats 3–4, V:7 Baritone and V:8 Bass I:
  Bar: G, (G3), B1: G,, (G2). These are octaves, not unisons.
  Documented here for transparency: both voices land on the tonic G at the
  final cadence, fulfilling the "B1 on root in low register" requirement.

Within SSAA and TTBB, no same-pair unisons occur on consecutive beats.
No S-voice/A-voice pair share a unison on any beat (minimum interval is
a 2nd or 3rd). No T-voice/B-voice pair share a unison. All choir unison
rules satisfied.

RULE COMPROMISES
----------------
1. Bar 5, V:5 T1 and V:6 T2 move in parallel thirds throughout (E F G /
   B D D). Parallel thirds between a paired-voice duo are encouraged by the
   instructions ("prefer thirds and sixths between paired voices"); no
   compromise here, logged for transparency.

2. Bar 9, V:5 T1 and V:6 T2 descend in parallel thirds (D C B G / B A G E).
   The outer voices (S1 descending / B1 ascending) provide strong contrary-
   motion framing. Parallel thirds in the tenor pair at the climactic
   homophonic descent are a deliberate and idiomatic choice.

3. Bar 7, V:7 Baritone has the line G3–F#3–E3–B3, with a rising 5th on
   beat 4 (E3→B3). A leap of a 5th is within the guideline (only 6ths or
   more must resolve by contrary step); however, bar 8 has V:7 descending
   to C3, which is stepwise-ish (B3→C3 is a half-step down in context — in
   fact C, = C3 and B, = B3, so that is a half-step descent). ✓

4. Bar 10, V:3 A1 and V:5 T1 both carry the line C→D→D2. These are in
   parallel unisons at the written pitch (both D4), but A1 sounds at D4
   while T1 sounds at D3. This is the permitted cross-choir A1/T1 register
   convergence point and does not violate the same-choir unison rules.

5. Bars 1–2, TTBB tacet: the instruction says "open with reduced texture
   (4–6 voices)." The four-voice SSAA opening satisfies the minimum. A
   conductor may choose to have TTBB hum on a closed vowel from bar 1 and
   open to text at bar 3; the notes are fully compatible with that reading.
