ARRANGER NOTES — He Leadeth Me (SSAATTBB arrangement)
=======================================================
Source: Open Hymnal Project #202, words J. H. Gilmore 1862,
        music W. B. Bradbury 1864 ("He Leadeth Me").
Arranged for 8-voice SSAATTBB chorus + lever harp, 2026.


TRANSPOSITION
─────────────
Original key:  D major (2 sharps: F#, C#)
Chosen key:    D major (2 sharps) — no transposition required.
Semitones:     0 (no change).

Lever harp (Eb storage tuning): raise levers on F and C strings only.
  Ab lever: down (Eb)
  Eb lever: down (Eb)
  Bb lever: down (Bb)
  F  lever: UP   → F#
  C  lever: UP   → C#
  G  lever: down (G)
  D  lever: down (D)

All 8 voices remain comfortably within professional ranges in D major;
no range-forced transposition was necessary.


HARMONIC SUBSTITUTIONS
──────────────────────
No chromatic harmonies appear in the original SATB inner voices.
All chords in "He Leadeth Me" are diatonic in D major: I, IV, V, V7,
ii, and vi. No secondary dominants or modal mixture appear.
Therefore no harmonic substitutions were required.


DIATONIC VIOLATIONS CAUGHT
───────────────────────────
No accidentals appear in any of the original inner voices (alto, tenor,
bass) beyond those already in the D-major key signature. Every note in
the source is diatonic. No replacements needed.

All 8 voices in the arrangement are diatonic. S1 (Soprano I) carries
the original soprano melody verbatim; no chromatic ornaments were added.
All C and F naturals are excluded; only C# and F# appear (via key
signature). Voices 2–8 strictly diatonic throughout.


TEXTURE PLAN
─────────────
The arrangement opens with SSAA carrying the hymn melody in homophonic
texture (S1 soprano, S2 a third below, A1 independent alto line,
A2 in contrary motion to A1) while TTBB provides rhythmic and harmonic
support from the first beat — approximately 7–8 voices from the outset
but with SSAA leading the melodic energy (Verse 1). Verse 2 introduces
an antiphonal character: Bar (V:7) diverges from B1 (V:8) with its own
independent line rather than shadowing the bass at the third, while the
upper voices continue their melodic flow, creating a call-and-response
quality within the TTBB pairing. Refrain A moves to full 8-voice
homophony with maximum vertical spread and increased rhythmic energy
(the refrain has more stepwise motion in S1 and a higher tessatura).
Refrain B (the final statement) heightens this further with S1 touching
e' (E5) as the melodic apex, and the arrangement drives to the fermata
cadence with a stepwise descent in all voices converging on the D major
tonic chord, underpinned by a convergent contrary-motion effect in the
tenor pair. The four texture types used: (1) melody-led SSAA homophony
with supporting TTBB, (2) antiphonal SSAA/TTBB dialogue with independent
Bar line, (3) full 8-voice block homophony at both refrain statements,
(4) convergent contrary motion into cadential fermata.


UNISON LOG
──────────
Verse 1, bar 2 (bar beginning "B G G B" in S1), beat 1:
  A1 = G3, A2 = G3 — within-pair unison between Alto I and Alto II.
  Both voices arrive on G3 simultaneously at the start of the G-major
  chord. This is an acceptable occasional unison (the rule permits A1+A2
  unisons for blend); both voices move apart immediately after. Strong
  beat; intentional convergence on the tonic of the dominant harmony.

Refrain B, bar 2 (beats 1–4 "D D D D" in T1, "G, G, G, B," in T2):
  No unisons within pairs; T1 and T2 remain a fifth and sixth apart.

Final cadence bars (Refrain A and B, last bar):
  B1 (V:8) lands on A, (A2); Baritone (V:7) lands on A, (A2) as well
  in Refrain A last bar at the V cadence — within-pair Bar/B1 unison
  on A2. Strong beat, half-cadence emphasis. Both voices resolve to D
  at the phrase beginning of Refrain B.

A2 (V:4) and T1 (V:5) share the pitch D4 freely at several points
throughout the refrains (A2 written D = D4 concert, T1 written D =
D4 written = D3 sounding). These are cross-choir unisons at the
register break and are explicitly permitted.

RULE COMPROMISES
─────────────────
1. Original bass A,, (A1) raised to A, (A2) in B1 throughout.
   The original SATB bass part uses A,, (A1, contra-A) at every
   half-cadence (V chord). A1 is below the professional B1 floor of
   C2. Raising to A2 preserves the dominant harmony and keeps the
   bass grounded without any loss of harmonic function. This is the
   only departure from the original bass line.

2. A1/A2 within-pair unison in Verse 1 bar 2, beat 1 (G3):
   The contrary-motion splitting strategy produced a simultaneous
   arrival on G3 for both alto voices. This is permitted under the
   note-sharing rules (A1+A2 may share unisons occasionally for
   blend). Both voices diverge immediately (A1 rises to B3, A2 remains
   on G3) so no consecutive unison occurs.

3. T2 (V:6) written at or very near its floor (G, written = G2
   sounding) throughout the arrangement. The original tenor line in
   bass clef sits at F#2–D4, much of it below T2's sounding floor of
   G2. The T2 line was recomposed in contrary motion to T1 but
   consistently had to stay at G2–B2 sounding in the lower register to
   provide meaningful harmonic content below T1 without going below the
   floor. Directors should consider assigning this part only to singers
   with confident low-register support.

4. Bar (V:7) and B1 (V:8) Verse 2 final bar: Bar lands on F, (F#2)
   while B1 is on D, (D2), giving an interval of a minor third (D2–F#2
   inverted). Both voices cadence to F# and D respectively on the
   fermata tonic chord — standard root-position close voicing. No
   voice leading rules broken; noted for completeness.

5. V:3 (A1) in Refrain A uses `A, B, A,` in the final bar (3 beats):
   the A1 line echoes the S2 inner-voice figure rather than introducing
   independent material. This is a deliberate blend choice at the
   cadence (both A1 and S2 converge on a 6–5 figure into the tonic).
   No rule broken; noted as a texture compromise for cadential cohesion.
