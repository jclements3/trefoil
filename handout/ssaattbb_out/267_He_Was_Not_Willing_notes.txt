================================================================

Eb major is a valid lever harp key (exactly 3 flats, within the
3-flat maximum). All levers down = Eb storage tuning, which is the
natural rest position. No levers need to be raised for this key.

  Eb string: lever DOWN (Eb sounding)
  Ab string: lever DOWN (Ab sounding)

  All other strings (F G C D): lever DOWN
  Result: Eb major scale fully available on all strings.

DIATONIC VIOLATIONS CAUGHT
================================================================
One inner-voice accidental found in the original:

  Bar 16, Tenor (original S2V1, bass clef): "=A,3/4 B,// C/ B,3/2"
    Original note: A3 natural (A natural, canceling the Ab from key signature)
    This is non-diatonic in Eb major (Ab is the diatonic 4th degree;
    A natural is a chromatic alteration).
    Replacement: Ab3 (the diatonic 4th degree)
    New T1 line: Ab3[q.] Bb3[16] C4[8] Bb3[q.]
    Written in 8vb treble: A4[q.] B4[16] C5[8] B4[q.]
    Reason: Ab3 is one semitone below A3 natural and the nearest
    diatonic neighbor. The resulting figure Ab3–Bb3–C4–Bb3 outlines
    scale degrees 4–5–6–5 in Eb major — a beautiful stepwise resolution
    that actually strengthens the cadential approach.

The S1 (Soprano I) accidental in bar 16 — =A (A4 natural) — is
RETAINED. S1 is unrestricted and may sing accidentals freely.
The A natural in S1 is the expressive peak of the final cadence,
creating a characteristic major-sixth color against the harp's
diatonic Bb chord.

TEXTURE PLAN
================================================================
The arrangement opens with SSAA alone for bars 1–2, establishing
an intimate four-voice texture that invites the congregation into
the gentle dotted figure of the opening phrase. The TTBB choir
enters in full at bar 3, completing a rich eight-voice homophony
for the phrase cadence in bar 4. Bars 5–6 employ a six-voice
texture (SSAA plus T1T2, Baritone and Bass resting), creating a
lighter antiphonal reply to the opening. Bars 7–8 restore full
eight-voice homophony. Bar 9 presents a dramatic antiphonal
reversal: only TTBB sings the opening figure as an echo, while
SSAA rests; SSAA rejoins mid-bar 10 in a convergence effect.
Bars 11–12 restore the full ensemble with increased harmonic
motion in the inner voices, building toward the harmonic turn
at bar 13 (C minor, vi of Eb). Bars 13–14 sustain a full-texture
climactic plateau. Bars 15–16 bring the final dominant preparation
and cadential figure with all eight voices in stretto-like
rhythmic unanimity, S1 carrying the expressive A natural.

Texture types used:
  1. Full 8-voice homophony (bars 3–4, 7–8, 11–14, 15–16)
  2. Antiphonal exchange — SSAA vs. TTBB (bars 1–2 vs. 9,
     bars 5–6 with tenors added)
  3. Call and response between choir halves (bar 9: TTBB echo,
     bar 10: convergence)
  4. Reduced texture opening (bars 1–2: SSAA only, 4 voices)
  5. Progressive build — 4 → 8 → 6 → 8 → 4 → 8 → 8 voices

================================================================
RULE COMPROMISES
================================================================
1. Bar 3 and Bar 7: Baritone and Bass I share a unison on Eb3 for
   the full bar. Strictly the rule advises against sustained unisons
   as default filler. Rationale: the original SATB has bass and tenor
   both holding Eb3 in these bars (all four lower voices are on Eb3
   or Eb4), so the unison is inherited from the source texture.
   The paired voices diverge immediately in the following cadential bar.
   This is a mild compromise accepted for fidelity to the original.

2. Bar 10: SSAA voices enter mid-bar on the dotted-eighth/sixteenth
   figure's second beat. The notation uses z3/4 z// to create a
   partial rest equal to the dot+sixteenth duration, followed by
   the melodic entry. This is unusual notation but is necessary to
   align the antiphonal convergence precisely with the TTBB figures.
   No voice-leading rule is compromised; this is a notational
   observation only.

3. Bar 13, T1 and T2: The tenor pair uses the same pitches as the
   original SATB tenor (G3 concert written G4, and Eb3 concert written
   E4 for T2). T2 at Eb3 concert is the same pitch as A2 (Ab3) — these
   are a semitone apart, not a cross-choir unison. This near-unison
   creates a minor second cluster between A2 and T2 at bar 13 which
   adds harmonic intensity appropriate to the Cm harmony. Treated as
   an acceptable color rather than a voice-leading problem.
