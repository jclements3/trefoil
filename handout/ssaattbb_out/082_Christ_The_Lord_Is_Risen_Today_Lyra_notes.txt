====================================================================
TEXTURE PLAN
====================================================================
The arrangement opens with antiphonal texture (bars 1–4): the SSAA
choir presents the melody in a reduced 4-voice texture while TTBB
supports with parallel harmonic movement, creating dialogue between
the choirs. The second phrase (bars 5–8) brings all 8 voices together
in full homophony with added stepwise contrary motion in the inner
voices, building energy toward the climax.

Phrase B (bars 9–12) is the dramatic climax: SSAA soars with S1
reaching D5 and A4, while TTBB provides call-and-response beneath in
an antiphonal exchange. The =B natural in S1 (bar 11) creates the
characteristic lever harp color — the diatonic inner voices continue
on their diatonic strings while S1 sings the chromatic leading tone
against them.

The final phrase (bars 13–16) returns to full 8-voice writing with
a quasi-stretto texture. SSAA presents the theme; T1/T2 move in
stepwise descending lines beneath while Bar and B1 add contrary-motion
counterpoint. The final cadence employs a 4–3 suspension in S2 and
A1 (beat 1 of bar 16: F/C over dominant, resolving to E/C then F)
before the tonic chord closes with B1 on F2.

Texture types used:
  1. Antiphonal (SSAA vs TTBB) — bars 1–4 and bars 9–12
  2. Full 8-voice homophony — bars 5–8
  3. Call and response between outer voices (S1 high, B1 low)
     with inner voices sustaining — bars 9–12
  4. Stretto-like entries with contrary motion — bars 13–16
  5. Cadential suspension (4–3) — bar 16

Both voices move away immediately by step — T1 descends E4→C4 (third), T2 rises E4→F4 (step up) then D4. The unison falls on the strong beat of the phrase ending and provides a grounded sonority before both parts diverge. or sixths throughout. At the final cadence, S2 and A1 converge
toward the same region but do not reach unison:

  This is a cross-pair unison (S2 is in the S-pair, A1 in the A-pair),

====================================================================
RULE COMPROMISES
====================================================================
1. Bar 16 final chord: S2 (`F2` = F4 half) and A1 (`F2` = F4 half)
   land on the same pitch. This is a cross-pair unison between the
   S-pair and A-pair, which is explicitly permitted by the rules.
   The compromise is that S2 ends on F4 (slightly low for its comfort
   range of A3–F5 top) rather than D4, but F4 is still well within
   range and provides a clean tonic triad voicing in the final chord.

2. Bar 13, phrase C, S2: `d d f f` (D4 D4 F4 F4) — this is identical
   to phrase A, giving the return phrase a traditional re-statement
   quality. The instruction encourages the final phrase to use stretto.
   The stretto effect is achieved primarily in bars 15–16 with the
   offset contrary motion in the TTBB voices (T1/T2 descending in
   contrary motion, Bar ascending) rather than a strict temporal stretto.
   This was chosen because the repeat of phrase A at bar 13 does not
   provide new melodic material to stagger canonically.

3. Phrase A, all voices: The SSAA and TTBB choirs both move in parallel
   thirds/sixths throughout bars 1–4 without strong contrary motion
   between choirs. This was accepted because bars 1–4 establish the
   antiphonal texture simply, and contrary motion is built up
   progressively in the repeat (bars 5–8) and climax (bars 9–12).
   The instruction to "not open the arrangement at full range" is
   honored — extreme high/low notes are reserved for phrase B.
