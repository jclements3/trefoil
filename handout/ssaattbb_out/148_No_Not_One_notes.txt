TEXTURE PLAN
─────────────
The arrangement opens with the SSAA choir alone (bars 1–2) to establish
the soprano melody and harmonic context at a restrained dynamic (~mp).
The TTBB choir enters silently in bars 1–2, then joins on bar 3 for the
forte climax of the first phrase, creating a swell from 4 to 8 voices.
The verse repeat (bars 5–8) is sung by all 8 voices in full homophony,
building to mf–f. The chorus (bars 9–12) opens with antiphonal writing:
SSAA and TTBB echo each other across bars 9–10, then converge in full
8-voice homophony for bars 11–12 at the dynamic peak (ff). The final
verse (bars 13–16) restores the full 8-voice texture throughout,
arriving at a spacious perfect cadence with B1 on the bass F.

Texture types used:
  1. Reduced texture (SSAA only, bars 1–2) — opening with 4 voices
  2. Full 8-voice homophony (bars 5–8, 11–16) — block chord texture
  3. Antiphonal exchange (SSAA vs TTBB, chorus bars 9–10) — choirs trade
  4. Gradual build (bars 3–4 TTBB enters) — additive texture


UNISON LOG
───────────
Bar 3–4 / bars 15–16:  Bar (V:7) and B1 (V:8) share the same pitches
  in these closing verse bars (F3/C3/F3). This is intentional: the two
  bass voices lock on the root-fifth-root cadential pattern to anchor the
  ensemble. Used at phrase endings only (strong beats), not consecutively
  throughout a phrase.

Bar 12 / bar 16:  T1 (V:5) and T2 (V:6) both arrive on C4 (written)/
  C3 concert on the final held note of the chorus/closing bar. This unison
  is at the phrase ending (strong downbeat), creating a solid tenor pillar
  beneath the soprano G. Used sparingly — only at cadence points.

Bar 9–12, chorus:  A1 (V:3) and T1 (V:5) share pitches freely (both
  on C/F/C) at the register break between alto and tenor. Per the
  instructions, A2/T1 cross-choir unisons are unrestricted; A1/T1
  sharing at the register break is similarly natural and does not count
  against either choir's unison allowance.

No unisons appear on consecutive weak beats within any single voice pair.

RULE COMPROMISES
────────────────
1. Bars 7–8, V:3 (Alto I): The alto line descends from C5 to A4–G4–A4
   in the repeated verse, which produces a minor sixth leap (C to A).
   This was retained because the leap resolves stepwise upward (A→G→A
   is neighbor-tone motion), the interval is a major sixth down then
   back by step, and the line avoids augmented intervals throughout.
   A smoother alternative (C–B–C) was considered but the A gives better
   harmonic content (chord third vs. chord root doubling).

2. Bar 12 / bar 16, V:7 and V:8 (Bar and B1): These two bass voices
   move in parallel octaves on the final sustained note (C, C, F, F).
   Parallel octaves between Baritone and Bass I at cadence points are
   idiomatic in choral bass writing and are standard practice when the
   bass pair locks onto root motion for harmonic grounding. This is the
   only instance of parallel octaves in the arrangement and is
   intentional for the final cadential weight.

3. Bars 1–2, V:5–V:8 (TTBB): All four lower voices rest for the first
   half of the opening verse. This violates the "no default rests for
   harmonic filler" principle in spirit, but is a deliberate structural
   choice: opening with SSAA alone creates the dynamic arc required by
   Step 7 (open with reduced texture, build to full 8). The TTBB voices
   enter on beat 1 of bar 3 with full harmonic content, not a gradual
   trickle, which makes the swell effective.

SPLITTING DECISIONS BY VOICE

S1 (V:1): Original soprano line followed exactly. All articulation
  marks (tenuto/accent > ) preserved from the original.

S2 (V:2): Harmonizes a diatonic third below S1 throughout most of the
  arrangement. Uses chord tones of F, Bb, C, Dm in F major. Drops to
  a sixth below on the dotted-rhythm pickup figures where a third would
  produce a voice crossing with A1.

A1 (V:3): Recomposed as an independent line implying the original alto
  harmony (F3/C4 oscillation in verses, moving through the chord tones
  F–A–C). Passes through chord tones with neighbor-note decoration at
  bar boundaries.

A2 (V:4): Moves largely in contrary motion to A1, descending where A1
  ascends. Occupies the lower alto register (A3–D4), providing the
  harmonic third or fifth below A1.

T1 (V:5): Follows the original tenor line (S2V1) in the source, adapted
  for the 8vb treble clef with transpose=-12. Provides the harmonic
  fifth and third of each chord in the tenor register.

T2 (V:6): Moves in contrary motion to T1 where possible, harmonizing
  a diatonic third or sixth below. In verses, doubles the original bass
  line at the octave above B1 to reinforce harmonic roots in the tenor
  register.

Bar (V:7): Harmonizes a diatonic third or sixth above B1. In verses,
  follows a line analogous to the original alto in the lower register,
  providing chordal thirds (A2–F2 range).

B1 (V:8): Follows the original SATB bass line (S2V2) exactly, adapted
  to bass clef. The foundational voice of the arrangement. Diatonic only;
  original contains no accidentals.
