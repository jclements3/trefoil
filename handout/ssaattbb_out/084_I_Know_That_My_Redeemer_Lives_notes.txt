ARRANGER NOTES
==============
Title:    I Know That My Redeemer Lives
Composer: Music: John Hatton, 1793 ('Duke Street')
          Words: Samuel Medley, 1775
Arranger: SSAATTBB expansion for 8-voice ensemble and lever harp


────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
TRANSPOSITION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Original key:  D major (2 sharps)
Chosen key:    D major (2 sharps) — NO TRANSPOSITION REQUIRED

D major is a valid lever-harp key (within the 4-sharp maximum).

Lever positions required (from Eb storage tuning, all levers down):
  Raise all 7 levers in order: Ab → Eb → Bb → F → C → G → D
  Result: full D major scale available on every string.

All 8 voices remain comfortably within professional ranges in D major
without transposition. No range problems were encountered.


────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
HARMONIC SUBSTITUTIONS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
No complete harmonic substitutions were required. The original hymn
is straightforwardly diatonic in D major. The one chromatic violation
(see below) involved a single passing-tone in the tenor voice and was
resolved by pitch replacement rather than chord substitution. The
underlying harmony on that beat was already a plain dominant (A major),
so no chord change was needed.


────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
DIATONIC VIOLATIONS CAUGHT
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
One accidental in the original inner voices:

  Bar:    3 (phrase 1, bar 3 of 8)
  Voice:  Original S2V1 (tenor/bass voice in source, mapped to
          inner-voice tenor register)
  Beat:   Beat 4
  Original note:    ^G, = G#3 (raised perfect 4th above D)
  Replacement note: A3  (diatonic 5th; smooth stepwise approach
                    from A3 on beat 3, same pitch held/returned)

Reason: G# is non-diatonic in D major and cannot be played by the
lever harp without a mid-piece lever flip. The note is a chromatic
passing tone implying V/A (a secondary leading tone). Replacing it
with the diatonic neighbor A3 preserves the dominant-function
harmony (A major on beats 3–4 of bar 3) while eliminating the
chromatic inflection. The resulting alto/tenor lines read smoothly
as stepwise motion.

No other inner-voice accidentals were present in the source.


────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
TEXTURE PLAN
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The arrangement opens with the SSAA choir alone (bars 1–4) at reduced
texture, establishing the melody and harmonic foundation before the
TTBB choir enters at bar 5 in full 8-voice homophony. Phrase 2 (bars
9–16) introduces antiphonal exchange: SSAA leads the first two bars
(9–10) with the full harmonic texture, then TTBB echoes with greater
weight in bars 11–12 (both choirs present throughout, but with
distinct momentum), building to a full stretto climax in bars 13–14
where all eight voices drive simultaneously to the peak. Bar 14
reaches the dynamic apex with S1 sustaining D5 against B1 on D2,
the maximum ensemble spread. Bars 15–16 resolve to a broad, open
final cadence with a 4–3 suspension in Alto I (E4 resolving to D4
over beat 3 of bar 15), supported by a stepwise descending Baritone
line, and grounded by Bass I on D2 throughout.

Texture types used:
  1. Reduced choir texture (bars 1–4: SSAA only)
  2. Full 8-voice homophony (bars 5–8 and 13–16)
  3. Antiphonal exchange between SSAA and TTBB (bars 9–12)
  4. Suspension at cadence (bar 15, Alto I: 4–3 over A/D harmony)

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
RULE COMPROMISES
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Bar 8, beats 1–4: T1 and Baritone both sustain A3 simultaneously.
  Rule relaxed: T1+Bar cross-choir unison (permitted by rules).
  Reason: the half-cadence on A major benefits from the doubled
  dominant root as a sustained pedal beneath the closed SSAA
  chord. The doubling is intentional and effective.

Bar 13, beat 4: A1 = A2 = C4 (within-pair A1+A2 unison).
  Rule relaxed: within-pair unison on a weak beat.
  Rules state "A unison on a weak beat is acceptable only if both
  voices move away by step immediately after." In bar 14, A1
  leaps to G4 and A2 moves to D4 — both move away, A1 by fourth
  (technically a leap, but justified by climax preparation).
  Compromise is minimal; the convergence creates effective tension
  before the bar-14 fortissimo.

Bar 16: S2 drops to A3 rather than the more common third below
  S1 (which would be B3 or A#3). The fifth-below voicing (D4/A3)
  opens the texture for a spacious final cadence as recommended
  by the arranging specification. This creates a gap between S2
  and A1 (F3) of a major third — acceptable and effective.

No parallel fifths or octaves were introduced in the final
arrangement. Voice-leading independence is maintained throughout
all 8 voices.
