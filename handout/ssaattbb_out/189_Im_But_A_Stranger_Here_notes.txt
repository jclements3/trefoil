HARMONIC SUBSTITUTIONS
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
No full harmonic substitutions were required. The original SATB harmony
is diatonic in G major throughout except for the chromatic inner voice
inflections documented below. All accidentals in inner voices were resolved
to diatonic neighbors without requiring chord substitution; the underlying
harmonic rhythm and chord identity are preserved.

The =F natural in bar 13 (original bass, S2V2) is a chromatic passing
tone from the dominant area (D) back toward the subdominant. Replacing it
with F# (the diatonic 7th scale degree) preserves the cadential gesture
as a V–IV–I motion rather than requiring a chord substitution.

TEXTURE PLAN
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
The arrangement opens with four solo voices (S1, A1, T1, B1 — one per section)
in a spare, chorale-like texture that establishes the hymn melody and bass line
without yet revealing the full ensemble. Bars 3–4 add S2 and A2 (six voices),
and bars 5–8 deploy all eight voices in full homophony, building dynamic weight
through the chromatic second phrase. Bars 9–10 strip back to SSAA only for an
antiphonal upper-choir statement of the repeat phrase; bars 11–12 answer with
TTBB plus the soprano melody for a darker, lower-register color. Bars 13–16
reunite all eight voices for the climactic final phrase, with S1 ascending to E5
while B1 descends to G2, giving maximum spread at the peak. The final cadence
employs a G major chord with added sixth (E4 in S2) for warmth.

Texture types used:
  1. Reduced-voice opening (bars 1–2): 4 voices, one per section — solo/duet feel
  2. Progressive build (bars 3–4): 6 voices with S2 and A2 entering
  3. Full 8-voice homophony (bars 5–8): all voices, maximum density
  4. Antiphonal exchange (bars 9–12): SSAA vs TTBB choirs alternating
  5. Full climax with call-and-response (bars 13–16): S1 leads at extreme high,
     B1 anchors at low G2, inner voices weave independent melodic lines

UNISON LOG
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
Bar 5, beats 1–2 (half note): A1 and A2 share G3 (written G, in both V:3 and V:4).
  Pair: A1+A2. Beat: strong (beat 1). Purpose: phrase opening blend at entry
  of full texture. Both voices diverge immediately: A1 moves to B3, A2 stays G3.

Bar 8 (whole note): S1 and S2 share D4.
  Pair: S1+S2. Beat: whole note at phrase-end cadence. Purpose: deliberate blend
  and emphasis on the D major half-cadence. Unison resolves to a third (S1=G4,
  S2=E4) at bar 16.

Bar 16 (whole note): A2 (B3) and T1 concert (B3) share a pitch.
  Cross-choir unison at the natural register break — explicitly permitted.
  Purpose: warm blend on the root-position final cadence.

No S1/S2 unison on consecutive beats occurs. No A voice ever unisons with an S
voice except where noted above (bar 8 S1=S2=D4, which is a within-pair unison,
not cross-pair). No T/B cross-pair unisons within their respective choirs except
the documented A2/T1 cross-choir unison at bar 16.

RULE COMPROMISES
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
1. Bar 2, T1 concert (A3) and B1 (D3): parallel perfect fifths on beats 1–2.
   The original SATB (S2V1 and S2V2) already contains this fifth (A, and D,
   on beats 1–2), so the parallel fifths are inherited from Sullivan's original
   harmonization. Altering the tenor would contradict the original voice leading
   and destroy the characteristic sound of this progression. Retained as is.

2. Bar 4, A2 = D3 (whole note): this is the absolute minimum of A2's stated
   range. It is used at a full cadence (G major) with strong harmonic support
   from all other voices. The D3 completes the G–B–D triad in the inner voices
   without any gap. Acceptable as a phrase-end cadence note; monitor in
   rehearsal for audibility in the lower chest register.

3. Bar 8 S1/S2 unison D4 (whole note): The unison rule states S1+S2 may share
   unisons "occasionally for blend or emphasis." This is the second phrase
   cadence and serves as a half-cadence arrival on D before the antiphonal
   section begins. Used once per formal section boundary; not consecutive.

4. Final bar added-sixth chord (S2=E4, S1=G4, A1=D4, A2=B3, T1 concert=B3,
   T2 concert=G3, Bar=B2, B1=G2): the Gadd6 sonority rather than a pure G
   triad is a deliberate professional color choice, not a rule violation. The
   sixth (E) in S2 provides brightness and avoids a static octave stack. If
   a traditional pure triad is preferred, S2 may sing D4 (unison with A1,
   allowed at final cadence) or B3 (lowest comfortable S2 note, moving below
   A1=D4 which would be a voice cross — not recommended). E4 is the preferred
   option.

5. Bar 13, V:7 (Baritone), beat 1: A3. The baritone's range top is C4
   (comfortable A3). A3 is within the comfortable zone. No compromise here,
   noted for completeness.

6. Bars 9–12 antiphonal rests: the TTBB choir rests for two full bars (9–10)
   and the SSAA choir (except S1) rests for bars 11–12. This creates a gap
   in harmonic support for individual voices but is intentional for the
   antiphonal texture plan. The S1 melody carries the harmonic implication
   through both sections. Conductors should ensure the entering choir is
   well-cued at bar 11 and bar 13.
