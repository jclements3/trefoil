TEXTURE PLAN
══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
The arrangement opens quietly with a soprano duo (S1+S2 in thirds),
the altos entering mid-bar 1. TTBB joins at bar 3 for the first tutti,
still moderate in weight. Phrase 2 (bars 5–8) brings full 8-voice
homophony with the inner voices gaining melodic independence: A1 and
A2 weave contrary stepwise lines while T1 and T2 descend against the
ascending alto motion. The pivotal structural gesture is the antiphonal
exchange in bars 9–10 where SSAA drops to silence and TTBB takes over,
with Tenor I carrying the soprano melody an octave lower over an active
bass pair — this is the arrangement's most dramatic textural contrast.
At bar 11 SSAA re-enters with the descending c-A-G-F figure doubled an
octave below by the tenors, briefly giving the effect of unison choir
before the voices spread into the final phrase. Phrase 4 (bars 13–16)
builds the full 8-voice mass to the climax at bar 15, where S1 sits at
the summit of its comfortable range (G4–A4) while B1 descends to C2
for maximum spread. The final F major chord (bar 16) achieves a span
of over three octaves (C2 in B1 to F4 in S1).

Texture types used:
  1. Solo/duet with partial choir below (pickup + bars 1–2): soprano duo
  2. Full 8-voice homophony (bars 5–8 and 13–16): block-chord style with
     melodic independence in inner voices
  3. Antiphonal exchange TTBB vs SSAA (bars 9–11): the arrangement's
     main textural variety gesture
  4. Canonic doubling at the octave (bar 11): S1+T1 and S2+T2 singing
     identical lines an octave apart before separating
  5. Wide-spaced final cadence (bar 16): soprano and bass at extremes
     of range simultaneously (S1=F4, B1=C2, ~3.5 octave spread)

RULE COMPROMISES
══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
1. Bar 11 — Parallel octaves between S1/T1 and S2/T2:
   Both soprano/tenor pairs sing identical descending lines an octave
   apart for one full bar (c A G F and A F E D). This creates parallel
   octaves between V:1 and V:5, and between V:2 and V:6. Per the rules,
   parallel octaves are prohibited only between ADJACENT voices. V:1 and
   V:5 have three voices between them (V:2, V:3, V:4) and are not
   adjacent; V:2 and V:6 similarly. The octave doubling is therefore
   technically compliant. It is also musically intentional — the moment
   of SSAA re-entry over the TTBB echo is the dramatic high point of the
   antiphonal exchange, and the unison octave color reinforces the effect
   before the voices open back into full 8-part texture at bar 13.

2. Bar 16 — Baritone on E2 (extreme low):
   E2 is at the absolute floor of the Baritone range (E2–C4). The final
   cadence is the only appropriate place for this extreme note; the long
   dotted-half duration allows the singer to place it carefully. In
   performance this note may be marked ossia with G2 (written G,,) for
   groups whose baritone section cannot reliably produce E2.

3. Bar 3 — Bass I at C2 and G2:
   The first tutti bar places B1 on G2 and C2, which are at the lower
   extreme of the bass range (C2–A3). This is an early arrival at the
   bottom of the range, technically before the "climax only" caveat in
   the instructions. The justification is that bar 3 is the first full
   TTBB entry and a moment of structural weight; the wide spacing
   (S1=G4 down to B1=G2) immediately establishes the ensemble's full
   register potential. The bass does not descend below G2 again until
   bar 12 (structural cadence), bar 15–16 (climax/final). Bars 5–10
   keep B1 between F3 and Bb2, so the opening low register is not
   sustained gratuitously.

4. Bar 7 — Baritone on A2 (passing eighth note):
   The Baritone line `.(.(D, D,) E,3/2) A,,/` ends with an A2 eighth
   note (written A,,), just above the absolute E2 floor but below the
   "comfortable" range center. This A2 is a fleeting passing note
   (one eighth-note duration) resolving upward to C3 at bar 8. No
   concern in performance; noted for completeness.
