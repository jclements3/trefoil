HARMONIC SUBSTITUTIONS
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
Bar 7: The original SATB implied a V/vi harmony (E major chord, containing G#)
  in the tenor voice (S2V1: C A, ^G,3/2 A,/). This tonicization of B minor
  was replaced with a diatonic reharmonization using vi (B minor): B D F# A.
  The melody note E4 in S1 (scale degree 2 of D major, diatonic) is retained.
  The B minor substitution (vi for V/vi) follows the rule "V/vi → III or v"
  — here v (B minor) is used as the nearest diatonic chord by root motion.

  Original chord: E major (V/vi), implied by G# in tenor.
  Replacement:    B minor (vi), using pitches B D F# A.
  Reason:         G# (G-sharp) is non-diatonic in D major; the lever harp
                  cannot provide it without a mid-piece lever flip.

No other complete harmonic substitutions were required. All remaining chords
(D, G, A, Bm, Em) are diatonic in D major.

DIATONIC VIOLATIONS CAUGHT AND REPLACED
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
Three accidentals in the original inner voices were replaced with diatonic
neighbors:

1. Bar 6, Tenor (S2V1), beat 2: ^G, (G#3) → G3 (G natural, scale degree 4).
   Context: .(F, ^G,) A, A, — the G# was a chromatic neighbor to A.
   Replacement: The tenor voices (T1, T2) rest in bars 6–7 (antiphonal
   texture). The G# figure is not reproduced. When the TTBB choir enters
   at bar 8, the harmony has resolved to A major using only diatonic tones.

2. Bar 7, Tenor (S2V1), beat 3: ^G, (G#3) in C A, ^G,3/2 A,/ → replaced
   by B minor reharmonization (see Harmonic Substitutions above). T1 and T2
   rest through bar 7; the SSAA choir uses only diatonic pitches B D F# A.

3. Bar 13, Tenor (S2V1), beat 4: =C (C natural, C3) → B3 / B2 (diatonic
   scale degree 6). The original tenor line (F, G,) A, =C contained a C
   natural, which is non-diatonic in D major (the key requires C#). The
   T1 line uses E3 at that beat; T2 uses B2, both diatonic.
   Bar number: 13. Voice: Tenor (T1 = E, T2 = B). Original: C natural.
   Replacement: T1 → E3, T2 → B2 (diatonic passing motion resolving
   from A3 downward).

Note on bar 6 S1 c (lowercase): In K:D, `c` = C#5 (diatonic, major 7th
of D). The note is NOT a chromatic accidental. S1 bar 6 is entirely
diatonic: D5 D5 C#5 A4.

Note on bar 8 original tenor =G (G natural): G is diatonic in D major
(scale degree 4). The natural sign in the original merely cancels the
previous ^G#; no violation exists. This note is retained in spirit via
the T1=E3/T2=A2 voicing of the A major chord.

TEXTURE PLAN
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
The arrangement opens quietly with SSAA alone (bars 1–2) on the first
"Holy, Holy, Holy," establishing a transparent, ethereal four-voice texture
before the full ensemble enters in bar 3 at "Lord God Almighty." An
antiphonal exchange divides the hymn at the structural midpoint: SSAA
sings bars 6–7 alone, the full choir unites in bar 8 on a sustained A major
chord, and TTBB replies alone in bars 9–10 (the melodic repeat). The climax
builds from bar 11 onward with the full 8-voice texture; bar 13 places S1
at D5, the highest point of the arrangement. The stretto cascade of bar 15
(each pair entering a beat apart on the falling scale) leads to the final
D major cadence with the ensemble spread from D3 (B1) to D5 (S1).

Texture types used:
  1. Reduced 4-voice texture (SSAA, bars 1–2): Solo choir opening.
  2. Full 8-voice homophony (bars 3–5, 11–14): Block-chord hymn style.
  3. Antiphonal exchange (SSAA bars 6–7, TTBB bars 9–10):
     The two choirs alternate across the structural midpoint.
  4. Call and response between outer voices (bar 5): S1 and B1 lead
     rhythmically while inner voices provide harmonic support.
  5. Stretto entries at cadence (bar 15): Each pair (S, A, T, Bar/B)
     enters in a descending cascade on the penultimate bar.

RULE COMPROMISES
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
1. Bar 10, Tenor II and Baritone (cross-choir): T2 (written C E D F,
   concert C#3 E3 D3 F#3) and Bar (C, E, D, F, = same concert pitches)
   double each other exactly throughout bar 10. This is permitted by the
   cross-choir unison rule and was chosen deliberately to give the TTBB
   antiphonal section a warm, blended sound as it fills in harmonically
   below T1. The doubling was not used as a default solution but as a
   conscious textural choice for the antiphonal reply.

2. Bar 11, Tenor I and Baritone (cross-choir): T1 (concert G3 A3 B3 C4)
   and Bar (G, A, B, C = same concert pitches) double each other exactly.
   Again permitted by cross-choir rules. The doubling mirrors bar 3 and
   gives the climax restatement structural weight. Bar diverges from T1 in
   subsequent bars as the harmony shifts.

3. Bar 14, T1 + T2 unison (beat 3, B2 concert): The T pair shares a
   unison on beat 3, a secondary strong beat. The rule discourages unisons
   on strong beats unless for emphasis. Here both voices move away by step
   immediately (T1 → C#3, T2 → A2), satisfying the "stepwise resolution"
   condition. Retained for the TTBB cadential emphasis paralleling A1+A2.

4. Bar 5, S2 beats 1 and 3 (A4 and A4): S2 forms a perfect fourth below
   S1 (S1=A4, S2=F#4 = major third; but S1=A4, S2=E4 = perfect fourth on
   beat 3). The arranging guide prefers thirds and sixths. The fourth arises
   from the inner melodic line of S2 (F#–E–F#–E) and is musically idiomatic
   in this hymn style; it was retained over an alternative that would have
   required S2 to skip back by a minor third.
