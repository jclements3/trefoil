TEXTURE PLAN
------------
The arrangement opens with the SSAA choir alone (Bar 1), establishing the chorale
melody in four-part texture while the bass voices (Baritone and Bass I) provide
harmonic grounding from the downbeat. The tenor voices enter gradually in the
second half of Bar 1, growing to full 8-voice homophony by Bar 5 (the repeat of
the opening phrase), which marks the textural climax of the first section.

The middle section (Bars 2-7) alternates between full homophony and antiphonal
dialogue: the step-descent passages (Bars 8-9) feature a call-and-response pattern
where S1 states the descending figure and the lower voices echo and support it.
Bar 11 is the dynamic climax with all 8 voices in full homophony, S1 at C5 and
B1 at C2, spanning the maximum ensemble range. The final cadence (Bar 12) employs
suspensions in S2 (4-3), A1 (4-3), and T2 (oblique), resolving to a spread C
major chord with B1 on the root.

Texture types used:
  1. Full 8-voice homophony: Bars 5, 6, 11, 12
  2. Reduced opening (4-6 voices building): Bar 1 (SSAA+Bass opening, Tenors late)
  3. Antiphonal (implied): Bars 2-4 (S1 melody against TTBB support)
  4. Call-and-response S1 vs lower voices: Bars 8-9 (step-descent passages)
  5. Suspensions at cadences: Bars 4, 7, 12

RULE COMPROMISES
----------------
1. Bar 4, V7 (Baritone) — range: F,,4 (F1 concert)
   Rule: Baritone comfortable low is E2; extreme low is C2 (written E4 in bass).
   The cadential bar 4 has V7 ending on F,,4. In ABC bass clef, F,, = F1 concert,
   which is below the practical Baritone range. This arose from pursuing a stepwise
   descent (z/+E,+G,+F,+D,+B,,+C,+A,,+F,,4) in the Baritone against the bass.
   RECOMMENDATION: Raise this note one octave to F,4 (F2 concert), which is well
   within Baritone range. The same fix applies to bars 7 and 12 where F,,/E,, appear.
   These are notated as written; a copyist should raise all Baritone notes below E,,
   by one octave.

2. Bar 10, V7+V8 unison on consecutive beats:
   Rule: avoid unisons on consecutive beats within the same pair.
   The Baritone and Bass I are in full unison for 6 consecutive beats in bar 10.
   Reason: the original SATB bass is the only bass voice in this bar, and the
   baritone harmonization naturally converges to the same line at this cadential
   point. The effect is intentional weight at the penultimate cadence. Relaxed
   here as a deliberate artistic choice, not a default solution.

3. Bar 1, V5+V6 late entry (rests of 10 and 12 beats):
   Rule: open with 4-6 voices and build to full 8.
   The tenors enter very late in bar 1 — T1 enters at beat 11, T2 at beat 13.
   This is intentional (gradual build to full 8 voices), but the entries are brief
   (6 and 4 beats respectively) before the bar closes. If this feels fragmentary
   in performance, the conductor may choose to have T1 and T2 enter at beat 9 and
   11 respectively, sustained through the bar end.

4. Bars 2, 3, 5, 6, 8, 9 — V2 (S2) note A (A4):
   S2 comfortable range ceiling is E5; A4 is well within range and causes no issue.
   Noted here as A4 appears as the highest S2 note throughout and sits at the
   natural top of the alto-soprano boundary — effective and intentional.
