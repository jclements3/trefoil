HARMONIC SUBSTITUTIONS
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
The original tenor (S2V1) voice contains =A (A natural) in several bars.
A natural is not diatonic in Eb major (Ab is the diatonic 4th scale degree).
The =A natural notes arise from a V/iv or secondary dominant context
(functioning as the leading tone of Bb minor, i.e., a V7/iv implication).

Substitution applied globally: replace =A natural with Ab (the diatonic
neighbor one semitone above) throughout T1, T2, Baritone where it occurs.
The harmonic context shifts from an implied secondary dominant V7/iv to a
plain iv minor chord (Bb minor), which is fully diatonic in Eb major.
This preserves the subdominant-flavored color while remaining harp-compatible.

Specific bars with =A in original tenor → replaced with Ab:
  Bar 4  (pickup phrase, beat 3): =A, → A, (Ab3) in T1/T2
  Bar 9  (beat 3): =A, → A, (Ab3) in T1/T2
  Bar 14 (repeat, beat 3): same substitution
  Bar 19 (repeat, beat 3): same substitution
  Bar 25 (final section): same substitution

Original chord at these points: implied Bb dominant 7th (Bb D F Ab + A natural = chromatic)
Replacement chord: Bb minor (Bb Db F Ab) → adjusted to Bb minor diatonic (Bb D F Ab, using
  diatonic D rather than Db to stay entirely in Eb major). Effective harmonic color:
  the phrase cadences on Eb via a iv-I or ii°-I instead of V7/iv-IV.

TEXTURE PLAN
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
The arrangement opens with reduced texture: the pickup beat (2 beats) has
only S1 and the lower voices entering on the downbeat, while S2/A1/A2 enter at
m.1 to build the texture gradually from 5 to 8 voices. Phrases 1-2 and their
restatement (phrases 3-4) use full 8-voice homophony for the declarative hymn
character. The antiphonal section (mm.21-24) employs SSAA vs TTBB exchange:
the upper choir carries the sequential passage while the lower choir echoes,
creating dialogue and dynamic relief before the climax. The final section
(mm.25-29) returns to full 8-voice homophony with a 4-2 suspension in the
alto and tenor at the penultimate bar, resolving into the final Eb chord.

Texture types used:
  1. Full 8-voice homophony — phrases 1-4 and final section (mm.1-20, 25-29)
  2. Antiphonal exchange SSAA vs TTBB — mm.21-24 new-material passage
  3. Reduced opening (pickup + m.1) — S1 solo pickup, TTBB join at m.1
  4. Suspension at cadence — beat 1-2 of m.28, alto and tenor 4-3 suspension

RULE COMPROMISES
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
1. mm.21-24, Bar+B1 unison: Baritone and Bass I move in parallel unison
   for 4 bars during the antiphonal passage. Strictly, the rule says to
   avoid consecutive unisons within a pair. This compromise is deliberate:
   the passage is a sequential descending pattern and the bass doubling
   reinforces the TTBB choir's foundational role during the antiphonal
   exchange. The unison is broken immediately at m.25 when Bar rises to
   C, while B1 stays on E,,. Duration: 4 bars, limited and purposeful.

2. m.4 and equivalent repeated bars: T1 line moves by step through Ab
   where the original had =A (A natural). This substitution creates a
   slightly smoother stepwise descent (Bb–Ab–G) rather than the chromatic
   semitone (Bb–A#/Ab) of the original, which actually improves the
   diatonic voice leading even as it removes the secondary dominant color.
   The harmonic tension at this cadential point is preserved through the
   soprano's sustained B (Bb) resolving to Eb.

3. Opening pickup: S2, A1, A2, T2 rest through the 2-beat pickup. This
   departs slightly from the principle of each voice being melodically
   independent from the very start, but follows the instruction to "open
   with reduced texture and build to full 8." The pickup is S1's melodic
   entry; S2 and the altos enter at the downbeat of m.1 (Choir I) and
   TTBB also enters at m.1, giving a clean choral downbeat effect.
