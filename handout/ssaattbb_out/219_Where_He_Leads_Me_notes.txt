ARRANGER NOTES — Where He Leads Me (SSAATTBB)
Source: Open Hymnal Project, 219_Where_He_Leads_Me.abc
Arranger: SSAATTBB expansion, 2026

════════════════════════════════════════════
TRANSPOSITION
════════════════════════════════════════════
Original key:   F major (1 flat: Bb)
Chosen key:     F major (no transposition required)
Semitones:      0
Direction:      none

F major is valid for lever harp in Eb storage tuning.
Lever positions required:
  Bb string → lever UP (raises Bb to B natural — but wait,
  F major needs Bb. In Eb storage tuning all levers down = Eb major.
  To reach F major from Eb major, raise: Eb→E, Ab→A, Db→D, Gb→G.
  Bb remains down (= Bb), which is correct for F major.

Lever configuration for F major (from Eb storage):
  Eb lever → UP  (E natural)
  Ab lever → UP  (A natural)
  Db lever → UP  (D natural)
  Gb lever → UP  (G natural)
  Bb lever → DOWN (Bb — needed by key signature)
  Cb lever → UP  (C natural) [standard]
  Fb lever → UP  (F natural) [standard]

In practice: raise levers on E, A, D, G strings; leave Bb down.
This gives the harp the seven diatonic notes of F major: F G A Bb C D E.

HARMONIC SUBSTITUTIONS
════════════════════════════════════════════
Bar 6 / Bar 14 (both refrain statements):

  Beat 4 of the Bb phrase: Original SATB alto has _E (Eb) on beat 4
  of the first refrain bar (S1V2: "F3/2 F/ F3/2 _E/").
  This creates a Bb7 color (Bb–D–F–Ab with passing Eb).

  Substitution: Replace Eb with E natural (diatonic to F major).
  The chord remains Bb major; the chromatic passing tone is removed.
  Reason: lever harp cannot sound Eb (lever would need to be flipped
  back down mid-piece from its raised E position).

  Beat 4 of bar 6 / 14: Original SATB alto has _D (Db) on the weak
  beat of the Bb passing bar (S1V2: "D/ D2 x/ D/ _D/").
  Db is outside F major and harp range.

  Substitution: Replace Db with D natural (diatonic).
  Reason: same lever constraint. Harmonically the Db appeared as a
  chromatic lower neighbor to D in a Bb context; D natural functions
  identically as an inner voice pedal point.

  The overall harmonic motion in bars 5–7 / 13–15 is interpreted as:
    F (I) → Bb (IV) → F/A (I6) → C (V) → F (I)
  rather than the chromatic Bb–Bb7–Bdim implied by the original
  inner voice chromaticism. This is a stronger diatonic progression
  and idiomatic for lever harp writing.

TEXTURE PLAN
════════════════════════════════════════════
The arrangement opens with a reduced pickup (all 8 voices but with
rests filling the first two beats, entering only on the anacrusis)
to establish the hymn character before the full texture blooms.

Verse 1 (bars 1–8): Full 8-voice homophony throughout, but with
moderate register spread. S1 stays in mid-range (A4–B4 maximum)
to reserve the upper register. TTBB voices are doubled at the octave
below SSAA to create rich blend rather than wide spread.

Verse 2 (bars 9–12): Antiphonal exchange. SSAA choir sings bars 9
alone (TTBB silent). TTBB choir answers with the pickup to bar 10
(SSAA silent on the downbeat pickup). Both choirs reunite at bar 11.
This provides textural contrast and models call-and-response between
the two choirs.

Refrain (both times, bars 5–8 and 13–16): Full 8-voice homophony
returns. The second refrain (bars 13–16) is marked as the structural
climax — S1 reaches c5 (high C, top of comfortable range), B1 moves
to F,3 (low F), creating maximum ensemble spread. The fermata bars
(7 and 15) use 4–3 suspension figures in the inner voices (A1 and T1
move G→F/E, creating resolution into the final F cadence) with
stretto-style alignment between SSAA and TTBB at the final cadence.

Texture types used:
  1. Full 8-voice homophony (block chords) — Verse 1 and both Refrains
  2. Antiphonal exchange (SSAA vs TTBB) — Verse 2, bars 9–10
  3. Call and response between outer voices (S1 high, B1 low) — Refrain climax
  4. Suspension/resolution at cadence — fermata bars 7 and 15

RULE COMPROMISES
════════════════════════════════════════════
1. Bar 6 / 14, V:8 (Bass I), note B,,2 (Bb1):
   This note falls below the stated comfortable bottom of B1 (C2).
   The original SATB bass carries B,,2 here (the Bb pedal of the
   Bb major chord). Retained to preserve the original harmonic bass
   line. Conductor discretion advised — may be raised to F,2 if needed.
   Reason for retention: removing the Bb bass note would weaken the
   IV chord in the most structurally important harmonic phrase (the
   refrain), and the B,, pedal is characteristic of the original
   setting.

2. Parallel motion in inner voices, bars 1, 5, 9, 13:
   A1 and A2 move largely in parallel thirds on the Bb phrase
   (C–C / A–A and D–D / B–B). This is deliberate for a homophonic
   hymn texture and idiomatic for choral writing. Strict contrary
   motion between A1 and A2 in this context would create awkward
   voice leading against the outer voices.

3. Verse 2 antiphonal bars (9–10): the pickup beat creates a
   staggered entry where SSAA sings the downbeat pickup alone.
   T1, T2, Bar, B1 are silent for most of bar 9. This technically
   reduces the beat-by-beat voice count below 8 for several beats.
   This is the intentional antiphonal texture and not a rule violation
   per se, but is noted for transparency.

4. Bar 7 / 15 (fermata bar): The S2 voice is given G4 at the fermata
   rather than the original F-chord tone, creating a brief 4–3
   suspension (G resolving to F) to add harmonic interest at the
   cadence. This is an added passing suspension not present in the
   original. The resolution happens within the bar.
