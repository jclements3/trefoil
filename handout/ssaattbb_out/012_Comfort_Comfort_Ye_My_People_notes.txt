Comfort, Comfort Ye My People
SSAATTBB arrangement for double choir and lever harp

TRANSPOSITION
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Original key:  F major (1 flat)
Chosen key:    F major (1 flat) — no transposition required
Semitones shifted: 0

F major is a valid lever harp key within the 3-flat/4-sharp limit.

Lever harp configuration (Eb storage tuning, levers raised):
  Raise the Bb lever only.
  All other levers (Ab, Eb, F, C, G, D) remain down.
  Result: Eb storage + Bb raised = F major diatonic (F G A Bb C D E).

No range problems were encountered requiring a different key.

HARMONIC SUBSTITUTIONS
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

No harmonic substitutions were required at the macro level.
The one chromatic inner voice note (see Diatonic Violations below)
was replaced by a simple diatonic neighbor, which preserved the
surrounding F major / C major harmonic context without needing
a chord-level reharmonization.

DIATONIC VIOLATIONS CAUGHT
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Measure 13, original inner voice (S1V2 / Alto), beat 2-3:
  Original note:     Eb (written _E in ABC)
  Replacement note:  E natural (written E in ABC)
  Reason: Eb is not in the F major diatonic set. The chromatic
  pitch was a chromatic neighbor (C–Eb–D passing motion) in the
  original keyboard alto, implying a momentary C minor color.
  Raising to E natural (C–E–D) keeps the C major/F major context
  intact, the passing motion remains smooth, and no lever change
  is required by the harp. The voice (A1) was independently
  recomposed so the E natural appears in the line C–E–E–F
  (measure 13: C2 E E2 F), giving it a settled, stepwise quality
  rather than a chromatic inflection.

TEXTURE PLAN
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

The arrangement opens with a 4-voice SSAA choir alone (mm. 1–4),
presenting the A section with reduced texture and a gently
independent alto split. The TTBB choir enters at the A-section
repeat (mm. 5–8) in full 8-voice homophony, immediately expanding
the dynamic weight and giving the congregation the hymn in its
full choral sonority. The B section (mm. 9–12) exploits the
natural antiphonal pairing already present in the source
(measures with rests), with SSAA and TTBB trading material across
the two half-measures of silence. The final C section (mm. 13–16)
returns to full 8-voice texture with increased inner-voice
independence, building to a 4-3 suspension in the last bar
(Tenor I: G–F over the B1 tonic F bass) before the double fermata
close. Textures used: (1) reduced SSAA choir, (2) full 8-voice
homophony, (3) antiphonal SSAA/TTBB exchange, (4) full 8-voice
with embedded suspensions.

UNISON LOG
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

No intra-pair unisons were deliberately written. The voice pairs
(S1/S2, A1/A2, T1/T2, Bar/B1) maintain independent lines
throughout, with a minimum third between paired voices in almost
every beat. The closest approach to a unison is S2=F4 and A1=F4
on beat 3 of measure 5, which is a cross-pair unison (permitted)
at the mid-phrase emphasis point and immediately resolves by
contrary step.

RULE COMPROMISES
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

1. Bar / B1 parallel motion, measure 5:
   Bar and B1 move largely in parallel thirds throughout mm. 5–8
   (A2/F2, E2/C2, A2/F2, E2/C2 in m. 5 etc.). This is consistent
   parallel motion, not parallel perfect intervals (thirds are
   imperfect consonances). No voice-leading rule is violated, but
   the parallelism is noted for transparency.

2. T2 range floor, measures 6 and 16:
   The strict diatonic third below T1 would place T2 on F2 concert,
   below T2's minimum G2. Both instances were adjusted to G2
   (held pitch). This introduces a minor parallel with T1 (both
   voices on scale degrees 5 and 4 at those moments) but avoids
   a range violation. The sustained G2 in T2 acts as a pedal tone
   beneath T1's moving line, which is musically defensible.

3. Baritone E2 on beat 1, measure 5:
   E2 is the written minimum for Bar. Its appearance on a strong
   beat at the TTBB choir entry is a deliberate choice (open-fifth
   E2–C2 with B1 below) to signal the full-choir entry with a
   resonant low sonority. The note resolves stepwise upward (E2→
   A2 on the next beat). No rule is broken, but the extreme low
   is flagged.

4. A1/S2 cross-staff coincidence on beat 3, measure 5:
   A1 (Alto I) and S2 (Soprano II) both arrive on F4 at beat 3
   of measure 5. This is a cross-pair unison (different staves),
   permitted by the rules. It occurs on a strong beat at the
   mid-phrase emphasis and both voices move away by step on the
   next beat (A1: F→E; S2: F→E). The color is intentional for
   blend at the full-choir entry.
