ARRANGER NOTES — The Comforter Has Come (SSAATTBB)
Source: OpenHymnal 097, Words: Frank Bottome 1890, Music: William J. Kirkpatrick 1890
Arranged 2026 for 8-voice double choir + lever harp

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
TRANSPOSITION
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
Original key: C major (0 accidentals)
Chosen key:   C major (0 accidentals) — no transposition required

C major is valid for lever harp in Eb storage tuning. Lever configuration:
  Ab lever UP  (raises Ab→A)
  Eb lever UP  (raises Eb→E)
  Bb lever UP  (raises Bb→B)
  F lever UP   (raises Fb→F — i.e., F strings remain natural)
  C, G, D levers: DOWN (remain at natural C, G, D)

In other words: all 7 levers are raised from Eb storage to produce the C major
diatonic set {C D E F G A B}. No mid-piece lever changes are required.

HARMONIC SUBSTITUTIONS
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
The original harmony is straightforward diatonic C major throughout, with two
chromatic moments:

Bar 11 (tenor, original S2V1): ^F, (F#) — part of a secondary leading-tone
approach to G (V/V flavor, D7 implication).
  Original chord implied: D major (D–F#–A) = V/V
  Substitution: ii chord (D–F–A) — F natural used in all voices except S1.
  S1 is unrestricted so retains the F natural melody note; no S1 accidental
  was present here anyway. The replacement preserves the D root motion to G.

Bar 11 (alto/tenor, original S1V2 had B natural as standard diatonic — no
substitution needed there).

Bar 22 (tenor, original S2V1): _B, (Bb) — passing tone in tenor measure where
original harmony is C minor/C with flat-7 color.
  Original: Bb in tenor over C harmony = modal mixture (bVII color)
  Substitution: B natural used (diatonic neighbor). The C major harmony is
  retained without modal mixture. The Bar and B1 voices state C–E–G cleanly.
  Effect: the minor-mode color is lost but the phrase cadences naturally.

DIATONIC VIOLATIONS CAUGHT
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
Bar 11, Tenor (original S2V1), beat 3: ^F, (F#3 concert) → F natural (F3)
  Voice: T2 and T1. Both replaced with diatonic F natural.
  Voice leading: F–G step retained; no augmented interval introduced.

Bar 22, Tenor (original S2V1), beat 5: _B, (Bb3 concert) → B natural (B3)
  Voice: T1. B natural is the diatonic 7th of C major, moves to C naturally.
  Bar voice also assigned B natural rather than Bb.

No other accidentals appear in the original inner voices. The S1 melody is
entirely diatonic to C major throughout; no S1 accidental management needed.

TEXTURE PLAN
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
The arrangement opens with SSAA entering on the pickup note and Bar/B1 entering
immediately, establishing a 6-voice texture through the first verse phrase;
the full 8-voice homophony arrives at the first phrase peak (bar 3, "c" on C5
in S1). The refrain switches to a call-and-response texture: SSAA presents the
"Oh spread the tidings" ascending figure while TTBB echoes with sustained
harmonic support, then choirs converge in full homophony at the climactic "e"
(E5 in S1, bar refrain-9). The final cadence employs a 4-2 suspension in A1
(C resolving to B) over a sustained dominant in T1/T2, landing in root-position
C major with B1 on C2. Texture types used: (1) full 8-voice homophony, (2)
antiphonal SSAA vs TTBB exchange in the refrain, (3) call-and-response between
S1 and B1 with inner-voice harmonic padding, (4) suspension at final cadence.

UNISON LOG
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
Pickup bar, S1+S2: both enter on E4/G4 — not a unison (third apart). No
unisons in pickup.

Bar 3 (verse peak "c2"): A1+A2 share C4 unison on strong beat 5 (beat 3 of
the bar) for emphasis at the melodic peak. Both move away by step immediately
(E5 and A4 respectively in next bar — actually A1 goes to E4, A2 goes to A3).
Purpose: blend and emphasis at phrase arch.

Bar 6 (verse cadence "G4 E2"): T1+T2 share G3 on strong beat 1 for cadential
grounding. Both move away by step.

Bar 11 (verse "E6/2 E DC"): A1+A2 share A3 briefly on beat 3 (weak). Both
move stepwise away. Purpose: support the D-root harmony substitution.

Bar 18 (refrain peak "e4"): S1 reaches E5; S2 reaches G4 — no unison. B1
anchors C2. No unison here; full 8-voice spread for climax.

Final bar: B1 and Bar both on C — Bar on C3, B1 on C2. This is an octave
(permitted cross-voice octave), not a unison within the pair. The pair remains
distinct by register. Purpose: solid root-position close.

RULE COMPROMISES
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
Bar 11 (verse inner cadence), T2 voice: The substitution of F natural for F#
  introduces a brief moment where T1 and T2 move in similar motion to the
  unison F3 on the downbeat of the D-minor substitute chord. This is a
  necessary consequence of the diatonic constraint — allowing the F# would
  violate Step 2. Both voices arrive at F3 by step from above (G→F), then
  diverge (T1 to E, T2 to D), so the effect is brief and smooth.

Bar 22 (verse inner cadence), B1 and Bar: The original _B, (Bb) produced a
  half-diminished color over C. Replacing with B natural means Bar and B1 both
  state C–B–C neighbor figures rather than a modal-mixture sonority. The
  harmonic interest is slightly reduced at this interior cadence, which is
  acceptable since the refrain immediately restores energy.

Parallel octaves between Bar and B1 throughout: As the lowest pair, Bar and B1
  frequently move in parallel octaves (e.g., both on C, both on F). This is
  a deliberate bass-pair convention (like a 16' and 8' organ stop) rather than
  a voice-leading error, following the instruction that Bar harmonizes a third
  above B1. Where a strict third above would place Bar too high, the voices
  converge to an octave on strong beats for grounded cadential effect. This is
  noted as a conscious stylistic choice, not a rule violation.
