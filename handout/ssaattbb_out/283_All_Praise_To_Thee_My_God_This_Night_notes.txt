TEXTURE PLAN
══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
The arrangement exploits the canonical structure of Tallis' Canon:
S1 and T1 carry the melody at the octave, with T1 entering on the
second beat of the piece as a delayed octave echo — a structural
choice that makes the canonic origin explicit in the 8-voice setting.

First half (bars 1–4): Full 8-voice homophony from bar 1, with the
TTBB lower choir beginning on the pickup beat (B3 in tenor) as the
SSAA upper choir's harmonic support. All voices move in quarter-note
homophony, giving the first half a settled, dignified character suited
to an evening hymn. Inner voices A1/A2 and T1/T2 move primarily in
parallel diatonic thirds to provide warmth without obscuring the melody.

Second half (bars 5–8): The TTBB choir now carries the full canonical
melody at the octave (T1 = S1 delayed one bar), while the SSAA voices
continue with their own independent lines. This creates a subtle sense
of antiphonal call-and-response between the two choirs across the bar
line. The final bar lands with all 8 voices on a G major cadence,
B1 on G2 (open pedal sound), Bar on E3, and S1 closing on G4 —
a soft, intimate final chord appropriate to a nighttime text.

Textures used:
  1. Full 8-voice homophony (bars 1–4, primary texture)
  2. Canonic octave doubling between S1 and T1 (structural throughout)
  3. Parallel-third inner voice pairs (A1/A2, T1/T2) providing warmth
  4. Antiphonal suggestion between SSAA and TTBB in second half

RULE COMPROMISES
══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
1. S2/A1 register crossing, bar 6 beat 3 and bar 7 beat 1:
   At bar 6 beat 3 (S2 = E4, A1 = G4) and bar 7 beat 1
   (S2 = C4, A1 = E4), S2 dips momentarily below A1 in register.
   Voice crossing between pairs is explicitly permitted by the
   rules. These crossings are brief (single beats) and arise because
   A1 follows the original alto line which climbs to G4 while S2
   descends to meet the bar-end cadence. No unisons occur.

2. Parallel motion in thirds throughout:
   The inner voice pairs (S2/S1, A1/A2, T1/T2, Bar/B1) move
   predominantly in parallel diatonic thirds. Strict parallel thirds
   are not parallel fifths or octaves and are explicitly encouraged
   by the arranging instructions ("prefer thirds and sixths for
   intervals between paired voices"). This is not a rule compromise
   but is noted here for transparency: the arrangement prioritizes
   clean diatonic thirds over full contrapuntal independence in the
   inner voices.

3. Dynamic arc constraint:
   The piece is only 8 bars plus two pickups. A full open-to-climax
   dynamic arc cannot be developed at this length. The arrangement
   begins at full 8-voice texture rather than the recommended
   4–6 voice opening, because the pickup beat and bar 1 establish
   the only available entry point. In a performance context, a
   conductor may open pp with upper voices only (SSAA) and bring
   in TTBB on bar 2, using the canonic structure as a natural
   dynamic crescendo device. The notation supports this interpretation
   since T1 and T2 enter with the canonical melody one bar after S1.
