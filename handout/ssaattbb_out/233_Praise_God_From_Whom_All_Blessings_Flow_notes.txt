ARRANGER NOTES
Praise God From Whom All Blessings Flow (Old 100th / Doxology)
SSAATTBB Arrangement for Double Choir and Lever Harp
============================================================


TRANSPOSITION
-------------
Original key:    G major (1 sharp: F#)
Chosen key:      G major (1 sharp: F#)
Semitones:       0 (no transposition required)

G major is valid for lever harp in Eb storage tuning. Required lever
positions: raise all seven levers from Eb storage to reach G major.
In Eb storage (all levers down), strings are Eb Ab Bb Cb Db Eb Fb Gb.
Levers to raise: Ab→A, Bb→B, Cb→C, Db→D, Eb→E, Fb→F, Gb→G, then
additionally raise F→F# for the one sharp of G major. This is within
the stated valid range (1 sharp). Practically, the harp is pre-set in
G major before the piece begins; no mid-piece lever changes are needed.

Harp levers raised for G major (from Eb storage): all seven, plus
the F lever one notch further to F#. All other naturals hold.

No chromatic harmony substitutions were required. The 'Old 100th' in

I (G major), ii (A minor), IV (C major), V (D major), vi (E minor).

  Bar 2: I – V – I

DIATONIC VIOLATIONS CAUGHT
---------------------------
No accidentals appear in any inner voice of the original SATB. The
original 'Old 100th' is fully diatonic in G major throughout. No
replacements were needed in any voice.

All 8 voices in this arrangement use only the 7 diatonic pitches of
G major: G A B C D E F#. The soprano (S1) carries no accidentals in
the original, so the diatonic constraint applies uniformly to all 8
voices without exception.


VOICE MODIFICATION LOG
----------------------
The following changes were made to original SATB lines to solve
voice-ordering (S2-above-A1) constraints inherent in 8-part expansion:

Bar 1, Alto I (A1), beat 5: original D4 lowered to C4.
  Reason: S1=E4 and original A1=D4 left only a major 2nd gap, making
  it impossible to place S2 above A1 without crossing. C4 (the diatonic
  passing tone) creates a smooth D4-C4-B3 stepwise descent into bar 2
  and opens a full major 3rd gap for S2=D4 to sit comfortably above.

Bar 3, Alto I (A1), beat 6: original G4 lowered to E4.
  Reason: S1=G4 on the same beat made A1=G4 impossible (S2 cannot fit
  between identical pitches). The revised E4 creates a smooth
  F#4→E4 stepwise descent (replacing the original F#4→G4 neighbor
  figure) and is harmonically equivalent on the V chord.

Bar 5, Alto I (A1), beats 1–2: original G4 lowered to E4.
  Reason: S1=G4 on beats 1-2. Original A1=G4 identical to S1, leaving
  no room for S2. E4 maintains the implied G major harmony (as the
  chord's major 6th/mediant color) and proceeds naturally to F#4
  on beat 3.

Bar 6, Alto I (A1), beats 1–2: original E4 lowered to C4.
  Reason: S1=E4 on beats 1-2. Original A1=E4 was identical to S1,
  again leaving no room for S2. C4 (the minor 7th color on the vi
  chord = E minor) creates a richer harmonic texture and leads
  naturally stepwise to D4 on beat 3.

Bar 7, Alto I (A1), beat 4: original G4 lowered to E4.
  Reason: S1=G4 on beat 4. Original A1=G4 = S1 unison; S2 cannot
  sit between them. E4 produces a smoother stepwise interior line
  (D4→E4→F#4→E4) and is harmonically equivalent on the G major chord.

All modified notes remain diatonic in G major. S2 and A2 were revised
correspondingly to maintain correct spacing throughout.


TEXTURE PLAN
------------
The arrangement employs full 8-voice homophony throughout all 8 bars,
appropriate for this brief devotional text (each phrase spans only
4 bars and the whole is typically sung once or twice). Opening at mf
in a broad, resonant texture, the ensemble builds intensity through
bar 7, where S1 reaches its melodic peak at D5 while T1 (sounding)
ascends to E4 — the widest soprano-to-tenor spread of the piece.
The final cadence (bar 8) resolves with sustained motion, Baritone
moving from G3 through A3 to D3, and Bass I settling on G2 with a
walking approach through B2-C3-D3. The inner voices (A1, A2, T1, T2)
provide genuine melodic independence: the altos move largely in
parallel thirds descending stepwise, the tenors sustain and elaborate
the harmonic support with contrary inner motion, and the Baritone
traces a neighbor-note line around A3 throughout — a deliberate
pedal-point character for a hymnic setting.

Texture types used:
  1. Full 8-voice homophony — bars 1–8 (primary texture, appropriate
     to the Doxology's congregational character)
  2. Contrary inner-voice motion — alto pair (A1 up, A2 down or A2
     up, A1 stationary) in bars 3, 5, 7 creates two-strand
     counterpoint within the alto register
  3. Tenor climax spread — bar 7 beats 5–6 (T1 sounding D4–E4,
     T2 sounding B3–C4) paired with S1's D5–C5 creates maximum
     ensemble range before the final cadence

RULE COMPROMISES
----------------
1. Baritone, bar 7, beat 1: Perfect-5th upward leap (D3→A3).
   The Baritone closes bar 6 on D3 (cadential low point). Bar 7 opens
   with Bass I on G3, requiring Bar to be above G3. G3 itself conflicts
   with T2 (sounding G3 on beat 1 of bar 7), and A3 is the nearest
   available diatonic note above G3 that avoids all T-voice unisons.
   The resulting P5 leap is the minimum necessary to re-enter the
   correct register. After the leap, Bar moves entirely by step
   (A3→A3→A3→G3→G3) through the remainder of bar 7 and then
   G3→A3→G3→D3 through bar 8, providing the stepwise contrary
   resolution across multiple beats.

2. S2 bar 5, beats 1–2: S2=F#4 (written F) sits a minor 2nd below
   S1=G4. This is a 2nd rather than the preferred 3rd, forced by the
   constraint that S2 must remain above the (revised) A1=E4. The F#4
   is the leading tone, giving the beat a brief V-of-I color within
   the I chord — an incidental sharpening of the harmonic tension
   that resolves as S2 ascends to G4 on beat 3.

3. Parallel 3rds throughout paired voices: S1+S2, A1+A2, T1+T2 all
   move in parallel 3rds (or 2nds) across most of the arrangement.
   This is intentional — in a short homophonic chorale-style piece with
   8 voices distributed into pairs, parallel 3rds within pairs is
   standard professional practice (analogous to divided strings in
   orchestral writing). No parallel 5ths or octaves are created
   between these adjacent pairs.

4. Multiple inner-voice modifications (see Voice Modification Log above):
   Alto I is altered in bars 1, 3, 5, 6, and 7. All changes are
   diatonic, stepwise or by 3rd, and improve voice leading. The
   harmonic implication is maintained in every case. The modifications
   are a structural necessity of 8-part expansion: the original SATB
   alto voice frequently doubles or approaches the soprano, leaving
   no room for the inserted S2 voice.
