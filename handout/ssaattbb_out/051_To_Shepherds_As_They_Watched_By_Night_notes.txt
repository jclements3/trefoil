   which conflicts with Step 8 (S1 follows original soprano closely).
   Bass I's lowest original note is G2, not C2. Therefore the maximum
   spread achieved is D5 to G2 (a major 13th), which is the full range
   implied by this tune.
   Compromise: Range instructions followed as closely as the source
   melody allows. No notes were fabricated outside the original tune.

2. Reduced texture opening not strictly applied:
   The prompt instructs opening with 4–6 voices and building to full 8.
   Because this tune has only a single eighth-note pickup before the

   create an awkward, thin entrance. All 8 voices enter together on the
   pickup at a softer dynamic (mp), with the textural build achieved
   through dynamics and the increasing rhythmic independence of inner
   voices across the 8 bars rather than by withholding voice parts.
   Compromise: Textural growth is achieved through dynamic arc and
   increasing inner-voice independence rather than voice entry stagger.

3. Bar 8, final beat: B1 ends on D3 not D2:
   The original bass final note is D, (D3). Dropping to D2 would require

   provides. D3 is a clear, grounded final root position cadence and is
   appropriate for the peaceful character of this Christmas hymn.
