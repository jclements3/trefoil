ARRANGER NOTES
"Awake, My Heart, With Gladness" — SSAATTBB Arrangement
Paul Gerhardt / Johann Crüger (1648)
Arranged from "Mehrstimmiges ChoralBuch" 1906, Hymn #14

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
TRANSPOSITION
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Original key:  Eb major (3 flats)
Chosen key:    Eb major (3 flats) — no transposition required
Semitones shifted: 0

Eb major is a valid lever harp key (3 flats = exactly 3 levers raised
from Eb storage tuning). Lever positions required:

  All levers down (storage tuning) = Eb major already.
  No levers need to be raised for Eb major — the storage tuning IS
  Eb major. (The harp's Eb-storage tuning means Eb is the default state
  with all levers lowered; Eb major requires zero lever changes.)

All 8 voices stay comfortably within professional ranges in Eb major.
No range-driven transposition was needed.

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
HARMONIC SUBSTITUTIONS
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

No full harmonic reharmonizations were required. The original chorale
is diatonically well-behaved in Eb major. The accidentals that appear
in the original inner voices are chromatic passing tones and raised
leading tones that were replaced by their diatonic neighbors (see
DIATONIC VIOLATIONS CAUGHT below). These replacements fall on weak
beats or passing positions and do not disrupt the underlying harmony.

Bar 6 / Bar 13 (repeat): The original tenor voice (S2V1) contained
=B, and =A, (Bnatural and Anatural) producing a momentary secondary
dominant flavor (V of vi = C major triad). This was resolved by
retaining Bb and Ab (diatonic), which produces v minor (Cm) instead.
The harmonic rhythm and bass motion are unchanged.

Bar 19: The =E in the original tenor (Enatural3, a raised leading
tone of F) was replaced with Eb (diatonic). The harmony remains on
the subdominant side of the phrase and the diatonic version is
equally satisfying in context.

DIATONIC VIOLATIONS CAUGHT
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

The following original inner-voice accidentals were replaced with
diatonic neighbors. Eb major diatonic scale: Eb F G Ab Bb C D.

  Voice: Tenor I (V:5, original S2V1)
  Original: (=B,3/2 =A,/) B,  [Bnatural, Anatural — both chromatic]
  Replacement: (B3/2 A/) B     [Bb, Ab — diatonic]
  Rationale: =B, (Bnat) → B, (Bb); =A, (Anat) → A, (Ab).
  Both replacements are the diatonic versions one semitone below
  the chromatic note. Voice leading preserved: stepwise descent
  Bb→Ab→Bb remains smooth.

Bar 18 (Section B1, first B section):
  Voice: Alto I (V:3, original S1V2)
  Original: F E _D   [Db — chromatic: Db is one below diatonic D]
  Replacement: F E D  [D natural — diatonic]
  Rationale: _D (Db4) → D (D4); nearest diatonic neighbor (step up).
  The scalar descent F–Eb–D remains stepwise and singable.

Bar 19 (Section B1):
  Voice: Tenor I (V:5, original S2V1)
  Original: (G,3/2 F,/) =E,   [Enatural — chromatic in Eb key]
  Replacement: (G3/2 F/) E    [Eb — diatonic]
  Rationale: =E, (Enat3) → E (Eb3); nearest diatonic neighbor (step down).
  The decorative descent G–F–E(b) loses one semitone of leading-tone

No violations were found in the bass voice (original S2V2) or soprano
voice (S1V1). The soprano retains all original accidentals as S1 is
unrestricted by the diatonic constraint.

TEXTURE PLAN
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

The arrangement opens with four voices (S1, A1, T1, B1) in bars 1–7,
presenting the original SATB chorale melody in lean, transparent
scoring. At bar 8 (the written-out A-section repeat) all eight voices
enter simultaneously in full homophonic texture, each pair moving in
parallel thirds, creating the first dynamic surge. The B section
(bars 15–20) introduces layered texture: SSAA carries the melodic
argument while TTBB sustains a slower-moving harmonic counterpoint
beneath, then the roles exchange at bar 18. The final B section
(bars 21–26) drives to full 8-voice homophonic climax, with a stretto
device in bars 21–22 where the soprano pair and tenor pair echo the
dotted-rhythm motive one beat apart before converging on the
penultimate phrase.

Texture types used:
  1. Reduced 4-voice SATB (bars 1–7) — open, lean start
  2. Full 8-voice homophony (bars 8–14) — first full entry
  3. Layered choir dialogue (bars 15–20) — SSAA melodic / TTBB harmonic
     exchanging at bar 18
  4. Stretto / overlapping entries between soprano and tenor pairs
     (bars 21–22) — dotted motive imitated at the beat
  5. Full 8-voice climactic homophony (bars 23–26) — maximum spread

RULE COMPROMISES
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

1. T2 / B1 near-collision, bar 8 beat 1 (and analogous bar 1 of
   A repeat). Concert pitch T2 = Eb3; B1 = Eb3. These are the same
   concert pitch — technically a cross-pair T/B unison (forbidden
   per Step 5). The compromise: T2 was shifted to C3 on beat 1 (one
   diatonic third lower), which avoids the unison and also gives T2
   a more independent melodic entry. The interval T1/T2 on beat 1
   becomes a perfect fifth rather than a third; this is acceptable as
   an occasional departure at a structural downbeat.

2. Baritone (V:7) departures from strict "third above B1": Several
   bars require Baritone to depart from the parallel-thirds ideal to
   avoid cross-pair T/B unisons. In bars 9–10, Baritone moves to a
   fourth or fifth above B1 (rather than a third) when the third
   would collide with T2. In each case the Baritone line remains
   stepwise and diatonic. The departures are documented by bar:
     Bar 9:  Bar = G,3 (G3), B1 = Ab2 → interval P4 (not M3)
     Bar 10: Bar = D,3 (D3), B1 = Bb2 → interval M3 ✓
     Bar 11: Bar = G,3 (G3), B1 = Eb3 → interval M3 ✓
     Bar 15: Bar = G,3 (G3 stepwise descent), not strictly a third above
             all three beats; F3 and Eb3 beats 2–3 are thirds above B1
             beats 2–3 (D3, C3 respectively).

3. Alto I bar 8 (Section A repeat): A1 uses E D E (Eb4, D4, Eb4)
   rather than E E E (as in bars 1–7) to avoid the S2/A1 cross-pair
   unison on beat 2 (both would land on Eb4). The neighbor-tone
   figure Eb–D–Eb is musically natural and adds slight melodic
   interest to the repeated A section.

4. Stretto in bars 21–22 is approximate: S1/S2 pair and T1/T2 pair
   both carry the dotted-rhythm ascending motive starting on beat 1,
   making it a simultaneous double statement rather than a strict
   stretto (offset entries). True stretto at the final cadence would
   require one pair to enter one or two beats later, which is
   incompatible with the original melody's rhythmic profile in these
   bars. The "stretto" effect is achieved instead by T1/T2 echoing the
   same rhythmic shape as S1/S2 at the same time but a sixth lower,
   producing a parallel double-choir reinforcement of the climax.

5. Augmented melodic interval check: In bar 22, S1 sings =A (Anat4)
   followed by B (Bb4) — an augmented unison (chromatic semitone up).
   This is permitted for S1 (unrestricted), but the interval is
   technically a chromatic step rather than a diatonic step. All other
   voices in bar 22 move by diatonic step or hold. No inner voice
   presents an augmented melodic interval.

6. Bars 15–17 and 18–20 (TTBB sustained harmony under SSAA melody):
   The four TTBB voices in bars 15–17 hold relatively static patterns
   to support the SSAA melodic layer. This produces brief passages of
   less than fully independent TTBB voice leading — an acceptable
   textural compromise to achieve the antiphonal exchange effect
   described in the texture plan.
