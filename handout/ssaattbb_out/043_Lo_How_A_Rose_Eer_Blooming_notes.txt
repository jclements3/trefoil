TRANSPOSITION
══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Original key: F major (1 flat)
Chosen key:   F major (1 flat) — no transposition required
Semitones shifted: 0

F major is valid for the lever harp in Eb storage tuning. Only the Bb
string lever needs to be raised (Eb→E is not needed; Bb lever raised
gives the single Bb string in the diatonic F major scale). All other
levers remain down.

Harp lever positions for F major:
  Ab lever: DOWN (Ab string sounds Ab — not used in F major, but
            the harp string is Ab2/Ab3/Ab4/Ab5; lever must be UP
            to produce A natural for the scale degree 3 of F major)

Wait — clarification: in Eb storage tuning, raising levers sharpens:
  Ab→A: raise to get A natural (scale degree 3 of F major) — UP
  Eb→E: raise to get E natural (scale degree 7 of F major) — UP
  Bb→B: this would give B natural — NOT raised; Bb stays as Bb
         (scale degree 4 of F major) — DOWN

Correct lever positions for F major (Eb storage):
  Ab lever: UP   (A natural: scale degree 3)
  Eb lever: UP   (E natural: scale degree 7)
  Bb lever: DOWN (Bb: scale degree 4 — keep flat)
  F lever:  DOWN
  C lever:  DOWN
  G lever:  DOWN
  D lever:  DOWN

The harp plays all 7 notes of F major diatonic (F G A Bb C D E).
S1 accidentals (none in this piece) would be the singer's alone.

TEXTURE PLAN
══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

The arrangement opens with a 4-voice pickup (S1 S2 Baritone B1 only),
quiet and contemplative. Full 8-voice homophony enters at bar 1 of the
first A section, establishing the Praetorius block-chord texture. The
second A section shifts to antiphonal writing: SSAA carry the melody
with ornamented inner voices while TTBB sustain the harmonic foundation
below, creating a spatial separation across the ensemble.

The B section ("When half-spent was the night") builds through all 8
voices in continuous motion (bars 5-6), then drives through two rapid
2-beat stretto bars (bars 7-8) before the full recapitulation phrase
(bars 9-11). The final cadence brings all voices to rest on F major:
B1 on the root F2, Baritone on A2 (third), T1/T2 pair on C3/A2, A2
on A3, A1 on C4, S2 on C4, S1 on F4. Three distinct textures are
employed: (1) homophony in A1; (2) antiphonal SSAA/TTBB in A2; (3)
stretto-driven 8-voice convergence in B.

RULE COMPROMISES
══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

1. S2/A1 unison at final cadence (bar 11, B section):
   Both S2 (V2) and A1 (V3) resolve to C4 on the final F major
   chord. The rule prohibits S and A voices from sharing a unison.
   Reason for relaxation: C4 is the natural resolution for both
   voice lines (S2 descending D4-C4; A1 sustaining C4 from the
   previous bar). At a final cadence of a short chorale-style piece,
   the doubling of the tonic fifth in the inner voices is standard
   practice and the blend is musically effective. Separating them
   would require forcing one voice to an awkward non-chord tone at
   the moment of rest. This is the sole rule relaxation in the
   arrangement.

2. T2 pedal in B section bar 9 (all 6 beats on G2):
   The instruction prefers melodic independence over purely harmonic
   filler. T2 sustaining G2 for 6 beats (replacing the original
   G2-F#2-G2 figure with G2-G2-G2) is melodically static. Reason:
   F#2 is non-diatonic and cannot be used; the nearest diatonic
   alternatives F2 or G2 both produce a repeated-note figure since
   F#2 was a lower chromatic neighbor. Choosing G2 (upper neighbor
   suppressed to unison) is the least disruptive diatonic
   substitution. T1's motion above (B,2-C2-B,2) provides
   melodic interest in the tenor pair during this bar.

3. A2 in second A section falls to F3 (F,) in bar 1:
   The A2 range minimum is D3; F3 is well within range. However,
   in the second A bar 1 the A2 line (F, A, B, A, = F3 A3 Bb3 A3)
   descends to F3 on the downbeat, which places it below the
   concurrent A1 line (A G F E = A4 G4 F4 E4). The A1 is
   considerably higher than A2 throughout this bar, giving a wide
   spread within the alto pair. This is musically effective and
   not a voice-leading violation; it is noted here for clarity.
