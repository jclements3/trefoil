SSAATTBB ARRANGER NOTES
"Songs of Thankfulness and Praise" — 'St. George's Windsor'
Christopher Wordsworth (words) / George Job Elvey (music)
Setting for 8-voice ensemble and lever harp, 2026.

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
TRANSPOSITION
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Original key: G major (1 sharp)
Chosen key:   G major (1 sharp) — no transposition required

G major is valid for lever harp (1 sharp, within the 3-flat–4-sharp limit).

Harp lever positions required:
  F lever: raised (F → F#)
  All other levers: down (Eb storage position)

The piece uses only the seven diatonic pitches G A B C D E F# throughout
all inner and bass voices, with S1 permitted chromatic passing tones.

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
HARMONIC SUBSTITUTIONS
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

No full harmonic reharmonizations were required. The original SATB
harmonies are entirely functional within G major, and all chromatic
inner-voice notes were resolved by scalar neighbor substitution
(see Diatonic Violations Caught below) without needing to replace
chords wholesale.

Bar 6, beat 4: Original SATB has S1 on C#5 (^c) above an implied Em
  or G major chord. S1 retains the C# freely (S1 is unrestricted).
  The other voices remain diatonic: S2=A4, A1=E4, A2=C4, T1=G3, T2=G3,
  Bar=G3, B1=E3 — producing a D9 or Em6 color beneath the C# melody.
  This is intentional harp writing: the diatonic strings sustain while
  S1's chromatic note adds characteristic color.

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
DIATONIC VIOLATIONS CAUGHT
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

The following accidentals appeared in original SATB inner voices and
were replaced with diatonic neighbors:

1. Bar 4 (first half), Alto voice (S1V2): original ^D (D#4)
   → replaced with D4 (diatonic neighbor below)
   Context: end of phrase 1, approaching E4 in bar 5. D4 (minor third
   of a Bm chord) leads equally well to E4 by step, and preserves the
   Bm/G ambiguity at the phrase break without secondary dominant
   brightness. The chord becomes Bm (B D F#) rather than B major.

2. Bar 7, beat 4, Alto voice (S1V2): original ^C (C#4)
   → replaced with C4 (diatonic neighbor below)
   Context: bar 7 harmony is Am on beat 4 (A C E). C4 fits Am perfectly
   and resolves naturally to D4 in bar 8. Using C# would imply A major
   (a secondary dominant V/D), which is replaced by plain Am. Voice
   leading: D4 → E4 → D4 → C4 in A1, a smooth stepwise descent.

3. Bar 11, beat 4, Alto voice (S1V2): original =F (Fnat, F4)
   → retained as F# (F4 written as plain F in K:G = F#4)
   Context: bar 11 harmony is G major. F natural would imply a G7 chord
   (G B D F) or passing diminished color. Keeping F# preserves pure G
   major and allows the harp to sustain without a lever flip. A1 uses
   F#4 as a melodic passing tone (E4→F#4) at beat 4.

4. Bar 11, beat 2-4, Bass voice (S2V2): original =F, (Fnat, F#3 line
   had a natural F in beat 2-4 of bar 11)
   → retained as F# (plain F, in K:G = F#3)
   Context: same as above. B1 descends G3-F#3-E3-D3, keeping all notes
   diatonic. The G7 substitution is absorbed into a passing G major
   descent in the bass.

5. Bar 12, beat 4, Alto voice (S1V2): original ^G (G#4)
   → replaced with A4 (diatonic neighbor above)
   Context: bar 12 is approached as Am or C major. G#4 implies either
   A major or E major tonicization. A4 gives a clear Am chord tone
   (root of Am or fifth of D major), resolves well, and creates an
   expressive 4-3 suspension figure in A1: E4-E4-A4 (bar 12) → E4 (bar 13).

6. Bar 12, beat 3, Bass voice (S2V2): original ^G, (G#2)
   → replaced with G,, (G2, diatonic neighbor below)
   Context: the original G#2 in the bass would imply an E major or B major
   chord. G2 keeps the harmony in C major / Am territory (G2 as the 7th
   of Am7 or the 5th of C/G). This also prevents a dissonant collision
   between the harp's diatonic F# string and a G# in the bass voice.

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
TEXTURE PLAN
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

The arrangement follows a four-phrase structure matching the original
hymn tune's four musical periods (each ~4 bars):

Phrase 1 (pickup + bars 1-4): SSAA alone, reserved 4-voice texture.
  The upper choir sings unaccompanied, establishing the melody in
  a chamber-like, intimate sound. TTBB rests entirely.

Phrase 2 (bars 5-8): All 8 voices enter simultaneously at bar 5.
  Full homophonic texture in block chords. TTBB enters clean (not on
  the pickup, so their entry falls on the strong beat of bar 5). The
  sudden addition of 4 lower voices creates a dramatic increase in
  warmth and weight exactly at the word "Manifest."

Phrase 3 (bars 9-12): Antiphonal exchange between choirs.
  - Bars 9-10: SSAA leads the melody while TTBB sustains whole-note
    chord pads (D major in bar 9, G major in bar 10), giving the lower
    choir a cushioning harmonic role.
  - Bars 11-12: TTBB becomes melodically active (T1 follows the original
    tenor line, T2 in contrary motion, Bar and B1 in stepwise descent)
    while SSAA continues the melody in S1 with simplified harmonic support
    in S2/A1/A2. This antiphonal interplay brings textural variety and
    gives each choir its own moment of leadership.

Phrase 4 (bars 13-16): Full 8-voice writing builds to the climax at
  bar 14 and resolves to the final cadence. Bar 14 beat 3 is the
  structural apex: S1 on E5 and B1 on C3 form the widest vertical
  span of the piece (C major chord across three octaves). Bar 15 features
  a stretto effect: T1 mirrors the S1 descent (d G B A) a major tenth
  below, producing a canonic echo in the two outer soprano/tenor lines.
  The final cadence (bar 16) settles to a complete G major chord with
  B1 on the root G2.

Texture types employed:
  1. Reduced 4-voice opening (SSAA alone) — reserved opening
  2. Full 8-voice homophony (phrase 2, bars 5-8)
  3. Antiphonal exchange (phrases 3a/3b, bars 9-12)
  4. Canonic/stretto imitation between S1 and T1 (bar 15)

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
UNISON LOG
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

No intra-pair unisons occur in the arrangement. All eight voices
maintain distinct pitches throughout. (S2 and A1 are always separated
by at least a major second; T1 and T2 likewise; Bar and B1 likewise.)

Cross-choir unisons (A2 and T1 sharing a pitch) occur naturally at
the register break and are permitted by the rules:

  Bar 9: A2=B3 (treble B,) and T1(snd)=A3 — not a unison
  Bar 10: A2=B3 and T1(snd)=B3 — unison A2/T1 (cross-choir) on strong
    beats 1-4 (whole note). This is the natural register-break overlap
    and is explicitly permitted. Both voices are holding the third of
    the G major chord.

  Bar 14 beat 3: T2(snd)=G3 and Bar=G3 — cross-choir unison at the
    climax on a strong beat. Both are holding the fifth/root of the
    C major climax chord. The cross-choir unison adds resonance here.

RANGE LOG

S1 (Soprano I):
  - Highest note: e (E5) at bar 12 beats 1-4 and bar 14 beat 3.
    E5 is well within the comfortable top (G5); no concern.
  - S1 also reaches e at bar 12 (phrase 3 peak) and bar 14 (phrase 4
    climax). Both are structural high points, musically justified.

S2 (Soprano II):
  - Highest note: c (C5) at bar 12 and bar 14 beat 3.
    C5 is within comfortable S2 range (top F5). No concern.
  - Lowest note: B, (B3) at bars 7-8 cadence.
    B3 is just above S2 minimum A3. Acceptable.

A1 (Alto I):
  - Highest note: A (A4) at bar 12 beat 3 and bar 14 beat 3 (G4).
    Both within A1 comfortable range. No concern.
  - Lowest note: F, (F#3) at bar 8 first half.
    F#3 = F3+1 semitone; A1 minimum is F3. Essentially at the floor.
    This is a cadential half note on a strong beat in a D major chord.
    Acceptable; singers should support with good breath.

A2 (Alto II):
  - Highest note: E (E4) at bar 14 beat 3 (climax).
    E4 is within A2 comfortable range (top B4). No concern.
  - Lowest note: D, (D3) at bar 8 first half; E, (E3) at bar 7 beat 4.
    D3 is A2's minimum. Cadential, supported by the full ensemble.

T1 (Tenor I, written an octave up):

    E4 sounding is exactly the "comfortable top" for T1. Used at a

  - Lowest sounding note: F#3, at bar 8 second half (written F).

T2 (Tenor II, written an octave up):

  - Lowest sounding note: D3, at bars 5-8 (written D = D4, snd D3).

  - Note: F, is never written in T2 (would sound F#2, below G2 minimum).

Baritone (V7):
  - Highest note: C (C4 = middle C in bass clef) at bar 13 beats 1-2.

  - Lowest note: D,, (D2? — not used). Actual lowest: G,, (G2) at

  - Highest note: A, (A3) at bar 13 beats 1-2; G, (G3) at bar 14.

RULE COMPROMISES
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

1. Bar 7, T2 (V6) vs Baritone (V7) cross-pair ordering:
   At bar 7 beat 4: T2 sounding = D3, Bar = A3. T2 is below Bar by a
   fifth. This is a cross-pair crossing between the Tenor pair (T1-T2)
   and the Baritone-Bass pair (Bar-B1), which is explicitly permitted
   by the rules ("voice crossing between pairs is permitted where
   musically effective"). The crossing occurs because T2 reaches its
   lower register on a descending line (G3-E3-D3) while Bar ascends
   to fill an inner harmonic voice. Both lines are melodically logical.

2. Bar 6, Bar (V7) sustained G3 vs T2 (V6) sounding G3:
   At bars 6 beat 3, T2 written G (snd G3) and Bar written G, = G3
   produce a cross-choir unison G3. This is permitted (cross-choir
   unisons are explicitly allowed and do not count against either choir's
   unison allowance). The shared G3 adds resonance on a weak beat.

3. Bar 14, Bar (V7) using C4 (absolute top of Bar range):
   Bar reaches C (C4 in bass = middle C) on beats 1-2 of bar 13 and
   beat 1 of bar 14. This is at the extreme top of the Baritone range
   (comfortable top A3; absolute maximum C4). Used during the building
   approach to the phrase 4 climax. The line resolves immediately
   downward (C4→A3→F#3 in bar 13; B3→G3→E3 in bar 14). Singers should
   use full support and project confidently into this register; it is
   musically effective for the climax approach.

4. Bar 12, S1 E5 vs S1 "comfortable top G5":
   S1 reaches E5 at bar 12 (phrase 3 peak) and again at bar 14 beat 3
   (phrase 4 climax). E5 is well within the comfortable S1 range (G5
   comfortable top, A5 absolute maximum). No compromise needed here —
   included in range log for completeness.

5. Parallel fifths avoided throughout with one minor caveat:
   In the inner voices (A1-A2) at bars 1 and 3, both voices move in
   similar (though not parallel) motion. The intervals are checked
   beat-to-beat: no consecutive fifths or octaves occur. All voice
   leading was composed to preserve contrary or oblique motion as the
   default between adjacent voice pairs.
