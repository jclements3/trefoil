Semitones shifted: 0

  Raise Ab → Eb → Bb → F → C → G → D
  For A major (3 sharps: F# C# G#), raise the following levers:
    F-string levers → F#
    C-string levers → C#

  All other strings remain at storage pitch (Eb, Bb, Ab, etc. → not used)

HARMONIC SUBSTITUTIONS
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
Bar 5 (original S2V2): (E, ^D,) =D, in the bass — E–D#–D chromatic
  passing motion.
  Original chord: E major moving through a D# passing tone (chromatic).
  Replacement: The bass line was adjusted to E,2 D, (diatonic), omitting
  the D# entirely. The harmony moves from E major to D major (IV of A)
  via stepwise diatonic descent, preserving the voice leading intent
  without the chromatic inflection.

Bar 11 (original S1V1): (A F) ^D — the D# in the melody is the S1
  voice and is therefore permitted unrestricted. No substitution applied
  to S1. However, the inner voices on that beat were kept diatonic:
  the original tenor had (F, A,) F, implying a D major or F# minor
  chord; the alto had C4 B3 implying A major. The inner voices were
  adjusted to support a plain A major / F# minor harmony without
  requiring D# in any non-S1 part.

Bar 16 (original S1V2 pickup): ^D (D# in alto/pickup)
  Original: Alto pickup D#4, suggesting a B7 secondary dominant (V/E).
  Replacement: Replaced with D natural (diatonic scale degree 4).
  The resulting chord on the pickup beat becomes Bm (B–D–F#), a
  diatonic substitute. The resolution to B–E–G# in the following
  bar is smooth via stepwise motion. In the SSAATTBB version, S2
  takes D4 on the pickup, supporting a clean Bm harmony.

DIATONIC VIOLATIONS CAUGHT
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
Bar 5, Voice S2V2 (Bass), note 2: ^D, (D#2) → replaced with D, (D2)
  The chromatic passing tone D# between E and D in the bass was
  removed; the bass now moves E–D diatonically. No augmented interval
  is introduced. The slight sharpening of the passing motion is lost
  but voice leading remains smooth.

Bar 5, Voice S2V2 (Bass), note 3: =D, (D natural, after the chromatic
  D#) — D natural IS diatonic in A major (scale degree 4), so no
  violation; retained as written.

Bar 16 (pickup), Voice S1V2 (Alto): ^D (D#4) → replaced with D (D4).
  Nearest diatonic neighbor downward. Context: pickup into phrase 3,
  resolves upward by step to E in bar 17. D natural serves equally well
  as an approach tone from below (B→D→E across the barline) and avoids
  the augmented 2nd that would arise if D# appeared against the A major
  diatonic field.

Note on S2V2 bar 5: the original also has =D, following ^D,. This =D,
  is actually a natural-sign cancellation of the preceding sharp, meaning
  the note IS D natural (diatonic). So the only violation in that bar is
  the ^D, itself, which was removed.

TEXTURE PLAN
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
The arrangement opens (phrase 1) with a slightly reduced texture in which
the lower voices (T2, Bar) enter more cautiously while S1 and B1 carry the
principal melodic and bass lines, giving a 6-voice feel at the outset;
phrase 2 brings full 8-voice homophony with all voices engaged in block
chord style for the first climactic cadence. Phrase 3 introduces
antiphonal exchange between the SSAA choir and the TTBB choir: the upper
voices present the melodic line while the lower voices echo rhythmically
beneath on alternating beats, creating a call-and-response within the
ensemble. Phrase 4 (the final phrase with the intro bracket) builds to
full 8-voice stretto texture with S1 reaching high e'' (E5) at the
climax and B1 anchoring at A,,2 (A1), achieving maximum ensemble spread;
the final cadence settles all voices to an A major root-position chord
with B1 on A1–A2.

Texture types employed:
  1. Full 8-voice homophony (phrase 2 and phrase 4 climax)
  2. Antiphonal exchange SSAA vs. TTBB (phrase 3)
  3. Canonic imitation between S1 and T1 (phrase 3, bars 17–19, T1
     enters a bar behind S1 at the lower octave)
  4. Call and response between outer voices S1 and B1 (phrase 1)

  strong beat — grounding the half-cadence; both voices move away by
  step immediately after into the pickup of phrase 3.

Phrase 4 bar 23: T1 and B1 share the same rhythmic figure A,2 G, at
  the phrase opening — this is not a pitch unison (T1 sounds A3/G#3,
  B1 at A,,/G,,) but an octave doubling for emphasis at the return;
  not counted as an inner-voice unison.

A2 and T1 register cross: In phrase 3 and 4, A2 descends to D,3–E,3
  range and T1 ascends to A,–B, range (sounding octave lower), placing
  them at the natural register break. These cross-choir pitch sharings
  are intentional per the arranging rules.

No consecutive-beat unisons occur within any pair.

RULE COMPROMISES
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
1. Bar 8 (phrase 2 opening), V8 (B1): The original bass has A,,2 A,,
   (A1–A1–A1) in the ABC. Retained as a sustained pedal note for three
   beats. This creates parallel octaves with V7 (Bar) which also sits on
   A,, moving similarly. Rule 1 (no parallel octaves) is relaxed here
   because the doubled bass octave at the phrase opening reinforces the
   tonic arrival after the half-cadence; the effect is grounding rather
   than a voice-leading error. Both voices move apart immediately in bar 9.

2. Phrase 3, V7 and V8 (Bar and B1) share identical lines for two bars
   (bars 16–17 in the SSAATTBB numbering). This violates the spirit of
   melodic independence for Bar but was chosen to provide a rock-solid
   bass foundation under the antiphonal texture. Bar diverges from B1
   from bar 18 onward, moving a third above. The compromise is noted and
   the director may wish to have Bar ornament with a passing tone on beat
   2 of bar 17 (C, → B,, → A,,) to restore independence.

3. Phrase 4, B1 final bars: The very low A,, and G,, notes (A1, G1)
   approach the absolute lower limit of a professional bass voice. This is
   intentional for climax spread but represents a range push. If the
   ensemble's B1 cannot produce a resonant G1, the director should raise
   B1 by one octave for bar 27 only (A, G,, → A,, G,,, i.e., keep A,,
   but raise G, to G,, if needed — or simply sing A,2 A,, for the last
   two measures, ending on A,, at the final cadence only).

4. Bar 11 (phrase 2), inner voices: The original harmony implied by
   (A F) ^D in S1 is an F#m/D# or D#dim passing chord. Since D# is
   forbidden in inner voices, the inner voices support A major and F#
   minor instead, producing a slight harmonic simplification. The melodic
   color of D# in S1 still implies the chromatic inflection to the
   listener; the diatonic inner voices provide support without contradiction.
   This is the intended behavior per the diatonic constraint rules.
