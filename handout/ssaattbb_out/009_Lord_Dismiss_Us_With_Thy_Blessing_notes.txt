ARRANGER NOTES
Lord, Dismiss Us With Thy Blessing
'Regent Square' — Henry Smart, 1867
SSAATTBB arrangement for lever harp accompaniment

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
TRANSPOSITION
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
Original key: C major (0 accidentals)
Chosen key:   C major — no transposition required
Semitones shifted: 0

Harp lever positions for C major (Eb storage tuning):
  Ab lever UP  (A strings raised to A natural)
  Eb lever UP  (E strings raised to E natural)
  Bb lever UP  (B strings raised to B natural)
  All other levers down (F, C, G, D remain as natural from storage)
  Result: all 7 strings diatonic in C major.

No range problems were encountered that would require choosing a
different key.

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
HARMONIC SUBSTITUTIONS
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
Three chromatic harmonies in the original SATB were reharmonized
to remain playable on the diatonic lever harp:

Bar 6 (phrase 2, bar 2), beat 2:
  Original chord: E major (V/vi — secondary dominant of A minor)
  The tenor carried G#3 (raised leading tone to A minor).
  Replacement: E minor (iii — fully diatonic)
  G#3 replaced with G3 in all inner voices (T1, T2).
  The harmonic color shifts from a bright tonicization to the
  mediant minor, preserving the move toward A minor on beat 3.

Bar 7 (phrase 2, bar 3), beat 4:
  Original chord: D major (V/V — secondary dominant of G major)
  The alto carried F#4 (raised 3rd of D major).
  Replacement: D minor 7 (ii7 — fully diatonic in C major)
  F#4 replaced with F4 in A1.
  The subdominant-seventh sound still leads effectively to the
  G major half cadence on bar 8.

Bar 9 (phrase 3, bar 1), beat 4:
  Original chord: C dominant 7 (I7 with Bb — borrowed from F major)
  The tenor carried Bb3 (flat 7 of C major).
  Replacement: C major 7 (Imaj7 — fully diatonic)
  Bb3 replaced with B3 in T1.
  The major-7 sonority (D5, E4, B3, C3) provides a luminous
  color on the way to the climactic IV chord in bar 10, arguably
  more effective than the original C7 in this 8-voice texture.

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
DIATONIC VIOLATIONS CAUGHT
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
Bar 6, beat 2 — Original S2V1 (tenor): ^G, (G#3)
  Replaced with G3 in T1 and T2. Reason: diatonic constraint.

Bar 7, beat 4 — Original S1V2 (alto): ^F (F#4)
  Replaced with F4 in A1. Reason: diatonic constraint.

Bar 9, beat 4 — Original S2V1 (tenor): _B, (Bb3)
  Replaced with B3 in T1. Reason: diatonic constraint.

S1 (Soprano I) carries all accidentals freely as the unrestricted
melody voice. No accidentals appear in S1 of this hymn, so the
diatonic and melody-voice rules coincide throughout.

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
TEXTURE PLAN
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
The arrangement opens with only five voices (SSAA + B1) on the
phrase 1 pickup, adding T1 on bar 1 beat 3, T2 and Baritone on
bar 3, reaching full eight voices at the bar 4 cadence. Phrase 2
is presented in full eight-voice homophony with the SSAA choir
carrying melodic weight in bars 5-6 while the TTBB choir
provides more active counterpoint in bars 6-7 (antiphonal
exchange). Phrase 3 unleashes the climax: S1 ascends to F5 on
bar 10 with S2 reaching D5 simultaneously, while B1 holds G2-C3
in the bass for maximum spread; the descending inner lines of
bars 11-12 provide a stretto-like convergence to the final C
major cadence with B1 on C3, Bar on E3, and all voices settling
by step.

Texture types employed:
  1. Reduced-texture opening (5 voices, SSAA + B1) — phrase 1
  2. Full 8-voice homophony — phrase 2 bars 5-8
  3. Antiphonal exchange (SSAA vs TTBB) — phrase 2 bars 6-7
  4. Climactic full-spread (S1 at F5, B1 at C3) — phrase 3 bar 10
  5. Stepwise stretto convergence at final cadence — bars 11-12

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
UNISON LOG
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
Bar 4, Bar+B1 (phrase 1 cadence):
  Bar=C3, B1=C3. Strong beat (half note cadence).
  Purpose: grounded bass unison to close phrase 1 with finality.
  Both voices move away by step in the phrase 2 pickup (Bar→E3,
  B1→C3→G2).

Bar 8, end (phrase 2 cadence):
  No within-pair unisons at this cadence; voices spread across
  G major chord for a broad half cadence.

Bar 12, final cadence:
  No within-pair unisons. All voices distinct: S1=C5, S2=E4,
  A1=G3, A2=E3, T1=G3(concert), T2=G2(concert), Bar=E3, B1=C3.
  Note: A1 and T1 share G3 (cross-choir pitch — explicitly
  permitted at the register break).

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
RANGE LOG
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
Bar 1, S1 beat 3: E5 — within comfortable range (top of phrase 1,
  contextually appropriate as the highest note of phrase 1).

Bar 10, S1 beats 3-4: F5, E5 — climax of the piece. F5 is the
  top of S1 comfortable range (A5 is maximum). Used only here for
  the emotional peak on "Thanks and praise for thy salvation."

Bar 10, S2 beat 3: D5 — high for S2 (comfortable top E5). Within
  range, approached by step from A4 via F4.

Bar 10, T1 beats 1-2: A3, G3 (concert) — mid-range, comfortable.
  T1 descends through F3 at beat 3, E3 at beat 4 — within the
  B2-G4 comfortable range.

Bar 11, T2 beats 2-4: G2 (concert) — bottom of T2 comfortable
  range (G2 is the very low end). Used here as the harmony
  thins toward the final cadence. Singers should be coached to
  support this note well; it is not a concern if the ensemble is
  professional.

Bar 11-12, B1: A3 (bar 11 beat 2) → G2 (bar 11 beat 4) → C3
  (bar 12). The G2 at bar 11 beat 4 is at the bottom of the
  comfortable range (C2-A3 comfortable to F3). G2 is within the
  absolute range (C2-A3) and is acceptable at this cadential
  approach.

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
RULE COMPROMISES
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
Bar 1, A1 (C4) vs A2 pickup→bar1:
  The motion from A2=E3 (pickup) to A2=A3 (bar 1, beat 1) is a
  4th upward, while A1 moves C4→C4 (stay). This introduces
  parallel motion but not parallel 5ths. Accepted.

Phrase 3 bar 2 (bar 9), A1-A2 motion beats 2-3:
  A1: C4→G4 (5th up), A2: A3→E4 (5th up). Both move up a
  diatonic 5th simultaneously. The interval between A1 and A2
  remains a 3rd in both cases (C4-A3=3rd, G4-E4=3rd), so these
  are parallel thirds, not parallel 5ths. Strictly acceptable,
  but the parallel gesture was noted.

Bar 11, Bar voice repeated A3:
  The Baritone holds A3 across all four beats of bar 11 as a
  sustained inner pedal tone. This is harmonically valid (A3 is
  the 6th of C major, 3rd of F major, and 5th of D minor — all
  chords present in bar 11) but reduces the Baritone's melodic
  independence at this bar. Justified as a structural anchor
  during the final descent of all other voices.

Phrase 2, S2 vs A1 voice crossing:
  At several points in phrase 2 (notably bars 6-7), A1 ascends
  while S2 moves in contrary motion, bringing A1 (E4-F4) close
  to S2 (G4). No crossing within either pair, and no S-A unisons
  occur. The closeness of spacing was intentional for blend in
  the full-texture phrase.
