ARRANGER NOTES — I Am Jesus' Little Lamb (SSAATTBB)
Source hymn: Open Hymnal Project #277, X:277
Arranger: 2026 SSAATTBB expansion for 8-voice ensemble with lever harp

════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
TRANSPOSITION
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Original key:  C major (0 accidentals)
Chosen key:    C major (0 accidentals) — no transposition required
Semitones:     0 (no shift)

The source hymn is already in C major, which falls within the valid
harp range (3 flats to 4 sharps). C major is the identity tuning for
the lever harp in Eb storage: the player raises all 7 levers to sharp
Eb→E, Bb→B, Ab→A, Db→D, Gb→G, Cb→C, Fb→F, producing the C major
diatonic scale with all levers up.

Harp lever positions for C major (from storage Eb):
  Lever 1 (Eb strings) → raised to E  [ALL Eb levers raised]
  Lever 2 (Bb strings) → raised to B  [ALL Bb levers raised]
  Lever 3 (Ab strings) → raised to A  [ALL Ab levers raised]
  Lever 4 (Db strings) → raised to D  [ALL Db levers raised]
  Lever 5 (Gb strings) → raised to G  [ALL Gb levers raised]
  Lever 6 (Cb strings) → raised to C  [ALL Cb levers raised]
  Lever 7 (Fb strings) → raised to F  [ALL Fb levers raised]

All 8 voices remain comfortably within professional ranges in C major.
No range problems were encountered requiring selection of an alternative key.

HARMONIC SUBSTITUTIONS
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

No full harmonic substitutions were required. The original SATB harmony
is overwhelmingly diatonic in C major. The chromatic notes in inner
voices (see Diatonic Violations below) were handled by moving individual
notes rather than substituting entire chords.

The implied harmony in bars 5 and 7 (original inner voices had F# passing
tones suggesting a secondary dominant or raised 4th) was reinterpreted as
follows:

  Bar 5, beat 3 (and identically Bar 7, beat 3):
    Original: implied D7/A or passing V/ii with F# in tenor and bass
    Replaced: diatonic passing motion through Am (A-C-E), keeping F natural
    in all inner voices; the soprano D5 eighth note implies Dm/ii briefly
    before resolving through C/I on the following C5 eighth

  Bar 11, beats 3-4:
    Original: G major (V) with B4 in soprano implying G/B inversion
    Retained: diatonic G major — no substitution needed, all notes natural

DIATONIC VIOLATIONS CAUGHT
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

The following accidentals appeared in the original SATB inner voices and
were replaced with diatonic neighbors:

ALTO (S1V2 in source):

    Replaced: E4 (natural, already the diatonic note)
    Alto line revised from F4-Eb4-E4-D4 to C4-C4-B3-A3 (stepwise descent),
    preserving the implied IV harmony without chromatic inflection.

  Bar 3, beat 3 — original: E natural after Eb (chromatic lower-neighbor)
    The entire bar 3 alto was recomposed as a melodic stepwise descent
    (C4-C4-B3-A3) avoiding both the Eb and the awkward chromatic return.

  Bar 5, beat 2 (and bar 7, beat 2) — original: C#4 (^C)
    Replaced: no C# appears in any inner voice.
    The S1V2 line "C ^C D D" became a recomposed melodic arch in A1/A2
    (C4-D4-E4-F4 in A1; A3-G3-G3-D4 in A2) using only diatonic pitches.

TENOR (S2V1 in source):

  Bar 5, beat 3 (and bar 7, beat 3) — original: F#3 (^F,)
    Replaced: F3 natural (diatonic).
    The tenor passage "F G ^F A" became part of the recomposed T1/T2
    lines using only diatonic motion (T1: A3-B3-C4-B3; T2: F3-G3-A3-F3).

BASS (S2V2 in source):

  Bar 3, beat 2 — original: F#2 (^F,)
    Replaced: G2 (ascending by step instead of by augmented step).
    B1 bar 3: F2-G2-G2-G2 (all natural).

    Replaced: F2 natural.
    B1 bar 5 and 7: F2-E2-D2-F2 (passing motion using diatonic F natural).

Note on soprano accidentals retained:
  S1 bar 5 beat 4: D5/C5 eighth pair with original =c (explicit natural,
    already diatonic in C major — no change needed)
  S1 bar 7 beat 4: D5/Cb5 eighth pair (_c) — Cb5 is a chromatic note
    in the soprano (S1). Per arrangement rules, S1 accidentals are
    unrestricted. This Cb is retained as written in the source and creates
    the characteristic modal color (Lydian-flat-7 inflection) against the
    harp's diatonic C natural strings. No other voice doubles this Cb.

TEXTURE PLAN
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

The arrangement opens in reduced SSAA texture (pickup through bar 1)
with only four voices, establishing an intimate, hymn-like quality before
TTBB enters quietly on the second half of bar 2 (beats 3-4) on the pickup
to phrase 1's repeat. By bar 3 all eight voices are present in full
block homophony for the phrase 1 restatement, creating an immediate sense
of expansion and weight. Phrase 2 (bars 5-8) continues in full 8-voice
texture with contrary motion in the alto pair (A1 ascending, A2 mirroring)
and canonic parallel-thirds motion in the tenor pair (T1: A-B-C-B arch,
T2: F-G-A-F), providing genuine melodic independence in the middle voices.
The final section (bars 9-12) builds to a climax in bar 11, where S1 peaks
at D5 (approaching its high register), T1 mirrors the soprano line an octave
below (A3-D4-C4-B3), and B1 grounds the texture at F2 and G2; the final
cadence resolves to a full C major chord spanning C2 to C5.

Texture types used:
  1. Reduced 4-voice texture (SSAA only) — pickup and bar 1
  2. Progressive entry — TTBB joins on beat 3 of bar 2
  3. Full 8-voice block homophony — bars 3-4
  4. Contrary-motion inner voices (A1 ascending vs A2 descending,
     T1 arch vs T2 answering motion) — bars 5-8
  5. Canonic upper-voice doubling (T1 mirrors S1 at the octave in bar 11)
     with S1/T1 in parallel at the climax
  6. Full spread climax — bar 11, S1 at D5, B1 at D2-G2

RULE COMPROMISES
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

1. Bar 6 / bar 8, beat 2-3 — V7 (Baritone): B2 held as half note
   while V8 (B1) moves independently underneath.
   In bar 8 the Bar sustains B2 on beats 1-2 while B1 has G2-G2,
   creating a major 3rd for two consecutive beats. This is not a rule
   violation but is noted: the sustained Bar note was chosen for
   simplicity and a pedal-tone effect at the cadential return.

2. Bar 10, beat 3 — V7=G2, V8=C2: interval of a fifth between Bar and B1.
   The arrangement guidelines prefer thirds between paired voices; this
   fifth occurs briefly as a passing beat in the cadential approach.
   Accepted as a one-beat exception supporting the stepwise bass motion.

3. Bar 9, beat 2 — V7=G2, V8=C2: another fifth between Bar and B1.
   Same reasoning: passing beat in a descending stepwise bass line.
   Only one beat; not consecutive. Accepted.

4. Bar 11, beat 2 — T1 and T2 in unison on A3 (concert).
   The rules allow unisons "for climax or deliberate blend."
   This is the peak of the climax bar and is used intentionally.
   Both voices immediately diverge to a fourth (T1→D4, T2→G3)
   on beat 3. Accepted.

5. Soprano S1V2 (original alto) contained the chromatic passage
   "C ^C D D" (bars 5 and 7). Rather than preserving this line in V:2
   (Soprano II), the S2 was assigned thirds-below-S1 throughout, and the
   original alto chromatic content was entirely omitted (replaced by the
   diatonic A1/A2 recomposition). This was the cleanest solution to keep
   S2 diatonic while giving A1/A2 genuine melodic independence.

6. The original bass had F1 and G1 (S2V2 bars 1 and 6: F,, G,,).
   These were raised by an octave to F2 and G2 in B1 (V:8) throughout.
   At F1/G1 concert, the notes fall below the practical range of any
   professional bass voice and would be inaudible in ensemble texture.
   No rule is violated; the original notation appears to be an error or
   an organ-bass doubling not intended for voice.
