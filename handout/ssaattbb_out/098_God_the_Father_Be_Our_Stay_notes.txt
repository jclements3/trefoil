TRANSPOSITION
----------------------------------------------------------------
Original key:  D major (2 sharps: F#, C#)
Chosen key:    D major (no transposition required)
Semitones:     0 (no change)
Direction:     N/A

D major is valid for lever harp in Eb storage tuning (≤4 sharps maximum).
Lever positions required for D major:
  Raise Ab  → A natural
  Raise Eb  → E natural
  Raise Bb  → B natural
  Raise F   → F# (one of the key-signature sharps)
  Raise C   → C# (one of the key-signature sharps)
  Leave G and D levers at Eb-storage position (G already natural, D natural)
Five levers raised; no mid-piece lever flip required. All 8 voices and the
harp share the same 7 diatonic pitches (D E F# G A B C#) throughout.


HARMONIC SUBSTITUTIONS
----------------------------------------------------------------
Bar 20 (second statement of B-section bar 5, S2V1 original):
  Original: S1V2 has ^D (D#) in the figure E2 ^D3/2 B,/ suggesting a
  brief V/ii tonicization (A7 resolving to Bm with raised leading tone D#).
  Replacement: The chord is treated as a plain D major harmony. Inner voices
  use D natural throughout this bar. The soprano (V1/S1) retains the
  chromatic coloring from the original soprano line without the inner-voice
  D# that would violate the diatonic constraint.
  Reason: harp cannot play D# without a lever flip; substituting D major
  preserves forward momentum with negligible loss of color.

Bar 21 (second statement of B-section bar 6, S1V2 and S2V1 originals):
  Original: S1V2 has (A3/2 ^G/) suggesting V/vi coloring (E major tonicizing
  A minor). S2V1 has (A, ^G,) confirming the raised 7th in the tenor.
  Replacement: Both voices use G natural (G, not G#) throughout, rendering
  the harmony as a plain Em or G major chord. V5/T1 uses (A, G,) diatonic;
  V2/S2 uses (A G) diatonic.
  Reason: G# is outside the D-major diatonic set and unavailable on the
  lever harp mid-piece. The diatonic substitute (Em/G chord) maintains
  strong downward voice leading into the cadence.

Bar 22 (second statement, original S1V2 bar 7 cadence):
  Original: S1V2 has =D (D natural) after the key-signature D major context,
  suggesting a momentary Dorian or modal inflection. Retained as D natural
  (diatonic in D major) — no substitution needed; the natural sign was
  redundant in D major context.

TEXTURE PLAN
----------------------------------------------------------------
The arrangement opens with the SSAA choir alone (bars 1–4) to establish
the chorale character at reduced dynamic before the full TTBB choir enters
at bar 5 for a complete 8-voice homophonic statement of the first phrase.
The B-section (bars 10–15) is given antiphonally to the TTBB choir alone,
creating a strong contrast of register and timbre, before the full ensemble
reunites for the second statement (bars 16–22) in rich 8-voice counterpoint
with independent melodic lines in all four inner voices. The final section
(bars 23–26) uses stretto-like entries building toward the climax in bar 24
where S1 reaches A5 (the highest note of the arrangement) and B1 descends
to D2, achieving maximum ensemble spread, before all voices cadence together
on unison D in their respective octaves.

Texture types used:
  1. Reduced choir (SSAA alone): bars 1–4
  2. Full 8-voice homophony: bars 5–9 and bars 23–26
  3. Antiphonal exchange (SSAA vs TTBB): bars 10–15 (TTBB alone vs. bars 1–9
     SSAA, then re-joined at bar 16)
  4. Independent melodic counterpoint (all inner voices singable lines): bars 16–22
  5. Climactic stretto with maximum registral spread: bars 23–24

RULE COMPROMISES
----------------------------------------------------------------
1. Bar 20 (V2/S2), parallel approach to bar 22 cadence:
   S2 moves from D4→D4→C#4 in parallel 10ths with S1 (D5→E5→F#5 area).
   Checked: no parallel fifths or octaves between S1 and S2 at this point;
   the parallel 10ths are standard choral practice. No rule violation.

2. Bars 10–15, Baritone (V7):
   The Baritone line was composed as a 3rd above B1 but the exact intervals
   vary (sometimes a 4th or 5th above) where a strict 3rd would produce
   voice crossing with B1 or parallel octaves with T2. These are accepted
   deviations to preserve smooth voice leading; the baritone remains
   consistently above B1 at all times (no crossing).

3. Bar 15, final beat (V6/T2): T2 arrives on D3, which is also the note
   of B1 at the cross-choir register break. This cross-choir unison is
   explicitly permitted by the arranging rules (A2/T1 boundary) and aids
   the smooth blend into the tutti entry at bar 16. Not a rule compromise.

4. Bars 16–22, A2 (V4) range:
   A2 descends to F,3 (F#3 concert) at bar 22 cadence — this is the lower
   boundary of the Alto II range (D3–B4; low F#3 is one step above the
   floor). Accepted as a cadential descent; the voice resolves stepwise.
