ARRANGER NOTES
Jesus Grant That Balm and Healing — SSAATTBB Arrangement
Music: Johann Balthasar König, 1738. Words: Johann Heermann, 1644.
Based on Open Hymnal Project, 2008 Revision. Public domain.

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
TRANSPOSITION
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Original key: C major (key signature: 0 accidentals).
Chosen key: C major (unchanged). No transposition required.

C major is valid for the lever harp in Eb storage tuning.
All 7 levers must be raised in order:
  Ab → Eb → Bb → F → C → G → D
This brings all strings from Eb major up to C major.

Modal note: The hymn uses C major key signature but cadences on A,
indicating Aeolian mode (A natural minor). The final chord is A minor (A C E).
This is common in Lutheran chorale practice and creates no harp difficulties —
A minor uses the same 7 diatonic pitches as C major.

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
HARMONIC SUBSTITUTIONS
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

The original harmony is modal/diatonic and requires no chord-level
reharmonization. The accidentals found in the source (all ^G = G#) function
as raised leading tones to A within the Aeolian mode. No secondary dominants
or chromatic chord substitutions were required.

All G# occurrences in inner voices (original alto bar 4/8 ^G, tenor bar 2/6 ^G,)
were handled via diatonic note substitution (see Diatonic Violations below)
rather than chord-level reharmonization, since the underlying harmony remains
i or V in A Aeolian throughout.

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
DIATONIC VIOLATIONS CAUGHT
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

All violations are in the original SATB inner voices. S1 retains all accidentals.
All replacements use the nearest diatonic neighbor that preserves voice leading.

Bar 2, beat 3 — Original Alto (S1V2): ^G4 (G#4)
  → Replaced with E4 (diatonic, a third below S1's ^G4)
  Reason: Alto S2 harmonizes S1 a diatonic third below; E4 is the
  correct diatonic partner for S1's leading tone G#4.
  (Same substitution applied in bar 6 — repeat of phrase 1.)

Bar 2, beats 1-3 — Original Tenor (S2V1): ^G2 (G#2 at bar 2 beat 4)
  → Tenor sounding voice (T1/T2) raised to B2/G2 diatonic neighbor.
  Original tenor ended phrase 1 on ^G, (G#2, sounding). Replaced with B2 (T1)
  and G2 (T2), the nearest diatonic tones that maintain voice-leading continuity
  toward C3 at the next phrase.
  (Same substitution applied in bar 6, 14 beat 3, 15 beat 1, 16 beat 2.)

Bar 4, beat 3 — Original Alto (S1V2): ^G4 (G#4)
  → Replaced with F4 in S2 (diatonic third below is the E4 position, but
  cadential phrase has S2 on F4 then G4 for smooth resolution).
  A2 moves to A3 (root doubling) as compensation.
  (Same substitution applied in bar 8.)

Bar 10 — Original Tenor (S2V1): _B, (Bb2, sounding)
  → Replaced with B2 natural (diatonic). The Bb appeared as a neighbor/passing
  tone in the original tenor. B2 natural is the diatonic neighbor and preserves
  stepwise motion. T1 sustains C3 across bar 10 to smooth the substitution.

Bar 14, beats 3-4 — Original Tenor (S2V1): ^G, (G#2, sounding)
  → Replaced with B2 (T1) and G2 (T2). Leading-tone function absorbed by
  S1 which freely retains ^G4 in bar 14. Inner tenor voices step to B2/G2
  as diatonic neighbors to the following A-minor arrival.

Bar 15-16 — Original Tenor (S2V1): ^G, (G#2) at multiple points
  → Replaced with B2 (T1) and G2 (T2) throughout. Same rationale as above.
  T1 pedals on B2-C3 alternation; T2 pedals on G2-A2 alternation.

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
TEXTURE PLAN
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

The arrangement opens with a quiet 3-voice texture (S1 S2 A1) over a
silent bass, allowing the modal melody to emerge with transparency (bars 1-4, piano).
Bass I enters at bar 2 to anchor phrase 1 harmonically but the TTBB inner
voices remain silent. The full 8-voice ensemble arrives at the phrase 1 repeat
(bars 5-8, mf), adding A2 and all four TTBB voices simultaneously for an
immediate increase in warmth and weight.

Bars 9-12 (Phrase 3, the B section) use antiphonal texture: SSAA carry the
melody in bars 9-10 while T1/T2 sustain a gentle harmonic pedal beneath;
in bars 11-12 the SSAA inner voices settle into a sustained fill while T1/T2
take melodic leadership with stepwise contrary motion, accompanied by
Bar and B1. This choir-vs-choir exchange provides textural contrast before
the final phrase.

Bars 13-17 (Phrase 4, climax and final cadence) return to full homophony
with all 8 voices. S1 ascends to C5 (bars 13 and 15-16) for the only
sustained high-register moment of the piece. The cadential bar 16 features
a gentle stretto effect as all voices converge stepwise toward the final A minor
chord, with B1 descending to A2 (a full two octaves below S1's A4) for
maximum vertical spread. The final sustained A minor chord is unadorned and
broadly spaced across all 8 voices.

Textures used:
  1. Reduced 3-voice with solo bass entry (bars 1-4)
  2. Full 8-voice homophony (bars 5-8, 13-17)
  3. Antiphonal exchange SSAA vs TTBB (bars 9-12)

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
UNISON LOG
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

T1 + T2 (bars 9-10): T1 and T2 both sustain C4 written (C3 sounding) across
bar 10 beats 1-4. This is a deliberate blend unison during the antiphonal
transition where TTBB takes over harmonic support from SSAA. Both voices
move away stepwise at bar 11.

T1 + T2 (bar 12): Both voices approach B3 written (B2 sounding) as a pedal.
T2 moves G3-G3-G3 while T1 holds B3-B3-B3. Technically not a unison
(T1=B3, T2=G3 written, a third apart) but both voices settle into sustained
tones for cadential repose.

A2 + T1 (register break, freely permitted): A2 sounding A3-D4 region and
T1 sounding C3-F3 region do not overlap; the cross-choir unison exemption
is not needed but the voices complement each other at the register break
in bars 9-12.

No within-pair unisons occur in S1+S2 or Bar+B1. A1+A2 have no unisons.

RANGE LOG
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

S1 — bar 13-15: c' (C5). This is within the S1 comfortable range (top G5,
  extreme A5). C5 is well-placed as the climax height for this quiet hymn;
  it avoids straining the voice while still providing a clear textural peak.
  S1 also reaches b (B4) at bars 3, 4, 7, 8, 15, 16 — comfortable ✓.

S1 — bar 1: a (A4), bar 9: g (G4). Opening range is conservative (A4 top).
  This follows the instruction to open at reduced range and reserve peaks
  for the climax. ✓

B1 — bars 9-12: C, (C2) and G, (G2). These are the lowest notes in the
  arrangement. C2 is the minimum of B1's comfortable range and appears
  only in bars 9 and 12 (phrase cadence points). Original bass notes A1
  and G1 were raised by one octave throughout bars 9-12 to stay within
  range. B1 final chord (bar 17): A, (A2) — root of A minor in a
  comfortable mid-low position. ✓

Baritone — bar 8 / bar 17: E, (E2). Minimum for Baritone. Appears only at
  cadence points (bars 8, 17) and in passing (bar 6, 11). Not a concern. ✓

T1 — sounding B2 at bars 7, 14-16. This is the minimum sounding pitch for T1
  and is approached by step from C3 in each case. No concern. ✓

T2 — sounding G2 at multiple points in bars 5-16. G2 is T2's minimum and is
  well supported by Bar and B1 below. Approached by step each time. ✓

A2 — bar 9: E, (E3 in treble). Bottom of the A2 active range; well above
  the minimum D3. No concern. ✓

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
RULE COMPROMISES
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

1. S2 / A1 momentary cross-voices (bars 1, 5):
   S2 beat 2 = C4; A1 beat 2 = D4. A1 briefly sits one step above S2.
   This is a deliberate consequence of moving A1 from C4 to D4 to avoid
   a forbidden S2/A1 unison. Voice crossing between pairs (S and A) is
   explicitly permitted by the rules ("Voice crossing between pairs is
   permitted where musically effective"). The cross lasts one beat and both
   voices move away immediately. Bars 1, 2, 5, 6.

2. T1 sounding range (bars 9-12):
   The original tenor in phrase 3 descends to G#2 (G2 after diatonic substitution),
   which is below T1's minimum sounding pitch of B2. T1 was recomposed
   as a sustained pedal on C3/B2 (written c/B in 8vb treble) to stay within
   range, sacrificing melodic interest for the antiphonal texture plan. T2
   carries the lower harmonic fill at G2-A2 in this section.

3. B1 octave adjustment (bars 9-12 and 4/8):
   The original bass voice in phrase 3 descends to A1-G1-F1 (below B1's
   minimum C2). The entire passage was transposed up one octave. This changes
   the registral relationship between Bar and B1 in these bars (both now
   sit in the E2-C3 region rather than spanning two octaves), but is
   necessary to keep B1 singable. The original bass in phrase 1 ended on A1
   (bars 4 and 8); these were adjusted to C2 (root, an octave above).

4. T1 melodic simplification (bars 9-12):
   To avoid range problems, T1 was simplified to a C3-B2 alternating pedal
   rather than following the original tenor line. The original tenor had
   stepwise motion (C3-D3-C3-G2 | A2-Bb2-C3) which could not be realized
   at T1 sounding range without going below minimum. The pedal supports
   the SSAA antiphonal melody without competing with it, which is musically
   appropriate for the reduced-texture bars.

5. Bar 16 B1 cadence:
   The original bass phrase 4 cadence included ^G2 (G#2) as a leading tone —
   a diatonic violation for B1. Replaced with E2 (lower neighbor diatonic
   pitch), giving a iv–i cadential bass motion (E2–C2–E2) rather than the
   original VII–i. The harmonic effect is a plagal-flavored cadence
   (Am/E → C/E → Am) which suits the quiet penitential text well.
