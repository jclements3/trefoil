TEXTURE PLAN
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The arrangement opens with a moderately full texture (all 8 voices
present from the first pickup) but in a relatively close, middle-
register voicing that avoids extreme ranges — consistent with the
instruction to reserve climax moments. Phrase 1 establishes the
hymn tune in homophonic 8-voice texture with S1 carrying the melody
and all inner voices moving in thirds and sixths. Phrase 2 introduces
subtle antiphonal coloring: SSAA voices carry the flowing upper
texture while TTBB voices move in a quieter, contrary-motion weave
beneath, creating a call-and-response effect within the full texture.
Phrase 3 is the full climactic statement — all 8 voices at their most
active register, with T1 reaching up to E4 (concert) and B1 down to
G2, producing maximum ensemble spread; S1 reaches D5, the highest
point of the arrangement. Phrase 4 winds down in a gathering cadence:
the antiphonal feel returns briefly (SSAA and TTBB trade motion in
bars 23–25), before all 8 voices converge on the final G major cadence
with a suspension-resolving texture in the inner voices (A2 and T2
resolve stepwise to the root).

Texture types used:
  1. Full 8-voice homophony — phrases 1 and 3 (bars 1–7, 15–21)
  2. Antiphonal exchange (SSAA vs TTBB coloring) — phrase 2 and
     opening of phrase 4
  3. Contrary motion between paired voices throughout (A1/A2, T1/T2)
  4. Call and response between outer voices (S1 melody vs B1 bass)
     with inner voices as harmonic support — all four phrases
  5. Imitative entry feel at each phrase pickup (all voices re-enter
     on the same pickup pitch class but in different registers)

   within acceptable range. No true rule compromise; logged for
   transparency.

3. Diatonic substitute for secondary dominant (Phrase 1, bar 4):
   Replacing V/vi (D major) with vi (A minor) slightly reduces
   harmonic tension at the third phrase-internal cadence point.
   This is the intended behavior per Step 2 of the arranging

   The vi chord still provides harmonic color and the stepwise
   resolution of the soprano A4 is preserved.
