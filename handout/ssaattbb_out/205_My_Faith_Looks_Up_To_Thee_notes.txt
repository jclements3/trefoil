TEXTURE PLAN
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The arrangement opens with the SSAA choir alone (bars 1–2), establishing
the hymn tune at moderate dynamic (mp). The TTBB choir enters at bar 3,
initially in homophony beneath the SSAA, building to a full 8-voice texture
from bar 5 onward. Bars 5–8 and 9–12 (the repeated phrase of the original)
are treated antiphonally between SSAA (carrying the melody) and TTBB (providing
rhythmic and harmonic grounding), with inner voices given independent melodic
contour through passing tones and neighbor motion. The final phrase (bars 13–17)
brings all 8 voices together in full homophony, cresting at bar 15 where
Soprano I reaches A5 and the bass anchors on G2 for maximum spread, then
resolving to a root-position D major cadence at bar 17 with a 4-3 suspension
in the inner voices (implied by the stepwise approach). Texture types employed:
(1) reduced choir opening (SSAA only, bars 1–2); (2) full 8-voice homophony
(bars 5–8 and 13–17); (3) antiphonal exchange between SSAA and TTBB
(bars 3–4 and 9–12); (4) call-and-response between outer voices S1 and B1
with inner harmonic support (bars 14–15).

RULE COMPROMISES
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
1. Bar 15 — Baritone (V7) at G2 (G,,):
   Baritone comfortable range tops out at A3 and bottoms at E2. G2 is within
   absolute range but below the comfortable low. Permitted because this is the
   structural climax of the piece and the voice resolves upward immediately
   to A2 then D3 at the final cadence.

2. Bar 13, beat 3 — parallel motion tendency between V3 (Alto I) and V4
   (Alto II) as both move by step in similar motion toward F#4/D4:
   This is parallel thirds (not parallel fifths or octaves) and is
   fully acceptable stylistically; no true parallel fifths/octaves introduced.

3. Bars 7–8 — Bar (V7) and Bass I (V8) move in octave doubling for two full
   bars to establish the dominant approach. This is a stylistic choice for
   bass solidity at the midpoint half-cadence, not a violation per se, but
   it reduces independence of the bass pair. Justified by the hymn's need
   for a clear half-cadence anchor (A major) at bar 6/7.

4. Bars 1–2 — TTBB choir is silent (rests). The prompt asks for opening
   with reduced texture (4–6 voices); SSAA alone = 4 voices. T/B entry
   at bar 3 adds 4 more, building naturally. This fulfills the dynamic arc
   requirement (open with reduced texture, build to full 8). The SSAA choir
   covering bars 1–2 alone is intentional.

5. V8 (Bass I) bar 4 beat 4: The original bass note was A,,2 = A2 in the
   source, which is within Bass I range (C2 minimum). However, in the ABC
   draft, A,,,2 notation was used in error. The correct note A,,2 (A2) is
   within range and should be notated as A,, in bass clef. This is a
   notational correction, not a rule compromise.

ADDITIONAL NOTES FOR PERFORMERS
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• The hymn tune "Olivet" is in D major throughout; no modulations occur.
  The single chromatic note (E7/G# in the original) has been smoothed to
  E minor, giving the approach to the dominant a slightly modal, gentle color
  appropriate to the text ("My faith looks up to Thee").

• The lever harp should play sustained chords matching the diatonic inner
  voices. The harp's resonance will naturally support the sustained quarter
  notes in the TTBB choir.

• Recommended dynamic arc: bars 1–2 mp (SSAA alone), bars 3–4 mf (TTBB
  enters), bars 5–8 mf–f, bars 9–12 f (antiphonal exchange at forte),
  bars 13–15 ff (climax), bar 16 dim., bar 17 p or pp (final chord fades).

• The final chord should be held with a fermata and released together,
  allowing the harp's overtones to decay naturally.
