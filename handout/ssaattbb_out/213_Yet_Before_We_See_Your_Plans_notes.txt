TEXTURE PLAN
-------------
The arrangement builds through four structural sections:

  Bars 1–4   (Section A, first statement):
    SSAA only (4 voices, mezzo-forte). Soprano I carries the tune; Soprano II
    harmonizes a diatonic third below; Alto I ascends with an independent
    counter-melody in contrary motion; Alto II descends in mirror contrary motion
    to Alto I. This creates a transparent, blended choral texture opening the
    piece at reduced dynamic.

  Bars 5–8   (Section A, repeat with full ensemble):
    All 8 voices enter for the first time (forte). TTBB joins the established
    SSAA texture. Tenor I and Tenor II double the original SATB tenor register
    (split into two independent lines a third apart). Baritone harmonizes a
    third above Bass I. Bass I anchors on the root pedal tone, giving the repeat
    a sense of arrival and increased weight.

  Bars 9–12  (Section B, developmental):
    Full 8 voices with antiphonal design. SSAA choir leads bars 9–10; the
    addition of more angular, independent inner-voice lines (Alto I peaks on D5,
    the extreme top of its range) provides the arrangement's climax. TTBB
    responds in imitative shadow beneath. Bars 11–12 echo bars 9–10 with slight
    variation in Alto I and Alto II lines, building toward the final cadence.

  Bars 13–16 (Section A', final):
    Full 8 voices throughout. The final bar deploys a multi-voice suspension
    cadence: Alto I descends through E4–D4–C4 (7–6–5 against the bass),
    Alto II through D4–C4–F3, and the Baritone through A3–G3–F3 — all
    resolving to an F major triad in root position on the half-note. S1 and S2
    close in unison on F4 at the phrase-ending half note for emphasis.

Texture types used:
  1. Reduced SSAA homophony (bars 1–4) — solo choir opening
  2. Full 8-voice homophony (bars 5–8) — choral entry
  3. Antiphonal exchange SSAA vs. TTBB (bars 9–12) — developmental contrast
  4. Stretto-style suspension cadence in all voices (bar 16) — final cadence


UNISON LOG
-----------
Bar 4 (and 8, 16):  S1 + S2 — beat 3–4 (half note)
  Both voices arrive on F4 at the cadence point. Strong-beat phrase-ending
  unison for blend and emphasis. Voices diverge at the start of the next phrase.
  Purpose: cadential closure.

Bar 16 (final):  S1 + S2 — beat 3–4 (half note)
  Final cadential unison on F4 (root of F major). Same as bar 4/8 pattern;
  used here for maximum phrase-ending finality and ensemble blend.
  Purpose: final cadence, deliberate closure.

No within-pair unisons occur in TTBB. No consecutive-beat unisons anywhere.
The A2/T1 cross-choir register overlap (A2 occasionally in the A3–F3 range,
T1 sounding A3–D3) is intentional and aids ensemble blend at the break.


RANGE LOG
----------
Alto I, bar 9 and 11:  d (D5) — extreme top of A1 range
  Both occurrences fall on the third beat of the bar, at the peak of the
  Section B melodic ascent (A c c c / A1: c d c B). D5 is the absolute top
  of the A1 range but is musically justified as the ensemble climax of Section B.
  Voice moves by step both before (c=C5) and after (c=C5), so the approach
  and resolution are smooth. This is a climax moment, not a concern.

Bass I, bars 2, 10, 14:  B,, (Bb2)
  Bb2 is the second note above the bottom of B1's range (C2). Occurs on the
  second beat as part of the descending bass line Bb2–G2. Within acceptable
  range. Not a concern; this matches the original SATB bass line.

Bass I, bar 16:  F,, (F2)
  Final root arrival on F2. This is comfortable for Bass I (within C2–F3 range)
  and provides a strong low-register closure matching the instruction that the
  final cadence has B1 on the root in low register.

Tenor II, bar 8:  B, (sounds Bb2) — written as B, in 8vb treble notation
  Bb2 is above the T2 bottom (G2) and acceptable. Occurs on the cadential
  approach, moving to A2 then F3. Not a concern.

Baritone, bars 9–12 and 13–16:  E, (E3) on approach to cadences
  E3 is well within the Baritone range (E2–C4). Not a concern.


RULE COMPROMISES
-----------------
Bar 16, Alto I above Soprano II (cross-pair):
  Alto I reaches E4 on beat 1 of bar 16, while Soprano II is at F4 — Alto I
  is one step below Soprano II. On beat 2, Alto I is at D4 while Soprano II
  is at E4 — still one step below. No crossing occurs. (Previously considered
  c=C5 for A1 bar 16, which would have placed A1 above S1 at A4; this was
  revised to E4–D4–C4 to eliminate any cross-pair inversion.)

Bar 9 and 11, Alto I above Soprano I (cross-pair):
  Alto I peaks at d (D5) while Soprano I is at c (C5) on beat 2. This is a
  momentary cross-pair inversion. The rules explicitly permit "voice crossing
  between pairs where musically effective." The D5 is a genuine climax note
  — Alto I leads a rising inner-voice line while Soprano I is on a sustained
  repeated C5 — and the inversion is musically purposeful rather than a
  voice-leading accident. Both voices move by step. Retained as effective.

Bar 5–8, Bass I on C2 throughout bars 5 and 7:
  The B1 voice sustains C2 for four beats in bars 5 and 7 to provide a pedal
  foundation while the upper voices carry melodic activity. The repeated note
  is technically monotone but serves the harmonic grounding function. Baritone
  provides contrary stepwise motion above to compensate.
