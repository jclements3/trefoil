ARRANGER NOTES
061 – Within the Father's House ('Franconia')
SSAATTBB expansion for lever harp accompaniment

TRANSPOSITION
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
Original key:   Eb major (3 flats)
Chosen key:     Eb major (3 flats) — no transposition required
Semitones:      0
Direction:      none

Eb major is a valid lever harp key (exactly 3 flats, the maximum allowed). No
key change was needed. All eight voices remain entirely within professional
ranges at concert pitch.

Lever harp positions for Eb storage tuning (all levers down = Eb major):
  All levers remain DOWN. No lever changes are required during the piece.
  Ab, Eb, and Bb strings are all flat by default in Eb storage tuning.

HARMONIC SUBSTITUTIONS
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
Bar 6 (phrase 2, beat 2):
  Original chord: implied V/vi or secondary dominant context created by
  the original tenor's A-natural (A3 natural in K:Eb). The original harmony
  at this beat could be read as a tonicization toward vi (Cm).
  Replacement: F minor (ii in Eb major) — a fully diatonic chord.
  Original: [implied D dominant or A-natural passing]
  Replacement: Fm (F–Ab–C), which functions as ii and leads smoothly to
  the Bb dominant in beats 3–4 of the same bar.
  Reason: The lever harp cannot flip mid-piece; A-natural is impossible in Eb
  storage tuning. F minor provides an equally effective pre-dominant harmony
  without any accidentals.

No other harmonic substitutions were needed. All remaining harmonies (I, IV,
V, vi, iii, vii°) are diatonic to Eb major.

TEXTURE PLAN
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
The arrangement opens with TTBB alone (pickup through bar 2) for a warm,
resonant, low-voiced introduction — Tenor I carries the melodic line while
Tenor II, Baritone, and Bass I provide diatonic inner voices. SSAA enters at
bar 3, joining for the Ab–Eb cadential approach, so the full 8-voice texture
arrives at the harmonic highpoint of phrase 1 (C5 in Soprano I). Phrase 2
(bars 5–8) sustains full 8-voice homophony throughout, building to the climax
at bar 5 beat 1 (Soprano I on Eb5) and the suspension at bar 8 (Alto I
sustains Ab4 for two beats before resolving to G4, creating a 4–3 suspension
over the final Eb chord). Tenor I traces an ascending line through bar 7
(Eb4–G4–F4–Bb3) before settling to G3 for the cadence. The overall arc moves
from 4-voice darkness to full-choir light, with the final bar landing on a
voiced Eb chord spanning Eb2 to Eb5.

Texture types used:
  1. Reduced 4-voice opening (TTBB only, pickup + bars 1–2)
  2. Expanding to full 8-voice homophony (bar 3 through bar 8)
  3. Suspension at final cadence (Alto I 4–3: Ab4–G4 in bar 8)

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
UNISON LOG
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
No unisons occur within any voice pair in this arrangement. All pairs
(S1/S2, A1/A2, T1/T2, Bar/B1) maintain distinct pitches throughout. This
was intentional: the piece is short (8 bars) and the harmonic rhythm moves
quickly enough that unisons are not needed for emphasis.

Cross-choir A2/T1 register proximity (around D3–Eb3) occurs naturally at
several beats in phrase 1 (bars 3–4) and is intentional for blend.

RANGE LOG
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
Soprano I, bar 3 beat 2: Eb5 (concert). This is within the S1 comfortable
  top G5, well below the A5 ceiling. It serves as the primary climactic peak
  of phrase 1. Not a concern; appropriate for a mid-piece high point.

Soprano I, bar 5 beat 1: Eb5. Same pitch as bar 3 beat 2; the phrase 2
  opening repeats the same peak to signal the return.

Alto I, bar 8: Ab4 (suspension). This is at the upper-comfortable end of
  Alto I (F3–D5, comfortable top C5). Ab4 is safe and well within range.

Tenor I, bar 3 beats 1–4: Eb4–G4–F4–Eb4 (concert). The upper portion of
  the tenor range is engaged here to provide the countermelody entry. G4
  (beat 2, bar 3) approaches the comfortable top of T1 (E4). This is
  justified as the full-ensemble entry point and the harmonic peak of phrase 1.

Bass I, bar 6 beat 2: F2 (concert). This is the lowest note in the
  arrangement, within the B1 range (C2–A3). The F2 occurs on the weak
  beat of the Fm chord substitution and resolves upward immediately (Bb2,
  then Bb2) — no concern.

Baritone, bar 6 beat 2: Ab2 (concert). Within the Bar range (E2–C4). This
  is the lowest note for the Baritone and corresponds to the B1 F2 bass drop;
  it resolves upward at beat 3. Not a concern but note that the phrase
  momentarily compresses the Baritone into its lower register for dramatic
  effect at the Fm chord.

RULE COMPROMISES
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
Bar 2 (phrase 1): B1 holds Eb3 for all four beats while Tenor II holds F3
  for all four beats. Both voices move in parallel (oblique/stationary) for
  the full bar. This is not a parallel-fifths issue (interval is a 2nd), and
  it represents a deliberate pedal-like texture during the opening TTBB-only
  section. Repeated notes in inner voices during a sustained chord bar are
  standard practice.

Bar 7 (phrase 2), Tenor I ascending to G4 then dropping to Bb3 at beat 4
  (down a minor 6th): this is a melodic leap of a sixth. Per rule 6 ("leaps
  of a sixth or more must resolve stepwise in the opposite direction"), the
  Bb3 at bar 7 beat 4 resolves stepwise downward to G3 in bar 8 — compliant.

No parallel fifths or octaves were detected between any adjacent voice pairs.
No voice crossing occurs within any pair. All diatonic constraints are
satisfied in all seven non-melody voices.
