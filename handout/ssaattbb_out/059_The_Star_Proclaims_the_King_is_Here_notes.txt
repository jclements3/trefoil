ARRANGER NOTES
059 — The Star Proclaims the King is Here
(Wo Gott Zum Haus, SSAATTBB arrangement)
============================================================

TRANSPOSITION
-------------
Original key:  Eb major (3 flats)
Chosen key:    Eb major (3 flats) — no transposition required
Semitones:     0 (no shift)

Harp lever positions for Eb storage tuning (all levers down = Eb major):
All levers remain DOWN. No lever changes required during the piece.
The harp naturally provides the Eb major diatonic field: Eb F G Ab Bb C D.

Eb major is the ideal key for this arrangement — it matches the harp's
rest-state tuning exactly, requiring zero mid-piece lever flips.


HARMONIC SUBSTITUTIONS
----------------------
No full chord substitutions were required. The original harmony is
entirely diatonic to Eb major except for the chromatic passing tone A-natural
in the melody (S1, bar 2 beat 4). That note is retained in S1 only (melody
is unrestricted) and is not harmonized by any other voice. The underlying
harmony at that beat is C minor (C Eb G), which is fully diatonic to Eb major;
A-natural is treated as a chromatic upper-neighbor passing tone in the soprano
melody against a Cm sonority in the remaining seven voices.


DIATONIC VIOLATIONS CAUGHT
---------------------------
Bar 7, beat 1, original alto (S1V2): note E-natural (written =E in ABC).
  - Replacement: Eb4 (diatonic tonic), written E in K:Eb (key signature applies).
  - This note was the only inner-voice accidental in the source SATB.
  - Context: the opening of the sintro passage; Cm harmony makes Eb4 the
    natural choice (chord third), and the line moves Eb4–Eb4(8th)–G4–Eb4,
    which retains the falling-then-rising shape of the original.


TEXTURE PLAN
------------
The piece is a single through-composed chorale in 4/4 (9 bars including
the opening full-measure "pickup" on the tonic chord). The dynamic arc
opens with the full 8-voice texture from bar 1, using close spacing in
the middle register to avoid committing the extreme ranges too early.
The texture broadens through bar 3 (Bb major, tenors reaching D4 concert
and bass reaching Bb2) and arrives at the climactic interior phrase (bars 7–8,
sintro section) where S1 ascends to Eb5, T1 falls to Ab3, and B1 reaches
its low point at G2/Ab2. The final cadence in bar 9 settles all voices
onto the Eb major tonic in a sustained whole-note homophony.

Texture types used:
1. Full 8-voice homophony (all bars) — the chorale style of the source is
   maintained throughout as the primary texture.
2. Melodic stratification / contrary motion between paired voices (A1/A2 in
   bar 6 moves Eb4–F4–Eb4–D4 against A2's C4–D4–C4–Bb3, and T1/T2 in bar 6
   move Bb3–D4–C4–C4 against T2's G3–Bb3–Ab3–Ab3), creating independent
   inner-voice lines within the homophonic frame.
3. Melodic ornamentation in S1 (bars 7–8) against sustained inner-voice
   motion, approximating a solo-with-support texture at the peak of the phrase.


UNISON LOG
----------
Bar 9 (final cadence):
  - S1 = S2 = Eb4 (unison): strong beat (whole note), purpose = final tonic
    arrival, deliberate blend at cadence close. Both voices move to the
    unison by step (S1 from F4, S2 from E4) — effective and justified.
  - A1 = A2 = C4... A1=Eb4, A2=C4 — NOT a unison. Corrected: no unison
    in the A pair at bar 9 (A1=Eb4, A2=C4).
  - T1 written = G4, T2 written = Eb4: not a unison.
  - Bar = G3, B1 = Eb3: not a unison.

No other intra-pair unisons occur. The S1/S2 unison on the final note is
the sole intentional unison in the arrangement, placed on the strongest
possible metric position (whole-note cadence).


RANGE LOG
---------
Bar 1 (B1): Eb3 — middle of the B1 range (C2–A3); comfortable, not extreme.
  Note: The original SATB bass sits in the Eb3–F3 area throughout, meaning
  B1 never reaches its extreme low register (C2–D2). This is appropriate for
  a hymn-style texture where extreme lows are reserved for climax.

Bar 3 (T1 concert): D4 — top of T1 comfortable range (T1 comfortable top E4);
  well within limits.

Bar 7 (S1): Eb5 — this is the melodic peak, a half-step above the comfortable
  top of G5 but well within the professional range ceiling of A5. No concern;
  it is intentional as the sintro climax point.

Bar 7 (B1): G2 — low point of B1 in this piece; within comfortable range
  (C2–A3). Not a concern.

Bar 7 (B1): Ab2 — immediately follows G2; both are comfortably within range.

Bar 6 (B1): F2 — lowest note in the arrangement (original bass descent F,, in
  ABC). This is within B1's professional range (C2–A3). No concern.

Bar 4 (Baritone): Ab3 — within comfortable Baritone range (E2–C4). Fine.

General note: the original SATB source does not exploit extreme registers;
consequently this SSAATTBB arrangement also stays well within professional
norms throughout. The S1 Eb5 in bar 7 is the only note near an upper limit.


RULE COMPROMISES
----------------
1. Opening texture — full 8 voices from bar 1 (rule recommends "open with
   reduced texture, 4–6 voices"). The source is a single-phrase German chorale
   with no internal repeat structure that would afford a natural place to
   withhold voices. Beginning with all 8 voices in close spacing (within a
   12th, Eb2–Eb4) achieves the "reduced effect" implicitly; the outer registers
   open up only at bar 7 (S1 ascending to Eb5, B1 descending to G2/Ab2).
   This is the best available compromise for a one-phrase chorale.

2. Antiphonal and canonic textures not achievable — the source is a
   continuous 9-bar chorale phrase with no structural repetition that would
   permit SSAA vs TTBB call-and-response or soprano-tenor canon. The texture
   variety target (3+ types) is met through homophony, melodic stratification,
   and the S1 ornamental peak in bars 7–8.

3. Bar 5, S2 rhythm: `C3/2 G/ A A` — the eighth note G4 on the upbeat of
   beat 1 creates a momentary cross-rhythm against S1's `E3/2 B/`. Both
   voices split beat 1 at the same subdivision (dotted-quarter + eighth), so
   the effect is parallel rhythmic motion rather than independence. This was
   preferred over giving S2 a static dotted quarter on C4, which would have
   created an empty harmonic gap.

4. Baritone bar 7 beat 3 (concert Bb2): this is at the low end of the
   Baritone comfortable range (E2–C4 with comfortable top A3). Bb2 is below
   the comfortable bottom but still within the professional range floor (E2).
   It was chosen to follow the bass descent and create a complete Cm sonority
   (C3 Eb3 G3 Bb2) without parallel fifths.
