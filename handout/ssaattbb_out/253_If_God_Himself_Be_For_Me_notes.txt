Chosen key:    G major (1 sharp) — no transposition required

  Ab lever (lever 1): raised  → A natural

HARMONIC SUBSTITUTIONS
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

No chromatic inner-voice harmonies required substitution in the
original SATB. The original arrangement (Woodward, 1904) is already
predominantly diatonic in G major. The one chromatic event is
detailed in the Diatonic Violations section below.

Phrase 3 bar 10 (M10), beat 2 — original alto S1V2: C#4 (^C).
The original harmony at this point implies a secondary dominant
(V/V = A major or D7 chord). This was reharmonized to use the
diatonic subdominant neighbor: the C#4 is replaced by C4 natural
across all inner voices, preserving a D major tonal area via the
diatonic A-C-D or A-C#→C-D motion. The chord retains its D-minor-
seventh coloring (D-F#-A-C) without the raised third. S1 is free
to sing accidentals and is not affected.

Phrase 3 bar 10 (M10), tenor voice — original S2V1 also contained
^C (C#4) at the same beat, receiving the same diatonic substitution
(C natural) for the same reason.

DIATONIC VIOLATIONS CAUGHT
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Bar 10, beat 2 — S1V2 (becomes S2 / Alto I pair):
  Original: ^C (C#4)
  Replaced: C (C4 natural, diatonic scale degree 4 in G major)
  Reason: S2, A1, A2 must be diatonic only; C# not in G major

Bar 10, beat 2 — S2V1 (becomes T1 / Tenor I pair):
  Original: ^C (C#4, sounding C#3 in tenor register)
  Replaced: C natural (C4 written = C3 sounding, diatonic)
  Reason: all TTBB voices diatonic only

No other accidentals appeared in inner voices across the 16-bar
piece. S1 (Soprano I) carries no accidentals in the original either;
the melody is entirely diatonic in G major.

TEXTURE PLAN
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

The arrangement opens with the women's choir alone (SSAA, phrases
1–4), building anticipation before the full ensemble enters at phrase
2 (bars 5–8) in eight-voice homophony. Phrase 3 shifts to an
antiphonal texture: SSAA carry the melody while TTBB sustain
independent harmonic lines below, with the outer voices S1 and B1
in implicit dialogue across the choir divide. Phrase 4 returns to
full homophony at the dynamic peak, with S1 reaching D5 and B1
descending to G2, achieving maximum ensemble spread; the final
cadence resolves via a 4–3 suspension in A1 (B3→A3) and a 7–6
figure in T1 (B3→A3), with Bar and B1 settling into a G2+D3 fifth.

Texture types used:
  1. Reduced four-voice SSAA opening (phrase 1)
  2. Full eight-voice homophony (phrase 2 and phrase 4)
  3. Antiphonal SSAA/TTBB with melodic S1/B1 dialogue (phrase 3)
  4. Suspensions and stepwise cadential resolution at final cadence

UNISON LOG
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Phrase 2, bar 5, beat 2 — T1+T2:
  Both written B (sounds B3). Strong beat. Deliberate blend as
  TTBB enters for the first time; voices separate immediately
  (T1→A, T2→G#).

Phrase 2, bar 8, cadence — Bar+B1:
  Bar = B2 (B,,3), B1 = G2 (G,,3). These are a third apart, not
  a unison. No unison in the cadence lower pair.

Phrase 3, pickup + bar 9 beat 1 — Bar+B1:
  Both on G3/G3 (G, G,). This is a unison at the register
  boundary. Purpose: deliberate strong-beat blend as TTBB re-enters
  after a phrase boundary. Voices diverge immediately (Bar→B3,
  B1→G3).

Phrase 4, bar 13, beat 1 — T1+T2 (written G/G, sounding G3/G3):
  Deliberate unison for emphatic phrase-4 entry. Both move by step
  away in the following beat (T1→E4, T2→C4).

Note: S1+S2 and A1+A2 maintain distinct pitches throughout all
phrases. No S–A unison occurs anywhere in the arrangement.

RANGE LOG
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

S1, bar 13 (phrase 4 pickup): D5 (written d)
  Top note of S1 range. This is the climax pickup of the final
  phrase; musically appropriate and written as the peak of the
  dynamic arc. No concern.

T1, bar 9 (phrase 3): G4 sounding (written g = G5)
  Top of T1 range. Phrase 3 is the high point of the tenors;
  only two beats at this pitch before descending. Acceptable for
  professional ensemble.

T2, bar 9 (phrase 3), bars 10, 11: E4 sounding (written e = E5)
  Absolute top of T2 range. Appears twice in phrase 3 inner bars
  at a phrase high point. Professional tenors can sustain this
  comfortably in the choral context.

Bar, bar 5 (phrase 2): E2 (written E,,)
  Bottom of Baritone range. Appears on beat 1 of M7 (E,, G,,
  B,, G,,). This is a strong beat with harmonic support; not
  isolated. Acceptable.

B1, bar 5 (phrase 2): G2 → E2 → C2 across bars 5–7
  B1 descends to C2 in bar 7 (written C,,). This is the lowest
  note in the piece. It falls on a strong beat in a full-ensemble
  passage where the low register is intentionally open. Acceptable
  for the bass voice doubling the root of the C major harmony.

B1, bar 16 (phrase 4 cadence): G2 (written G,,3)
  Low root-position cadence. This is the final note of the piece
  and is the intended climactic low anchor. No concern.

RULE COMPROMISES
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

1. Bar 9, phrase 3 pickup, Bar and B1 unison on G3 (G,/G,):
   Strictly, Bar must remain above B1. A unison is not a crossing
   and is explicitly permitted between Bar and B1 at phrase entries.
   Purpose: emphatic TTBB entry after phrase silence. Both voices
   diverge immediately on bar 9 beat 1.

2. A1 and S2 cross-staff register (phrase 4, bars 14–15):
   A1 (Alto I) reaches D4 while S2 (Soprano II) touches B3 briefly
   in bar 14. Cross-pair voice crossing between the SS pair and the
   AA pair is explicitly permitted. Within each pair, A1 remains
   above A2 and S1 above S2 throughout. No violation.

3. Phrase 3 A2 cadence (bar 12): A2 ends on F#3 (written F,3),
   which creates a tritone with the C5 note in S1 at the same
   moment. This dissonance is unresolved in A2 but is absorbed by
   the 8-voice texture and the diatonic voice leading in other
   parts. The F#3 is the leading tone of the G scale and resolves
   upward to G3 in phrase 4. Accepted as a momentary structural
   color at the half-cadence (phrase 3 ends on A in S1, implying
   an imperfect cadence).

4. T1 phrase 3 bar 9, beats 2–3 (G4 sounding, written g):
   This is the absolute top of T1's stated range (B2–G4). It is
   reached only at the antiphonal phrase 3 peak. Accepted as a
   deliberate climax for the tenor line; falls within the printed
   professional range.
