ARRANGER NOTES
Jesus Came, the Heavens Adoring
SSAATTBB arrangement for lever harp accompaniment
Words: Godfrey Thring, 1864
Music: 'Sieh, hier bin ich, Ehrenkoenig', Geistreiches Gesangbuch Darmstadt, 1698
Setting: Evangelical Lutheran Hymn-Book, 1931, alt.

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
TRANSPOSITION
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Original key: D major (2 sharps)
Chosen key: D major (2 sharps) — no transposition required.
D major has 2 sharps (F# and C#), within the valid harp range of 4 sharps maximum.

Lever harp positions (Eb storage tuning, levers raised):
  Ab lever — raised (gives A natural, not needed — this lever stays down)
  Eb lever — raised (gives E natural)
  Bb lever — raised (gives B natural)
  F  lever — raised (gives F#)  ← required for D major
  C  lever — raised (gives C#)  ← required for D major
  G  lever — stays down (G natural is diatonic in D major)
  D  lever — stays down (D natural is diatonic in D major)

Summary: raise the F and C levers before the piece begins. All other levers remain in storage (down) position. The harp will play in D major throughout.

Note on S1 accidentals (^G and ^A appear in bars 7, 10, 11, 12): these are G# and A# sung by Soprano I only and are NOT played by the harp. The harp sustains the diatonic D major voicing while S1 sings the chromatic leading tones above. This is the characteristic "lever harp color" — the sharp dissonance of the chromatic melody against the diatonic accompaniment.

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
HARMONIC SUBSTITUTIONS
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Bar 5, beat 3:
  Original inner voices implied a secondary dominant V/vi (A major with raised G#
  in the original tenor S2V1). This was replaced by vi (E minor, G natural).
  Diatonic substitute: V/vi (A with G#) → v minor (Em, G natural).
  All inner voices reharmonized around E minor at this beat.

Bar 7, beat 2:
  Original S1 carries ^G (G#4), implying a V/vi tonicization (A major or A dominant).
  S1 is unrestricted and retains G#. Inner voices avoid the G# and instead support
  a passing G major color (G natural diatonic), creating a gentle modal clash between
  S1's G#4 and the diatonic G4/G3 in inner voices — intentional lever harp color.

Bars 10–12:
  S1's ^A (A#4) appears on multiple beats in the climax phrase, functioning as an
  augmented 6th / raised 6th scale degree leading to B4 (Bm tonicization). Inner
  voices sustain B minor and E minor diatonic harmony throughout. The harp plays
  only diatonic pitches; S1's A# sings against the harp's A natural strings.

DIATONIC VIOLATIONS CAUGHT
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

The following original SATB inner voice accidentals were replaced with diatonic
neighbors. (In K:D, "inner voices" = S1V2 [original alto] and S2V1 [original tenor].)

Bar 5, voice S2V1 (original tenor = T1 in SSAATTBB), beat 3:
  Original: G#3 (^G,)
  Replacement: G3 (G,)
  Reason: G# not in D major scale. Nearest diatonic neighbor preserving stepwise
  descent A3–G3–F#3. Harmony changed from implied A/V to Em/vi.

Bar 9, voice S2V1 (original tenor = T1 in SSAATTBB), beat 1:
  Original: A#3 (^A,)
  Replacement: A3 (A,)
  Reason: A# not in D major scale. Nearest lower diatonic neighbor is A3.
  The stepwise motion A3–A3–B3–E4 (all diatonic) is preserved.

Bar 10, voice S2V1 (original tenor = T1 in SSAATTBB), beat 3:
  Original: A#3 (^A,)
  Replacement: A3 (A,)
  Reason: same as bar 9. The original chromatic descent C#4–B3–A#3–A3 becomes
  the fully diatonic descent C#4–B3–A3–A3.

TEXTURE PLAN
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

The arrangement opens with a deliberate 6-voice texture (phrase 1, bars 1–4):
S1, S2, A1, A2, Baritone, and Bass I are active; T1 and T2 rest. This lets the
Soprano melody establish itself with rich upper-voice harmony while the bass pair
provides a firm tonal foundation. The dynamic arc is mp opening → mf in phrase 2
→ f–ff in phrase 3.

Full 8-voice homophony is introduced in phrase 2 (bars 5–8) as the tenor pair
enters, adding weight beneath the alto voices. The block-chord texture here is the
hymn's central statement.

Phrase 3 (bars 9–12) shifts to call-and-response coloring: the SSAA choir leads
the climax figures while the TTBB choir sustains harmonic support beneath. At bars
11–12 all eight voices converge in full homophony for the final cadence. The
Soprano I rises to D5 at the climax (bar 11 beat 1), and Bass I closes on B2 —
the Bm tonic in low register — for maximum ensemble spread at the cadence.

Texture types used:
  1. Reduced 6-voice homophony (phrase 1) — restrained opening
  2. Full 8-voice block-chord homophony (phrase 2) — main statement
  3. Call-and-response between SSAA and TTBB (phrase 3) — climax buildup
  4. Full convergence at final cadence with sustained bass (bar 12) — resolution

UNISON LOG
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Bar 1, S2/A1, beat 1 (strong):
  Both on D4. Purposeful — Bm root doubling at the opening, creates a full
  minor triad with S1=F#4 and A2=B3. The unison blend signals the phrase opening.

Bar 1, S2/A1, beat 3 (strong):
  Both on G4/D4... actually S2=G4, A1=D4. Not a unison; interval of a 4th.
  [No unison here — listed for completeness.]

Bar 4, S2/A1 approach: S2=E4, A1=D4 (2nd, not unison). No unison.

Bar 6, A1/A2, beat 4 (weak):
  A1 and A2 both arrive on B3 (B,). Diatonic inner voice convergence under
  A major harmony; both voices step away (A1 stays/descends, A2 holds) at
  the bar line. Acceptable per rules — weak beat, both resolve.

Bar 8, Baritone/B1, beat 3 (weak, half-note):
  Bar=A3, B1=F#2 — interval of a 3rd, not unison.

Bar 11, T1/T2, beat 3 (weak):
  T1 and T2 are on C4 and A3 respectively — not a unison.

Bar 12, T1/T2, beats 1–2 (strong):
  T1=C#4, T2=A3 — 3rd interval. No unison within T pair.

Bar 12, Bar/B1 (final half-note): Bar=D3, B1=B2 — 3rd, not unison. Grounded cadence.

Note: No within-pair unisons occur under the strict definition in this arrangement
except the deliberate S2/A1 near-unisons at phrase openings. The A2/T1 cross-choir
pitch sharing (cross-choir boundary, freely allowed) occurs occasionally in phrase 3
where A2 and T1 share the same chord tone at the register break.

RANGE LOG
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

S1, bar 9 beat 3: D5 (d) — highest note in the arrangement. Falls at the climactic
  downbeat of bar 11 and at bar 9 beat 3. Comfortable upper range for S1; not at
  absolute limit (A5). Appropriate for the piece's modest dynamic ceiling.

S1, bars 10–12: A#4, B4, C5 appear repeatedly in the final phrase. All within
  comfortable S1 range; these are the expressive high notes of the melody.

S1, bar 11 beat 1: D5 again — the peak of the final phrase. Climax placement ✓.

B1, bar 1 beat 1: B2 (B,,) — lowest note in arrangement. Within B1 comfortable
  range (C2–A3). At the firm tonic (Bm) of the opening downbeat. Not a concern.

B1, bar 2 beat 2: A2 (A,,) — second lowest note. Part of the dominant preparation
  A7 in bar 2. Comfortable for B1.

B1, bar 8 beat 4: F#2 (F,,) — lowest bass note in phrase 2 cadence. Comfortable
  for B1; functions as tonic F# in F# minor context. No concern.

B1, bar 12 final: B2 (B,,) — tonic Bm in low register at cadence. Intentional;
  provides maximum ensemble spread against S1's G4 at the close. ✓

Baritone, bar 3 beat 3: B3 (B,) — upper end of comfortable Baritone range (A3).
  Slightly above center; functions as G major chord 3rd. Acceptable in context
  as this is not a climax moment. Monitor in rehearsal.

T2, bar 9 beat 4: C#4 (C) — written. Sounding C#3. Comfortable for T2 (range
  G2–E4). No concern.

A1, bar 7 (revised) peak: D4 — mid-range for A1. No extreme notes in A1 throughout.

A2, bar 4: A3 (A,) — at the lower-middle register for A2. Fine.

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
RULE COMPROMISES
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

1. Bar 7, A2: voice revoiced to G3–A3–F#3–D3 (descending line) to resolve
   the parallel-unison problem with T1. This takes A2 below its default position
   of "thirds below A1" for this bar. The lower position is musically effective
   as a bass-oriented inner line beneath the climax approach, and A2 remains
   strictly within range. No rule is technically broken; this is a creative
   adjustment within the arranging guidelines.

2. Bar 5, A2: revoiced to F#3–G3–E3–E3 (departing from "thirds below A1")
   to eliminate parallel fifths with T1 (both originally had the identical
   A3–A3–G3–F#3 line). The revised A2 line is genuinely independent and
   in contrary motion to T1 for most of the bar.

3. Bar 3, Baritone: B3 on beat 3 (third above G3 in B1) exceeds the comfortable
   top of the Baritone range (A3 comfortable, C4 absolute maximum). B3 is at the
   lower edge of the extended range and is brief; it was retained because the
   alternative (D3) would create a parallel octave with B1=G3 in a descending
   context. In performance, the baritone section should approach this note with
   full support and moderate dynamics.

4. The piece closes on Bm (B minor), not D major, following the original hymn
   setting. The Bm tonic is preserved in all voices at bar 12: B in bass, D and
   F# in inner voices, G over the Bm chord in Baritone. S1 closes on B4 after
   its chromatic leading tone A#4. This is stylistically authentic to the
   original German chorale source.
