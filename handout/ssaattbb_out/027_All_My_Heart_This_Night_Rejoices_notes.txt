All My Heart This Night Rejoices (Ebeling, 1666)
SSAATTBB arrangement from Open Hymnal Project SATB source

TRANSPOSITION
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Original key: G major (1 sharp)
Chosen key:   G major (1 sharp) — no transposition required
Semitones shifted: 0

G major is a valid lever harp key (within the 3 flats / 4 sharps limit).

Lever positions for G major from Eb storage tuning (all levers down = Eb major):
  Bb lever → raised to B
  Eb lever → raised to E
  Ab lever → raised to A
  F  lever → raised to F#
  (C, G, D levers remain down — those strings are already natural)
Result: 4 levers raised, giving the 7 diatonic pitches G A B C D E F#.

The original SATB soprano sits in the G3–E4 range. For SSAATTBB, S1
takes the melody transposed up one octave (G4–E5), which places it in
the natural professional soprano register without altering the key.

DIATONIC VIOLATIONS CAUGHT
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Measure 11, beat 4, Alto (original V: S1V2):
  Original note: C# (^C in ABC, raised leading tone implying A major,
                 i.e. V/ii tonicization toward D minor area)
  Replacement:   C natural (diatonic 7th of D major / minor 3rd of Am)
  Reason: All non-melody voices must be diatonic. C natural preserves
  the descending inner-voice motion (D D D C) and reharmonizes the beat
  as A minor rather than A major. The resulting chord (G F# E C A) reads
  as a diatonic passing Am/C sonority leading into the D major whole note
  of measure 12. S1 sings no accidental here, so the harp's C natural
  string is unobstructed.

TEXTURE PLAN
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

The arrangement opens with SSAA alone (mm. 1–2), a four-voice chorale
texture that establishes the hymn tune gently. Tenors join in measure 3
and the full eight voices arrive in measure 4 for a brief homophonic
climax on the sustained D5. Measures 5–6 are given to SSAA alone, and
measures 7–8 answer antiphonally with TTBB alone — the two choirs echo
the same harmonic phrase in contrasting registers. Full eight-voice
homophony returns at measures 9–10. Measures 11–12 strip back to a
soprano/bass melodic duo (S1 descends the cadential G–F#–E–D) with the
inner six voices sustaining a calm background chord — the most transparent
moment in the piece. The final repeated phrase (mm. 13–16) rebuilds to
full eight-part writing, with tenors in parallel motion to the sopranos
at the octave throughout measure 15 (stretto unison between S1/T1 and
S2/T2 pairs), and the final bar settling on a root-position G major chord
with B1 on the low G2.

Texture types used:
  1. Full 8-voice homophony (mm. 3–4, 9–10, 13–16)
  2. Antiphonal exchange — SSAA vs TTBB (mm. 5–8)
  3. Solo/duo with sustained inner voices (mm. 11–12)
  4. Stretto/parallel octave doubling between soprano and tenor pairs (m. 15)
  5. Reduced 4-voice opening (mm. 1–2)

RULE COMPROMISES
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

1. Measure 12, Alto II (V:4): sustains f4 (F#4) throughout. Over the
   D major whole note, F#4 is the third of the chord — correct — but
   A2 has been descending stepwise and F#4 represents a minor-third skip
   upward from the preceding E4 area. The skip was accepted to keep A2
   below A1 (D4 in m. 12) while providing a chord tone. No voice-leading
   rule is technically broken (the leap is less than a sixth; no augmented
   interval); this note is flagged as a mild concern.

2. Measure 13–14, Bar voice (V:7): Bar follows a line (A2 B2 C3 / G2 A2 B2)
   that occasionally forms a perfect fourth or fifth against B1 rather than
   the preferred third or sixth. These intervals arise on passing beats and
   resolve by step. No parallel 5ths occur because the voices move in
   contrary or oblique motion.

3. Measures 7–8, SSAA tacet / measures 5–6, TTBB tacet: the antiphonal
   plan leaves four voices silent for two full measures each time. This is
   an intentional textural device (antiphonal exchange), not a voice-leading
   compromise. Rests are written as z4 whole-measure rests.

4. Measure 6, A2 (V:4) beat 3–4: B2 (B3) is a minor third below A1's
   D4 on those beats rather than a preferred third or sixth from an upper
   voice. The interval from S2 (G4) to A2 (B3) is a minor 6th — acceptable.
   The compromise is that A2's beat-2-to-beat-3 motion (D4→B3) is a falling
   minor third rather than a step; this was necessary to avoid a parallel
   octave with S1 (which also descends) and to give A2 a satisfying
   resolution onto B3 for the G major arrival.
