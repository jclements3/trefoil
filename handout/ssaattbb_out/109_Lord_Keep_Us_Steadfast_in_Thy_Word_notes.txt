TEXTURE PLAN
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
The arrangement opens with a four-voice SSAA choir alone
(pickup and bar 1), establishing the chorale melody before
the lower choir enters. The TTBB choir joins at bar 2
(the first internal cadence point), creating an immediate
increase in depth and register. From bar 3 onward the full
eight voices sing together in homophony, building steadily
to the climax phrase (bars 5–7) where Soprano I reaches A4
in the running melodic phrase and Bass I descends to E2.
A controlled antiphonal moment occurs in bar 5: Alto I and
Alto II yield to the tenors and basses on beats 3–4, then
the full ensemble reunites for the final push to cadence.
Bar 7 features a 4–3 suspension (A3→G3 in Alto I, C4→B3 in
Soprano II) resolving into the final bar 8 chord. The piece
closes on an E minor chord — the original Phrygian-inflected
cadence of the Hassler setting — with the bass on E2 for
maximum gravity.

Texture types employed:
  1. Reduced opening — SSAA choir alone (pickup, bar 1)
  2. Full 8-voice homophony — bars 3, 4, 6, 8
  3. Antiphonal exchange — bar 5 (SSAA vs TTBB)
  4. Suspension at cadence — bars 7→8 (S2 and A1)

RULE COMPROMISES
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
Bar 5, Alto I and Alto II: Alto I rests on beats 1–2 and 5–6
while Alto II also rests on those beats (both rest together
during the antiphonal TTBB passage on beats 3–4, and rest on
beats 1–2 and 5–6 as written). This was a deliberate texture
choice to create antiphonal contrast. The rule that "every
voice must be a singable independent melodic line" is relaxed
for these two beats of rest — the Alto pair effectively sits
out the TTBB antiphonal response. This is within the spirit of
the antiphonal texture instruction.

Bar 7, S2 beats 5–6: S2 sings C4, C4 (two repeated quarter
notes before resolving to B3 in bar 8). Repeated notes are
technically not a "melodic" line in the strict sense, but serve
the suspension function (preparation on beat 5, suspension on
beat 6, resolution in bar 8). This is standard suspension
technique and not considered a compromise.

Parallel motion check note: S2 and A1 move in parallel thirds
through much of the piece. This is intentional (paired voice
writing) and does not constitute parallel fifths or octaves.
All parallel octaves/fifths were avoided between non-paired
voices through careful interval management.
