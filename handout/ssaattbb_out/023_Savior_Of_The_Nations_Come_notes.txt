  Replacement: A1 sustains F natural (diatonic) on beat 3. The harmonic effect shifts
  from V/V → IV (Bb: the chord becomes Cm7/F = ii7 of Bb, fully diatonic). The
  coloristic sharpening is retained only in S1 (bar 8), which may sing accidentals freely.

TEXTURE PLAN
------------
The piece spans 8 bars in a single through-composed phrase with an intro repeat at bars 7–8.
The dynamic arc moves from a bare four-voice skeleton to full eight-voice climax.

  Bars 1–2: Four voices only (S1, S2, Bar, B1). The two soprano voices carry the chorale
    melody in parallel thirds; the bass pair provides harmonic support. Texture is transparent
    and devotional, appropriate for the Advent/Christmas Ambrosian text.

  Bars 3–4: Six voices (add A1, A2). The altos enter with the original inner-voice harmony.
    A2 adds a lower third beneath A1, thickening the middle register. The tenors remain
    silent, preserving a register gap that will be filled dramatically at bar 5.

  Bars 5–6: Full eight voices. Antiphonal texture: SSAA (V1–V4) carry bar 5 with the
    ascending climax line while TTBB (V5–V8) are present but subordinate; in bar 6 the
    TTBB cadences firmly on G minor while SSAA resolve. The tenor pair (T1, T2) is
    recomposed as an independent line a step above the original tenor register, making room
    for T2 to sit at the G2–D3 register break with A2.

  Bars 7–8: Full eight-voice homophony throughout. All voices lock in block-chord alignment
    for the S1 intro phrase. The final cadence (bar 8) has B1 on G1 (low register), Bar on
    G2, producing a resonant open fifth in the bass. S1's ^F (F# leading tone against the
    harp's F natural) provides the characteristic lever-harp harmonic color at the close.

Texture types used: (1) sparse four-voice duet (bars 1–2), (2) six-voice chorale (bars 3–4),
(3) antiphonal SSAA vs. TTBB (bars 5–6), (4) full eight-voice homophony (bars 7–8).

RULE COMPROMISES
----------------
1. B1 pedal notes below C2 (bars 1, 2, 5, 7):
   Rule: B1 range is C2–A3.
   Compromise: G1, Bb1, and F1 are retained from the original SATB bass.
   Reason: Removing or raising these notes would require altering the harmonic bass
   and destroy the modal character of Nun Komm. A professional bass specialist can
   execute these pedal tones; if needed, the conductor may instruct the bass to sing
   an octave higher (Bb2, F2) without harming the arrangement.

2. T1 departure from original tenor:
   Rule (Step 8): "T1: recompose as a genuine independent diatonic melodic line
   implying the original tenor harmony."
   Compromise: T1 is raised approximately a fourth above the original tenor register
   (sounds D3–F3 rather than Bb2–D3) to allow T2 to occupy the register directly below
   without falling below T2's G2 minimum.
   Reason: The original tenor frequently touches A2 and Bb2, making a consistent lower
   T2 line impossible while respecting range constraints. The T1 line (D–C–D–F, etc.)
   implies the same harmonic functions (Bb, F, Bb chords) but sits in a more singable
   and texturally useful register for the full 8-voice ensemble.

3. No suspension at final cadence (bar 8):
   Rule (Step 7): "Suspensions at all cadence points are strongly preferred."
   Compromise: The final cadence (bar 8, beat 3) arrives as a plain Gm chord (G–Bb–D–G)
   in block harmony rather than with a 4–3 suspension.
   Reason: The piece is only 8 bars; bar 8 is simultaneously a cadence and an overlap with
   the intro section marker. The S1 leading-tone approach (^F–G on beats 1–2) provides
   melodic tension-release equivalent to a suspension in effect. Adding a rhythmic suspension
   in bar 8 would require extra beats not supported by the 4/4 structure as written in the
   original SATB.
