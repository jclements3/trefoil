HARMONIC SUBSTITUTIONS
════════════════════════════════════════════════════

The original SATB hymn is in D natural/harmonic minor (using F major key signature).
All chromatic accidentals in the original are C# (raised 7th of D harmonic minor),
appearing in the inner voices as part of A major / A7 chords (V / V7 of Dm).

Since all voices except S1 must be diatonic to F major (no C#), every A7 chord
was replaced with its diatonic substitute. S1 never carried these accidentals
(the soprano line is entirely diatonic), so S1 is unchanged throughout.

Bar 2, beats 1-2:
  Original inner harmony: A7 (A-C#-E-G) — V7 of Dm
  Replacement harmony:    Am7 (A-C-E-G) — diatonic v7 of Dm
  Reason: C# not diatonic to F major; Am7 preserves the dominant function softly.

Bar 3, beats 3-4:
  Original inner harmony: A7 — V7 of Dm
  Replacement harmony:    Am — diatonic v of Dm
  Reason: same as bar 2.

Bar 4, beats 1-4:
  Original inner harmony: A7 / A — half cadence on A major
  Replacement harmony:    Am — diatonic v of Dm (half cadence)
  Reason: The original C# appeared in both alto and bass. Both replaced diatonically.
  The harmonic tension of the half cadence is preserved through Am (natural minor v),
  which still functions as a dominant-area chord in Dm context.

Bar 7, beat 2:
  Original inner harmony: A7 (passing)
  Replacement harmony:    C major passing chord (diatonic)
  Reason: C# in alto replaced by C natural, shifting the harmony to C major as a
  brief subdominant-area passing chord. Voice leading remains smooth.

Bar 8, beats 1-2:
  Original inner harmony: A7 (penultimate cadential chord)
  Replacement harmony:    Am — diatonic v of Dm
  Reason: final cadential approach retains tension through Am without C#.
  The final Dm resolution (beat 3-4) is fully convincing.

TEXTURE PLAN
════════════════════════════════════════════════════

The arrangement opens with six voices (SSAATT — Baritone and Bass I silent) in bars 1-4,
establishing the modal D-minor character with a transparent texture. The full eight-voice
ensemble enters at bar 5, building through an antiphonal approach (upper SSAA choir
answered by TTBB inner motion) to the climax in bar 6, where S1 reaches C5 (top of its
range) and T1 reaches G4 (concert, top of its comfortable range) simultaneously.
Bars 7-8 provide a measured release through stepwise contrary motion and a suspension-
inflected cadence: Baritone suspends Bb2->C3 against the descending outer voices,
landing on an open Dm chord (D3 in Bass I, F3 in Baritone, A3 in A1/T2, D4 in A2/S2/T1).

Texture types used:
1. Reduced 6-voice homophony (bars 1-4): SSAA + TT, no bass. Opens the arrangement
   with choral blend before committing to full sonority.
2. Full 8-voice homophony (bars 5-6): All eight voices in block-chord homophony,
   maximizing ensemble weight at the dynamic climax.
3. Antiphonal exchange (implicit, bars 5-6): The upper SSAA choir carries the melodic
   line (S1 prominent) while TTBB provides harmonic support with more stepwise motion,
   creating an antiphonal blend rather than strict alternation.
4. Cadential suspension (bar 8): Baritone voice (B,, C,) — Bb2 suspending to C3 —
   against a converging cadence in all other voices, providing the suspension resolution
   called for at cadence points.

RULE COMPROMISES
════════════════════════════════════════════════════

1. T1 and T2 reach top of range at climax (bars 5-6):
   Rule: "Do not open the arrangement at full range — reserve extreme high and low
   notes for climax moments only."
   Status: COMPLIED. The extreme notes occur precisely at the climax (bars 5-6),
   not at the opening.

2. A2 bar 4, beat 4: F3 (F,) — low end of A2's range (D3-Bb4, min D3):
   F3 is above the D3 minimum. Technically no violation. Flagged as it is on the
   lower third of the range; the following bar raises A2 immediately to F3-C4.

3. Bar beat 5 bars 1-2 (C4): Baritone at its ceiling:
   Rule: "Comfortable top A3." C4 is the absolute ceiling.
   Compromise: C4 used for two beats at the section 2 entry (the climax onset).
   The voice enters from rest and the C4 functions as a strong, resonant entry note.
   This is a deliberate choice to maximize ensemble spread at the climax.

4. Parallel 3rds between Bar and B1 throughout bars 5-8:
   All intervals between Baritone and Bass I are major or minor 3rds (no 5ths or
   octaves). This is an intentional design choice — not a rule compromise.

5. Diatonic substitutions soften harmonic tension at cadences:
   The original A7 (V7/i) cadential chords become Am7 (v7/i). This is mandated
   by the diatonic constraint and explicitly anticipated by the prompt's instruction
   to substitute "V major/dominant 7th -> v minor." The cadences remain convincing
   in the D-minor modal context because Am has strong subdominant-area function.
