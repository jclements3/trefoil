ARRANGER NOTES
Whiter Than Snow — SSAATTBB Arrangement
James Nicholson / William Gustavus Fischer, 1872
Arranged for 8-voice double choir and lever harp

════════════════════════════════════════════════════
TRANSPOSITION
════════════════════════════════════════════════════
Original key:  A major (3 sharps: F# C# G#)
Chosen key:    A major (3 sharps) — NO TRANSPOSITION REQUIRED
Reason: A major falls within the valid lever harp range (≤ 4 sharps).
  3 sharps is comfortably achievable on a lever harp in Eb storage tuning.

Lever positions for A major (raise levers to sharpen):
  F → F#  (raise F lever on all octaves)
  C → C#  (raise C lever on all octaves)
  G → G#  (raise G lever on all octaves)
  A, B, D, E remain natural (levers down)

All 8 voices checked in A major — ranges confirmed within professional limits.
No range issues requiring key change.

TEXTURE PLAN
════════════════════════════════════════════════════
The arrangement opens in a restrained 8-voice homophony for the verse
(bars 1–5), with the TTBB choir providing a steady harmonic foundation
while SSAA carries the melodic interest. The refrain ("Whiter than
snow") emerges as full 8-voice block-chord homophony at bar 16 for
maximum warmth, building to the highest registral spread.

Texture types used:
  1. Full 8-voice homophony (verse bars 1–5, refrain bars 16–19)
  2. Call and response between SSAA and TTBB choirs (bars 6–10,
     antiphonal exchange on the repeated verse phrase)
  3. Canonic imitation between S1/T1 at the unison an octave apart
     (bars 11–15, climactic phrase leading to the double bar)
  4. Solo/duet texture implied in the intro/tag section (bars 22–26),
     where S1 and B1 carry the outer lines with sustained inner voices
  5. Stretto-like convergence at the final cadence (bar 25–26) with
     all voices arriving on A simultaneously

Dynamic arc: mp (opening verse) → mf (second verse phrase) → f (refrain)
→ ff (climax bar 13 "B6/2 A B2") → mf (refrain repeat/tag) → p final
cadence. S1 reaches B4–c5 range throughout; climax at bar 22 tag with
d5 (the highest note in S1, used sparingly for the intro tag high point).
B1 anchors on A2 at the final cadence.

RULE COMPROMISES
════════════════════════════════════════════════════
Bar 4 (A2/T1 cross-register): A2 and T1 share the pitch A at the phrase
  boundary. Per Step 5 rules this cross-choir unison is explicitly
  permitted and was used deliberately for ensemble blend at the tonic
  return.

Bar 15 (full-choir unison convergence): All 4 voice pairs converge to
  unisons on the final cadence. The rule advising against unisons as a
  "default solution to a voice leading problem" was relaxed here because
  the convergence is a deliberate artistic choice — the hymn text calls
  for a unified, reverent close — not a voice-leading workaround.
  This occurs only once across the entire piece.

Bars 5–8 (S2 and A1 move in parallel thirds for two bars): The instruction
  prefers contrary or oblique motion when splitting a SATB note. In these
  bars S1V2 (now S2) and the new A1 line both follow the descending alto
  figure in parallel thirds for melodic naturalness. The motion is parallel
  thirds rather than fifths or octaves, which is acceptable stylistically;
  the compromise was noted and the lines were given slightly different
  rhythmic placement to preserve individuality.

Bars 22–26 (intro tag, Bar and B1 near-unison): The Baritone and Bass I
  share D2 in bar 22 and A2/A2 in bar 26. The tag functions as a closing
  gesture where a rich, grounded bass is more important than strict
  independence. The two lines diverge rhythmically within each bar to
  maintain some independence even when sharing pitch class.
