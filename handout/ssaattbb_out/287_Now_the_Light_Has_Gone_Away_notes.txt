ARRANGER NOTES — "Now the Light Has Gone Away"
SSAATTBB arrangement for 8-voice ensemble with lever harp
Frances Ridley Havergal / Liederbuch für Kleinkinder-Schulen, 1842

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
TRANSPOSITION
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Original key: F major (1 flat)
Chosen key: F major (1 flat) — no transposition required.
Semitones shifted: 0.

F major is a valid lever harp key (1 flat ≤ 3-flat maximum). All
8 voices remain well within professional ranges at concert pitch.

Lever harp setup for F major (Eb storage tuning, levers down = Eb):
  Raise the Bb lever (one lever above Eb baseline).
  Strings sounding: F G A Bb C D E — F major diatonic.
  No mid-piece lever flips required; the arrangement is fully diatonic
  in all inner voices.

HARMONIC SUBSTITUTIONS
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Bar 6, beats 1–2: The original harmony implied passing chromaticism
(the Eb in the original alto created a secondary-dominant color on
beat 1, suggesting a brief tonicization). This was replaced with
a smooth Gm7 → Dm progression using only diatonic tones:
  Beat 1: Gm (G–Bb–D–F) — diatonic ii in F, passing color.
  Beat 2: Dm (D–F–A) — diatonic vi in F.
  Reason: Eb in inner voice violates the lever harp diatonic
  constraint. The Gm → Dm motion preserves the descending bass
  (F–F–Bb) and the sense of harmonic motion toward IV.

Bar 6, beats 3–4: Bb major (IV) retained as written — already
diatonic. No substitution needed.

No other chromatic harmonies required substitution.

TEXTURE PLAN
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

The arrangement opens with all 8 voices in gentle block-chord
homophony (bars 1–4), using the dotted lullaby rhythm to establish
the intimate character of the text ("Now the light has gone away").
The dynamic arc builds through bars 5–6 as the full ensemble
sustains the repeated-note climax figure, with Soprano I reaching
D5 and the bass dropping toward Bb2 on the final Bb major chord of
bar 6. Bar 7 introduces a suspended I64 cadential preparation, and
bar 8 resolves with a stepwise descent in all voices to the tonic,
Bass I landing on F2 for a grounded close.

Textures employed:
  1. Full 8-voice homophony (bars 1–8, primary texture).
  2. Antiphonal register contrast: SSAA voices sustain chord tones
     in the upper register (bars 3–5) while TTBB voices provide
     independent contrary motion in the lower register.
  3. Stepwise contrary motion between paired voices (e.g., A1 and A2
     move in contrary motion throughout; T1 and T2 likewise).
  4. Suspension preparation at bar 7–8 cadence: the Bb3 in S2 and
     Bb3 in T1 (bar 8 beats 1–2) resolve stepwise to the tonic
     chord in beats 3–4.

UNISON LOG
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

No within-pair unisons occur in this arrangement. All voice pairs
(S1/S2, A1/A2, T1/T2, Bar/B1) maintain distinct pitches throughout
all 8 bars. The 4+4 distribution (all voices independent) is used
consistently.

Cross-choir near-unisons (A2 ≈ T1 register):
  Bar 1: A2=G3, T1=E4 concert — distinct, no unison.
  Bar 5: A2=Bb3, T1=E4 concert — distinct.
  (The A2/T1 register break is smooth but never a literal unison.)

RULE COMPROMISES
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

1. Bar 2, beat 1, Tenor I (concert F4): This is one step above T1's
   stated comfortable top (E4). The note falls on a passing dominant
   beat and is brief (one quarter note). Relaxed because the line
   needs to connect smoothly from bar 1's E4 up to the dotted F4
   that reflects the soprano's own E–F ascent. If uncomfortable in
   performance, T1 can substitute D4 (a 3rd below the soprano E4)
   without harmonic consequence.

2. Soprano II, bar 6 beat 2→3: ascending minor 6th (D4→Bb4).
   The voice leading rule requires a leap of a 6th to resolve
   stepwise in the opposite direction. This is satisfied: S2 steps
   from Bb4 down to A4 in bar 7 beat 1. The leap is accepted because
   it drives S2 to its climax note alongside the soprano's D5, and
   the resolution is immediate.

3. Alto I and Alto II, bar 6 beat 2→3: both voices make ascending
   motion of a 6th or more (A1: Bb3→G4; A2: G3→E4). These are
   simultaneous similar-motion 6ths between the alto pair on a
   building beat approaching the climax. Both resolve stepwise
   downward in bar 7 (A1: G4→F4; A2: E4→D4). Accepted for the
   sake of the climax build; the parallel similar motion is mitigated
   by the pair moving to a 3rd interval (G4–E4) rather than an
   octave or 5th.

4. Bass I, bar 8 beats 3–4 (F2): descending below comfortable range.
   Accepted as the intended final cadential depth per the arranging
   plan. Optional: If the bass section cannot sustain F2 with
   adequate tone, raise to F3 (one octave) — the harmony is
   unaffected, and Bass I remains on the tonic root.
