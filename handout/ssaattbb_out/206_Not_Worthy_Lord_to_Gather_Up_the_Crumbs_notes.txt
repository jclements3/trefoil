ARRANGER NOTES
==============
Not Worthy, Lord, to Gather Up the Crumbs
'Eventide' — SSAATTBB Arrangement
Words: Edward Henry Bickersteth, 1872 / Music: William H. Monk, 1861

TRANSPOSITION
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Original key:  Eb major (3 flats: Bb, Eb, Ab)
Chosen key:    Eb major (3 flats) — no transposition required
Semitones shifted: 0

Lever harp configuration for Eb major (Eb storage tuning, all levers down):
  All levers down = Eb major. No levers need to be raised.
  The harp naturally produces Eb, Bb, Ab on every string.
  This is the simplest possible harp setup for this arrangement.

All 8 voices remain well within professional ranges in Eb.
No transposition was considered because the original key is valid.

HARMONIC SUBSTITUTIONS
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Bar 7 (beats 3-4): The original soprano line moves G - A-natural (written =A in ABC).
  This A-natural functions as the major 3rd of an F-major chord (V/IV) or as part
  of a brief tonicization of Bb major (V/V). The original inner voices support this
  with chromatic motion (see Diatonic Violations below).
  Substitution: Inner voices (S2, A1, A2, T1, T2, Bar) use the diatonic Bb-major
  chord (V) instead of F major, making the A-natural in S1 a characteristic
  dissonance over a Bb-harmony background — exactly the lever-harp idiom where S1
  sings freely against the diatonic harp strings.
  Original harmony: F major (V/V) → Bb major (V)
  Diatonic substitute: Bb major (V) throughout beats 3-4 of bar 7

No other bars required harmonic substitution. The remaining chromatic motion
(original alto bar 11) was handled at the individual-note level (see below).

DIATONIC VIOLATIONS CAUGHT
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Bar 11, original Alto (S1V2), beats 3-4: =E =E (E-natural)
  E-natural is chromatic in Eb major. In the original SATB, the alto holds
  E-natural over an Ab bass, producing a coloristic major-7th effect (Abmaj7).
  Replacement in A1: C5 - Bb4 - Bb4 - Ab4 (descending diatonic line)
  Replacement in A2: Eb4 - F4 - G4 - G4 (ascending contrary motion)
  Reason: Both replacements preserve the overall harmonic character of the bar
  (iv / Abmaj area moving toward Bb) without requiring any lever flip.
  The descending A1 line (C-Bb-Bb-Ab) provides smooth soprano-to-alto voice
  leading, while A2 fills the texture with ascending contrary motion.

Bar 7, original Alto (S1V2), beat 4: The original alto holds E (Eb) against the
  soprano's =A — this is diatonic (Eb is not chromatic), so no substitution needed
  in the alto itself. Only the harmonic context of the soprano's =A required the
  substitution noted above.

No other chromatic notes appear in the inner voices of the original SATB.

TEXTURE PLAN
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

The arrangement opens with six voices (SSAA + Baritone + Bass I) in bars 1-4,
establishing the hymn's serene character with a reduced but harmonically complete
texture; the Tenor pair is withheld to create a sense of arrival when they enter
at bar 5 with the full TTBB choir. Bars 5-8 use antiphonal exchange: SSAA voices
carry the melodic line while TTBB provide independent harmonic counterpoint below,
with T1 closely shadowing the original tenor. Bars 9-12 shift to a call-and-response
texture, with S1 and B1 forming an outer melodic frame while inner voices sustain
harmonic support — recalling the opening with slight intensification. The final
section (bars 13-16) builds to full 8-voice homophony at the climax (bar 14,
S1 on Ab5 / B1 on Ab1, maximum spread), then contracts to a simple descending
cadence with stretto-like convergence in bar 15-16, settling on the Eb unison.

Textures employed:
  1. Reduced opening (bars 1-4): 6-voice texture, TTBB pair withheld
  2. Antiphonal exchange (bars 5-8): SSAA choir vs TTBB choir in dialogue
  3. Call and response, outer voices (bars 9-12): S1/B1 frame, inner harmony
  4. Full 8-voice homophony (bars 13-16): climax and final cadence
  5. Stretto-like convergence at final cadence (bars 15-16)

UNISON LOG
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

No deliberate unisons within a voice pair (S1=S2, A1=A2, T1=T2, Bar=B1) were
composed into the arrangement. The voice pairs maintain distinct pitches throughout.

Possible near-unisons of interest:
  Bar 4:  S2=Eb4, A1=G4 — a 3rd apart, not unison. Both pairs settle to their
          phrase-end notes independently.
  Bar 8:  T1=F3(concert), T2=Bb2(concert) — a 5th apart, clear separation.
  Bar 16: A2=Eb4, A1=G4 — 3rd at final cadence. S2=C4 distinct from S1=Eb4.

All voice pairs maintain minimum 2nd separation throughout; no within-pair unisons
were used even at phrase endings, preferring sustained 3rds and 6ths instead.

RULE COMPROMISES
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

1. BARITONE BAR 2 RANGE (Bb1 = B,,):
   Rule: Baritone comfortable bottom is E2.
   Compromise: Baritone holds Bb1 on beats 3-4 of bar 2 (and bar 10) to parallel
   the bass pair's G1 (Bass I) and maintain a 3rd-above relationship (Bb1 above G1).
   Reason: Forcing the baritone up to D2 or F2 in these bars would leave the low
   bass register exposed; the original SATB already wrote these bass notes, and
   having the baritone quietly double the bass area preserves ensemble warmth in
   the opening phrase. A professional baritone can manage Bb1 quietly.
   Mitigation: The note is marked for director's discretion; the suggested
   alternative is D,(D2) if the singer cannot reach Bb1 comfortably.

2. BASS I G1 RANGE (G,,):
   Rule: Bass I comfortable bottom is C2.
   Compromise: The original SATB bass already contained G,,2 (G1) in bars 2 and 10.
   These notes are retained because removing them would require reharmonizing the
   cadential bass motion (G-root pedal under I6/4) which is structurally important.
   Mitigation: Performers may substitute G, (G2) an octave up with director approval.

3. TENOR II EXTREME LOW (Eb2 concert, bar 6 beat 3 and bar 16):
   Rule: T2 comfortable bottom is G2.
   Compromise: T2 briefly touches Eb2 (written E, in 8vb treble) at bar 6 beat 3
   as part of a stepwise descent (F2–Eb2–F2) and sustains it at bar 16 as the
   final cadential note. At bar 6, the note is a passing quarter note and resolves
   immediately upward. At bar 16, it is the final chord tone (Eb) held in a quiet
   closing dynamic — manageable for a professional tenor at the breath's end.
   Mitigation: Bar 6 T2 may be sung on G, (G2) instead, moving G2-G2-F2 rather
   than Eb2-F2; bar 16 may be taken up an octave (written E, → E, is already the
   correct note; if unable, rest through the final bar).

4. S1 ACCIDENTAL =A BAR 7 VS INNER VOICES:
   Rule: S1 accidentals (=A, A-natural) are never doubled by any other voice.
   Status: SATISFIED. No inner voice uses A-natural in bar 7; all inner voices
   use diatonic pitches (F4, D4, Eb4, F4 in S2; Ab4, Bb4, G4, Ab4 in A1; etc.)
   The =A in S1 stands alone as an unaccompanied chromatic color tone — exactly
   the intended lever-harp idiom.

5. MELODIC LEAP RESOLUTION — ALTO I BAR 13:
   Rule: Leaps of a 6th or more must resolve stepwise in opposite direction.
   Observation: A1 bar 12 ends on Ab4; A1 bar 13 begins on Bb4 (a 2nd, fine).
   Bar 13: B2 c B = Bb4(half) C5 Bb4. The rise to C5 is a step; the descent from
   C5 to Bb4 resolves by step downward. No 6th leaps occur in A1. Rule satisfied.

6. PARALLEL MOTION S1/S2 BARS 1-4:
   S1 and S2 move almost entirely in parallel 3rds through bars 1-4, as expected
   from the splitting strategy. This produces frequent parallel 3rds but no parallel
   5ths or octaves. Parallel 3rds are idiomatic and intentional in this texture.
   Spot-check bar 2→3: S1 moves E→c (ascending 7th), S2 moves C→A (ascending 6th).
   The voices expand rather than contract at this phrase peak — good contrary
   widening. No rule violation.
