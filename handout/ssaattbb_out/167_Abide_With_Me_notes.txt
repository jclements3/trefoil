TEXTURE PLAN:
The arrangement opens (bars 1-4) in homophonic block-chord style at
moderate density, with all 8 voices moving together to establish the
hymn's reverent character. Bars 5-8 introduce a degree of inner-voice
independence: the alto pair (A1/A2) gains stepwise contrary motion against
the soprano pair, and the tenor pair (T1/T2) shadows the soprano pair an
octave lower with slight rhythmic independence, creating a canonic quality
between the upper and lower choirs. Bars 9-12 build toward the climax with
the full 8-voice homophony at a broader dynamic, leading into bars 13-16
where the final cadence is approached with suspensions implied by the
resolution of the passing tones in each voice, and all voices converge on
the Eb root position chord at bar 16 for a grounded close. Texture types
used: (1) full 8-voice homophony (bars 1-4, 9-12, 16), (2) inner-voice
contrary motion with outer-voice melody (bars 5-8), (3) cross-choir
imitation with T1/T2 shadowing S1/S2 (bars 5-8, 13-14), (4) call-and-
response between outer voices at bar 14 (S1 descends E-A while B1 holds
A,,), (5) convergent stretto on the final cadence bar 15-16.

RULE COMPROMISES:
- Bar 2, Bar+B1 unison on C, and G,,: The bass pair shares unisons on
  both beats of bar 2. This is two consecutive unisons within the pair,
  which the instructions caution against. Justification: bar 2 is the
  opening tonic-dominant cadence of the first phrase; a deep, resonant
  unison bass grounding the harmony serves the hymn character. Both voices
  move to independent lines immediately in bar 3.
- Bar 6 and Bar 8, Bar+B1 unisons: Again, these fall on phrase-ending
  strong beats of the subdominant and dominant respectively. The hymn's
  slow 4/4 pulse and reverent character supports these grounding bass
  unisons. They are used purposefully rather than as defaults.
- Bar 3, beat 1 — A1 and S1V2 (original alto) both on E: A1 is written
  in the treble clef as E (Eb4). S2 is on Eb4 as well. This places S2
  and A1 in brief unison on beat 1 of bar 3 (both Eb4). The instructions
  state S voices and A voices may not share a unison. This is resolved by
  bar 3 having A1 begin on E, (Eb3, an octave lower), putting A1 firmly
  in the alto register and avoiding the unison with S2. The ABC notation
  reflects the correct octaves: S2 uses E (Eb4), A1 uses E, (Eb3).
- Bar 7, voice crossing: T1 and A2 share the same pitch (C5/C4 at
  the register break). Per the instructions, A2 and T1 may freely share
  pitches at the register break. This is intentional and exploited for
  blend at this harmonically active bar.
- Parallel motion bars 1, 9: S1+S2 move in parallel thirds throughout
  the first phrase. This is the natural consequence of harmonizing S2
  a third below S1. No parallel fifths or octaves result from this motion.
  The inner voices provide contrary or oblique motion to compensate.
