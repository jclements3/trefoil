TRANSPOSITION

Original key:  E major (4 sharps: F# C# G# D#)
Chosen key:    E major (4 sharps) — no transposition required
Semitones:     0 (unchanged)

E major is valid: it sits at the maximum permitted sharpward extent (4 sharps).
No closer valid key exists in either direction.

Harp lever positions for E major (from Eb storage tuning, all levers down = Eb):
  Raise ALL seven levers:
    Eb → E   (every E string)
    Bb → B   (every B string)
    Ab → A   (every A string)
    Db → D#  (every D string)
    Gb → G#  (every G string)
    Cb → C#  (every C string)
    Fb → F#  (every F string)
  All 7 levers raised. This is a valid but maximally lever-intensive setting.

Range note: The melody sits in the E4–E5 soprano register — the lower-moderate
soprano range. This is a quiet, devotional hymn; no voice reaches an extreme
high note, which is consistent with the reverent character of the text.

TEXTURE PLAN
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
The arrangement opens with a 4-voice antiphonal texture: Soprano I and
Soprano II carry the melody supported by Baritone and Bass I, while Alto I,
Alto II, Tenor I, and Tenor II rest for the first two bars. Alto I and Alto II
enter at Bar 3 (6 voices), and all 8 voices join at Bar 4, building to the
half-cadence at Bar 6. Section 2 (the extended phrase) sustains the full
8-voice texture throughout, achieving the textural and dynamic climax at Bars
10–12 where the soprano pair reaches B4–E5 while the bass pair anchors B1–E2.
The final cadence (Bars 12–13) uses parallel motion in both soprano pairs
against sustained bass voices, arriving on a full open E major chord.

Textures employed:
  1. Reduced 4-voice (outer pairs only): Bars pickup through 2.
  2. Gradual build to 6 voices: Bar 3 (A1, T1 enter).
  3. Full 8-voice homophony: Bars 4–6 and all of Section 2.
  4. Call and response between soprano and bass pairs across the section join
     (fermata at bar 6 followed by the Section 2 pickup relaunch at G#4).

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
RULE COMPROMISES
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
1. Bass I range (B1): Three instances of B1 (MIDI 35), one semitone below
   the stated professional minimum C2. All three occur at structurally
   emphasized beats (half cadence, V7 approach, climax). The original SATB
   bass descends to B1 (and below, into G#1 and A1 in the original), so
   this is inherent to the source material. No alternative valid key exists
   to raise the register. Noted and documented above.

2. Bar 2, Bass I: The original bass voice had B1–B1 at Bar 2 (I chord open
   fifth). To avoid Bar/B1 voice crossing (the Baritone at G#2 would fall
   below the original B1), the Bass I at Bar 2 was changed to E2–E2 (root
   of I). This is a slight departure from the original bass line but creates
   a fuller root-position I chord and keeps the bass pair in correct order.

3. Texture in Bars 1–2: Instructions prefer 4–6 voice opening. Alto I, Alto II,
   Tenor I, and Tenor II all rest during bars 1–2, giving a 4-voice (S1 S2
   Bar B1) opening. This is at the low end of the recommended 4–6 range, but
   appropriate for a quiet devotional hymn that builds naturally.
