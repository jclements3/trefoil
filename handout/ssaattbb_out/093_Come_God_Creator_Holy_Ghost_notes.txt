TEXTURE PLAN
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
The arrangement uses a graduated build across two phrases.
Phrase 1 (mm. 1–5) is conceived as moderately full 8-voice
homophony, with all voices entering on the downbeat in a calm,
hymn-like block texture; voices move largely in parallel thirds
and sixths within each pair, creating a warm choral blend
without climactic weight. Phrase 2 (mm. 6–11) introduces subtle
antiphonal color: the SSAA choir carries the descending melodic
figures while the TTBB choir provides rhythmically aligned
harmonic support, converging to full 8-voice homophony at the
final fermata chord (mm. 10–11), where S1 reaches D5 and B1
descends to G1–C2 for maximum spread. The final chord (m. 11,
beats 3–4) presents a complete C major chord across four octaves:
S1=G4, S2=E4, A1=C4, A2=G3, T1=E3, T2=C3, Bar=E2, B1=C2.

Texture types used:
1. Full 8-voice homophony (primary texture throughout, mm. 1–5
   and mm. 7–11)
2. Call and response / antiphonal shading (SSAA vs TTBB,
   mm. 6–7, where the upper choir carries melodic descents
   while the lower choir provides slower harmonic rhythm)
3. Paired-voice contrary motion within pairs (A1/A2 and T1/T2
   frequently move in contrary or oblique motion as specified
   in the splitting strategy)

RULE COMPROMISES
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
1. Bass I extreme low notes (mm. 2, 10–11):
   Bar 2 beats 3–4 (A1, G1), bar 10 beat 3 (B1), bar 11
   beats 1–2 (G1) fall below the stated B1 range floor of C2.
   These pitches are taken directly from the original Bach
   setting and are retained for fidelity. Recommendation: if
   the bass cannot sing A1/G1 comfortably, transpose those
   specific beats up an octave only (A2/G2), which remains
   consonant with the harmony above.

2. Bar/B1 unison in m. 9–10 (F2):
   The tied F2 in Bar and B1 produces a brief octave/unison
   overlap at the approach to the final cadential passage.
   This arises from the original bass line's F-A-F motion in
   m. 9 and the need to keep Bar above B1. The voices separate
   stepwise immediately (Bar→E2→D2; B1→C2→B1), so the unison
   functions as a momentary root doubling before resolution
   rather than a voice-leading error.

3. Parallel thirds between T1 and T2 (mm. 9–11):
   The tenor pair moves in consistent parallel thirds through
   the cadential passage. While parallel thirds are permitted,
   sustained parallel motion in a single interval over several
   beats reduces melodic independence. This was accepted as a
   pragmatic solution: the tenor harmony in this short hymn
   does not offer sufficient contrapuntal space to add contrary
   motion without creating voice crossings or diatonic
   violations. The parallelism is mitigated by the contrary
   motion in the alto pair (A1/A2) above.
