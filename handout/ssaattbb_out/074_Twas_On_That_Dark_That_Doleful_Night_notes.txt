HARMONIC SUBSTITUTIONS
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
Bar 10 — original harmony implied A major (secondary dominant V/ii,
  Alto voice had D4–C#4). The C# in the inner voice was replaced
  with a diatonic approach to Am/Gm territory. The beat-2 harmony
  was recast as Gm→F (diatonic motion) rather than V/ii.
  Original chord: [implied A major / V of Dm]
  Replacement: Gm (III→i motion in D minor context)
  Reason: lever harp cannot play C# mid-piece; diatonic Gm provides
  smooth contrary motion in all inner voices.

Bar 12 — original alto sustained C#4 (raised 3rd of A major chord,
  functioning as V of Dm). Replaced entire bar with Dm/F sonority
  using only diatonic pitches (F A C E in various voices).
  Original chord: A major (dominant of Dm)
  Replacement: Dm with added 6th color (diatonic)
  Reason: same as bar 10; the Dm color sustains the hymn's
  meditative quality without requiring a lever change.

Bar 15, beat 3–4 — S1 sings ^E (E#4, raised 4th of Bb/F context,
  functioning as a leading tone into F). All other voices remain
  diatonic. No harmonic substitution was required; the harp's
  diatonic F string produces an intentional half-step clash against
  S1's E#, a characteristic lever-harp color.

TEXTURE PLAN
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
The arrangement opens with full 8-voice homophony at a moderate
dynamic, building inner-voice independence through the second phrase
(bars 5–8). The third phrase (bars 9–12) features antiphonal
coloring: the SSAA choir holds sustained harmonies in bars 9–10
while the TTBB choir carries rhythmic motion, then swap roles in
bars 11–12 for a homorhythmic cadence. The final phrase (bars
13–16) returns to full homophony with increased rhythmic density in
the inner voices (A1, A2, T1, T2), building to the cadential
suspension at bar 15 and resolving to a sustained final chord in
bar 16 with B1 anchored on D3 (tonic root of Dm final) beneath
Bar on F3, creating a grounded close.

Texture types used:
  1. Full 8-voice homophony (bars 1–4, 13–16)
  2. Antiphonal SSAA/TTBB exchange (bars 9–12)
  3. Inner-voice contrary motion with outer-voice pedal (bars 5–8)
  4. Call and response between outer voices S1 and B1 with inner
     harmonic support (bars 6–7, repeated at 8–9)

RULE COMPROMISES
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
Bar 14, B1: Original SATB bass ascends to A3 (A, in ABC bass clef),
  which sits just above the comfortable top of B1 (F3). The note
  was retained verbatim to preserve the original harmonic gesture
  (IV–V–I approach to the cadence). In performance the bass
  section may lighten the tone on this note. Bar (Baritone) is
  placed above at C3–C4 area to avoid voice crossing; this required
  Bar to leap upward from A3 (bar 13 close) to C4 (bar 14 beat 1),
  a minor 3rd — within acceptable leap range, resolving stepwise
  down to A2 in bar 15.

Bar 6, T1: The original tenor slurred pairs (F,G,)(A,B,) were
  retained as quarter-note stepwise ascent F3–G3–A3–Bb3. T2 was
  given parallel thirds below (D3–E3–F3–G3). This creates extended
  parallel thirds between T1 and T2 across the full bar. Parallel
  thirds are not a voice-leading error, but the absence of contrary
  motion here is a deliberate compromise for smooth inner-voice
  texture in a bar that already has active outer voices (S1 and B1
  moving in contrary motion).

Bar 3, A2: Descends to D3 (D, in treble ABC) — the minimum of A2's
  range (D3). Acceptable; approached by step from E3 and left by
  step upward. No concern.
