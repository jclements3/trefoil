══════════════════════════════════════════════════
The arrangement opens with SSAA alone in bars 1–1 (antiphonal
introduction — upper choir presents the first phrase in 4-voice
texture). TTBB joins at bar 2 for full 8-voice homophony through bar 8,
building to the first cadence. Section A' (bars 9–12) revisits the
opening material with T1 shadowing S1 at the octave below in loose
canonic imitation (one-bar delay is implied in the inner-voice motion).
Section B' (bars 13–16) brings the full ensemble to the climax: S1
reaches Eb5/E5 (bar 13) while B1 grounds on Eb2 at the final cadence
(bar 16), maximizing the ensemble spread. The final bar uses a
suspension figure in S2 (G→F→E) and Bar/B1 (Bb→Bb→Eb pedal) for a
prepared authentic cadence.

Texture types used:
  1. Antiphonal — bars 1 (SSAA introduces alone before TTBB enters)
  2. Full 8-voice homophony — bars 2–8, 13–16
  3. Canonic imitation — bars 9–12, T1 imitating S1 melodic contour
     at the octave below with slight rhythmic offset in the inner motion
  4. Suspended cadence — bar 16 final chord, S2 neighbor tone resolution,
     Bar sustained Bb resolving to Eb

RULE COMPROMISES
══════════════════════════════════════════════════
1. Bar 7–8, Baritone+Bass I near-unison in bass pair:
   The original SATB bass stays on Bb2 for most of bars 7–8.
   Baritone was given the same figure a third above but occasionally
   shares a unison at the resolution. Rule 5 (prefer thirds/sixths)
   is relaxed at the cadence point per the instruction that unisons
   at cadences serve emphasis. Both voices move to their final Eb
   positions immediately, satisfying the "move away by step" requirement.

2. Bar 15, inner voice eighth-note fragments:
   The original S2V1 and S2V2 contain 3/4 and // rhythmic values
   (triplet-like dotted eighths). These are preserved in T1/T2 for
   rhythmic consistency with the original setting. This creates brief
   moments where T1 and T2 move in similar motion rather than contrary
   motion. The departure from Rule 4 (prefer contrary motion) is
   justified because the original cadential figure has a specific
   character (a descending pickup into the final bar) that would be
   lost through voice-leading correction.

3. Bar 12 (S1V1 =B natural):
   S1 sings B-natural (chromatic leading tone to C). Per the prompt,
   S1 may sing accidentals freely. No other voice doubles this pitch.
   S2 sings G4, A1 sings E4, A2 sings C4 — all diatonic Eb-major
   tones, so the chromatic S1 color is unambiguous and unsupported
   by the ensemble (intentional lever-harp color).
