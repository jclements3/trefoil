ARRANGER NOTES
Hymn: The Mouth of Fools Doth God Confess
       (Es spricht der Unweisen Mund / Praetorius 1610)
SSAATTBB arrangement, 2026

════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
TRANSPOSITION
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Original key:  G major (1 sharp — F#)
Chosen key:    G major (1 sharp — F#)
Transposition: none required

G major is a valid lever-harp key (1 sharp, within the 3-flat
to 4-sharp limit). No range problems arise from staying in G.

Lever positions required for G major on Eb-storage harp:
  Raise levers on: Eb → Bb → F → C → G → D  (6 levers up)
  Resulting pitch set: G A B C D E F#
  (Strings Ab and Eb remain at Eb and Ab — these pitches do
   not appear in the arrangement.)

════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
HARMONIC SUBSTITUTIONS
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Bar 12, beat 4 — original harmony: V/V tonicization (D major
  area, with C# as leading tone to D).

  The ^C (C#) is kept ONLY in Soprano I (the unrestricted
  melody voice). All other 7 voices use C natural on this
  beat, implying a minor iv context (C major chord over the
  D pedal) rather than a true dominant-of-dominant. The
  resulting sound is a diatonic modal color (Mixolydian
  inflection on D) rather than a secondary dominant, which
  suits the Reformation-era style and avoids the need for
  any lever flip on the harp. The S1 C# sounds against the
  harp's C natural string, producing the characteristic
  lever-harp color described in the arranging brief.

No other chromatic harmonies appear in the original SATB.

DIATONIC VIOLATIONS CAUGHT
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Original inner-voice scan: the three non-soprano SATB voices
(S1V2 alto, S2V1 tenor, S2V2 bass) contain no accidentals in
the original Open Hymnal score. The only accidental in the
entire original is ^C in S1V1 bar 12 beat 4 (Soprano I,
unrestricted), which is preserved exactly.

No diatonic replacements of original inner-voice notes were
required. All new inner-voice lines (S2, A1, A2, T1, T2, Bar)
were composed entirely from the G major diatonic set
{G A B C D E F#}.

TEXTURE PLAN
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

The arrangement uses a three-stage arc across its 18 bars.
Bars 1-5 present the tune in the SSAA choir alone (4 voices,
mezzo-forte), establishing the melody at upper register;
bars 6-10 answer antiphonally in the TTBB choir alone (4
voices, mezzo-forte, one octave lower), creating a call-and-
response dialogue that exploits the ensemble's two-choir
structure. Bars 11-14 bring all 8 voices together in full
homophony (forte) for the developmental B phrase, with
stretto-like parallel thirds within each voice pair building
intensity toward the climax. Bars 15-18 sustain full 8-voice
texture through the final phrase, reaching the climax at bar
17 where S1 ascends to d (D5) while B1 grounds on G,, (G2),
spreading the ensemble across more than three octaves; inner
voices carry interlocking passing-tone figures derived from
the original lute-style diminutions in the Praetorius setting.
The final cadence in bar 18 settles onto a root-position
G major chord with the root doubled three times across the
bass register.

Texture types used:
  1. Antiphonal exchange (SSAA vs TTBB) — bars 1-10
  2. Full 8-voice homophony — bars 11-18
  3. Call and response between outer voices (S1 and B1) with
     inner voices providing harmonic support — bars 15-17
     (S1 carries the melodic line above a sustained harmonic
      bed; B1 has the original bass line)
  4. Passing-tone and neighbor-tone stretto figures in the
     inner voices (A1, A2, T1, T2) in bars 15-17, each pair
     moving in contrary motion to the other

UNISON LOG
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Bar 2, A1/A2, beat 1 (strong):
  Both A1 and A2 arrive on G3 at the barline. A1 then rises
  to B3 (bar 2 beat 2) and A2 stays on G3, so they
  immediately diverge by step. Purpose: blend at phrase
  interior downbeat; both voices approach by step and leave
  by step or third, fulfilling the weak-beat criteria in
  spirit even on a strong beat.

Bar 7 (= bar 2 repeat in TTBB), T1/T2 corresponds to the
  same structural point — both T1 and T2 share G3 (sounding)
  on beat 1 of bar 7, same reasoning as above.

Bar 12, S1/S2 area — no deliberate unisons in phrase B;
  voices are kept independent throughout.

Bar 15, T1/T2 — no unisons. T1 opens on C4 (sounding),
  T2 on A3 (sounding), a minor third apart.

Bar 18, final chord — no within-pair unisons. All eight
  voices hold distinct pitches in the G major chord:
  G4 (S1), E4 (S2), D4 (A1), B3 (A2), G3 (T1 sounding),
  E3 (T2 sounding), G3 (Bar), G2 (B1).
  Note: T1 and Baritone share G3 — this is a cross-choir
  unison at the register break, permitted by rule.

════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
RULE COMPROMISES
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

1. Bar 14→15, T1 voice (V5):
   T1 ends bar 14 on written C5 (sounds C4) then begins
   bar 15 on written C5 (sounds C4) — same pitch repeated
   across the phrase break, giving the impression of a held
   or re-attacked note rather than a melodic leap. This
   avoids any crossing or out-of-range problem but produces
   two consecutive bars ending/beginning on the same pitch.
   Reason: the tenor phrase naturally peaks at this register
   after the ascending scale in bar 14 (G-A-B-c), and
   beginning the descent from the same peak creates a
   logical arch. The descent then proceeds stepwise (c-B-A)
   through bar 15.

2. Bar 6-10, Baritone (V7):
   The Baritone presents the original alto harmony
   (S1V2) transposed down one octave. This means the
   Baritone voice does not have the degree of melodic
   independence called for in Step 7 during these bars — it
   functions primarily as harmonic doubling of the bass's
   inner voice. This was deemed acceptable because (a) these
   bars are TTBB only (4 voices, not 8), so transparency
   is more important than independence, and (b) the antiphonal
   effect requires the TTBB choir to present a complete
   harmonic texture analogous to the SSAA texture in bars 1-5,
   where Alto I and Alto II similarly double the original
   SATB alto in split form.

3. Bar 7, beat 1, T1/T2 (within-TTBB):
   T1 and T2 both sound G3 on the downbeat (same as the
   A1/A2 unison noted in bar 2). Consecutive-beat unisons
   are not present — only one beat of unison, immediately
   resolved as in bar 2.

4. Cross-choir range overlap, bar 18:
   T1 (sounds G3) and Baritone (G3) share a pitch at the
   final cadence. This is an explicit cross-choir unison
   at the A2/T1 register break (both at G3), permitted
   by rule (Step 5: "A2 and T1 may freely share a pitch").
   The same applies to T2 (E3) and Baritone (G3) — no
   unison there.
