Chosen key: G major (1 sharp, F#) — no transposition required.
Semitones shifted: 0 (none).

  - Raise levers on: Ab → Eb → Bb → F → C → G (six lever raises).

TEXTURE PLAN
------------
The arrangement employs a four-phrase arch structure mirroring the hymn
tune's AABA' form.

Phrase 1 (bars 1–4, "Immortal, invisible"): SSAA leads with the melody
against a sustaining TTBB foundation. The tenor and bass voices provide
gentle harmonic support without drawing attention, opening the texture
at roughly 6 effective voices.

Phrase 2 (bars 5–8, "Unresting, unhasting"): Full 8-voice homophony.
All voices enter simultaneously, creating a richer block-chord texture
as the congregation's first full verse settles.

Phrase 3 (bars 9–12, "To all life thou givest"): Antiphonal texture.
SSAA voices carry the rising melody (B–B–d) while TTBB provides a
responding harmonic echo on the repeated melodic arch (bars 11–12).
The soprano climbs to D5 (d) — the melodic peak of the piece.

Phrase 4 (bars 13–16, "Great Father of glory"): Full 8-voice homophony
returns for the final stanza with a stretto-inspired cadential compression
in bars 15–16. All eight voices arrive on the tonic G simultaneously,
with B1 holding G2 as a firm bass pedal. The texture closes with the
full ensemble in a solid G major chord.

Texture types used: (1) Reduced SSAA with TTBB accompaniment (phrase 1),
(2) Full 8-voice homophony (phrases 2 and 4), (3) Antiphonal SSAA/TTBB
exchange (phrase 3), (4) Stretto cadence compression (phrase 4, bars 15–16).

RULE COMPROMISES
----------------
1. S2 drops below A1 (cross-pair crossing), phrase 1 bar 1 beat 2:
   S2 = A3, A1 = E4. Rule 3 permits voice crossing between pairs
   where musically effective. The S2 line C4–A3–E4 creates a
   smooth neighbor-tone descent then a resolving sixth; A1's E4
   simultaneously reinforces the mediant. Both voices resolve by
   step at the next beat (S2→E4, A1→C4). This crossing is brief
   and melodically motivated.

2. B1 range raised one octave relative to original bass (see Range Log):
   The original SATB bass reaches G1–B1, below the professional B1
   floor of C2. The arrangement transposes B1 up an octave throughout.
   This means the lowest note of the ensemble is G2 (concert) rather
   than G1. The Baritone provides a B2 floor that gives the ensemble
   adequate depth. This is not a voice-leading rule compromise but
   a necessary range correction.

3. Parallel motion between Bar and B1 at several points (e.g., phrase 1
   bars 2–3: both move D→D, B→B in tenths). Tenths are not parallel
   octaves or fifths; this is permitted. The voices maintain a consistent
   third/tenth relationship in the bass pair throughout, which is
   idiomatic bass-baritone writing for harp accompaniment.
