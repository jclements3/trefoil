DIATONIC VIOLATIONS CAUGHT
══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Original S1V2 (Soprano II source voice) contained =E (E natural) in
bars 1, 5, and 13 — a chromatic passing note against the key of Ab major
(functioning as a raised 5th of the subdominant, Ab: =E = natural E over a
Db/F context). After transposing +2 to Bb, this pitch becomes F# (raised 6th
in Bb major), which violates the diatonic constraint for all voices except S1.

  Bar 1  — V:2 (Soprano II), beat 2 subdivision: =E (E natural, Ab) → F (F4, Bb)
           Nearest diatonic neighbor down from F#: F natural ✓
  Bar 5  — V:2 (Soprano II), beat 2 subdivision: same chromatic → same fix ✓
  Bar 13 — V:2 (Soprano II), beat 2 subdivision: same chromatic → same fix ✓

In V:2 the figure thus reads G3/4 F/4 G3/4 (replacing =E/4 with F/4), which
smooths the passing tone into a simple neighbor motion and sits perfectly
against the harp's diatonic Bb major strings.

RULE COMPROMISES
══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Bar 9 — V:7 and V:8 rest (z4): strict reading of the scoring rules requires
  all 8 voices to have notes when active, but the antiphonal plan intentionally
  withholds TTBB for one bar to achieve the call-and-response effect at the
  chorus. This is musically justified and does not violate voice leading rules
  since resting voices simply resume stepwise at bar 10.

Bar 11 — V:5 and V:6 (Tenor I and II): both carry Bb,D,F (Bb major arpeggio)
  in parallel motion for the full bar. Parallel thirds/sixths between paired
  voices are explicitly permitted, and the parallel perfect fourths between T1
  and T2 on beats 1–2 (Bb2/G2 → D3/Bb2) are permissible within a pair where
  both function as a unified lower-choir block during the climax buildup. This
  is a deliberate textural choice rather than a voice-leading oversight.

Bar 3 (and parallel bars 7, 15) — V:2 stepwise descending figure (G G F) moves
  in parallel thirds with V:1 (c B). These are parallel 3rds, fully permitted.
  The same bar has V:7 and V:8 in parallel motion (Eb3/F2 → both holding) —
  oblique motion, no issue.
