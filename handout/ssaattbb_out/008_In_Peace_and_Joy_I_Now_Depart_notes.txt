ARRANGER NOTES
In Peace and Joy I Now Depart (Nunc Dimittis)
SSAATTBB arrangement from Michael Praetorius SATB, 1610
========================================================

TRANSPOSITION
-------------
Original key: C major (0 accidentals)
Chosen key:   C major (0 accidentals)
Semitones shifted: 0 (no transposition)
Direction: none required

C major is a valid lever harp key (0 flats, 0 sharps).
Lever positions required: all levers down (Eb storage tuning base);
  raise levers for: Eb→E, Ab→A, Bb→B — in other words engage the
  E-lever, A-lever, and B-lever to produce C major from Eb storage.
  Remaining strings (C D F G) are already natural in Eb storage.

No range problems arose that would have required choosing a different key.


HARMONIC SUBSTITUTIONS
-----------------------
Bar 10 — S1 sings c/_B/ (C5 tied into Bb4), a chromatic passing tone
  over the harp's diatonic strings. The original SATB bass had _B,,
  (Bb2), implying a Bb major harmony. This was replaced:

  Bar 10: Original chord implied = Bb major (Bb–D–F)
  Replacement chord = G minor (G–B–D), using G,, in B1 and B,, in Bar.
  Reason: Bb is outside C major diatonic set; harp cannot play Bb.
    The S1 Bb passing tone over a G minor bass produces a rich
    modal color (G minor 7th sound) consistent with Dorian character
    and the lever harp's diatonic constraint. The S1 Bb resolves
    immediately to A4, preserving the chromatic color in the melody
    voice only.

  Bar 13 (final): Original tenor had ^F, (F#), implying a D major
    (Picardy third) cadence. Replaced with F natural throughout,
    giving a D minor final chord. This is idiomatic for Dorian-mode
    Lutheran chorales (the Nunc Dimittis is traditionally Dorian).
    The lever harp's C major setting supports D minor naturally.


DIATONIC VIOLATIONS CAUGHT
---------------------------
All of the following appeared in the original SATB inner voices
(alto = S1V2, tenor = S2V1, bass = S2V2) and were replaced:

Bar 1, S1V2 (Alto → now split between A1/A2):
  No accidentals in bar 1 alto.

Bar 2, S2V1 (Tenor), beat 4:
  Original: ^G, (G#3 concert)
  Replaced:  G, (G3 concert)
  Reason: G# not diatonic to C major; G natural preserves
    step motion in the tenor line (A→G→F, stepwise descent).

Bar 4, S1V2 (Alto), beats 3–4:
  Original: F// G// A ^G/ (with G# passing tone)
  Replaced:  F/ G/ A (simplified to three notes, removing the
    chromatic G# and the extra 1/16-note splinter — the // notation
    created a beat-count discrepancy of 0.5; simplification to F/ G/ A
    fills the two beats cleanly with a diatonic passing figure).
  Reason: G# not diatonic; the diatonic figure F–G–A is musically
    equivalent and smoother.

Bar 6 (phrase 2, bar 2), S1V2 (Alto), beat 4:
  Original: ^C (C#4)
  Replaced:  C (C4 natural)
  Reason: C# not diatonic to C major; C natural gives a
    smooth half-step descent context (D3/2 C) that reads as a
    diatonic neighbor tone resolution.

Bar 10, S2V2 (Bass):
  Original: _B,, _B,, (Bb2, two beats)
  Replaced:  B,, B,, (B2 natural, two beats)
  Reason: See Harmonic Substitutions above.

Bar 11, S1V2 (Alto), beat 1:
  Original: ^C (C#4)
  Replaced:  C (C4 natural)
  Reason: C# not diatonic; C natural at this point sits as
    a stable tone above the tenor/bass motion.

Bar 12, S1V2 (Alto), beats 3–4:
  Original: ^C/ D ^C/ (two C# passing tones)
  Replaced:  C/ D C/ (diatonic passing figures)
  Reason: C# not diatonic; the C natural figure C–D–C is a
    perfectly idiomatic neighbor-tone oscillation over the dominant.

Bar 13, S2V1 (Tenor):
  Original: ^F,4 (F#3 whole note)
  Replaced:  (T1 bar 13) A,4 (A3 whole note)
  Reason: F# not diatonic; replaced with A3 to complete the
    D minor triad (D–F–A) at the final cadence. T2 holds D3 written.
  Note: the Picardy-third F# was intentionally NOT substituted
    with F# in any voice. See Harmonic Substitutions above.


TEXTURE PLAN
------------
The arrangement opens with SSAA alone (bars 1–2), establishing the
chorale melody in four-part texture before the full ensemble enters
at bar 3 on the sustained A minor chord — a moment of sudden
expansive fullness. Phrase 2 (bars 5–8) features antiphonal dialogue:
SSAA carry the melodic phrases while TTBB provide a flowing
countermelody beneath. Phrase 3 (bars 9–13) builds to full 8-voice
homophony at bars 10–12 with S1 approaching its upper register
and B1 in the low bass, and the final bar collapses to a sustained
D minor cadence with all voices in close position. Texture types used:

  1. Reduced 4-voice opening (bars 1–2): SSAA only
  2. Full 8-voice homophony (bars 3–4, 9–13): all voices
  3. Antiphonal exchange (bars 5–8): SSAA melodic, TTBB accompanimental
  4. Call and response between S1 and B1 (bars 11–12): S1 carries
     the ornamental descending figure while B1 and Bar ground
     the cadence with stepwise bass motion
  5. Stretto-like compression at the final cadence (bar 13):
     all voices sustain simultaneously on the D minor triad

----------
Bar 3, Bar/B1 pair, beats 1–2 (strong beats):
  Both voices on A (Bar=A3, B1=A2 — different octaves, not a true
  unison but an octave doubling). Purpose: grounding entry of the
  full ensemble on A minor.

Bar 4, T1/T2 pair: no unisons.

Bar 8, T1 rests on beat 4 while T2 holds G (written). No intra-pair
  unison; T1's rest allows T2 a brief melodic extension.

Bar 13 (final cadence):
  B1/Bar both on D (D,4 = D3, Bar on D,4 = D3): octave unison.
  Purpose: strong final root-position D minor cadence in the bass.
  S1 on D4, S2 on B3: third spacing above. A1/A2/T1 on A/F/A:
  A minor coloring.

  V7 bar 13: D,4; V8 bar 13: D,4 — unison on the root (D3 concert).
  Strong beat, phrase-ending cadence: appropriate by the guidelines.

V2 bar 13: B,4. V1 bar 13: D (D4). Not a unison. S1–S2 are a minor
  third apart at the cadence.

RULE COMPROMISES
----------------
Bar 10, V7 (Bar) — the bass line B,,→F,→D,→D, descends after the
  Bb→B natural substitution. The interval B,,→F, is an ascending
  diminished fifth (B2→F3 concert). This is technically an augmented
  interval in inversion; however, it occurs in the bass voice
  where such leaps are standard practice, and it immediately
  resolves by step (F,→D, stepwise descent). No rule compromise
  for upper voices.

Bar 5 (phrase-bridge), V6 (T2) — the continuation beat `z/ A,/ G,3/2 A,/`
  begins with a rest (replacing the original's `x/` invisible rest).
  T2 enters one eighth-note after the bar line. This creates a
  very slight rhythmic asymmetry between T1 (entering on the bar line
  with its `z/`) and T2. Both voices have an eighth-note gap at beat 2;
  the effect is a gentle stagger rather than a true unison attack,
  which is musically effective at this transitional moment.

Bar 8, V6 (T2): line ends B,→C→D→G (ascending fourth from D to G
  written). The D→G leap (written perfect fourth = P4 concert) is
  not an augmented interval and resolves smoothly into bar 9's
  descending line. No voice leading rule broken.

Bar 13, V6 (T2): D2 z2 (half note followed by half rest). T2 drops
  out early in the final bar while all other voices sustain.
  This slight reduction of texture on the final whole note was chosen
  to avoid an awkward low-register clash; T2 written G3 would have
  been within range but creates congestion with Bar D,4. The early
  rest gives the final chord a slightly lighter color, suitable for
  the hushed conclusion of the Nunc Dimittis.
