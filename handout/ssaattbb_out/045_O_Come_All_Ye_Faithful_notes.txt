ARRANGER NOTES
O Come, All Ye Faithful — SSAATTBB Arrangement
================================================================

TRANSPOSITION
--------------
Original key:  G major (1 sharp: F#)
Chosen key:    G major (1 sharp: F#) — no transposition required
Semitones shifted: 0

G major is a valid key for the lever harp in Eb storage tuning.
Harp lever positions required: raise Eb→E, Ab→A, Bb→B, Db→D
(i.e., levers raised on E, A, B, D strings — all natural; F# is
the only non-natural string, already raised from Eb storage).

Actually in Eb storage tuning:
  All levers down = Eb Ab Bb Eb Bb Eb Ab
  To play G major (G A B C D E F#):
    Raise Ab→A (A strings), Bb→B (B strings),
    Raise Eb→E (E strings), Db→D (D strings).
  F# requires raising F→F# (the F strings).
  So: levers raised on A, B, D, E, and F# strings.
  This is 5 lever changes from storage — within normal lever
  harp practice for a static key setup.

Nearest invalid key check: G major IS valid; no skip needed.


HARMONIC SUBSTITUTIONS
-----------------------
No wholesale harmonic substitutions were required. The original
harmonization is entirely diatonic to G major except for the
chromatic passing tones in S1 (F natural in bars 5, 6, 7, 12, 18)
which are confined to S1 and never doubled by any other voice.

The original SATB alto (S1V2) had secondary-dominant implications
through raised leading tones (^C = C# = raised 4th in G major,
implying D major tonicization). These were treated as diatonic
violations rather than full harmonic substitutions — see below.


DIATONIC VIOLATIONS CAUGHT
----------------------------
The following accidentals appeared in original inner voices and
were replaced with diatonic neighbors:

Bar 5, original S1V2 (alto): (B,^C) — B3, C#4
  → Replaced C# with C natural in S2 (V:2): C4
  Reason: C# is not diatonic to G major; nearest diatonic
  neighbor below is C natural (D4 would move away from the
  descending line); C natural preserved the stepwise descent
  G→F→E implied by the soprano and maintained smooth inner
  voice motion.

Bar 7, original S1V2 (alto): (D2 ^C3/2) D/ — D, C#, D
  → Replaced C# with C natural in S2 (V:2): C4
  Reason: same as bar 5. C natural maintains a clean 4-3
  suspension figure descending to the half cadence on D.

Bar 29 of source / equivalent to Bar 17 refrain,
original S1V2: D/^C/ — D, C# (anacrusis figure)
  → Replaced C# with C natural in S2 (V:2): C natural
  Reason: same diatonic constraint; the stepwise approach
  to the final D in S2 works equally well with C natural,
  preserving the scalar descent.

Original S2V2 (bass), bar 5: A,, (A1 / A2 in concert pitch)
  → Raised to C, (C3 / C2 concert) in Bass I (V:8)
  Reason: A1 is below the Bass I professional range minimum
  (C2). The nearest diatonic note within range is C2 (C,),
  which functions as the third of the A minor chord implied
  by the harmonic context and provides a smooth stepwise
  approach from E2 (the preceding downbeat note E,).


TEXTURE PLAN
-------------
The arrangement unfolds in four phases of increasing density,
building from an intimate 4-voice opening to a full 8-voice
climax at the refrain.

PHASE 1 (pickup and bars 1–4): SSAA only (4 voices). The
upper choir presents the opening phrase a cappella, emphasizing
the hymn's simplicity. Voice pairs move in parallel thirds and
sixths throughout, with gentle contrary motion between A1 and A2.

PHASE 2 (bars 5–8): TTBB joins on the second phrase for full
8-voice texture. The antiphonal contrast — upper choir alone,
then all 8 voices — is the primary textural gesture. The
chromatic F natural in S1 (a Dorian inflection) plays against
the fully diatonic inner voices and harp accompaniment,
producing the characteristic lever-harp color.

PHASE 3 (bars 9–13): Full 8-voice call-and-response. S1 peaks
at D5 (bar 9, the high point of the piece). Inner voices provide
a rolling harmonic background while the outer voices (S1 and B1)
carry the melodic arc. Canonic imitation between A1/T1 (bars 11
and 13) adds contrapuntal interest.

PHRASE 4 (bars 14–16): Tutti homophony with all 8 voices.
The repeat of the opening melody is now harmonized in full
8-part block chords, building intensity toward the refrain.

REFRAIN (bars 17–20 — "O come let us adore Him"): Maximum
spread. S1 soars to C5 in bar 17 and the final cadential sweep
(bars 18–19) reaches C5 again via the soaring F-G-C motive. B1
anchors on G2 for the final cadence, and all 8 voices conclude
on a G major chord spanning G2 to G4.

Textures used:
  1. SSAA semi-chorus (bars 1–4) — Solo/reduced texture
  2. Antiphonal entry — TTBB joining SSAA (bar 5)
  3. Full 8-voice homophony (bars 14–16 and final refrain)
  4. Call-and-response outer voices (S1 and B1) with inner
     harmonic support (bars 9–13)
  5. Canonic imitation between A1 and T1 (bars 11, 13)


UNISON LOG
-----------
Pickup, all SSAA voices: each voice enters on a different
  chord tone (G, E, D, B) — no unisons.

Bar 16, beat 4, V:5 (T1) and V:6 (T2): both on B, (written),
  sounding B2. Unison at the sintro pickup — strong beat,
  phrase boundary. Purpose: both tenor voices arrive together
  at the refrain launch, creating a unified downbeat effect.
  This unison is immediately followed by divergence in bar 17.

Bar 20, final, V:1 (S1) and V:2 (S2): both on G (G4).
  Unison at the final cadence — strong beat (beat 1), phrase
  ending. Purpose: S1 and S2 blend on the tonic at the
  cadence for a warm, unified soprano line. This is the only
  S1+S2 unison in the arrangement and is placed deliberately
  at the final resolution.

Bar 20, final, V:3 (A1) and V:6 (T2) cross-choir: A1=D4, T2
  written D=D4 sounds D3 — octave unison at natural register
  break. Permitted under the cross-choir unison rule.

Bar 20, final, V:5 (T1) and V:8 (Bass I) cross-choir: T1
  written G=G4 sounds G3; B1=G,=G3 — unison at G3. Cross-choir,
  permitted.


RANGE LOG
----------
Bar 9, V:1 (S1): d (D5) — climax note, highest pitch in the
  arrangement. Falls at the natural climax of phrase 3.
  Comfortable within S1 professional range (C4–A5).

Bar 17, V:1 (S1): c (C5) — high register, beginning of refrain
  climax. Within comfortable range.

Bar 18, V:1 (S1): c (C5) approached by leap from =F (F4 natural)
  — a minor 6th upward leap. This leap must resolve by stepwise
  descent (bar 19: B4–A4–G4), which it does. ✓

Bar 5, V:8 (Bass I): E, (E2 in bass) — near the lower limit
  of Bass I range (C2). Not a concern; normal hymn-bass range.

Bar 8, V:5 (T1): D (sounds D3) — mid-register, not extreme.

Bar 5–7, V:5 (T1): B, (sounds B2) — at the floor of T1 range.
  Used as the lowest possible note for T1. Approaches it for
  only 1–2 beats at a time and recovers by step upward. This
  is acceptable at the phrase interior; the voice does not
  linger at the extreme.

Bar 6, V:6 (T2): G, (sounds G2) — at the floor of T2 range.
  Single beats only; immediately moves upward by step. ✓

Bar 9, V:6 (T2): G, (sounds G2) — same floor note, also
  approached and left by step. ✓


RULE COMPROMISES
-----------------
1. Bar 12, V:5 (T1) and V:4 (A2): both use similar descending
   figures (B,–C–B,–D in T1, D–E–D–B, in A2). On beat 1 these
   are a major 10th apart (B2 vs D4) so no parallel octave
   results; however the motion is similar (both voices reach
   their respective third-scale degrees). Accepted because the
   inner voices need to navigate the descending cadential figure
   and there are limited diatonic options at this range.

2. Bar 10, V:7 (Baritone) and V:8 (Bass I): both use staccato
   half-note pairs (A,2 G,2 vs F,2 G,2). The second halves land
   on G,=G3 (Bar) and G,=G3 (B1) — in unison on the second
   half! This is a cross-pair unison (Bar+B1) at a moderate
   beat point. Accepted: it falls on the weak second half of a
   staccato gesture, both voices move away immediately in bar 11,
   and the unison reinforces the half-cadential arrival on G.

3. Parallel motion between V:3 (A1) and V:5 (T1) occurs at
   the 10th throughout much of the arrangement. Parallel tenths
   are not prohibited (only parallel octaves and fifths are
   banned), and the two voices are in different choirs, so this
   is musically desirable rather than problematic.

4. Bar 13, V:6 (T2): C D C E (sounds C3–D3–C3–E3). This voice
   follows an ascending scale fragment that brings it close to
   the Baritone register. The Baritone (V:7) was raised to
   E3–F#3–E3–G3 to remain above T2, creating a slightly higher
   Baritone tessitura than typical in this bar. No voice
   leading rule is violated; this is a register management note.

5. The original SATB soprano in bars 5, 6, 7, 12, 18 uses F
   natural (a Dorian/modal inflection against G major). Per the
   arranging rules, S1 may sing accidentals freely; all other
   voices remain strictly diatonic. The F natural in S1 against
   the F# implied by the key signature and doubled by inner
   voices and harp creates a brief but intentional harmonic
   ambiguity — this is a characteristic color of lever harp
   writing and is preserved as written.
