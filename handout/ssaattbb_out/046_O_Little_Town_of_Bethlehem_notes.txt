HARMONIC SUBSTITUTIONS
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
Bars 10–12 (phrase 3, "how silently" mysterious passage):
  Original harmony: A major (V/ii, secondary dominant of Gm)
  Reason for substitution: A major contains C# and E# (G#), neither of
    which is diatonic in F major, and the lever harp cannot play them
    without a flip.
  Substitution: Am (vi of C major, i of A minor) = A C E, all diatonic
    in F major.
  Effect: S1 continues to sing E4 freely (E is the 5th of Am and also
    diatonic in F major, so it sits perfectly against the harp's strings).
    The mysterious, hovering quality of the original passage is fully
    preserved — Am is just as ambiguous and ethereal as the original
    A major in context.

Bar 12 (phrase 3 cadence):
  Original: A major tonic arrival (bass A2, soprano A4, alto C#4).
  Substitution: Am cadence (A C E) with diatonic substitution in all
    inner voices. B1 arrives on D2 (Dm root) because — given that the
    Am sub governs bars 10–12 — ending on D3 (Dm) provides a cleaner
    voice-leading resolution than holding on A3, which would imply a
    deceptive cadence to vi from outside F major. The phrase 4 pickup
    on C4 (S1) then functions as a III–I progression back to F, giving
    the arrangement a satisfying modal color.

TEXTURE PLAN
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
The arrangement traces a clear dynamic arc across four phrases.
Phrase 1 ("O little town of Bethlehem") opens in a hushed, chorale
texture with SSAA carrying the melody while TTBB sustain long-held
harmonic tones beneath — a reduced texture of 8 voices, but with
TTBB functioning as a quiet pedal layer. Phrase 2 ("For Christ is born
of Mary") activates TTBB with stepwise countermotion, introducing
antiphonal contrast between the two choirs (call-and-response between
SSAA melodic motion and TTBB supporting harmonic movement). Phrase 3
("How silently") uses SSAA descending parallel sixths against TTBB
sustained chords (Am substitute) — an antiphonal solo/duet texture
with held chords beneath, evoking the hushed mystery of the text.
Phrase 4 ("O holy child") returns to full 8-voice homophony for the
climax: S1 ascends to D5 (bar 14 beat 4), B1 drops to F2, producing
maximum ensemble spread; the final cadence features S1 and S2 in
deliberate unison on F4 while lower voices resolve in stretto motion.

Texture types used:
  1. Full 8-voice homophony (phrase 4, bar 13–16)
  2. Antiphonal exchange — SSAA melody vs TTBB sustained support
     (phrases 1–2)
  3. Call and response — SSAA parallel sixths vs TTBB chords (phrase 3)
  4. Solo/duet with harmonic support — SSAA soprano pair leads phrase 3
     while A1/A2 and TTBB provide sustained harmonic pad

RULE COMPROMISES
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
1. Phrase 4, bar 13, S1 + S2 unison (C4, beats 1–2 effectively):
   S1 melody in phrase 4 opens on C4 (middle C), a lower note than
   any other phrase pickup (which use A4). With S2 at A3 (its bottom
   range), the gap between S1=C4 and S2=A3 is a minor third — the
   narrowest available without a crossing. This is not a unison
   compromise, but it is worth noting that S2 is pushed to its lower
   limit in phrase 4 bars 13–14 to allow A1/A2 room below. If S2
   voices have difficulty sustaining A3 at the required dynamic,
   conductor may raise S2 to C4 in bars 13 and accept a unison with
   S1 on beats 1–2 of bar 13 (S1/S2 unison is permitted occasionally).

2. Phrase 3, bar 12, B1 = D2:
   The Am/Dm reharmonization places B1 on D2 at the phrase 3 cadence
   (substituting for the original A2 of the A major cadence). D2 is
   within the written B1 range (C2–A3) but is very low and will need
   a strong professional bass. If the voice cannot reach D2 comfortably,
   substitute F2 (the F major dominant below, a common bass resolution)
   or hold D3.

3. Parallel 3rds and 6ths throughout:
   In phrase 3, SSAA voices move in parallel descending thirds
   (S1/S2 and A1/A2 in parallel sixths in some bars). Strict academic
   counterpoint discourages parallel 3rds in excess, but this is
   standard practice in choral arranging for expressive effect and
   is intentional here to evoke the "silently, silently" text.

4. Phrase 4, A1/A2 register:
   A1 and A2 are kept in the F3–A3 range throughout phrase 4 to give
   harmonic support beneath a high S1/S2 while leaving room for T1/T2
   above Bar/B1. The A2 bottom note D3 (bar 13 downward) is at the
   minimum of the A2 range and should be sung gently; the voices open
   up naturally as A1/A2 ascend in bars 15–16.
