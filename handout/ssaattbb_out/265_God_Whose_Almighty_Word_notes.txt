TEXTURE PLAN
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

The arrangement opens with SSAA alone (bars 1-3) for a transparent,
luminous sound, then expands to all 8 voices at bar 4. A brief
antiphonal exchange at bars 7-8 (SSAA choir then TTBB choir in
response) provides contrast before full 8-voice counterpoint builds
through bars 9-12. The climactic refrain (bars 13-16) reaches
maximum spread: S1 at E5, T1 at G4 concert (top of range), and B1
rooted on G2, the final bar resolving through suspensions to a
unison-pair cadence.

Textures used:
1. Reduced opening (bars 1-3): SSAA only — 4 voices
2. Full 8-voice homophony (bars 4-6, 13-16)
3. Antiphonal exchange (bar 7: SSAA; bar 8: TTBB)
4. Independent inner counterpoint (bars 9-12): all inner voices
   carry independent stepwise lines with passing and neighbour tones
5. Suspension writing at the final cadence (bar 16):
   A1 E4→D4 (6-5), T1 D4→C4→B3 (descending chain), T2 B3→A2→G2

UNISON LOG
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Bar 4, beat 2 — A1+A2: both on D4. Weak beat; both voices move
  away immediately (A1 stays D, A2 drops to B3 on beat 3).
  Purpose: smooth passing connection.

Bar 10, beat 3 — T1+T2: both on G3 concert (written G in 8vb).
  Weak beat, phrase ending. Both step away in bar 11.
  Purpose: cadential convergence, blend.

Bar 12, beat 3 — T2+Bar: both on B2 (cross-pair, not counted
  against T pair quota). Weak phrase-ending beat.
  Purpose: convergence to tonic approach.

Bar 13, beat 1 — Bar+B1: both on G2. Strong beat (climax entry).
  Purpose: deliberate powerful foundation for the refrain.
  Per rules: "Bar+B1 may share unisons for a grounded bass sound."

Bar 16, beats 1-2 — S1+S2: both on G4. Final cadence.
  Purpose: blend and warmth at the close.

Bar 16, beats 1-2 — Bar+B1: both on G2. Final cadence.
  Purpose: root-position bass unison for finality.

Bar 16, beat 3 — T1+T2: both on B3 (T1 ends on B4 written = B3
  concert; T2 ends on G3 written = G2 concert — actually NOT a
  unison: T1=B3, T2=G2). No unison here.

Bar 16, beat 2-3 — A2+T1 (cross-choir): A2 sustains B3; T1 passes
  through C4→B3. They share B3 on beat 3. Cross-choir unison at
  the register break — explicitly permitted and aids blend.

Bar (Baritone), bar 6 — A2 (A,3 in bass): low for baritone but
  within minimum E2 range. Not a concern.

B1, bar 16 — G2 (G, in bass): G2 is above the minimum C2.
  The instructions prefer C2-E2 for the final bass note. G2 is
  the tonic of G major and is the appropriate final note; descending
  to C2 or D2 would misrepresent the tonal centre. G2 accepted.

A2, bar 13 beat 1 — G3 (G, in treble convention): lower bound of A2
  comfortable range (minimum D3). G3 is above D3; not a concern.

RULE COMPROMISES
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

1. Bar 4, beat 1: T1 (D4 concert) and S1 (G4) sound in octave
   relation across choirs [D4/G4 is a 4th, no issue], but T2 (B3)
   and B1 (D2) are in the same voice class. No parallel issue found.
   No rule compromised.

2. Bar 13, A1 (C4) momentarily rises above S2 (E4) going into
   bar 13 from bar 12. Cross-pair voice crossing is explicitly
   permitted ("Voice crossing between pairs is permitted where
   musically effective"). Used here for the ascending arpeggio
   spread. Bar 13 beat 1: A1=C4 below S2=E4 — no crossing exists.

3. Bar 16, B1 final note: G2 rather than the C2-E2 recommended
   for bass climax finality. G major requires G as the tonal root.
   No lower G is available within B1 range (min C2). Compromise
   accepted; G2 remains the tonic fundamental.

4. Bar 5, T2 bar 5 beat 2: B3 concert creates a cross-bar pattern
   where T1 and T2 are in a tight cluster (D4-B3) over the C3-D7
   harmony. The voice leading is smooth but the inner cluster
   (within 3 semitones) produces a briefly dense sound at bar 5
   beat 2. Accepted for harmonic completeness.

5. Bars 10, 12, Bar (Baritone) and B1: Bar ascends (D3-C3-B2 in
   bar 10, B2-A2-B2 in bar 12) while B1 holds G2, creating a
   momentary spread of only a minor third at the lowest (B2/G2).
   Narrow spacing in the bass is acceptable at phrase endings where
   the harmony is clear. No rule formally broken.
