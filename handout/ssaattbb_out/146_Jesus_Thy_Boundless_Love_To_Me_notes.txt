HARMONIC SUBSTITUTIONS
-----------------------
Bar 8 (S2V1 original = tenor, phrase 2 pickup):
  Original tenor had ^A, (A#, raised 4th degree) implying a V/V
  secondary dominant (A# leading to B in a B major tonicization).
  Replacement: B natural (diatonic), reharmonizing with a plain V
  chord (B major) rather than the secondary dominant.
  Reason: lever harp cannot play A# (not in E major diatonic set);
  inner voice must remain diatonic; the V chord substitution preserves
  the cadential function without the chromatic color.

Bar 22 (second phrase, same tenor voice — same ^A, occurrence):
  Same substitution applied. Original ^A, → B natural (diatonic).
  Harmonic context shifts from V/V passing motion to simple approach
  via the dominant scale degree.

TEXTURE PLAN
------------
The arrangement opens with SSAA alone (bars 1-2, pickup through bar 2),
establishing a flowing, transparent chorale texture in three voices while
TTBB rests. The lower choir enters at bar 3 in a first full-ensemble block,
creating immediate harmonic depth. Phrases 1 and 2 alternate between moments
of SSAA-led melody (with TTBB providing sustained chordal support) and
antiphonal call-and-response passages where TTBB echoes the harmonic phrase
just presented by SSAA (bars 8-14). Phrase 3 (bars 15-20) achieves full
8-voice homophony with all voices moving together, building intensity toward
the climax. Phrase 4 (bars 21-26) brings stretto-like entries and canonic
hints between soprano and tenor pairs, with S1 reaching its peak at E5 in
bar 23 while B1 grounds the texture on low E2 at the final cadence.

Texture types used:
  1. Reduced texture (SSAA only): bars 1-2
  2. Full 8-voice homophony: bars 3-7, 15-20
  3. Antiphonal exchange (SSAA vs TTBB): bars 8-14
  4. Stretto/canonic entries between S1/T1 and S2/T2: bars 21-26
  5. Call and response between outer voices (S1 and B1): bars 22-24

RULE COMPROMISES
----------------
Bar 3, V:2 (Soprano II) — note c (C#5): This places S2 at a third above
  S1's E4 rather than a third below. S1 is on E4, S2 on C#5 momentarily,
  then S2 drops to c (C#4 region) immediately. The brief inversion of
  spacing was accepted to avoid a voice crossing issue arising from S1's
  pickup approach from below. On beat 1 of bar 3, S1 arrives at G4 and
  S2 is on G4 as well (unison noted above); from beat 2 the separation
  is restored. One beat of near-unison with immediate divergence — within
  unison usage guidelines.

Bar 22-23, T1 (V:5) — leap of a fourth (B, to e, written, sounds B3 to
  E4): A rising fourth in the tenor at the climactic stretto entry. The
  leap is a perfect fourth (not a sixth or more) and thus does not trigger
  the mandatory stepwise resolution rule. However it is noted here as an
  angular moment in an otherwise stepwise line. The leap is justified by
  the stretto imitation of the soprano's same rising-fourth figure.

Bar 4, A2 (V:4) — note d, following c, (D# following C#): The motion
  C#4 → D#4 is an ascending half-step in E major (the 6th to 7th degree).
  This is fully diatonic and follows naturally from the chord progression,
  but the semitone step produces a slightly angular sound in the alto line.
  It was retained because the alternative (skipping to E4) would create
  a difficult leap from the preceding note, and the diatonic half-step
  is preferable to an augmented interval.
