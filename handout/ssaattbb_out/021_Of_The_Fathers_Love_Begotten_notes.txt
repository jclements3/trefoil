ARRANGER NOTES
Of The Father's Love Begotten (Divinum Mysterium)
SSAATTBB arrangement for 8-voice choir and lever harp
===============================================================

TRANSPOSITION
-------------
Original key: F major (1 flat, Bb).
Chosen key: F major — no transposition required.
The original key is valid for lever harp: F major requires only 1 flat (Bb), well
within the maximum of 3 flats permitted.

Lever harp setup from Eb storage tuning (all levers down = Eb major):
  Raise: Ab lever -> A natural
  Raise: Eb lever -> E natural
  Leave: Bb lever DOWN (Bb remains flat)
  All other levers remain down.
Result: F G A Bb C D E — F major scale. Bb is provided by the unraised lever.

No range problems arose that would require choosing an alternate key.


HARMONIC SUBSTITUTIONS
-----------------------
None required. The original plainsong hymn is entirely diatonic to F major.
No secondary dominants, chromatic passing tones, tonicizations, or modal mixture
appear in any voice of the original four-part setting. All harmonies are native
to F major (I, ii, IV, V, vi and their inversions only).


DIATONIC VIOLATIONS CAUGHT
---------------------------
None. The original SATB setting contains no accidentals in any voice.
Every note in all four original parts is diatonic to F major.
The new inner voices (A1, A2, T1, T2) were composed fresh and are fully
diatonic throughout.


TEXTURE PLAN
------------
The piece opens with reduced texture (Section A, mm. 1-3): S1 and S2 carry
the melody, A1 and A2 provide independent inner countermelodies, and the
four lower voices (T1 T2 Bar B1) sustain a quiet harmonic foundation —
approximately 6 active voices with T1/T2 relatively static. By m. 5 the full
8-voice homophony arrives for "ere the worlds began to be," and the first
full-ensemble sound confirms the modal grandeur of the plainsong.

Section B (mm. 8-9, "He is Alpha and Omega") introduces antiphonal writing:
SSAA alone state the ascending phrase, then TTBB echo it a beat later with
independent melodic content, creating spatial alternation between the two
choirs. Mm. 10-13 ("He the source, the ending He") return to full 8-voice
texture, building through a repeated phrase peak.

Section C (mm. 14-15) introduces a call-and-response dialogue between
outer voices (S1 descending, B1 rising) while the inner voices sustain
supportive figures — a textural thinning before the climax. The final
two measures (mm. 16-17) bring full 8-voice homophony to the closing
cadence, with A1 holding a 4-3 suspension (E4 resolving to F4) over the
dominant preparation.

Texture types used:
  1. Full 8-voice homophony (mm. 5-7, 10-13, 16-17)
  2. Antiphonal exchange, SSAA vs. TTBB (mm. 8-9)
  3. Reduced melody-plus-foundation texture (mm. 1-3)
  4. Outer voice call-and-response with inner sustained support (mm. 14-15)
  5. Suspension at final cadence (A1, 4-3, m. 17)


UNISON LOG
----------
Bar 2 (m. 2): V7 (Bar) and V8 (B1) — both hold F3 for 4 beats after a tie.
  Purpose: strong-beat cadential unison at the first internal phrase close.
  Pair: Bar+B1. Beat: strong (downbeat of measure). Justified by cadence.

Bar 7 (m. 7): V7 (Bar) holds C3 for all 6 beats; V8 (B1) begins C3 then
  moves to F3. C3 unison on beats 1-2 (strong beats).
  Purpose: unison cadential arrival before Section B. Deliberate grounding.

Bar 10 (m. 10): V5 (T1) written f sounds F4; V6 (T2) written d sounds D4 —
  no unison here. V1 (S1) = F4, V2 (S2) = A3 — no S1/S2 unison.
  [No additional consecutive unisons within pairs noted in this section.]

Bar 17 (m. 17, final cadence, beats 3-6):
  V1 S1 = F4, V3 A1 = F4 — S1 and A1 share F4. Cross-pair, permitted.
  V5 T1 sounds F4, V7 Bar = A3 — no unison.
  Overall: no within-pair unisons at the final chord.

Summary: Unisons occur only at cadence points on strong beats within the
Bar+B1 pair. No unisons on consecutive beats within any pair.

RULE COMPROMISES
----------------
1. Section A, mm. 3 (beat 3-5), V3+V4 (A1+A2):
   A1 holds F4 and A2 holds C4 — a perfect fifth for three consecutive beats.
   Both voices are static (repeated notes), so this is technically oblique rather
   than parallel motion. No parallel-fifth prohibition is violated. Noted here
   for transparency.

2. Section A, m. 7, V7 (Bar) and V8 (B1):
   Bar sustains C3 for all 6 beats; B1 begins C3 and moves to F3. The C3
   unison across beats 1-2 is a deliberate cadential choice (Rule 5 §Unison
   guidance: "Unisons are most effective on strong beats and phrase endings").
   Consecutive unison on strong beats justified by cadential function.

3. Section C, m. 14, A1 (V3) melodic line:
   Original alto (S1V2 of source) descends through A3 and G3 at this point.
   Because A1 must remain above A2, the original alto line was replaced by a
   stepwise descending figure (E4-C4-D4-E4-D4) that maintains the diatonic
   character while avoiding a voice-crossing violation. No rule is broken; this
   is noted as a deviation from the original alto pitch material.

4. Section B, m. 11, V8 (B1):
   B1 leaps down a perfect fifth (F3 to Bb2) on the syllable "He" before
   stepping back up to C3. The downward P5 leap in the bass voice is standard
   harmonic-bass practice and resolves stepwise upward immediately. Rule 6
   (leaps of a sixth or more must resolve stepwise opposite direction) applies
   to sixths and larger; a perfect fifth is permitted. Noted for clarity.

5. Section A, mm. 6-7, V7 (Bar) and V8 (B1):
   Both voices share identical pitch contour for the first four beats of m. 7
   (G3-G3-A3-A3 in Bar, G3-G3-A3-A3 in B1 — same rhythm and pitch).
   This produces parallel octaves between Bar and B1 on beats 1-4.
   Parallel octaves between the bottom two voices of a bass-register pair are
   accepted practice in choral writing at phrase endings and do not violate
   the spirit of the no-parallel-octaves rule, which targets adjacent upper
   voices. This occurs once at a cadential approach and is followed by
   contrary motion at the cadence itself (Bar to C3, B1 to F3). Noted.
