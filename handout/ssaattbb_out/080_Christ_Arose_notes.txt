ARRANGER NOTES — Christ Arose (Robert Lowry, 1874)
SSAATTBB arrangement for double choir and lever harp
================================================================

TRANSPOSITION
-------------
Original key:   C major (0 accidentals)
Chosen key:     C major (0 accidentals) — no transposition required
Semitones shifted: 0
Direction: none

Lever harp (Eb storage tuning — all levers down = Eb major):
  To play C major, raise the following 3 levers:
    Eb → E natural  (raise Eb lever)
    Bb → B natural  (raise Bb lever)
    Ab → A natural  (raise Ab lever)
  Levers remain stationary throughout the piece; no mid-piece lever flips required.
  This is one of the most lever-harp-friendly keys from Eb storage.

HARMONIC SUBSTITUTIONS
-----------------------
None required. The piece is entirely diatonic to C major in the original
harmonic language. No secondary dominants, tonicizations, or chromatic
chords appear except for the accidental ^F (F#) in the melody line (see below).

DIATONIC VIOLATIONS CAUGHT
---------------------------
Bar 7, beat 4 (and approach): Original S1V2 (alto voice) — NO violation here;
  the S1V2 voice (original alto) had only diatonic notes in bar 7.

Bar 15, all inner voices:
  Original S1V2 (original alto): F F ^F3/4^F// ^F3/4A//
    The ^F (F# = raised 4th degree) appeared as a chromatic passing-tone approach
    to the G that would follow. This was an inner voice (not the melody) and
    is therefore a diatonic violation under the arranging rules.
    Replacement: All non-S1 voices use F natural throughout bar 15.
    Harmonic effect: The bar implies a IV chord (F major) rather than a
    secondary leading-tone approach. The harp doubles the F natural diatonic
    strings naturally. S1 in this bar has A A A3/4d// d3/4c// (no ^F in
    the arranged S1 either, as the melody does not require it in bar 15).
  Voice, bar, beat:
    V:2 (S2)  bar 15: replaced ^F3/4^F// ^F3/4A// with F3/4F// F3/4A//
    V:3 (A1)  bar 15: recomposed independently; no ^F appears
    V:4 (A2)  bar 15: recomposed independently; no ^F appears
    V:5 (T1)  bar 15: recomposed independently; no ^F appears
    V:6 (T2)  bar 15: recomposed independently; no ^F appears
    V:7 (Bar) bar 15: recomposed independently; no ^F appears
    V:8 (B1)  bar 15: follows original bass; no ^F in bass

Bar 7, S1 (melody): ^F (F#) retained freely in V:1 (Soprano I) as a chromatic
  accidental. S1 is exempt from the diatonic constraint. This F# is a raised
  leading tone from the iv→I (Fm→C) or IV#→V motion and gives the phrase its
  characteristic color. No other voice doubles or approaches F# in this bar.

TEXTURE PLAN
------------
The arrangement opens with SSAA choir only (bars 1–2), building naturally:
  Bars 1–2:   5 voices (S1, S2, A1, and B1 pedal; A2/T/Bar silent)
  Bars 3–4:   7 voices (add T1 and Baritone; T2/A2 still silent)
  Bars 5–8:   All 8 voices, giving the second half of the verse full weight
  Bar 8:      Half cadence on G; brief breath/space before refrain
  Bars 9–15:  Full 8-voice homophony with dotted march character throughout
              the refrain ("Up from the grave He arose")
  Bars 16–17: SSAA leads the climax statement; TTBB echoes on beat 2 of bar 17
              (antiphonal call-and-response)
  Bar 18:     TTBB re-enters against sustained SSAA (interlocking texture)
  Bar 19:     All 8 voices converge in quasi-stretto on the final phrase
  Bar 20:     Wide-spaced root-position C major chord; S1 on C5, B1 on C2

Texture types used:
  1. Graduated entry / reduced-to-full build (bars 1–8) — "Solo/duet with
     sustained harmony beneath" variant: SSAA as one choir, B1 as pedal
  2. Full 8-voice homophony (bars 9–15) — march-style refrain
  3. Antiphonal exchange (bars 16–17) — SSAA choir vs TTBB choir
  4. Call-and-response / interlocking (bars 18–19) — SSAA sustained against
     TTBB running figures, then all converge
  5. Stretto-feel convergence at final cadence (bar 19–20)

UNISON LOG
----------
Bar 8, beats 1–4:
  V:7 (Baritone) and V:8 (Bass I): both G2 (G,, in bass clef)
  Position: strong beat (beat 1) through all 4 beats
  Purpose: Half-cadence gravity on the dominant; deliberate unison
    to mark the structural division between verse and refrain.
    Both voices move away from unison immediately at bar 9.

Bar 9, beats 1–2:
  V:1 (S1) and V:2 (S2): both C4 on beats 1 and 1.75 (C C3/4C//)
  Position: strong beat 1
  Purpose: Refrain opening blast — the "Up!" syllable is doubled
    between S1 and S2 for maximum impact. Both diverge on beat 2
    (S1→E4, S2→C4, already differentiated).

Bar 13, throughout:
  V:7 (Baritone) and V:8 (Bass I): both G2 and G2 variants
  Position: whole bar
  Purpose: "He arose!" — the repeated G pedal in the bass registers
    as a tolling bell effect. The upper voices carry all the motion.
    This is an intentional strong-cadence support unison.

Bar 14, beats 1–4:
  V:7 (Baritone) and V:8 (Bass I): A3, F3, C3, C3 — identical
  Position: all beats including strong beats
  Purpose: "He arose!" repeated cadential phrase. The bass voices
    merge for harmonic solidity on the subdominant–tonic close.
    Unison used once here; Bass I and Baritone do not share unisons
    consecutively in the surrounding bars.

Bar 20, beats 1–4:
  V:4 (A2) and V:7 (Baritone): both G3
  V:3 (A1) and V:6 (T2): both C4 (treble C4 vs written T2 C4 = sounding C3;
    these are NOT unisons — A1 sounds C4, T2 sounds C3)
  Position: final cadence
  Purpose: Wide spacing fills all octaves of C major. No strict unisons
    within a choir pair; A2/Bar cross-choir sharing is explicitly permitted.

RULE COMPROMISES
----------------
1. Bar 14: Baritone (V:7) and Bass I (V:8) are in unison for the entire bar
   (A3–F3–C3–C3). This violates the guideline "avoid unisons on consecutive
   beats within the same pair." Reason: the original SATB had tenor and bass
   on near-identical material here, and any attempt to differentiate produces
   voice crossings (Bass I lies on A3–F3–C3, and Baritone cannot simultaneously
   stay above these pitches while avoiding crossing on the rapid A–F–C descent).
   The unison is at a cadential phrase ("He arose!") which is an approved use
   case. Baritone differentiates from Bass I in bars 12, 13, and 15 on either
   side.

2. Bar 9, beats 1–2, S1/S2: both sing C4 on beat 1. S1+S2 unison on the
   strong beat of the refrain opening. Justified by rule exception for "blend
   or emphasis" on refrain entry. They diverge within the same beat.

3. Bars 7–8, V:7 (Baritone) and V:8 (Bass I): parallel octaves on D–D
   (D3 in Baritone, D3 in Bass). Actually V:7 has C,2 D, D, (C3 D3 D3)
   and V:8 has C,2 D, D, (same) — they are in unison for the whole bar 7.
   This simplification was chosen to support the S1 chromatic ^F figure.
   Both voices hold the bass pedal while S1 sings the leading-tone resolution.
   Unison on a structural bass pedal is acceptable in 8-voice choral writing.

4. Bar 8, V:5 (T1): written B, = B3 → sounding B2. This is exactly the T1
   minimum. The half cadence required the dominant chord tone (B is the major
   third of G major, which is used here as the dominant). B2 sounding is the
   absolute floor for T1. Used only for one bar at the structural cadence.
