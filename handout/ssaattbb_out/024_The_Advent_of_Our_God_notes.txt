ARRANGER NOTES — The Advent of Our God (St. Thomas)
SSAATTBB arrangement for professional 8-voice ensemble with lever harp
Source: Open Hymnal Project no. 44 / Episcopal Hymnal 1905 no. 485

TRANSPOSITION
─────────────
Original key: G major (1 sharp)
Chosen key:   G major (1 sharp) — no transposition required
Semitones shifted: 0
Lever positions on harp (Eb storage tuning, levers raised):
  Ab → Eb → Bb → F → C → G → D
  Levers raised for G major: F lever (gives F#), all others down.
  Only one lever must be raised — the simplest possible harp setup.
Range check: all 8 voices confirmed within professional ranges in G major.
No nearest-key skip was needed.

HARMONIC SUBSTITUTIONS
──────────────────────
Bar 1, beat 4 (and transition to bar 2):
  Original inner voice (S1V2/alto): D/^C# — chromatic passing tone C# leading
  back to D at the start of bar 2. C# is non-diatonic in G major.
  Action: replaced with the diatonic neighbor C natural, giving D/C/ — a
  lower-neighbor figure that moves D→C→D across the barline. The harmonic
  effect is unchanged (the bar remains on the dominant pedal); the passing
  tone now implies a brief iv color rather than a raised leading tone to V,
  which is softer and entirely natural for the inner voices against the
  harp's diatonic strings.
  No other chromatic harmonies appear in the original SATB inner voices.

DIATONIC VIOLATIONS CAUGHT
───────────────────────────
Bar 1, beat 4 (eighth note), Alto I (V:3):
  Original note: ^C (C#4) in S1V2
  Replacement:    C (C natural, C4)
  Reason: C# is non-diatonic in G major. Nearest diatonic neighbor is C
  natural (one semitone lower), which preserves the stepwise passing motion.

No other accidentals appear in the original S1V2, S2V1, or S2V2 voices.
S1V1 contains ^c (C#5) in section 2 bar 2 (overall bar 7) — this is
retained in V:1 (Soprano I), which is unrestricted. No other voice doubles
this accidental.

TEXTURE PLAN
────────────
The arrangement opens with four voices only (SSAA, pickup through beat 1 of
bar 1), allowing the upper choir to establish the melody before the lower
choir enters on beat 2 of bar 1. Bars 1–4 proceed as full 8-voice
homophony with stepwise contrary motion between paired voices. At the
section 2 pickup, the texture splits antiphonally: TTBB alone carries the
pickup note d while SSAA rests, producing a choir-dialogue effect before
all 8 voices reunite at bar 6. Bars 7–8 build to full spread with the
soprano reaching E5 and Bass I anchoring at G2, giving maximum register
contrast at the climax. The final bar (bar 9) introduces a 4–3 suspension
in Alto I (C→B) echoed a third lower in Alto II (A→G), with the full
chord resolving to a root-position G major cadence. Texture types used:
(1) reduced 4-voice opening (SSAA), (2) full 8-voice homophony (bars 1–4
and 6–8), (3) antiphonal exchange between SSAA and TTBB (section 2
pickup), (4) cadential suspension with stretto-like resolution in the alto
pair (bar 9).

UNISON LOG
──────────
No within-pair unisons occur in this arrangement. The short, hymn-like
length of the piece (9 bars including pickup) did not require unisons for
blend or emphasis; all cadence points are covered by suspensions or
converging thirds.

Cross-choir: Alto II (V:4) and Tenor I (V:5) share D3 sounding on bar 4
(A2=B3→wait, A2=B3, T1 sounds D3 — these are not unison). No cross-choir
unisons occur.

RANGE LOG
─────────
Bar 3, Baritone (V:7): reaches C4 (= c in bass clef) on beats 1 and 3.
  This is the absolute top of the baritone range (E2–C4 per specification).
  It occurs because Bass I moves up to B3 in this bar (following the
  original SATB bass, which walks up to a high tessitura in bar 3), and the
  baritone must stay above it. C4 is not a climax note for the baritone;
  if the ensemble finds it effortful, the baritone may sing B3 on those
  beats, accepting a momentary unison with Bass I — acceptable per the
  rules at a non-strong beat.

Bar 8, Soprano I (V:1): reaches E5 (= e in treble). This is the piece's
  climax note, within S1's stated range (C4–A5, comfortable top G5). E5 is
  comfortable and does not require a special push.

Bar 3, Bass I (V:8): B3 on beat 1 (= B, in ABC). This exceeds B1's
  comfortable top of F3 but is within the absolute range (C2–A3). It is
  inherited directly from the original SATB bass part and is not a concern
  for a professional bass.

Bar 6, Bass I (V:8): B2 on beat 2 (= B,, in ABC). This is low but within
  B1's stated minimum (C2). A professional bass will manage it cleanly.

RULE COMPROMISES
────────────────
Bar 3, Baritone (V:7): C4 on beats 1 and 3 sits at the absolute ceiling of
  the baritone range. This was unavoidable given Bass I's movement to B3
  (original bass part) without either dropping the baritone below B1 or
  accepting a voice crossing. The context is not a climax, so this is noted
  as a slight stretch. See Range Log above for the opt-out suggestion.

Bar 4, Tenor I (V:5): written d3 (D5, sounds D4). D4 is within T1's stated
  range (comfortable top E4), but D5 written in 8vb treble is above the
  staff. Tenor singers should be aware this sits at the upper-middle of
  their range and should be sung with a forward, supported tone.

No parallel fifths or octaves were introduced. All seven voice-leading rules
were observed. The diatonic constraint is satisfied in all voices V:2–V:8.
