ARRANGER NOTES
Hark! The Herald Angels Sing — SSAATTBB arrangement
Words: Charles Wesley, 1739, alt.
Music: 'Mendelssohn', Felix Mendelssohn, 1840. Setting: William H. Cummings, 1857.
Arranged from Open Hymnal Project SATB source.

TRANSPOSITION
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
Original key: F major (1 flat).
Chosen key: F major (1 flat). No transposition required.

F major is valid for lever harp in Eb storage tuning. Required lever raises:
  - Bb lever: raise to B-natural is NOT needed; Bb is diatonic and levers stay DOWN.
  - F lever raised (Ab→Eb→Bb→F): the F lever is raised to restore F natural from the
    Eb-storage flat. In practice, for K:F (one flat = Bb), the harp needs:
      Bb string: lever DOWN (plays Bb)
      All other strings: levers RAISED from Eb storage to natural pitch
    Net lever positions from Eb storage: Ab→A, Eb→E, Bb→DOWN (Bb), F→F, C→C, G→G, D→D.
  That is: six levers raised (all except Bb), one lever down (Bb).

All 8 vocal parts stay well within professional ranges at this pitch level.
No range-based reason to choose a different key.

HARMONIC SUBSTITUTIONS
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
No full harmonic substitutions were required. The original SATB harmony is entirely
diatonic in F major except for the chromatic passing tones listed below under
"Diatonic Violations Caught." All chords in the arrangement remain the same as the
original (F, Bb, C, Gm, Dm) without reharmonization.

The Gm passages in bars 13-14 (where the original bass descends Bb→D→G) were
retained as Gm with diatonic inner voices — no V/vi or secondary dominant substitution
was needed because the original bass itself is diatonic in F major.

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
DIATONIC VIOLATIONS CAUGHT
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
All accidentals below were in the original SATB inner voices (S1V2=alto, S2V2=bass).
S1 (Soprano I) in this arrangement retains the original soprano line unaltered; S1 has
no accidentals in the original, so none were added.

1. Original S1V2 (alto), bar 8: beat 2 — natural B (=B,)
   Context: "C =B, C2" (C–Bnat–C) — a lower neighbor ornament on C.
   In F major, B natural is non-diatonic (Bb is the scale degree).
   Replacement: The V:3 (Alto I) in bar 8 uses G–F–E2 (a diatonic stepwise descent),
   retaining the sense of downward motion into the cadence. The chromatic neighbor is
   absorbed into a passing-tone figure. Effective neighbor: E natural (scale degree 7).

2. Original S1V2 (alto), bar 14: beat 2 — augmented fourth F# (^F)
   Context: "G ^F G2" — a raised-6th approach to G, functioning as applied leading
   tone to G (V/ii region). In F major, F# is non-diatonic.
   Replacement: V:3 (Alto I) bar 14 uses "D D D2" — sustained D, the fifth of Gm,
   providing harmonic clarity over the Gm chord without chromatic color.
   V:4 (Alto II) bar 14 uses "B, A, B,2" — Bb3–A3–Bb3, a lower neighbor motion
   that preserves stepwise interest diatonically.

3. Original S2V2 (bass), bar 6: beats 3-4 — natural B (=B,,)
   Context: "D, C, (=B,,3/2 G,,/)" — chromatic descending bass D–C–Bnat–G.
   In F major, B natural is non-diatonic.
   Replacement: V:8 (Bass I) bar 6 uses "A, F, C,2" — retaining the original
   bass contour (downward motion) with entirely diatonic pitches. The chromatic
   half-step B–Bb is replaced by a diatonic skip, preserving the descending character.

4. Original S2V2 (bass), bar 7: beat 2 — natural B (=B,,)
   Context: "A,, =B,, C, E," — chromatic passing tone between A and C.
   Replacement: V:8 bar 7 uses "A, B, C C" — diatonic Bb2 as the passing tone
   between A2 and C3, preserving the stepwise ascending motion. The Bb is the correct
   diatonic passing tone in F major between A and C.

5. Original S2V2 (bass), bar 18: beats 2 and 4 — F# (^F,) and natural F (=F,)
   Context: "G, ^F, (G, =F,)" — a chromatic neighbor figure around G.
   In F major, F# is non-diatonic (=F, is in fact natural F, which IS diatonic,
   so only the ^F, is a violation).
   Replacement: V:8 bar 18 uses "G, G, G,2" — a sustained G2 pedal point through
   the bar, supporting the Gm sonority. The ornamental chromatic turn is replaced
   by a stable bass note, which strengthens the harmonic foundation.

TEXTURE PLAN
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
The arrangement opens with a reduced 4-voice texture (SSAA only, bars 1-4), the
altos themselves entering staggered (A1+A2 enter at bar 3 with the Bb major chord).
The full 8-voice texture arrives at bar 5 when TTBB joins for the repeat of the
opening phrase — giving the ensemble's first statement twice, the second with full
weight. Bars 9-12 use antiphonal exchange: SSAA carries the "Glory to the newborn
King" refrain on odd bars while TTBB answers on even bars, with both choirs briefly
overlapping only at the cadential beat. Bars 13-20 sustain full homophonic 8-voice
writing with a push toward climax in S1 (reaching D5 at bar 13) and Baritone/Bass
filling in the lower register. The final cadence (bars 19-20) uses stretto imitation
of the "sintro" phrase between the SSAA choir and TTBB choir, converging on the tonic
block chord.

Texture types used:
  1. Reduced opening (4 voices, bars 1-2; 6 voices, bars 3-4)
  2. Full 8-voice homophony (bars 5-8, 13-20)
  3. Antiphonal exchange SSAA vs TTBB (bars 9-12)
  4. Stretto entries at final cadence (bars 19-20)

RULE COMPROMISES
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
1. Bar 1 and bar 5, S1+S2 unison on beat 1 (C4):
   The original SATB soprano and alto both begin on C4. Rather than force an
   artificial split, the natural unison is retained for both phrases. The voices
   diverge immediately (S2 stays on C while S1 moves upward), limiting the unison
   to a single quarter note. This is the standard "opening unison for blend"
   device and is explicitly permitted by the arranging guidelines.

2. Bars 5-8, A2 (V:4) parallels S1 motion in bars 7-8:
   A2 uses "A G G3/2 E/ | E D C2" which mirrors S1's "c G G3/2 E/ | E D C2" at
   the octave below. This creates a doubling-at-the-octave relationship briefly
   between S1 and A2. The doubling is intentional: it thickens the arrival at the
   cadence (bar 8 resolution to F), and the two voices are separated by two octaves
   (S1=C5 sounding, A2=C4 sounding), which minimizes the impression of parallel
   octaves between adjacent voices. No adjacent-voice parallels are introduced by
   this octave doubling since S2, A1, and T1 all move independently.

3. Bars 9-12, SSAA voices are active while TTBB rests on odd bars:
   The arranging guidelines recommend "minimum 3 distinct pitches" in each choir at
   all times. During odd bars (9, 11) the TTBB choir is silent. This is a
   deliberate antiphonal texture choice and is explicitly listed as an acceptable
   texture type ("antiphonal exchange SSAA vs TTBB"). The SSAA choir maintains
   4 distinct pitches throughout its active bars.

4. Bars 13-16, T1 and T2 (V:5, V:6) both use simple repeated-note patterns
   (T1 holds d throughout bar 13, T2 holds B throughout bar 13):
   The Bb major pedal point in the bass (original tenor was also static: C-C-C-C)
   calls for harmonic stability rather than melodic elaboration. The inner voices
   provide rhythmic and harmonic filler here rather than independent melodic lines.
   This is a localized compromise; both T1 and T2 recover with stepwise motion in
   bars 15-16.
