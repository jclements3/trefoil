ARRANGER NOTES
On What Has Now Been Sown
SSAATTBB Arrangement for 8-voice choir and lever harp
===============================================================


TRANSPOSITION
-------------
Original key:  D major (2 sharps: F#, C#)
Chosen key:    D major (2 sharps) — no transposition required
Semitones shifted: 0
Direction: none

D major is a valid lever-harp key (2 sharps ≤ 4-sharp maximum). No
levers need to be raised from Eb storage tuning except those for
the two sharps of D major: the F lever and the C lever.

Harp lever positions for D major:
  Eb string → raise lever → E
  Bb string → raise lever → B
  F  string → raise lever → F# (required for D major)
  C  string → raise lever → C# (required for D major)
  G, D, A strings remain at natural (no lever raise needed)

No key-distance ties; nearest valid key was used directly.


HARMONIC SUBSTITUTIONS
-----------------------
The original SATB contains a secondary-dominant tonicisation of A major
in M. 6 (^G = G# in the soprano, ^G, = G# in the original tenor). The
harp cannot supply G# without a mid-piece lever flip.

M. 6 — Original harmony: A major / secondary dominant (V/V of D)
  Original chord: A major (A–C#–E with G# leading tone coloring)
  Treatment: S1 retains G# freely as the melody line (S1 is exempt from
  the diatonic constraint). All other 7 voices remain diatonic in D major,
  implying a Bm–Em–Bm motion that is harmonically consistent with the
  passage. The G# in S1 clashing against diatonic inner voices and the
  harp produces the characteristic lever-harp color of a raised chromatic
  melody against a diatonic accompaniment.
  Substitute chord for inner voices: Bm / Em diatonic motion.

No other chromatic harmonies were present in the original that required
full reharmonisation.


DIATONIC VIOLATIONS CAUGHT
---------------------------
M. 6, Tenor (S2V1 original), beat 1: Original note ^G, (G#3)
  Replacement: G (G3, sounding) — the natural G a semitone below,
  which fits smoothly within the Bm harmony implied by the other voices.

M. 7, Tenor (S2V1 original), beats 1–2: Original ^G,2 (G#3 half note)
  Replacement: G (G3, sounding, written as A in 8vb treble for Tenor I)
  and E (E3, sounding, for Tenor II) — diatonic neighbors that preserve
  stepwise motion in both tenor voices and support the D major harmony.

No other inner-voice accidentals were present in the original SATB.


TEXTURE PLAN
------------
The arrangement opens with SSAA alone (pickup + MM. 1–2) presenting
the first phrase in a four-voice texture, giving the listener time to
settle before the full ensemble enters. TTBB joins at M. 3 in a
confident unison entry (T1 and T2 in parallel thirds), expanding to
full 8-voice homophony.

Section 2 (MM. 5–10) maintains the full texture but introduces
independence between the choir pairs: in M. 6, the SSAA voices carry
the primary melodic interest while TTBB provides a diatonic underpinning,
creating an implicit antiphonal stratification. M. 8 features sustained
inner voices with a quasi-melodic line in T1/T2.

Section 3 (MM. 11–14) builds the final arch: M. 11 has all 8 voices
in ascending scalar motion (S1 leading the ascent E4–A4); M. 12 is the
full climax with S1 at E5, T1 at G4, T2 at E4, and Bar at its top
C4—maximum spread across all registers. Voices converge in contrary
motion in M. 12 (Bar descends C4→G3 while B1 ascends G2→C3, A2
descends B3→G3 while S1 ascends). The final cadence (M. 14) places
all 8 voices on a full D major chord in closed-to-open spacing.

Texture types used:
  1. Reduced 4-voice opening (SSAA), MM. pickup–2
  2. Full 8-voice homophony (block approach), MM. 3–5
  3. Stratified two-choir texture with independent lines, MM. 6–10
  4. Ascending parallel motion building to climax, M. 11
  5. Full 8-voice contrary-motion climax, M. 12
  6. Simultaneous final cadence (stretto-like arrival), M. 14

----------
M. 3, beat 1: T1 (G3) = Bar (G3) — cross-choir unison, permitted.
  Purpose: strong TTBB entry; Bar doubles T1 at unison for blend.
  (Note: this cross-choir unison was later resolved when Bar was
  revised to B3 at beat 1; the log reflects the final version.)

M. 3, beat 1, V7 Bar = B3; no within-pair unison.

M. 6, beat 2: Bar (C#3) = B1 (C#2) — within Bar-B1 pair, same pitch
  class in different octaves (not a unison). Noted for awareness.

M. 7, V7 Bar = B2, V8 B1 = E2 — no unison.

M. 11, beat 3: Bar (A3) = B1 (C3) — different pitches, no unison.

M. 12: Bar descends C4→B3→A3→G3, B1 ascends G2→A2→B2→C3.
  No within-pair unisons. Contrary motion throughout. ✓

M. 14: T1 (D4 sounds) = A1 (D4) — cross-choir A1-T1 unison.
  "Other cross-choir unisons are permitted." Final cadence arrival
  on D major root in multiple registers is intentional.

No prohibited within-pair unisons on consecutive beats.


RANGE LOG
---------
M. 2, S1: d3 c — D5 (dotted half), C#5. D5 is the lower part of the
  S1 upper range (comfortable). Not a concern; the sustained high note
  over M. 1's ascending figure is well prepared.

M. 6, S1: e (E5) — beat 3, the highest note in the arrangement.
  This is within S1's full range (C4–A5) and serves as the first
  approach to the top for climax preparation.

M. 8, T1 (V5): f3 (written; F#4 sounds) — within T1's comfortable
  range (max G4 sounds). Not a concern.

M. 9, T1 (V5): e2 (written; E4 sounds) — within T1's range. Fine.

M. 10, T1 (V5): f3 (written; F#4 sounds) — sustained 3 beats.
  Within T1 comfortable range ✓.

M. 12 — CLIMAX REGISTER LOG:
  S1: e (E5) — at top of range; reserved for climax ✓
  S2: c (C#5) — approaching top; very effective at climax ✓
  A1: F4, E4 — mid-range at climax; independent contrary motion ✓
  A2: B3→A3→G3→A3 — comfortable mid-range ✓
  T1 (sounds): D4→E4→F#4→G4 — G4 = maximum T1 range; climax ✓
  T2 (sounds): B3→C#4→D4→E4 — E4 = maximum T2 range; climax ✓
  Bar: C4→B3→A3→G3 — C4 = maximum Bar range; climax ✓
  B1: G2→A2→B2→C3 — mid-to-comfortable range for B1; solid ✓

M. 12, A1: F#4 half + E4 half — A1 stays in mid-range during the
  climax while other voices extend. This is intentional; A1 provides
  stable harmonic color (F#=third of D, E=added color) without
  competing with the extreme registers.

M. 13, S1: d2 c2 (D5–C#5) — relaxing from climax. Both within
  comfortable S1 range. ✓

M. 14, B1: D2 — near the lower limit of B1 (min C2). D2 provides
  a strong root in the final cadence. Acceptable for a professional
  bass at a final cadence point.


RULE COMPROMISES
----------------
1. M. 1, A1 (V3): A3→G3→F#3→B3 — the leap F#3→B3 (ascending
   perfect fourth) occurs on the last beat of M. 1. A fourth does not
   trigger the sixth-or-more resolution rule, and the motion from B3
   leads smoothly into D4 at M. 2. No rule violation; noted for
   awareness.

2. M. 2, A1 (V3): D4 sustained for 4 beats; A2 (V4) A3 sustained for
   4 beats. Both inner voices hold while S1 and S2 carry the melodic
   motion. This is appropriate for a hymn texture where inner voices
   sustain while outer voices move. No rule violation.

3. M. 4, A1 (V3): B3 sustained for 4 beats on the dominant chord
   (A major). B3 is the ninth (suspension color) over A major.
   The static inner voice is intentional under a held melodic E4 in S1.

4. M. 10, A1 (V3): `B3 E` — B4 (dotted half) then E4 (pickup beat).
   The B4 is 3 beats above S2's F#4, creating a cross-pair interval
   of a fourth (A1 above S2). Cross-pair crossings are permitted where
   musically effective; here the B4 in A1 gives a rich D major sixth
   chord color before resolving down to E4 for the section 3 pickup.

5. M. 11, Bar (V7): F#3→G3→A3→A3 — Bar repeats A3 on beats 3–4.
   The repeated note avoids a parallel issue at the approach to M. 12
   and supports the sustained tonic preparation before the climax. No
   rule violation.

6. M. 12, Bar (V7): C4→B3→A3→G3 descending while B1 ascends
   G2→A2→B2→C3. This contrary motion creates a brief Bar-B1 space
   expansion at the climax (C4 vs. G2 = compound 11th maximum spread
   within the pair) — an intentional textural device. At beat 4, Bar
   reaches G3 while B1 reaches C3: Bar > B1 ✓, no crossing.

7. M. 12, A2 (V4): B3→A3→G3→A3 descending/recovering while all
   other voices ascend. This intentional contrary motion prevents
   parallel octaves between S1 (ascending to E5) and A2.

8. M. 14, T1 (V5) and A1 (V3): both arrive on D4 (T1 sounds D4,
   A1 written D). This cross-choir A1-T1 unison at the final cadence
   is permitted and creates a strong unison on the root note, giving
   the final chord a grounded quality.
