Original key:   F major (1 flat)
Chosen key:     F major (1 flat) — no transposition needed.
Semitones:      0

The original key of F major is valid for the lever harp in Eb storage
tuning. Three levers must be raised from storage position:

  Ab lever → A natural   (3rd string group)
  Eb lever → E natural   (1st string group)
  Db lever → D natural   (7th string group)

The resulting open-string set is F G A Bb C D E = F major. The Bb
string requires no lever raise because Bb is already the fourth
degree of F major and is present in Eb storage tuning.

No nearest-key skip was needed; the original key was fully valid.

HARMONIC SUBSTITUTIONS
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Measure 4, beat 1-3 (half-phrase cadence):

  Original chord: A major (soprano A4, alto E4, tenor C#4, bass A3)
    — functions as V of D minor (V/vi), a secondary dominant
      tonicizing the submediant.

  Replacement chord: A minor (soprano A4, alto E4, tenor C4, bass A2)
    — vi in F major, fully diatonic.

  Reason: C#4 in the tenor is the only accidental in the piece and
  falls outside the diatonic constraint for all voices except S1. The
  nearest diatonic neighbor is C4 (one semitone down). With C4 in
  place the chord becomes A minor, which fits the half-phrase ending
  perfectly: the soprano has arrived on A4, the alto on E4 (minor 3rd
  above C4), and the bass can settle on A2 for a resonant root
  position. The shift from the expected A major to A minor creates an
  expressive modal shadow appropriate to the Lenten text, and the harp
  can play the chord without any lever adjustment.

No other harmonic substitutions were required. All other chords in
the original SATB are diatonic to F major.

TEXTURE PLAN
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

The arrangement opens with a quiet four-voice SSAA texture on the
phrase 1 pickup, then expands to full eight voices on the downbeat of
measure 1 as TTBB enters with stepwise counterpoint beneath the
established choral foundation. The midpoint of each phrase is set in
full homophony (measures 2-3 and 6-7), with all eight parts moving
together in parallel thirds within each pair. Phrase 2 begins with
S1 alone on its pickup quarter — an antiphonal call — before all eight
voices regroup at measure 5 beat 1 for the climactic ascent to C5 in
the soprano. The final cadence (measure 8) introduces the only
notated suspensions in the piece: S2 resolves E4-F4 (a 7-8 motion
against the soprano's held F4) and the Baritone resolves G3-F3 (a
9-8 motion against the bass pedal F2), giving the plain hymn tune a
moment of harmonic weight at its close.

Texture types used:
  1. Reduced opening — SSAA four-voice homophony (pickup + start of m1)
  2. Full eight-voice homophony — measures 2-3 and 6-7
  3. Antiphonal solo call — S1 alone on phrase 2 pickup
  4. Full 8-voice with suspension resolutions — measure 8 (final cadence)

RULE COMPROMISES
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

1. Parallel thirds throughout (all measures):
   The splitting strategy (S1+S2, A1+A2, T1+T2, Bar+B1 consistently
   in parallel thirds) means many adjacent pairs move in parallel
   thirds with each other across large stretches. This is intentional
   — the instruction specifies "prefer thirds and sixths for intervals
   between paired voices" — but it results in prolonged parallel thirds
   rather than constant contrary motion. Inner voices (A1, A2, T1, T2)
   carry melodic independence chiefly through their own pair-internal
   contrary motion and through cross-choir voice exchange, not through
   contrary motion against the paired soprano or bass voice.

2. Measure 1 bass leap F2 → C2 (falling fifth):
   B1 moves by a descending fifth from F2 to C2 on beats 1-2 of
   measure 1. This exceeds the "stepwise preferred" guideline. It is
   accepted because: (a) falling fifths are idiomatic in bass writing,
   (b) C2 is the root position fifth of F major providing harmonic
   support, and (c) the leap is immediately followed by stepwise
   return (C2 → F2 on beat 3).

3. S1/S2 unison on final beat (measure 8):
   S2 resolves its 7-8 suspension into unison with S1 on the final
   two beats of the piece. This is a cadential unison treated as
   deliberate blend, within the "S1+S2 may share unisons at phrase
   endings" provision. Not considered a compromise, but flagged for
   completeness.

4. Consecutive quarter-note beats in T1 on the same pitch (measure 6,
   beats 2-4: c c c = C4 concert):
   T1 holds C4 across three beats in measure 6 while other voices
   move. This creates an organum-like pedal effect in the inner tenor
   line. It is not a voice-leading violation but departs from the
   "every voice must be a singable independent melodic line" ideal for
   those three beats. The surrounding measures give T1 genuine melodic
   motion, and the held note provides harmonic stability beneath the
   soprano's climactic ascent in measures 5-6.
