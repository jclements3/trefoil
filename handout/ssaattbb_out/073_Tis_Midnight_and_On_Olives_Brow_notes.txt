-----------------------

  Original harmony: V/ii (secondary dominant — A major chord implying
    the raised leading tone C# = V of Dm). The original alto had ^C4
    (C-sharp 4).
  Replacement: Dm → ii minor (D minor function retained without the
    raised third). Inner voices use D4 (A1) and Bb3 (A2), implying
    a Dm7 color with the 7th in the alto.
  Reason: The raised leading tone C# is not available diatonically
    in F major. The nearest diatonic chord substitute for V/ii is

TEXTURE PLAN
------------
The arrangement opens with all 8 voices in restrained homophony
(piano), observing the nighttime meditation of the text. Phrase 1
(bars 1-4) uses close, sustained writing with the TTBB voices
supporting quietly beneath the SSAA melody. Phrase 2 (bars 5-8)
introduces antiphony: SSAA leads bars 5-6 and TTBB provides an
echoing response in bars 6-7, with the full ensemble reuniting at
bar 8 in a warm F major cadence. Phrase 3 (bars 9-12) builds toward
an A minor climax at bar 12, where all 8 voices hold a hollow open-
5th A minor chord — representing the solitary suffering of the text
("Gethsemane"). Phrase 4 (bars 13-16) is a declarative full-ensemble
statement (forte), with call-and-response between outer voices (S1
and B1) in bars 14-15, and a final D minor cadence in which S1 and S2
converge in unison and B1 grounds the harmony.

Texture types used:
  1. Full 8-voice homophony (bars 1-4, 9-12, 13-16)
  2. Antiphonal exchange SSAA vs TTBB (bars 5-7)
  3. Call-and-response between outer voices S1 and B1 (bars 14-15,
     with inner voices in sustained support)
  4. Solo color: Soprano I carries the chromatic ^E at bar 15 against
     a diatonic harmonic background — characteristic lever harp color

RULE COMPROMISES
-----------------
Bar 13, S1+S2 sustain unison for full bar:
  Rule: "Avoid unisons on consecutive beats within the same pair."
  Reason: All four beats of bar 13 are unison for S1/S2 and for
  T1/T2 (T1 sounds F3, T2 sounds D3 — not unison, so only S1/S2
  sustains unison throughout). The declarative forte restatement of
  the D minor motif ("No human eye could see — the martyrdom of thee")
  benefits from this unison doubling. This is a structural downbeat
  after a half-cadence, making it the most effective possible moment
  for sustained unison. Relaxed intentionally.

Bar 12, Bar+B1 unison for full bar:
  Rule: "Avoid unisons on consecutive beats within the same pair."
  Same reasoning applies: A minor cadential arrival, dramatic pause,
  penitential text. The hollow open-5th texture above (A3 in A1, C4
  in S2, E4 in S1) depends on a strong unison root in the bass pair.
  Relaxed intentionally.

Bar 3, A1+T1 parallel motion (both descend by step, beat 1-4):
  A1: F4→E4 and T1 sounds A3→G3. Parallel 3rds (interval stays a
  3rd throughout). No parallel 5ths or octaves. Fully compliant.

Bar 8-9, Bar sustained on A3 across two bars:
  Bar stays on A3 (A,) through bars 8 and 9. This is oblique motion
  relative to the moving B1 (F3 in both bars). Two consecutive bars
  on the same pitch in Bar is acceptable as the upper bass voice
  providing harmonic stability while B1 holds the pedal. No rule
  violation, noted for completeness.
