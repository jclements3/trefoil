HARMONIC SUBSTITUTIONS
══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
Bars 3 and 8 (beat 3): Original harmony is D major (V/V, secondary dominant).
  Original chord: D major (D F# A)
  Replacement: The chord is retained as D major in the S1 melody (^F kept there).
  All other voices use D and A only (omitting F#), implying D minor or an open
  D-A fifth. The soprano F# provides the chromatic color; the remaining 7 voices
  supply only diatonic D and A, so the harp is unaffected.
  Functional result: the moment still functions as V/V tonicization via the S1
  melody, while the diatonic inner voices support an open D-A sonority.

Bar 11 (beats 3-4, original): The original inner voices had C# (S1V2) and F# (S2V1,
  S2V2), creating secondary dominant color (D major and A major implications).
  Replacement: All inner voices use only C natural and F natural (diatonic).
  The resulting harmony on beats 3-4 is a pure D minor chord (D F A), substituting
  for the secondary dominant. The S1 d5 (D5) and the diatonic inner voices combine
  to give a clear D minor climax chord — slightly less chromatic but structurally
  strong and fully harp-compatible.

TEXTURE PLAN
══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
The arrangement opens with SSAA alone (bars 0-1 pickup through bar 1), then brings
in all 8 voices together for bars 1-4 (full homophony) to establish the hymn firmly.
The second stanza (bars 5-9) is treated antiphonally: SSAA choir presents bars 5-6,
TTBB choir answers with bars 7, and all 8 voices reunite at bar 8 for the cadential
push into bar 9. The final section (bars 10-14) sustains full 8-voice texture
throughout, building through the climax at bar 11 (S1 at D5, B1 at C2-F2 pedal
region) and resolving with a broad homophonic cadence in bar 14. Four texture types
are used: (1) reduced soprano-only opening, (2) full 8-voice block homophony,
(3) antiphonal SSAA/TTBB exchange, and (4) sustained pedal-point texture at the
final cadence.

RULE COMPROMISES
══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
1. Bar 3/8, beat 3 secondary dominant (V/V): The D major chord is not fully replaced
   but rather split — S1 retains the F# (melody unaffected), inner voices omit it.
   This is a deliberate compromise that satisfies the diatonic constraint for all
   accompanied voices while preserving the harmonic tension in the melody. The harp
   plays only F natural and is unaffected.

2. Bar 1, A1 (V3): Original alto at this beat would imply B3 on beat 4 (from S1V2
   material), creating an augmented 4th (F3→B3) in the A1 voice line. This was
   corrected by substituting D4 (F3→D4, a major 6th ascending), which is the 5th
   of the G major chord and resolves by step in the following bar (D4→C4). The leap
   of a major 6th strictly requires stepwise opposite resolution, which is met.

3. Bars 6 and 7: In the antiphonal section, each choir rests for two bars each. This
   means individual voices have 4-bar rest spans (bars 5-8 for TTBB and bars 7-8
   for SSAA). This is musically effective antiphony but means the tenors and basses
   do not sing the pickup phrase (bars 5-6). This is an intentional structural choice
   for textural contrast, not a voice leading compromise.

4. V8 (Bass I) range: As noted above, the original bass had notes at G1 and A1 which
   are below the professional bass minimum of C2. These were raised one octave. The
   original hymn setting appears to have been written for organ pedal (or a very low
   orchestral bass) rather than a solo professional voice. The octave substitution
   is necessary for singability and does not affect the harmonic function.
