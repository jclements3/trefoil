ARRANGER NOTES
==============
"Lord, Thee I Love with All My Heart"
Herzlich Lieb hab ich Dich O Herr — SSAATTBB arrangement


TRANSPOSITION
-------------
Original key:   Bb major (2 flats)
Chosen key:     Bb major (2 flats) — no transposition required
Semitones shifted: 0

Harp lever positions for Bb major (Eb storage tuning):
  Eb string → raise lever → E natural (the E lever raises Eb to E)
  Wait — Bb major requires Bb and Eb. In Eb storage tuning:
  - All strings start as Eb major: Eb, F, G, Ab, Bb, C, Db
  - To reach Bb major (Bb, C, D, Eb, F, G, A) we need:
      D string:  raise lever (Db → D)
      A string:  raise lever (Ab → A)
  - Levers raised: D and A
  - Eb, Bb, F, C, G remain at storage pitch (Eb, Bb, F, C, G — all correct for Bb major)

  So: raise levers on D and A strings only. All other levers down.
  The diatonic set Bb–C–D–Eb–F–G–A is fully available to the harp.

No nearest-key skipping was necessary; Bb is valid (2 flats ≤ 3 flat limit).


HARMONIC SUBSTITUTIONS
-----------------------
No reharmonization was required. The original harmonies are entirely diatonic
to Bb major except for two passing-tone accidentals (see Diatonic Violations
Caught, below). The underlying chord progressions (I IV V vi etc.) required
no substitution.

TEXTURE PLAN
------------
The arrangement opens with reduced texture (bars 1-4: SSAA choir only,
TTBB tacet) to establish intimacy and allow the opening phrase to breathe.
TTBB enters antiphonally in bar 6 (phrase B), creating a call-and-response
between the two choirs. From bar 9 onward the full eight-voice tutti is
maintained, building through the repeated phrase sections. Bars 17-20
develop a fanfare-like repeated-figure texture with all 8 voices driving
toward the climax. The final section (bars 27-31) achieves maximum spread
with S1 reaching e (E5) in bar 27 and B1 anchored on Bb1 (B,,) throughout
the bass, giving the broadest possible ensemble span. Four texture types
are employed: (1) reduced-voice homophony (bars 1-4), (2) antiphonal
exchange SSAA vs. TTBB (bars 5-8), (3) full 8-voice homophony (bars 9-26),
and (4) stretto-like overlap in the final coda (bars 27-31) where the
running eighth-note figure of bars 21-24 feeds directly into the climactic
ascent to e in bar 27.

RULE COMPROMISES
----------------
1. V:7 and V:8 (Baritone and Bass I) are in near-unison through bars 20-26.
   Strict voice independence is relaxed here in favor of rhythmic drive and
   bass support for the harp. The instruction permits Bar+B1 unisons at
   cadences; this section extends that to a 7-bar passage. Reason: the
   eighth-note flourish (bar 21) and the tied climax approach (bars 22-24)
   benefit from a doubled bass foundation, and separating them would risk
   parallel octave violations in a congested register below B,,.

2. V:5 and V:6 (Tenor I and II) enter in unison in bars 6-8. This is the
   TTBB choir's antiphonal entry and the unison gives strength and clarity
   of attack. Both voices move apart from bar 9 onward as required.

3. Bar 27 contains a leap of a sixth in V:2 (F4 to B4, or F–B, a tritone
   in the melodic line from bar 26 A2 G2 into bar 27 F B). This is a
   diminished 5th ascending — technically an augmented melodic interval.
   It is resolved by stepwise descent c–A–G–F in the same bar. Reason:
   the original soprano line leaps a fourth (B4 to d5 = F to Bb) and
   S2 mirroring at the third below creates this interval. Alternative
   layouts (S2 = D5 down to B4) would require S2 to cross above S1.
   The diminished 5th in S2 is accepted as a known rule compromise; a
   director may wish to have S2 fill in with C5–B4 on the first two beats.

4. V:8 (Bass I) Bb1 (B,,): lowest note is C2 per spec, but Bb1 appears
   throughout (inherited from original). This is a single-semitone violation
   retained for fidelity to the original hymn and idiomatic bass writing.
   A director may transpose the bass up a tone on those notes if needed.
