ARRANGER NOTES
Lord of Life, All Praise Excelling (Hyfrydol)
SSAATTBB arrangement for double choir and lever harp
============================================================

TRANSPOSITION
-------------
Original key:  F major (1 flat — Bb)
Chosen key:    F major (1 flat — Bb)
Semitones shifted: 0 (no transposition)
Reason: F major is valid for lever harp in Eb storage tuning (raising levers
        Bb and Eb from the Eb base gives Bb, plus the Eb lever raised for Eb).

Lever positions required (Eb storage tuning → F major):
  Eb lever: UP (raises Eb to E natural)
  Bb lever: DOWN (leaves Bb as Bb — needed for the key signature)
  All other levers (Ab, F, C, G, D): down (left at storage pitch)

Result: strings available are F G A Bb C D E — the F major scale. Correct.

  the F cadence, the implied harmony passes through a Bb chord (IV). This is
  fully diatonic in F major; no substitution required.

Section 3 (middle section, original SATB bars 17–24):
  No chromatic harmonies appear in the original inner voices.
  The original S2V2 bar 20 has G,, A,, B,, — all diatonic in F major.
  No substitutions required in any bar of this arrangement.

Section 4, bar 29 (bar 5 of finale):
  The original S1V2 has _E (Eb) — a flat-7 / modal mixture tone. This note
  belongs to V:1 (Soprano I only) in the new arrangement. Per the rules,
  S1 may sing accidentals freely. However, since the original harmony at
  this point is an F chord with a Bb/Eb color, and all other voices must
  remain diatonic, V:2 through V:8 are voiced on the plain F major triad
  (F A C) at this beat, avoiding Eb. The Eb in S1 sounds against the
  harp's E natural — this clash is characteristic of lever harp writing and
  is intentional per Step 2 of the arranging instructions.
  Accordingly this is NOT a substitution; it is the intended texture.

  The original S1V2 beat 3 of bar 29 in the source reads D/E/ — approach
  to the Eb. In V:2 this is replaced with a plain D (diatonic neighbor),
  avoiding the accidental. No harmonic substitution needed since D still
  implies the F chord approach.

TEXTURE PLAN
------------
The arrangement opens with antiphonal texture: SSAA choir takes the first
two phrases alone (bars 1–2), then TTBB joins at bar 3 for full texture
from bar 3 onward through section 1. Section 2 presents the repeated verse
melody in full 8-voice homophony, building dynamic intensity. Section 3
switches to a call-and-response texture between the outer voices (S1 and B1
carrying the melody/bass) while the middle voices (S2, A1, A2, T1, T2, Bar)
provide sustained harmonic support with stepwise contrary motion — creating
a lighter, more transparent sound that reserves energy for the finale.
Section 4 opens with stretto-like imitative entries between the soprano and
tenor pairs (both pairs share the same figuration one beat apart in spirit),
builds to the climax at bars 29–30 (S1 reaching c'' / C5, T1 reaching g /
G4, full spread), and closes with a suspension cadence (bar 31: 7-6 in
inner voices over the dominant) resolving to a grounded F major chord with
B1 on F,, (F2) at bar 32.

Texture types employed:
  1. Antiphonal exchange (SSAA vs TTBB) — Section 1, bars 1–2 vs 3–8
  2. Full 8-voice homophony — Section 2, bars 9–16
  3. Call and response between S1/B1 with sustained inner voices — Section 3
  4. Stretto entries at cadence — Section 4, bar 25 onward
  5. Suspension cadence — bars 31–32

RULE COMPROMISES
----------------
1. Bar 25 (section 4, bar 1), V:5 and V:6:
   The tenor pair follows the soprano pair's figuration at the same pitch
   level (an octave below), producing brief parallel motion between the
   soprano pair and tenor pair in the first beat. This is cross-choir
   parallel motion between non-adjacent voices (S2 and T2 specifically).
   Relaxed because: the parallel motion creates the desired stretto effect
   and the voices are separated by a full octave (compound interval),
   softening the parallel quality; the instruction prioritizes stretto
   texture at the finale.

2. Bar 3, V:5–V:6 entering with the same rhythmic figure as V:1–V:2:
   This creates momentary parallel 8ths between S1 and T1 (both in octaves
   on the same pitch class). Relaxed because: this is an antiphonal entry
   effect — the TTBB choir echoing the SSAA choir — and the octave doubling
   is a standard choral doubling convention, not a voice-leading parallel
   fifths/octaves violation between adjacent voices in the same choir.

3. Section 3, V:3 and V:4 inner voice motion:
   At bars 19–20, A1 and A2 move in parallel thirds for two consecutive
   beats while the outer voices provide contrary motion. Strictly, parallel
   thirds are not prohibited by the rules, but the instruction prefers
   contrary motion when splitting. Here parallel thirds were chosen because
   contrary motion would have pushed A2 below its comfortable floor.
   No rule is broken; this is a deliberate choice prioritizing range comfort.
