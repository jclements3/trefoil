ARRANGER NOTES
207 — O That The Lord Would Guide My Ways
SSAATTBB arrangement for 8-voice ensemble + lever harp
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════


TRANSPOSITION
─────────────
Original key:   Ab major (4 flats)
Chosen key:     G major (1 sharp: F#)
Direction:      Down 1 semitone
Reason:         Ab major exceeds the harp constraint of ≤3 flats or ≤4 sharps.
                The nearest valid key in either direction is G major (1 semitone
                down) or A major (3 sharps, 3 semitones up). G major was chosen
                because it requires the fewest lever adjustments from the Eb
                storage tuning, places all voices comfortably within professional
                ranges, and is the closest transposition.

Harp lever positions required for G major (from Eb storage):
  Eb strings → raise to E natural   (1st lever)
  Bb strings → raise to B natural   (2nd lever)
  F strings  → raise to F#          (3rd lever, the only sharp)
  C strings  → raise to C natural   (already done by raising Eb lever group)
  G strings  → raise to G natural   (already natural from Eb)
  D strings  → raise to D natural   (already natural from Eb)
  A strings  → raise to A natural   (already natural from Eb)
In practice: from Eb storage (all levers down), raise the Eb, Bb, and F lever
banks. All other strings are already diatonic in G major.

All 8 voices remain well within professional comfort ranges after transposition:
  S1 top: A4 (bar 4 and pickup 2: C5, which is comfortable soprano)
  B1 bottom: G2 (bars 1, 3, 7, 8 — well within bass range)
No range concerns required choosing a different key.


HARMONIC SUBSTITUTIONS
───────────────────────
No chromatic inner-voice harmonies appeared in this original setting. The hymn
tune 'Evan' (Lowell Mason, 1850) is entirely diatonic throughout, moving among
I, IV, V, and vi chords in Ab (G after transposition). No secondary dominants
or modal mixture occur in the original SATB. Therefore no harmonic substitutions
were required.

The original S2V1 (original tenor, shown in treble staff) contributed a
harmonically static inner voice (mostly Ab / G pedal). This was recomposed
entirely as a pair of fresh inner voices (T1 and T2) with independent melodic
contours rather than preserving the original note-for-note, since the
instruction specifies recomposing T1 and T2 as genuine independent lines.


DIATONIC VIOLATIONS CAUGHT
────────────────────────────
None. The original SATB setting (S1V1, S1V2, S2V1, S2V2) is entirely diatonic
throughout. After transposition to G major all notes fall on the 7 scale degrees
G A B C D E F#. No inner-voice accidentals required replacement.

S1 (Soprano I) inherits the original soprano line exactly and introduces no
accidentals in this diatonic setting. The characteristic lever-harp color
(S1 accidentals against diatonic harp strings) therefore does not arise here;
the arrangement is uniformly diatonic, which is ideal for a hymn performance
with lever harp accompaniment.


TEXTURE PLAN
─────────────
The arrangement opens with four voices (SSAA only on the pickup) and builds
progressively to the full eight-voice texture on bar 1's downbeat, reserving
the full ensemble spread for the body of each phrase. Bar 4 (half cadence) is
enriched with 4–3 suspensions in A1 (D4→C4) and a 3rd-degree suspension in S2
(F#4→E4), giving the V chord a meditative arrival before the
second phrase. The second phrase pickup introduces all eight voices on the V7
sound (D–F#–A–C), creating a bright re-entry that rises to the phrase's highest
melodic point (S1 reaching A4 in bar 5). Bar 7 represents the climax: S1
ascends to B4 while A1 and A2 rise in stretto-like contrary motion and T1
sustains B3 for maximum harmonic weight. The final bar lands on an open, ringing
G major chord spread across four octaves (G2–G4 in TTBB, B3–G4 in SSAA).

Texture types used:
  1. Full 8-voice homophony — bars 1, 2, 5, 6, 7, 8
  2. Antiphonal coloring — bar 3: SSAA carries the melodic material while TTBB
     provides a textural echo on beats 4–6 with T1 rising to D4
  3. Suspensions at cadence points — bar 4 (4-3 / 8-7 in A1, 3rd-degree
     suspension in S2); T2 holds G3 for all 6 beats; all resolve before phrase 2
  4. Call and response / reduced entry — pickup (SSAA only) answered by full
     ensemble; pickup 2 reintroduces all voices on the dominant seventh
  5. Climax buildup — bars 5–7 show gradual thickening of inner-voice motion
     (A1 and A2 rise while T1 sustains) leading to the final resolution

                    S2=E4), so no intra-pair unison occurs at the final chord.

No S–A intra-pair unisons occur anywhere. No T–B unisons occur outside the
intentional cadential bar-4 Bar+B1 unison logged above.

RULE COMPROMISES
─────────────────
1. Bar 3, T2 (written): T2 rises from D3 to C4 (concert) — a minor 7th upward
   leap. This is the only leap of a 6th or greater in any inner voice. The leap
   occurs in the tenor II voice when B1 rises from G2 to G3 (the inner-octave
   bass on beat 2), pulling the entire TTBB stack upward. The leap is preceded
   by D3 (static on beat 1) and immediately followed by A3 (stepwise down from
   C4 on beat 3), providing the required stepwise resolution in the opposite
   direction. No alternative was found that satisfied all voice-ordering,
   T-B-unison, and parallel-motion constraints simultaneously.

2. Bar 4, Bar + B1 unison throughout: The grounded unison D3 in both Baritone
   and Bass I is intentional at the half cadence and permitted by the guidelines.
   The same D3 unison continues into Pickup 2 for two beats, which is a slight
   extension of the cadential unison. This was retained because separating them
   on the pickup (a two-beat anacrusis) would require one voice to move to a
   non-tonic pitch against the dominant pedal, weakening the effect.

3. Bar 3, T2 beat 2 (C4 concert): This pitch places T2 momentarily above the
   typical T2 comfort zone (G2–E4) only in the sense that C4 is already within
   range but at its upper portion. No actual range violation occurred. The cross-
   staff stacking (T2 at C4 while T1 is at D4 immediately above) is slightly
   close but maintains correct ordering (T1 > T2 by a major second).

4. A2 voice (bar 3, beats 3–6): A2 holds E3 for four consecutive beats. This is
   somewhat static but intentional — A2 acts as a pedal point under the alto
   pair while A1 provides melodic motion. The held note avoids creating new
   parallel fifths that arose with all moving alternatives tested.


VOICE LEADING NOTES
────────────────────
— S1 follows the original soprano (S1V1 of the SATB source) exactly, transposed.
— S2 harmonizes a diatonic third below S1 throughout, occasionally a sixth (at
  phrase endings where the third would create a crossing with A1).
— A1 and A2 move in contrary motion whenever possible: when A1 rises (bar 7),
  A2 is held or moves obliquely; when S1 descends (bars 2, 6), A1 descends
  while A2 rises or holds.
— T1 follows a line that often mirrors A1 an octave lower but diverges at
  cadences and at moments where parallel octaves would otherwise arise.
— T2 provides the inner tenor fill; its most challenging moment is bar 3 beat 2
  where it must rise to C4 (concert) to clear the Baritone voice.
— Bar (Baritone) harmonizes a diatonic third above B1 throughout. The original
  SATB bass (S2V2 of the source) is preserved note-for-note in B1.
— All parallel fifths and octaves between all voice pairs were verified by
  systematic computation. No violations remain in the final arrangement.
— All inner voices (S2 A1 A2 T1 T2 Bar) are strictly diatonic in G major
  throughout. S1 introduces no accidentals (the original melody is diatonic).
