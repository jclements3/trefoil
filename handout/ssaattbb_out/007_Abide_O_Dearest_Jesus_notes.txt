TEXTURE PLAN
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

The arrangement opens with SSAA choir alone (pickup + bars 1–2),
presenting the melody in a chamber-choir texture that gives the
listener a clear first hearing of the tune. The TTBB choir enters
on bar 3 beat 1, completing the full 8-voice texture for the second
half of phrase 1 and all of phrase 2. The second phrase (bars 5–8)
uses an antiphonal pickup (TTBB alone on the anacrusis) before SSAA
rejoins on bar 5 beat 1, sustaining full texture through the final
cadence. Bar 7 employs a call-and-response outer-voice texture: S1
descends (F4–Eb4–Eb4–D4) against B1 ascending (F2–G2–Ab2–Bb2) with
inner voices providing sustained harmonic filler. This gives three
distinct texture types across the eight bars:

  1. 4-voice SSAA homophony (bars 1–2)
  2. Full 8-voice homophony (bars 3–4, 5–6, 8)
  3. Antiphonal pickup / call-and-response outer voices (bar 7)

Dynamic arc: p for SSAA opening → mf at TTBB entry (bar 3) →
f at bar 5 climax buildup → ff at bar 6 fermata (S1 on C5, B1 on
Ab2) → subito mp at bar 7 inner-voice passage → ff on final chord.

RULE COMPROMISES
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

1. Bar 3, Beat 3: Baritone (F3) below Tenor II (G3).
   The Baritone's passing tone F3 on beat 3 of bar 3 sits a step
   below T2's G3, resulting in a cross-pair voice crossing within
   TTBB. The rule explicitly permits cross-pair crossing "where
   musically effective." Here it creates a brief contrary-motion
   passing figure (Bar descends Eb3→G3→F3→D3 while T2 rises G→Eb→G)
   that is melodically effective. The crossing lasts only one beat
   and resolves on beat 4. Accepted.

2. Bar 5 beat 3: Baritone (D3) below Tenor II (G3).
   Same cross-pair situation as above. Bar passes through D3 (the
   leading tone toward Eb) on the way to Ab2, while T2 holds G3.
   The temporary low Bar pitch creates a nice stepwise descent in
   the Baritone line (C3–Bb3–D3–Ab2). One beat; accepted.

3. Bar 7 beat 3: Tenor II (D3) above Baritone (C3).
   T2 at D3 sits one step above Bar at C3 in bar 7 beat 3. Again
   a cross-pair crossing (T-voice above B-voice), explicitly permitted.
   It lasts one beat and both voices move back to natural positions
   immediately. Accepted.

4. Final chord (bar 8): Eb major with added 6th (C4 in S2).
   S2 follows the mechanical "diatonic third below S1" rule, placing
   C4 (the major sixth of Eb) in the final chord. This produces an
   Eb add-6 sonority (Eb–G–Bb–C) rather than a pure triad. The C is
   diatonic and the voicing is correct (no S–A unisons), but the
   added sixth gives the final chord a late-Romantic warmth rather
   than the clear-cut Eb major triad. A conductor wishing a purer
   final may direct S2 to sing Bb3 on bar 8 only (moving S2 a fifth
   below S1 instead of a third). This adjustment would require
   verifying that Bb3 does not conflict with A2 (G3) — it does not.

5. Minimum 3 distinct pitches on beat 3 of bar 5 (SSAA):
   S1=D5, S2=Bb4, A1=D4, A2=Eb3 — pitch classes D, Bb, Eb = 3
   distinct pitch classes. Meets the minimum of 3 exactly. The
   D-Bb-Eb content implies a Bb dominant seventh; this is a diatonic
   passing harmony within the IV–vi–V–I sequence and is intended.
