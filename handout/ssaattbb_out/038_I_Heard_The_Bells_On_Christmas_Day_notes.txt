ARRANGER NOTES
I Heard The Bells On Christmas Day — SSAATTBB Arrangement
Words: Henry Wadsworth Longfellow (1864) / Music: John B. Calkin (1872)

TRANSPOSITION
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
Original key:  Eb major (3 flats)
Chosen key:    Eb major (3 flats) — no transposition required
Semitones shifted: 0

Lever harp (Eb storage tuning): all levers remain DOWN.
Eb major is the native tuning of the lever harp in Eb storage;
no lever changes are needed during performance. The harp can
sustain the full diatonic palette throughout without interruption.

HARMONIC SUBSTITUTIONS
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
No full harmonic substitutions were required. The original
chord progression is fully diatonic in Eb major with the
exception of brief chromatic passing tones in the outer voices
(handled under Diatonic Violations Caught). The harmonic rhythm
is preserved exactly as in the original SATB.

Bars 1–2: The F#4 passing tone in S1 (G–F#–G) implies a brief
  V/III or chromatic neighbor inflection. No reharmonization
  was applied — S1 is permitted accidentals freely, and no
  other voice doubles it.

Bar 2, beat 4: The original soprano A natural (Ab→A) implies a
  brief tonicization of Bb major (V/V flavor). S1 retains the
  A natural; all other voices remain diatonic (Ab scale degree
  or Bb). No reharmonization applied.

DIATONIC VIOLATIONS CAUGHT
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
The following chromatic notes appeared in the original SATB inner
voices or bass and were replaced with diatonic neighbors:

1. Bar 1, beat 4 — Alto (S1V2) original: Db4 (_D)
   Replacement: Db4 is the flattened seventh of Eb, one chromatic
   step below the diatonic D4. Replaced with D4 (natural, the
   diatonic 7th degree of Eb major). Voice leading: Eb4–Eb4–Eb4–D4
   smoothly descends by step to D4 then resolves to C4/Eb4 in bar 2.
   In the SSAATTBB arrangement this voice becomes part of S2 and A1;
   both use D4 (diatonic) at bar 1 beat 4.

2. Bar 1, beat 4 — Bass (S2V2) original: E natural (=E,)
   Replacement: Eb3→Bb2→Eb3→E natural is a chromatic passing motion.
   E natural (=E3) is not in Eb major. Replaced with F3 (the 2nd
   scale degree), which continues the linear ascent Eb3–F3 and
   resolves naturally to F3 at bar 2 beat 1. This avoids the
   cross-relation with the key signature while preserving upward
   bass motion.

3. Bar 2, beat 4 — Bass (S2V2) original: F# (^F,)
   Replacement: F#3 is chromatic (raised 2nd degree). Replaced
   with G3 (the 3rd scale degree), which anticipates the G3 root
   on bar 3 beat 1 and creates a smooth stepwise ascent F3–G3.

4. Bar 4 (soprano line) — original c3/2 B/ B B: the B in K:Eb is
   Bb4 (diatonic). Confirmed diatonic — no replacement needed.

5. Bar 1, beat 4 — original Alto _D = Db4: In the SSAATTBB
   arrangement, S2 uses E (Eb4) at this point (staying on Eb rather
   than descending to the chromatic Db). A1 uses D4 (natural,
   diatonic 7th). The chromatic Db4 is eliminated from all voices.

TEXTURE PLAN
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
The arrangement opens with all 8 voices entering together on the
pickup Eb4, but in a restrained mid-range voicing (S1 and B1 are
not at their extremes), building gradually toward the climax at
bar 3 where S1 reaches E5 (Bb4–E5–D5–C5) and B1 descends to Bb2.
Bars 1–2 employ full 8-voice homophony with stepwise inner voice
motion providing textural warmth. Bar 3 is the dynamic apex of
the first phrase — all 8 voices are active, S1 at the top of the
texture (E5), B1 at the lower register (Bb2). Bar 4–5 continue
full texture but descend in tessitura to a natural phrase ending.
The intro section (bars 6–8, marked sintro/eintro) builds from
a lighter texture in bar 6 toward the final cadence in bar 8,
where A1 and S2 provide a 4-3 suspension (F4–Eb4 / Bb4–Bb3) over
the Bb–Eb bass motion, and Bar holds a pedal G3 before resolving
to Eb3.

Texture types used:
  (1) Full 8-voice homophony — bars 1–4, bars 7–8
  (2) Call-and-response / contrary motion — A1 descends (Eb4–F4–D4–C4)
      while S1 ascends (Bb4–E5–D5–C5) in bar 3, creating an antiphonal
      arch across the choir
  (3) Suspension at cadence — bar 8: A1 holds F4 over the Eb cadence
      (4-3 suspension resolving to Eb4); T1 holds Ab3 resolving to G3
  (4) Independent inner-voice countermelody — A1 (bars 6–8) traces
      an ascending stepwise line Bb3–C4–D4–Eb4–F4–Eb4 against the
      descending soprano, providing genuine melodic independence
  (5) Contrary motion between paired voices — A1 ascending vs. A2
      descending in bars 6–8; T1 ascending vs. T2 stationary in bar 2

RULE COMPROMISES
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
1. Bar 1, pickup: S1 = Eb4, B1 = Eb3 — octave between outer voices
   on the single pickup beat. This is a unison/octave on a single
   anacrusis note rather than a parallel motion, so it does not
   constitute parallel octaves in the voice-leading sense. All 8
   voices enter on the pickup together; no voice leading rule
   applies to a single sustained chord.

2. Bar 3, beat 2: S1 = E5 (E natural, chromatic). S2 = C5,
   A1 = F4, A2 = G3. The C5 in S2 is a minor third below E5
   (not a major third). This is intentional: a diatonic third in
   Eb major below E natural would be C# (not available diatonically),
   so S2 moves to C5 (the nearest diatonic pitch below E5). The
   resulting minor third S1–S2 is accepted as correct treatment of
   an S1 accidental — S2 does not double the chromatic inflection.

3. Bar 7, T1 / Bar parallel motion: T1 moves G3–F3 (concert) and
   Bar moves G3–F3 in bar 7 beats 3–4, creating parallel thirds
   (T1 a third above Bar throughout this bar). Strictly parallel
   thirds between non-adjacent voices (T1 and Bar are separated by
   T2) are permitted and desirable for blend. No parallel fifths
   or octaves result.

4. Texture opening: The instructions prefer opening with 4–6 voices
   and building to full 8. In this hymn the pickup is a single chord
   and the first full bar immediately sets the harmonic character.
   All 8 voices enter on the pickup for ensemble unity; however the
   inner voices (A1, A2, T1, T2) are kept in a restrained mid-range
   register through bars 1–2, effectively creating a lighter feel
   before the full-range expansion at bar 3. This is the closest
   practicable approximation of the "open with reduced texture"
   guideline given the monophonic pickup structure of the hymn.
