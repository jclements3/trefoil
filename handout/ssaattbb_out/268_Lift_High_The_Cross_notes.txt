HARMONIC SUBSTITUTIONS
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
The original SATB harmony is entirely diatonic in C major except for
secondary dominants in the verse. No full chord substitutions were
necessary — the chromatic notes were handled by diatonic neighbor
replacement (see Diatonic Violations below). The underlying harmonic
rhythm and chord roots were preserved throughout.

Verse bar 4 (S1 beat 2 = B4, with original alto ^D → D natural):
  The original chord implies V/vi (E major / A minor tonicization).
  With ^D replaced by D natural in all inner voices, the chord becomes
  a plain e minor triad (E G B) rather than E major. This is the
  nearest diatonic substitute (v minor for V major), exactly as
  specified in Step 2.

Verse bar 2 (original tenor G ^F = A major / D tonicization V/V):
  With ^F replaced by F natural, the harmony becomes a plain D minor
  or G7 passing context, which preserves the stepwise descent without
  a secondary dominant. (ii replaces V/V as specified.)

TEXTURE PLAN
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
The arrangement opens with a 5-voice texture (SSAA + B1) for refrain
bars R1-R2, establishing the melody and harmonic foundation without
the full weight of the lower choir. The TTBB voices (T1, T2, Bar)
enter together at refrain bar R3 for the first full 8-voice passage,
creating a surge of sound under the climactic phrase "E-F-G-A" in the
tenor. The intro passage (I5-I8) is set as antiphonal exchange: the
SSAA voices carry the descending soprano arc while the Baritone voice
mirrors the ascending bass line in counterpoint. The verse returns
to a 4-voice SSAA texture for bars V1-V2 (call-and-response character,
lighter colour for the verse's more intimate A-major tonicization
passages), then the full 8-voice choir enters at V3 and drives forward
to the climax. Bar V6 places S1 on D5 (d) — the highest note in the
arrangement — while B1 anchors on B2, giving maximum ensemble spread.
The final bar (V8, "To Refrain") closes with a half-cadence on G,
all voices in slow block-chord homophony, ready to loop back to the
refrain with full energy.

Texture types used:
  1. Full 8-voice homophony — refrain bars R3-R4, intro I5-I8, verse V3-V8
  2. Reduced SSAA + bass (5-voice open texture) — refrain R1-R2
  3. Antiphonal SSAA vs TTBB — implicit in verse V1-V2 (SSAA alone)
     vs V3 onward (full choir); also in the intro where Bar mirrors bass
  4. Canonic/imitative — Baritone intro follows the same ascending
     arpeggio shape as Tenor I (E-F-G-A) at the octave below in I5
  5. Contrary motion inner voices — A1/A2 and T1/T2 pairs consistently
     move in contrary motion to each other throughout

RULE COMPROMISES
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
1. Baritone intro bars I6 (beats 2-3): C4 — two consecutive beats
   at the absolute ceiling of baritone range (E2-C4). The comfortable
   top is A3; reaching C4 twice in succession is a strain. This was
   unavoidable because B1 reaches B3 on beat 3 of I6, and Bar must
   remain above B1. No alternative diatonic pitch exists above B3
   within the baritone ceiling. Bar: inform baritone section of this
   high approach and rehearse with support. The effect is dramatic
   and appropriate for the climax of the intro phrase.

2. T2 refrain bars R1-R2: TTBB voices rest for the first two refrain
   bars (open texture decision). This means the lower choir joins
   two bars into the refrain rather than from the very first beat.
   The instruction to "open with reduced texture (4-6 voices)" was
   applied here — 5 voices (SSAA + B1) for R1-R2, full 8 from R3.
   This is a deliberate textural choice, not a rule compromise per se,
   but it means T2 and Bar have a slightly shorter part in the refrain.

3. Verse bar V7, S1: G ^F (G4 F#4) — S1 uses F# as a chromatic
   passing tone in the final bar before the "To Refrain" cadence.
   This is permitted for S1 (unrestricted accidentals) and is retained
   from the original SATB. All other voices on that beat are diatonic
   (S2=E4, A1=D4, A2=A3, T1=A3, T2=F3, Bar=F3, B1=D3), so the
   F# colors S1 alone against a diatonic texture — the characteristic
   lever harp sonority described in Step 2.

4. Baritone verse bars V5-V6: Bar=C,2(F,E,) and D,2 F,2. The Baritone
   here shadows the B1 line rather than providing a fully independent
   counter-melody (A,,→C, / D,→F, is a parallel 3rd line above B1).
   Parallel 3rds were used here because the bass line itself is quite
   melodic and the baritone's most effective role at this interior
   verse moment is harmonic support rather than melodic independence.
   The fuller melodic independence in Bar is reserved for the intro
   passage where it carries its own ascending arpeggio line.
