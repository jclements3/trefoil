ARRANGER NOTES
032 Come, Your Heart and Voices Raising (Quem Pastores)
SSAATTBB arrangement for double choir and lever harp

================================================================
TRANSPOSITION
================================================================
Original key:  F major (1 flat)
Chosen key:    F major (1 flat) — no transposition required
Semitones:     0
Direction:     none

F major is valid for lever harp in Eb storage tuning. Required
lever positions (raised from storage):
  Bb lever UP  → produces Bb (already storage pitch; lever raised
                  one position from the Eb base)
  Eb lever remains DOWN (Eb not used in F major)
  Ab lever remains DOWN
  All others: F C G D levers raised to produce F G A C D

Effective harp diatonic set: F G A Bb C D E  (one-flat key, fully
realizable with standard lever-harp setup from Eb storage).

================================================================
HARMONIC SUBSTITUTIONS
================================================================
Bar 7, beat 3:
  Original:     G major (G B D) implied by B-natural in alto and
                G in tenor/bass — a V/ii tonicization of G minor
  Substitution: G minor (G Bb D) — replace B-natural with Bb
  Reason:       B-natural is not diatonic in F major; the lever harp
                cannot sound B-natural without a mid-piece lever flip.
                G minor is the nearest diatonic substitute (i → III
                replaces V/ii → ii by keeping the root G and lowering
                the third to Bb). Voice leading is preserved: the
                alto's Bb resolves down by step to A in bar 8, and
                the tenor continues stepwise motion.

Bar 12, beat 1–2:
  Original:     C# in bass (^C,) implying a secondary dominant
                (V/ii = A major moving to D minor)
  Substitution: C natural in bass — plain A minor (A C E) followed
                by D minor (D F A) on beat 3
  Reason:       C# is a chromatic note not available on the diatonic
                harp. A minor is the nearest diatonic chord substitute
                (vi replaces the secondary dominant). The descending
                bass line D-C-D in bars 12–13 retains smooth motion.

================================================================
DIATONIC VIOLATIONS CAUGHT
================================================================
Bar 7, V:2 (Soprano II), beat 3:
  Original note: B-natural (=B) in the original S1V2 (alto) voice
  Replacement:   Bb (B in key of F — the diatonic Bb)
  Nearest neighbor choice: Bb3 rather than C4 because Bb3 continues
  the descending motion D4→Bb3 in S2 and avoids a large skip.

Bar 12, V:8 (Bass I), beats 1–2:
  Original note: C# (^C,)
  Replacement:   C natural (C,) — the diatonic semitone below D
  Nearest neighbor: C natural is one semitone below C#, immediately
  diatonic, and maintains the bass motion C→D in that bar.

================================================================
TEXTURE PLAN
================================================================
The arrangement opens with full 8-voice homophony (bars 1–5) at a
moderate dynamic and middle register, with S1 in the comfortable
range (F4–C5) and B1 close to the original bass line. Bars 6–10
introduce antiphonal exchange: SSAA sings bars 6–7 while TTBB
provides sustained support, then TTBB comes forward in bars 8–9
with SSAA holding back, reuniting in full texture at bar 10 for the
phrase climax (S1 reaches D5). Bars 11–16 return to full 8-voice
texture with increasing intensity: bar 13 is the dynamic peak (S1
and S2 on Bb4 and G4 forte, B1 on the open low G2), and the final
cadence (bars 15–16) uses a 4–3 suspension figure in S2/A1 resolving
to the tonic with a spacious F major chord spread across nearly four
octaves (F2 to F4, with added D for color).

Textures used:
1. Full 8-voice homophony (bars 1–5, 11–16)
2. Antiphonal exchange SSAA vs. TTBB (bars 6–9)
3. Call and response between outer voices — S1 and B1 carry the
   hymn melody/bass in phrase 3 while inner voices provide
   counterpoint (bars 11–14)
4. Suspension and contrary motion at cadence (bars 15–16, S2 D→Bb→C
   against S1's F→D→E descent, with 4–3 motion in A1/A2)

UNISON LOG
================================================================
Bar 15, beat 3 — Baritone (V:7) and Bass I (V:8), strong beat:
  Both voices land on C3. This is a deliberate grounding at the
  dominant-to-tonic cadence point. Both voices move away immediately
  (Bar goes A2, B1 stays on C3 at bar 16 tonic).

Bar 16 — Alto I (V:3) and Tenor I (V:5 concert), sustained:
  Both voices sustain A3 for the full final bar. This cross-choir
  unison (A2/T1 register break) is explicitly permitted and aids
  blend in the final chord.

Bar 16 — Tenor II (V:6 concert) and Baritone (V:7):
  Both voices on A2 for the final bar. Cross-choir unison at the
  register break between TTBB voices; permitted and provides a warm
  doubled fifth below the soprano.

Bar 13, V:3 (Alto I) beat 3: Bb3; V:4 (Alto II) beat 3: G3 —
  no unison, interval is a third. No intra-pair unisons in this bar.

================================================================
RANGE LOG
================================================================
Bar 10, V:1 (Soprano I), beat 3: D5
  Approaches upper-mid range for soprano. Falls at the antiphonal
  reunion point (musical climax of phrase 2). Not a concern —
  comfortable for a professional soprano.

Bar 13, V:1 (Soprano I), all beats: Bb4
  Sustained high Bb, the loudest moment of the arrangement. This
  is the dynamic apex; no concern for professional voice.

Bar 13, V:8 (Bass I), all beats: G2
  Low G2, which sits two ledger lines below the bass staff. This
  is within the professional B1 range (C2–A3) and falls at the
  structural climax. Intended for maximum ensemble spread.

Bar 10, V:8 (Bass I), beat 1–2: A2; beat 3: Bb2
  Both notes are low but within professional B1 range. Bb2 is the
  lowest note of the arrangement (aside from G2 at bar 13). Falls
  at the end of the antiphonal section; effective for weight.

Bar 15–16, V:7 (Baritone): Bb2 (bar 15, beat 1–2), A2 (bar 16)
  Approaches lower range of Bar (minimum E2). Bb2 and A2 are well
  within range. Falls at the cadence — deliberate low register
  for grounding the final resolution.

Bar 7, V:5 (Tenor I), beat 3: C3 (concert)
  Lower end of T1 comfortable range. The original tenor had Bb2
  here (below T1 minimum B2 natural), so C3 was substituted as the
  nearest diatonic note within range.

================================================================
RULE COMPROMISES
================================================================
Bar 7, V:5 (Tenor I), beat 3 — range minimum:
  The original tenor (S2V1) has B3 (Bb3) on beat 3 of bar 7, which
  in context implies Bb2 concert when placed in the T1 8vb register.
  Bb2 is one semitone below the T1 concert minimum (B2 natural, MIDI
  47 vs. Bb2 MIDI 46). The nearest diatonic note within range is C3,
  which was used. The voice leading consequence is a minor 2nd leap
  (D3→C3) rather than a descending minor 3rd (D3→Bb2); this is
  entirely acceptable stepwise motion and does not affect the harmony
  (F major context).

Bar 6, V:7 (Baritone) — phrase beginning:
  The natural choice of a third above B1 (G3) would be Bb3, but the
  antiphonal texture required a lower register to contrast with the
  SSAA choir. Bb3 was used (written B, in bass clef) to keep the
  Bar/B1 pair in the lower-mid range for the antiphonal bars 6–7,
  with the pair rising to G3/E3 in bars 8–9 as TTBB takes the
  foreground. No voice leading rule was violated; this is a
  registral choice for textural contrast.
