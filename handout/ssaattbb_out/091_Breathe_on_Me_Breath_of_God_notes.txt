TEXTURE PLAN
────────────
The arrangement opens with all 8 voices in gentle homophony (bars 1-4), but
with the upper choir (SSAA) carrying the melodic weight and the lower choir
(TTBB) providing a sustained harmonic carpet, creating a sense of reduced
texture at the opening by keeping inner-voice motion minimal. The texture
builds through bars 5-7 as all voices become more rhythmically active,
with S1, A1, T1 animating independent lines simultaneously. Bars 7-8 push
toward climax with S1 reaching C#5 against B1 descending toward F#2,
achieving maximum ensemble spread. The final bar collapses to a hushed F#
minor whole-note chord across all 8 voices.

Texture types used:
  1. Full 8-voice homophony (bars 1-9 throughout, primary texture).
  2. Call and response between S1 and B1 (bars 5-7: S1 melodic; B1 follows
     the original bass line in contrary motion to the soprano contour).
  3. Inner-voice contrary motion (A1 ascending while A2 descends in bars 1, 5;
     T1 ascending while T2 holds or descends in bars 2, 6).
  4. Suspension at final cadence (bar 8-9: A2 resolves C#4→A3; T1 holds C#4
     across the bar line before settling to A3 at bar 9).

RULE COMPROMISES
────────────────
1. Bar 6, beat 2: A1 (C#5) rises above S2 (F#4), creating a brief cross-pair
   inversion between the Alto I and Soprano II lines. The rule "Voice crossing
   between pairs is permitted where musically effective" (Step 6, rule 3) covers
   this; the A1 line reaches its local high point in a melodic arc and returns
   stepwise. No unison occurs (interval is a perfect 4th). Accepted.

2. A2, bar 7, beats 3-4: A2 moves f e (F#4 → E4). This places A2 momentarily
   above A1 (which has a=A4 → f=F#4 at those beats): bar 7 beat 3 A2=F#4,
   A1=A4 — A2 < A1 ✓; beat 4 A2=E4, A1=F#4 — A2 < A1 ✓. No crossing within
   the A1/A2 pair; noted here for transparency.

3. Texture variety: given the hymn is only 9 bars, it was not possible to
   deploy all 6 texture types listed in Step 7 without fragmenting the musical
   line. Three types are clearly present (full homophony, call/response outer
   voices, inner-voice contrary motion). Stretto and antiphonal exchange were
   not employed because the 4/4 single-phrase structure offers insufficient
   length for convincing imitative entries without distorting the hymn melody.

4. S1 ending (bar 9): S1 closes on F#4 — the 6th scale degree of A major /
   the tonic root of F# minor. This matches the original SATB soprano exactly.
   Although the instruction prefers B1 on root in low register at the final
   cadence, B1=F#2 (root) and Bar=A2 (3rd) fulfill that intent; the soprano
   resting on the tonic of F# minor is musically correct for this piece.
