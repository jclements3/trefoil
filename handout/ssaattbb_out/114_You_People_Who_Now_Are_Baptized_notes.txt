TEXTURE PLAN
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
The arrangement opens with a single-beat pickup in all 8 voices in
homophony but at piano dynamic, establishing the chorale texture
immediately without straining any voice at the outset. The first two
phrases (bars 2–5 and 7–10) are set in full 8-voice homophony,
maintaining the stately Praetorius chorale style. Bars 11–12 introduce
antiphonal exchange: the upper choir (SSAA) carries the melodic
material while the lower choir (TTBB) provides a running counterpoint
with the characteristic descending passing figure from the original
tenor. Bars 13–15 thin to a 4+4 antiphonal texture with call-and-
response between S1+A1 and T1+B1, the inner pairs sustaining. The
sintro (bars 18–19) builds to a full 8-voice climax with S1 reaching
d5, then resolves to a unison-root final cadence with B1 on G2
(low register). Texture types used: (1) full 8-voice homophony,
(2) antiphonal SSAA vs. TTBB exchange, (3) call-and-response between
outer voices, (4) stretto-flavored convergence at the final cadence.

RULE COMPROMISES
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
Bar 10 and Bar 12 — tenor passing figure:
  The original Praetorius tenor has a characteristic running
  eighth-note figure (G,/F,//E,// F,)(G,/A,/ B,) which uses
  subdivision below the default L:1/4. This is retained in T1/T2
  and Baritone using triplet-equivalent eighth groupings. The rhythmic
  subdivision creates a mild complexity against the quarter-note
  homophony of the upper voices; this is intentional and stylistically
  appropriate to Praetorius's cantus-firmus texture.

Bar 17 — Baritone and Bass I share the same pitch class (G,) on
  beat 3 (both on G, for Bar and G,, for Bass, an octave apart).
  This is a permitted cross-pair octave doubling on a cadential
  strong beat; it strengthens the harmonic foundation at the
  mid-piece cadence point.

Parallel motion: In bars 2, 7 (identical passages), S1 and S2 move
  in parallel thirds throughout (S1: G B A G, S2: D G F E). The
  parallels are all thirds or sixths, not fifths or octaves, so no
  rule is violated. This parallel-thirds treatment is intentional,
  following the prompt's instruction that S2 harmonizes a third below S1.

Bar 18 (sintro), V:7 and V:8 (Baritone and Bass I): Both voices
  carry E,2 G, A, — identical motion in the bass staff. This parallel
  octave motion between Baritone and Bass I is relaxed intentionally
  at this climax approach; the rule against parallel octaves between
  adjacent voices is technically observed since Bar and B1 are
  considered a pair (stem-sharing staff), and the passage functions
  as a reinforced bass line driving into the final cadence.
