TEXTURE PLAN
------------
The arrangement follows a four-phase dynamic arc that opens with a reduced
soprano duet and builds to full 8-voice texture, with a canonic stretto for
the climactic second Gloria refrain.

Phase 1 — Verse 1 (bars 1-4): Two voices only (S1 + S2). The soprano pair
  presents the hymn melody in close diatonic thirds. All six other voices
  rest. This creates a chamber-like opening that reserves the full ensemble
  for maximum contrast on the verse 2 entry.
  Texture type: Duo (melody + diatonic third harmonization).

Phase 2 — Verse 2 (bars 5-8): Full 8 voices enter simultaneously. SSAA
  carry the hymn melody, with A1 providing contrary-motion inner independence
  and A2 sustaining diatonic chord tones in the upper-alto register (F3-A3).
  T1 and T2 (both written one octave above sounding pitch per transpose=-12)
  present the original SATB tenor in parallel thirds. Baritone and Bass I
  carry the original SATB bass at concert pitch, moving in thirds and sixths.
  Texture type: Full 8-voice homophony.

Phase 3 — Gloria Refrain 1 (bars 9-14): S1 carries the melismatic Gloria
  melody with its descending sequential runs; S2 shadows a diatonic third
  below in parallel sixteenth-note motion. A1 moves stepwise in contrary
  motion to the S1 contour; A2 sustains diatonic half-note chord tones in
  F3-A3. T1 and T2 (written) hold sustained half-note chord tones, providing
  a stable tenorial cushion beneath the flowing soprano. Baritone and Bass I
  carry the original SATB tenor/bass harmonic framework.
  Texture type: Melody-dominated homophony (S1 soloistic, other voices
  provide complementary rhythmic layers).

Phase 4 — Gloria Refrain 2 / Final (bars 15-21): SSAA present the full
  Gloria melody for the second and final time. T1 and T2 enter one bar later
  (bar 16) with canonic imitation of the Gloria contour at the lower octave,
  creating a stretto effect. Baritone and Bass I sustain the harmonic
  foundation, with Baritone reaching its lower range at the climactic E2
  in bar 18. All 8 voices converge at bar 19 for the final cadential sweep.
  Bass I closes on F2 (tonic root, low register) in bar 21 for a grounded
  final cadence. The final chord (bar 21) spreads S1=F4, S2=C4, A1=A3,
  A2=A3, T1=C3 (sounding), T2=A2 (sounding), Bar=A2, B1=F2.
  Texture types: Canonic imitation (SSAA leads, TTBB follows one bar later);
  stretto convergence; full 8-voice homophony on cadential close.

Textures used: (1) duo opening, (2) full 8-voice homophony, (3) melody-
  dominated homophony with layered rhythmic activity, (4) canonic imitation,
  (5) stretto convergence at final cadence — five distinct texture types.

RULE COMPROMISES
----------------
1. Passing-note contact between S2's running 16th-note line and A1's
   sustained notes (bars 9-11 and 15-17): S2's Gloria runs pass through
   diatonic chord tones that A1 simultaneously sustains (e.g., S2 passes
   through E4 while A1 holds E4 on its second half-note in bar 9; S2 passes
   through D4 while A1 holds D4 in bar 11). These create one-sixteenth-note
   pitch contacts between S-voice and A-voice on passing tones within a
   running line. In professional choral texture, a sustained inner voice
   sharing a momentary pitch with a running melodic voice is standard
   practice and does not constitute a structural unison. These contacts
   were left intact because eliminating them would require A1 to avoid all
   diatonic chord tones, which would destroy its melodic integrity and
   harmonic function. The contacts are not perceptible as unisons to the
   ear in context; the 16th-note motion of S2 renders them passing.
   Rule technically engaged: S voices and A voices should avoid unisons.
   Mitigation: no contact lasts longer than a 16th note; all occur mid-
   phrase rather than on strong structural beats.

2. Baritone E2 at bars 12 and 18 (half note at phrase close): E2 is the
   absolute lower limit of Baritone range, and the instruction specifies
   "reserve extreme high and low notes for climax moments only." These E2s
   occur at phrase closes within the Gloria refrain rather than at the
   final climax. The descent G2-F2-E2 is idiomatic and mirrors the original
   SATB tenor's stepwise cadential approach; removing it would require a
   large skip in the Baritone line or a consecutive-fifth clash with B1.
   Justification: the notes are half-note in duration (not brief passing
   tones), arrived at by smooth stepwise descent, and occur at structurally
   important cadential points. The Baritone's absolute low is reserved for
   meaningful cadential use rather than an arbitrary mid-phrase peak.

3. T1 and T2 canonic entries (bars 16-18): The canonic runs in TTBB follow
   the sixteenth-note contour of the Gloria melody but truncate the final
   note of each descending group to avoid going below T1's minimum Bb2
   (sounding). In the SSAA original, S2's run ends on A3 (sounding A3);
   the corresponding T1 run would end on A2 (sounding), which is below T1's
   Bb2 minimum. The runs therefore end on B (written Bb3, sounding Bb2) for
   T1 and on G (written G3, sounding G2) for T2 rather than continuing one
   step lower. The melodic contour is preserved; only the final scale degree
   of each group is raised by one step to stay within professional range.

4. A1 final bar (bar 21) unison with A2: A1 closes on A3, forming a unison
   with A2's A3. A1+A2 pair unison on the final tonic chord. The rules
   permit this "occasionally for emphasis." The final bar of the piece is the
   strongest possible justification. The unison was chosen over placing A1 on
   E4 (which would cross above S2's C4, creating a visually confusing score
   layout on the final chord) or on C4 (which would create an S-A cross-pair
   unison with S2's C4, which is explicitly prohibited). A3 for both alto
   voices gives a clean, balanced final chord within all voice-crossing rules.
