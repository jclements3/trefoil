ARRANGER NOTES
192 — Blest Be The Tie That Binds (Dennis, Nägeli)
SSAATTBB expansion from Open Hymnal SATB setting (Donna Snell, alt.)
=======================================================================


TRANSPOSITION
-------------
Original key: G major (1 sharp).
Chosen key:   G major (1 sharp) — no transposition required.
Harp lever positions: F lever raised (F → F#). All other levers remain
  in Eb-storage position (down).
G major is a valid lever-harp key (1 sharp, within the 4-sharp limit).


HARMONIC SUBSTITUTIONS
-----------------------
Section 1, Bar 3 (fermata bar), beat 3, original Alto:
  The original S1V2 reads (D E) =F — the natural F4 on beat 3 creates a
  diminished seventh / Neapolitan colouring over the G-major cadence.
  Because all inner voices must remain diatonic (F# in G major), the
  F-natural is replaced. Beat-3 alto is reassigned to E4 (S2), and A1
  moves to D4, A2 to G3 — preserving the cadential motion toward the
  fermata chord without any accidental. The harmony remains a plain
  I (G major) cadence rather than the fleeting chromatic colour of the
  original; the harp's diatonic F# doubles naturally.

No other chromatic harmonies requiring reharmonisation were present.
The original tenor (S2V1) and bass (S2V2) are entirely diatonic in
G major throughout.


DIATONIC VIOLATIONS CAUGHT
---------------------------
Section 1, Bar 3 (= fermata bar), Voice S1V2 (original Alto), beat 3:
  Original note: =F4 (F natural — not in G major key signature).
  Replacement:   E4 (diatonic neighbour, step below the original F4).
  Voice (S2 in SSAATTBB): receives E4 on beat 3. The resolution E4→F(pickup)
  of the following phrase continues naturally.

No accidentals appear in the original tenor or bass voices. The raised
leading tone in the dominant measures (F# throughout) is native to G major.


TEXTURE PLAN
------------
The arrangement opens with SSAA only for the first two bars (four-voice
chamber texture), allowing the voices to establish the melody and
supporting harmony in a transparent sonority. The full eight voices
enter together on bar 3 at the first fermata — this strong-beat entry
with sustained harmony underlines the structural weight of the cadence.
Section 2 uses full eight-voice writing throughout, building through
bars 11–12 (S1 reaches D5–E5) to the expressive peak at the !sintro!
mark, then settling to a warm G-major final cadence with the bass
sustaining G2 and S1 returning to G4. Texture types employed:
  (1) Reduced-voice opening (bars 1–2, SSAA only).
  (2) Full eight-voice homophony from bar 3 onward.
  (3) Melodic independence: all eight voices carry distinct stepwise
      lines — parallel thirds predominate in paired voices (S1/S2,
      A1/A2, T1/T2, Bar/B1), with contrary or oblique motion between
      pairs at phrase turns.
  (4) Structural antiphony implied by the SSAA-only opening resolved
      by the TTBB entry at the fermata (call-and-response across the
      two choirs at the phrase boundary).


UNISON LOG
----------
Section 1, Bar 3 (fermata), Pair Bar/B1, beats 1–2 (strong):
  Both Bar and B1 enter on G2 (sustained half note). Purpose: TTBB
  choir enters together on the tonic root beneath the full-choir fermata;
  the doubled bass root grounds the texture at this structural cadence.
  Both voices move away (Bar→C3, B1→C3) on beat 1 of the next bar
  (bar 4), separating immediately after the fermata is released.

Section 1, Bar 4, Pair Bar/B1, beat 1 (strong):
  Bar=C3, B1=C3 — unison on the tonic of the C3 harmony as both
  voices arrive at the subdominant-area chord. Single-beat unison;
  both separate immediately (Bar→A2, B1→E2 on beat 2).

Section 1, Bar 6 (pickup beat, beat 3), Pair Bar/B1:
  Bar=D3, B1=D3 at the pickup D that launches Section 2. This is a
  structural upbeat unison on the dominant root before the new phrase;
  both voices separate in Section 2 bar 1 (Bar→A2, B1→D3 then F#2).

Section 2, Bar 2 (= section 2 bar 2), Cross-choir T2/Bar, beat 2:
  T2 (sounds B2), Bar (sounds B2) — single cross-choir unison on a
  weak beat. Both voices move away by step immediately (T2→B2, Bar→D3).
  Cross-choir unisons at the register break are expressly permitted.

Section 2, Bars 2–4, Cross-choir T1/Bar, multiple beats:
  T1 and Bar share the D3 pitch on several beats (Section 2 bars 2,
  3, and 4). This doubling occurs at the natural register break between
  the lower SSAA choir and the upper TTBB choir and is intentional
  for ensemble blend at the transition zone. Cross-choir unisons of
  this kind are permitted by the arrangement rules.


RANGE LOG
----------
S1, Section 2 bar 4 (!sintro! beat 3): E5.
  E5 is the climactic peak of S1 in this arrangement. It falls within
  the professional S1 range (comfortable to G5, extreme to A5); E5 is
  well within the comfortable zone and lands at the structural high
  point marked !sintro!. No concern.

T1 (sounds), Section 2 bar 1, beat 3: C3.
  C3 is just above T1's lower limit (B2). This occurs in the inner
  voice on a passing-tone eighth note and is not an extreme; the line
  moves immediately back up to D3. Minor concern flagged; a competent
  tenor will manage this comfortably.

T2 (sounds), Section 1 bar 6 pickup + Section 2 bars 1 and 6: A2.
  A2 is near the lower end of T2's range (G2–E4). It occurs as a
  passing or held note in inner-voice motion; the surrounding D3 and
  B2 pitches frame it. The line does not dip below G2. Comfortable
  for a professional bass-baritone covering the T2 part.

Bar (Baritone), Section 1 bar 5, beat 2: B2.
  B2 is one step above the Baritone's lower limit (E2). Occurs passing
  through a descent from D3; immediate return to D3 on beat 3. Fine
  for a professional baritone.

B1, Section 2 bar 6, beat 3: D2.
  D2 sits just above the B1 lower limit (C2). Original bass line
  preserved unchanged; this note corresponds to the original S2V2
  score. Professional basses handle D2 routinely.


RULE COMPROMISES
----------------
Bar 3 (section 1), Pair Bar/B1: parallel unisons across the fermata
  entry. Both Baritone and Bass I enter simultaneously on G2 for a
  held fermata. The doubling is deliberate (maximum tonic-root weight
  at the arrival of the full choir) and resolves immediately to
  contrary motion. Flagged as an intentional structural choice rather
  than a default voicing shortcut.

Section 2 bar 2, Bar voice: the Baritone line reads B,2 D, (sounds
  B2 half, D3 quarter). On beat 1, Bar and T1 both sound B2 (cross-choir
  unison). This was retained because the alternative required an
  augmented melodic interval (A3→F#2) in the Baritone, which violates
  Rule 7 more severely than a cross-choir unison violates the unison
  guidelines.

Section 1, Bar 6 (section boundary): S2 and A2 voices have A, on
  the pickup to Section 2 (S2=F#4 pickup, A2=A3 pickup). These match
  the natural chord tones of the D-major dominant and create momentary
  close spacing (S2 F#4, A1 D4, A2 A3) at the beat-3 pickup. The
  three-voice SSAA layout remains open and the spacing is idiomatic.
