ARRANGER NOTES — Amazing Grace (New Britain)
SSAATTBB arr. from SATB original (Open Hymnal Project, 1900 Excell setting)
============================================================

TRANSPOSITION
-------------
Original key: G major (1 sharp, F#).
Chosen key:   G major (unchanged).
Semitones shifted: 0 — no transposition required.

Lever harp (Eb storage tuning) configuration for G major:
  Raise levers on: Ab -> Eb -> Bb -> F -> C -> G -> D
  That is: raise levers on Eb, Bb, F, C, G, D (6 levers raised from storage).
  Ab lever stays down.
  Result: all strings tuned to G major diatonic scale.

No range problems were encountered; the original key was retained.

No chromatic harmonies appear in the original SATB that required
substitution. The original setting is entirely diatonic in G major
(F# appears as the diatonic leading tone and is part of the key signature).

Bar 11 (C major, IV chord): original alto has C4-D4-C4 and tenor has
G3-E3-G3, both diatonic. No substitution needed.

Bar 14 (D major, V chord): all voices diatonic (D-F#-A with C4 in tenor
creating a D7 color). F# is diatonic in G major. No substitution needed.


DIATONIC VIOLATIONS CAUGHT

None. The original SATB inner voices contain no accidentals outside the

TEXTURE PLAN
------------
The arrangement opens with reduced texture (4 voices: S1, A1, T1, B1 only
on the pickup and bar 1), establishing the melody and bass framework before
the full choir enters. Bars 2-4 expand to include the remaining inner
voices, building to full 8-voice homophony by bar 5. The first climax
occurs at bar 7 (S1 sustains D5, the highest note, over a full-spread
chord). Bars 9-11 use antiphonal texture: the SSAA choir leads with the
running melodic figure while TTBB provides harmonic support, then the
patterns echo between the choirs. The final section (bars 12-16) returns
to full 8-voice homophony for a broad, sustained conclusion. Texture types
used: (1) opening solo/quartet texture (pickup-bar 1), (2) full 8-voice
homophony (bars 5-8, 12-16), (3) antiphonal SSAA vs TTBB (bars 9-11),
(4) sustained chord with melodic figure in outer voices (bars 7-8).

RULE COMPROMISES
----------------
Bar 3, T1: The original tenor has G3-G3 in bar 3. T1 is raised to D4-C4
  to prevent T voice/B voice unisons with Bar (G3) and B1 (E3, C3). This
  slightly departs from the original tenor line but implies the same Em/C
  harmonic motion and remains singable with smooth voice leading from B3
  (bar 2) to D4 (bar 3) to B3 (bar 4).

Bar 6, T1: Original tenor has G3-F3. T1 is raised to B3-A3 to prevent
  unison with Bar (D3-F3) and B1 (D3-C3). The F3/D3 register of the
  original tenor overlaps with the Baritone/Bass register, requiring T1/T2
  to sit higher throughout bars with D-bass pedal points.

Bars 2, 6, 13-14, Bar voice: The Baritone frequently uses intervals of
  a 5th or 6th above B1 rather than a strict 3rd, because a strict 3rd
  above B1 (e.g. F3 above D3) matches T2's natural position (E3-G3),
  creating T-B unisons. Widening Bar's interval from B1 clears the
  register and produces a richer bass sonority (e.g. Bar=F3 above B1=D3
  gives an open 3rd; Bar=A3 above B1=E3 gives a 4th/11th for color).

Bar 12, A1: The original alto has B3-D4. A1 was changed to G3 (held,
  dotted half) to prevent an S2-A1 unison on D4 (S2 follows S1's B3-D4
  figure in this bar). G3 is a valid G major chord tone providing root
  support in the alto register.

Bars 1/5, S2 bar 4: S2 takes D4-C4 rather than the natural 3rd-below
  (B3), because B3 would create a sustained S2-A1 unison (A1=B3 in bar 4).
  C4 serves as a mild suspension that passes to the S1 D4 pitch, giving S2
  an independent line.

General note on T2 voice: T2 is kept in the G3-B3 register throughout
  rather than mechanically following thirds below T1 when T1 dips to D3-F3.
  This is necessary because the lower tenor register (D3-F3) overlaps with
  the Baritone/Bass register (B2-D3), creating T-B unisons. T2 sustained
  on G3 or B3 provides a firm harmonic pillar while Bar and B1 handle the
  lower bass function. This approach treats T2 as a "high bass-register
  tenor" — consistent with professional TTBB writing practice where the
  second tenor sits well above the baritone.
