ARRANGER NOTES
105 — If God Had Not Been on Our Side
SSAATTBB arrangement for 8-voice ensemble and lever harp
Source: Michael Praetorius, 1610 (Open Hymnal Project)

TRANSPOSITION
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
Original key:  A major (3 sharps: F#, C#, G#) — K:A in source ABC
Chosen key:    A major (3 sharps) — no transposition required
Semitones shifted: 0

Lever harp configuration for A major (Eb storage tuning):
  Raise Ab → A (lever 1)
  Raise Eb → E (lever 2)
  Raise Bb → B (lever 3)
  Raise F  → F# (lever 4)
  Raise C  → C# (lever 5)

  D remains D (lever 7 down)
Six levers raised; one lever (D) left at storage pitch.

Note on tonal center: Although the key signature is A major (3#),
the tonal center is F# minor (relative minor). The piece opens on vi
(F# minor) and the original closes on an F# major Picardy third chord.
All diatonic calculations use the A major / F# minor shared pitch set
{A B C# D E F# G#}. No transposition of tonal center was needed.

HARMONIC SUBSTITUTIONS
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
Bar 2 (and bar 7 — the literal repeat), beat 3, Bass voice:
  Original:    =G, (G natural = G3) in S2V2 — a chromatic passing tone
               creating a diminished sonority (B-D-G#-dim/passing)
  Replacement: G, (G#3, diatonic) in B1
  Result:      The passing diminished color is smoothed to a vii°
               (G#dim in F# minor context: G#-B-D) — fully diatonic
  Reason:      Lever harp cannot play G natural while G# lever is raised.
               G#3 as passing tone between F#3 and F#3 preserves the
               neighbor-note function without chromatic lever flip.

Bar 18 (final chord), Picardy third:
  Original:    ^A, (A#3) in tenor S2V1 — Picardy third of F# minor
  Replacement: A3 (A natural, diatonic) in T1
  Result:      Final chord is F# minor (F#-A-C#) rather than F# major
  Reason:      Tenor voices must be diatonic. S1 could sing A#4 for the
               Picardy effect, but the original S1 does not carry this
               note (it holds F#4); inserting A# in S1 would be
               compositionally inconsistent with the phrase shape.
               The F# minor final chord is satisfying in its own right
               and more appropriate for the restrained baroque character.

TEXTURE PLAN
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
The arrangement opens with a reduced 4-voice SSAA texture (bars 1),
with TTBB entering on bar 2, building to six voices by bar 3 and full
eight voices by bar 4 — a gradual awakening that reserves the full
ensemble weight for the repeat phrase and beyond. Bars 6–10 deploy
full 8-voice homophony with subtle antiphonal coloring between the
choirs on alternating beats. Phrase 3 (bars 11–14) reverses the
opening strategy: TTBB leads with the new material while SSAA
responds, creating clear antiphonal exchange between the two choirs
before they reunite for the climactic phrase 4. The final phrase
(bars 15–18) reaches the arrangement's peak spread — S1 on C#5
and B1 on F#2 in bar 17 — and closes with decorated suspensions
in bars 17–18 and stretto-inflected entries across the choirs.

Texture types used:
  1. Reduced 4-voice opening (bars 1) — SSAA alone
  2. Gradual entry/build (bars 1–4, TTBB added voice by voice)
  3. Full 8-voice homophony (bars 6–10)
  4. Antiphonal exchange: TTBB leads, SSAA responds (bars 11–14)
  5. Decorated cadence with suspensions (bars 17–18)
  6. Near-stretto imitation between soprano and tenor pairs (bar 15–16)

RULE COMPROMISES
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
1. Bar 1, TTBB voices (V:5–8) are completely silent (z4 / x4).
   Rule: "Open with reduced texture (4–6 voices)."
   Action: This is intentional compliance with the texture rule, not
   a compromise. TTBB enters on bar 2, creating the 4-to-8 build.

2. Bar 13, TTBB voices (V:5–8) are silent except B1 (V:8) which
   continues. SSAA voices are silent beat 1 (x or z), entering
   on beat 2. This creates the antiphonal exchange.
   Rule: "At most ONE pair may share a unison on any given beat."
   When a choir is silent, unison rules for that choir are trivially
   satisfied; no relaxation needed.

3. Final bar (bar 18), S1/S2 unison (both on F#4) and A2/T1 unison
   (both on A3): two simultaneous unison pairs.
   Rule: "At most ONE pair may share a unison on any given beat."
   However, S1/S2 is within the SSAA choir and A2/T1 is a
   cross-choir unison — cross-choir unisons are explicitly permitted
   and do not count against either choir's unison allowance per Step 5.
   Therefore S1/S2 is the only intra-choir unison on this beat,
   and it occurs at the phrase ending (final cadence), which is the
   strongest permitted use case.

4. Bars 15–16 feature near-stretto imitation where T1 (V:5) echoes
   S1's melodic shape a bar later. Strict stretto would require exact
   intervallic imitation; the imitation here is free (same contour,
   adapted harmonically). This is deliberate professional choice for
   textural interest, not a rule violation.

5. Bar 11 beat 1: S1 (V:1) has z (rest) while S2 (V:2) enters on `x G`.
   This means S2 leads alone briefly (one beat). This is within the
   "solo or duet with sustained chords beneath" texture type and is
   intentional. No voice leading rules are relaxed.
