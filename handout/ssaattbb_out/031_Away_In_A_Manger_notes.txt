=========================================================

TRANSPOSITION
-------------
Original key:  F major (1 flat: Bb)
Chosen key:    F major (1 flat: Bb)
Semitones shifted: 0 (no transposition required)

F major is a valid lever harp key (1 flat, within the 3-flat maximum).

Lever harp setup for F major (from Eb storage tuning, all levers down = Eb):
  Raise levers on:  Bb strings, F strings
  (2 levers raised per octave: Bb→B natural raised, then re-applied as key Bb;
   F→F natural raised from the Eb string)
  Diatonic scale available on harp: F G A Bb C D E


HARMONIC SUBSTITUTIONS
-----------------------
No chromatic harmonies requiring substitution were present in the source.
The original Murray hymn is entirely diatonic in F major. All chords used:

  F major (I):   pickup, bars 1, 2, 4, 7, 8, 9, 10, 12, 14, 16, 17, 19
  Bb major (IV): bars 3, 11, 18 (beats 1-2)
  C major/C7 (V/V7): bars 5, 6, 13, 15


DIATONIC VIOLATIONS CAUGHT
---------------------------
None. The source SATB is entirely diatonic in F major. No inner-voice
accidentals required replacement.

Note: the Bb in S2 bar 5 (`B` in key F = Bb4) is diatonic. The "B natural"
appearance in ABC source for key F is automatically flattened by the key
signature — all B's in this arrangement are Bb.


TEXTURE PLAN
------------
The arrangement unfolds across three phrases with a deliberate crescendo
of texture. Phrase 1 (pickup + bars 1-6) opens with an intimate four-voice
setting (S1, S2, Baritone, Bass I) — a soprano duet over a quiet bass,
evoking the hushed lullaby character of the text. Phrase 2 (bars 7-12)
adds Tenor I and Tenor II, expanding to six voices as the text turns to the
cosmic imagery of the stars; the tenors shadow the soprano pair a tenth
below, creating a layered antiphonal effect between the vocal pairs. Phrase
3 (bars 13-19) brings in Alto I and Alto II for full eight-voice homophony,
with S1 reaching E5 at the climax (bar 13) and the final cadence (bars
18-19) settling with a IV-I suspension across all eight voices simultaneously,
B1 grounded on F2.

Texture types employed:
  1. Reduced texture (4 voices): Phrase 1 — S1 S2 Bar B1
  2. Partial choir (6 voices): Phrase 2 — adds T1 T2
  3. Full 8-voice homophony: Phrase 3 (bars 13-19)
  4. Cadential suspension: Bar 18, all voices (IV-I, held 2 beats then resolving)
  5. Call and response: S1 leading with S2 harmonizing in thirds throughout


UNISON LOG
-----------
No within-pair unisons occur in this arrangement. The lullaby character
and short-phrase structure made textural clarity through distinct voicing
the primary goal. The final chord (bar 19) features no within-pair unisons:
  S1=F4, S2=C4 (fifth)
  A1=A3, A2=F3 (third)
  T1 written f (sounds F4), T2 written c (sounds C4): sixth
  Bar=A3, B1=F2: third

The A1-T2 cross-choir unison at A3 (bar 19) is intentional and permitted:
it sits exactly at the natural register break between the choirs.


RANGE LOG
----------
Bar 6, V1 (Soprano I): e = E5 — approaches upper comfortable range;
  occurs at the end of Phrase 1 on the dominant harmony (C7), providing
  a forward-looking intensification into the star imagery of Phrase 2.
  E5 is well within stated range (comfortable to G5).

Bar 9, V5 (Tenor I, written e): sounds E4 — at the comfortable upper
  limit of T1. This is the middle of Phrase 2 and serves as an echo of
  the soprano's high register.

Bar 13, V1 (Soprano I): e3/2 d/ c = E5 D5 C5 — structural climax entry
  of full 8-voice texture. E5 is comfortable.

Bar 13, V5 (Tenor I, written g3/2 f/ e): sounds G4 F4 E4 — G4 is above
  the comfortable top of T1 (comfortable to E4, absolute to G4). This is
  intentional at the structural climax. The voice resolves down immediately
  in bar 14 (sounds C4 B3 A3).

Bar 13, V6 (Tenor II, written e3/2 d/ c): sounds E4 D4 C4 — E4 is at
  the absolute top of T2 range (G2-E4). Acceptable at the climax; resolves
  down immediately in bar 14.

Bar 19, V8 (Bass I): F,, = F2 — low register final tonic root, as specified
  for the final cadence. Within B1 range (C2-A3).

All voices remain in comfortable mid-range during Phrases 1 and 2, reserving
the upper register for Phrase 3 as required.


RULE COMPROMISES
-----------------
Bar 6, V1 (Soprano I) leap: S1 moves from F4 (bar 5 beat 3) to G4 (bar 6
  beat 1), then leaps to E5 (beat 2), resolving down to C5 (beat 3). The
  leap of a minor seventh (G4 to E5) exceeds the sixth-or-more rule. E5
  resolves down by a third to C5 rather than strictly by step. This was
  accepted because bar 6 is the climax of Phrase 1 (the first "Lord Jesus"
  setting) and the resolution C5 is a clear downward motion. Reformulating
  this phrase with a smaller leap would undermine the melodic climax.

Bar 13, V5 (Tenor I): see Range Log — G4 is at the absolute ceiling of T1.
  Accepted at the structural climax; resolves immediately.

Bar 14, S2-A1 close voicing: S2=F4 E4 C4 and A1=E4 D4 Bb3 create intervals
  of a minor second (beat 1) and major second (beats 2-3). Close-position
  upper-voice writing is standard practice at this register (middle voices
  in the F3-F4 band) and produces the characteristic choral blend sought;
  no rule is formally violated but the voicing is noted for singers to
  ensure the A1 voice is clearly heard.
