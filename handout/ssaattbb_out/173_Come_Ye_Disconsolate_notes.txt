TEXTURE PLAN
----------------------------------------------------------------
The arrangement opens with a 6-voice texture (bars 1–2, V:4 and V:6
resting) to create an intimate, prayer-like atmosphere; the second
soprano and lower tenor double the soprano and upper tenor lines at
the octave or third, giving a chorale-like quality to the first
phrase. Full 8-voice homophony enters at bar 5 for a natural swell
into the second phrase. Bars 9–12 introduce an antiphonal gesture:
the SSAA choir presents descending lines (sighing, "disconsolate")
while the TTBB choir answers with steadier ascending motion,
creating dialogue between the two sub-ensembles. The climax in bar
13 places Soprano I at E5 and Tenor I at G4 concert simultaneously
for the widest possible vertical spread, while Alto I ascends from
A3 through D4 and Alto II climbs from F3 to B3, building the chord
from below. The final cadence (bars 14–16) dissolves from F major
through G7 to a Cmaj6 close (the A3 in Soprano II is preserved from
the soprano line's 6/4 motion), ending with Bass I on C3, Baritone
on E3, and all upper voices grounding to long held notes.

Texture types used:
  1. Full 8-voice homophony: bars 5–8, bars 13–16
  2. Reduced 6-voice opening: bars 1–2
  3. Antiphonal SSAA vs TTBB exchange: bars 9–12

RULE COMPROMISES
----------------------------------------------------------------
1. Bar 1, A1 opening register (A3): The Alto I part opens on A3
   (A, in ABC), which is within the A1 range (F3–D5) but sits in
   the lower portion of the voice's tessitura. This was necessary
   to avoid a S–A unison with Soprano II (E4 at bar 1 beat 1-2).
   The low opening register reinforces the hushed opening texture
   and is vocally comfortable.

2. Bar 5, Bass I Bb substitution: The C7 color of the original
   (Bb in bass) is lost, slightly weakening the harmonic preparation
   for the F major bar 6. This is an unavoidable consequence of the
   lever-harp diatonic constraint. The F major arrival is still
   audible as a IV chord and the text phrase flows naturally.

3. Bar 7, Tenor II lower register (B2): The T2 voice ends bar 7 on
   B2 concert (within its G2–E4 range, lower limit G2). This sits
   at the bottom of the comfortable register for T2. It is used here
   to avoid a T–B unison with Bass I (D3) and to create a 7th chord
   colour (G–B–D–F) in the lower TTBB voices. The resolution upward
   in bars 8–9 compensates.

4. Bar 16, A1 final pitch (G3): Alto I closes on G3 (the fifth of
   C major). This is below the S2 final A3 but above A2's E3,
   creating a correct voice order S2(A3) > A1(G3) > A2(E3) that
   spans only a minor third between the two alto parts. The close
   spacing at the bottom of the SSAA texture is deliberate: the
   upper voices are well-spread (S1=C4, S2=A3), concentrating
   warmth in the middle register for the final Cmaj6 chord.

5. Bar 15, Baritone descending suspension (B2): The Baritone line
   moves B2→D3→E3 in bar 15, which is the final G7→C approach.
   The leap from D,2 (D3, bar 8/end of system 2) to the climax
   range and back to B2 involves larger motion than preferred.
   However, these wide leaps are separated by rests (the half-note
   rest in bar 8 and the entrance at bar 13), so no augmented
   melodic interval occurs. The B2 in bar 15 is approached by step
   downward from the previous bar's A,, (A2) and resolves upward
   to D3→E3, satisfying the resolution rule for low leaps.
