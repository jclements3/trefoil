DIATONIC VIOLATIONS — RANGE CLAMPING IN TENOR VOICES
══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

The original tenor (S2V1, bass clef) frequently descends below B2,
the minimum concert pitch for Tenor I (professional range B2–G4).
Specifically, the original tenor line habitually sits in the G2–C3
register (G2, A2, F#2), which is below T1's range floor.

Resolution: T1 (V:5) was substantially revoiced to an independent
diatonic melodic line occupying the range B2–G3, implying the same
harmonic function but lying an octave higher where necessary.
T2 (V:6) follows T1 at a diatonic third below, clamped to G2
minimum concert pitch. Where T2 would otherwise fall below G2
(e.g. bar 4, bars 5 in phrase A), a unison with T1 at G2 or a
held G2 was substituted.

Bars affected by tenor revoicing:
  Phrase 1a bars 1–6 (T1 and T2 fully revoiced)
  Phrase 2a bars 9–14 (same revoicing, same phrase repeat)
  Middle section bar 21: original tenor G2/A2 raised to G3/A3
  Middle section bar 22: original tenor A2 raised to A3 (pickup)
  Final cadence bar 24: original tenor G2/A2 raised to D3/E3

TEXTURE PLAN
══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

The arrangement builds from a reduced, SSAA-led opening to a full
eight-voice climax at the doxological final section. Four distinct
texture types are used.

Arc: pp (bars 1–2, SSAA alone, TTBB tacet) → mp (bars 3–6, SSAA
leads with TTBB entering on sustained harmonies) → mf (bars 7–8,
first full 8-voice homophonic cadence) → f (bars 9–14, TTBB takes
melodic prominence while SSAA continues) → ff (bars 15–16, strong
cadential tutti) → climax (bars 17–22, middle section with full
8-voice polyphony, S1 ascending to A4/B4, B1 descending to G2/E2)
→ final cadence (bars 23–26, stretto-like convergence on G major
with a broad spread of six octaves from G2 to G4).

Texture types employed:
1. SSAA choir alone (bars 1–2): reduced opening texture, four-voice
   treble choir without bass reinforcement.
2. Stratified entry (bars 3–6): TTBB enters on sustained chord tones
   while SSAA carries the melodic line — a layered quasi-antiphonal
   effect within a homophonic frame.
3. Full 8-voice homophony (bars 7–8, 15–16): all voices move together
   in chordal rhythms at phrase cadences.
4. Polyphonic full texture (bars 9–22): all eight voices sing with
   independent melodic lines, with contrary motion between pairs
   (S1 vs S2, A1 vs A2, T1 vs T2, Bar vs B1) providing inner-voice
   interest and genuine melodic independence throughout.
5. Convergent cadence (bars 23–26): all voices move stepwise toward
   the final G major triad, with S1 holding B4→A4→G4 while B1
   descends A2→G2, producing maximum ensemble spread at resolution.

RULE COMPROMISES
══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

1. T1 melodic independence vs. original tenor line (bars 1–16):
   The original SATB tenor (S2V1) lives almost entirely below
   Tenor I's professional minimum of B2. Rather than a strict
   3rd-above-tenor split, T1 and T2 were written as genuinely
   new diatonic melodic lines implying the original harmonic
   function but occupying the B2–G3 range. This is sanctioned
   by the prompt's instruction to "recompose as a genuine
   independent diatonic melodic line implying the original
   tenor harmony."

2. S2 bar 4 unison with S1 (S1=S2=B3, half note):
   At the D-major half cadence in bar 4, S2 holds B3 in unison
   with S1 to avoid crossing into A1's G3–F#3 territory. The
   unison falls on a phrase cadence (weak beat half-note), and
   both voices separate by step immediately (S1 rises to D4,
   S2 falls to A3 at the start of bar 5). Sanctioned by prompt.

3. A1 range low extreme (F#3 in bars 3–5 and repeated bars 11–13):
   A1 descends to F#3 in the inner phrase bars; this is just above
   the A1 minimum of F3. Acceptable for a professional alto
   in a quiet, supported context (TTBB entering on sustained
   chords beneath).

4. V:7 Baritone ascending leap of P5 in middle section bar 19:
   Bar moves from C3 to B3 (bar 18→19) which is a major 7th
   in the bar line — actually B2 → B3 crossing the bar, an octave
   leap. The B is immediately sustained as a neighbor-tone figure
   (B3–A3–B3–C4) so the leap is absorbed into a melodic shape
   rather than left unresolved. No rule violation but noted.

5. Parallel 3rds throughout most voice pairs:
   The primary splitting strategy (diatonic 3rds below S1 for S2,
   diatonic 3rds below A1 for A2, etc.) produces many bars of
   parallel 3rds within pairs. This is consistent with professional
   double-choir writing and is not a rule violation — parallel 3rds
   are explicitly encouraged in Step 6 ("Prefer thirds and sixths
   for intervals between paired voices").
