HARMONIC SUBSTITUTIONS
-----------------------
Bar 29 (verse 4, beat 2): S2 has _E (Eb) — this is a chromatic note
  (bVII7 color / modal mixture). S2 must be diatonic, so this note
  appears only in S1's melodic line as a free accidental (S1 is
  unrestricted). In the arrangement, S2 on bar 29 beat 3 uses E natural
  (diatonic F major), while S1 alone carries the Eb. No reharmonization
  was needed for the underlying chord — the original bass supports F
  major at this point, so the Eb in S1 reads as a chromatic passing
  color against a diatonic F major chord. All inner voices remain on
  diatonic tones of F major.

No other chromatic harmonies appear in the original SATB that required
chord substitution. The original setting is firmly diatonic in F major
throughout, with the sole exception noted above.

The piece is otherwise fully diatonic in F major.


TEXTURE PLAN
------------
Verse 1 (bars 1–8): Opening with full 8-voice homophony from bar 1,
  establishing the chorale texture and the characteristic warmth of
  Hyfrydol. TTBB and SSAA move together in block chords. Dynamics
  implied mp, building through the phrase.

Verse 2 (bars 9–16): Full 8-voice homophony for bars 9–12, then
  antiphonal exchange: SSAA alone in bar 13, TTBB alone in bar 14,
  SSAA alone in bar 15, all 8 reunite for the cadence at bar 16.
  This antiphonal texture provides the first moment of dramatic contrast.

Verse 3 (bars 17–24): Canonic/imitative texture — inner voices (A1,
  A2, T1, T2) introduce stepwise contrary-motion lines beneath S1's
  ascending melody. All 8 voices build toward the half cadence at bar 24
  (G major chord). Dynamics imply mf–f, climax approach.

Verse 4 (bars 25–32): Full 8-voice homophony with maximum spread.
  S1 reaches d5 (bar 30, the highest point of the arrangement). Stretto
  entries in the inner voices at bars 29–30 create momentum into the
  final cadence. Bar 32 closes with all 8 voices on a root-position
  F major chord, B1 on F2 (low register). The final cadence is plain
  block chords rather than suspension — the triple meter and the hymn's
  simple style suit a direct, grounded close.

Texture types used:
  1. Full 8-voice homophony (verses 1, 2 bars 9–12, verse 4)
  2. Antiphonal exchange — SSAA vs TTBB (verse 2 bars 13–15)
  3. Contrary-motion inner voices with canonic coloring (verse 3)
  4. Stretto entries at final cadence (bars 29–31)

RULE COMPROMISES
----------------
Bar 4 / Bar 12: Bar+B1 share the same pitch (C, = C3) on beat 1 of the
  cadential approach. This is a cross-pair unison within the TTBB choir
  between Baritone and Bass I. Permitted since Bar and B1 are adjacent
  voices and this occurs only on a strong beat approaching a cadence;
  both voices move away by step immediately after.

Bar 28: The ornamental figure (G/A/ B/A/) in S1 creates rapid 8th-note
  motion against quarter-note inner voices. This brief rhythmic
  heterophony between S1 and the other voices is intentional — it
  preserves the original soprano melody figure while keeping all other
  voices in simple quarter-note rhythms for clarity.

Bar 29: S2 carries _E (Eb) in the original SATB. Per the diatonic
  constraint, S2 in the SSAATTBB arrangement replaces this with E natural.
  S1 alone carries the Eb. This creates a momentary major-seventh
  clash between S1 (Eb) and S2 (E natural) on beat 3 of bar 29. This
  is an accepted consequence of the diatonic constraint and produces
  a characteristic lever-harp color (S1 chromatic against diatonic
  accompaniment).

Final cadence (bar 32): A plain block cadence was chosen over a
  suspension because Hyfrydol's character in triple meter is direct
  and hymnic. A 7–6 suspension was considered but rejected as overly
  elaborate for this congregational tune's style.
