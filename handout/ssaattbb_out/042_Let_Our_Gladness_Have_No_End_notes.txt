ARRANGER NOTES
042 — Let Our Gladness Have No End
("Narodil se Kristus Pán" / "Salvator Natus," 15th-century Bohemian carol)
SSAATTBB arrangement for 8-voice ensemble with lever harp

══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
TRANSPOSITION
══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Original key: G major (1 sharp)
Chosen key:   G major (1 sharp)
Semitones shifted: 0 (no transposition required)

G major is a valid lever harp key. It requires only one lever engagement
beyond the Eb storage tuning:

  Eb storage: all levers down = Eb Bb F C G D Ab
  Raise levers: Ab → Eb → Bb → F → C → G → D
  For G major (F# only): raise the D lever
    — All strings except F are at storage pitch
    — Raise F strings to F# (one lever position)

Lever positions for G major:
  Ab lever: DOWN (Ab remains)
  Eb lever: DOWN (Eb remains)
  Bb lever: DOWN (Bb remains)
  F  lever: UP   (raises F to F#)
  C  lever: DOWN (C remains)
  G  lever: DOWN (G remains)
  D  lever: DOWN (D remains)

No transposition was needed; the original key lies comfortably within
all 8 voice ranges in G major.

══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
HARMONIC SUBSTITUTIONS
══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

No full harmonic substitutions were required. The original SATB
harmonization is almost entirely diatonic in G major.

The one chromatic inner-voice moment (see Diatonic Violations section
below) was handled by diatonic neighbor substitution rather than full
reharmonization, because the overall harmonic context (Em/G-area
chords) remained coherent without changing the chord root.

══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
DIATONIC VIOLATIONS CAUGHT
══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Bar 12, Tenor I (V:5), beat 3:
  Original source note: ^D (D#4 concert) — from S2V1 "E E ^D E"
  This D# is the raised leading tone of E minor and implies A major
  (V/vi = secondary dominant), producing the tonicization D#-E.
  Replacement: D natural (D4 concert), written 'd' in the 8vb voice.
  Effect: The secondary dominant color is lost; the chord on beat 3
  becomes Em7 (E/G/B/D), which is the nearest diatonic substitute
  (v minor, per the V/vi → III or v rule). The stepwise neighbor
  motion E-E-D-E is retained with only the chromatic inflection removed.
  The harp's D natural at this beat is consistent with the diatonic
  lever setting and produces no clash.

TEXTURE PLAN
══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

The arrangement opens with SSAA alone (bars 1-4, 4 voices), establishing
the Bohemian carol melody in a light, chamber texture before TTBB enters
at bar 5 with a full 8-voice homophonic tutti. The suspension figure at
bars 9-11 (first statement) and bars 12-14 (repeat) allows the natural
phrase rhythm of the original to create contrasting densities: the dotted-
half suspension in S1/S2 anchors the upper voices while T1/T2/Bar/B1
continue active counterpoint beneath. Bar 15 (original rest measure) is
given to TTBB alone — a breath for the upper voices and a harmonic bridge
from G to C before the climactic finale. Bars 16-18 bring the full
ensemble to the peak: S1 reaches E5, T1 reaches G4 concert (G5 written),
and B1 closes on G2, spanning the full professional register. Textures
employed: (1) reduced SSAA opening, (2) full 8-voice homophony, (3)
sustained upper-voice suspension against active lower voices, (4) TTBB
alone in transitional bar, (5) full climactic finale with call and response
between S1 melody and T1 countermelody.

RULE COMPROMISES
══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

1. Baritone above comfortable range (bars 5-6, 13):
   Bar 5 beat 1 and bar 6 beat 2: Baritone reaches D4, one step above
   the stated "comfortable top A3" and above the strict range ceiling C4.
   Reason: The Baritone line strictly follows a diatonic third above B1.
   At these beats B1 holds B3, making D4 the correct harmonic third.
   The notes appear on strong beats (downbeats) at the full-ensemble
   entry (forte), where the upper Baritone register is supported by
   breath pressure and ensemble energy. A professional baritone can
   manage D4 at forte; the duration is a single quarter note in each case.
   Alternative: lower B1 at these beats (B3→B2) so Bar = D3; this
   was not done to preserve the original bass line integrity.

2. Parallel thirds A1-A2 (bars 9, 12):
   In bars 9 and 12, A1 and A2 move in consistent parallel thirds
   (D-E / B-C pattern). Parallel thirds between adjacent voices are
   a classical norm and are not a rule violation, but the instruction
   prefers contrary or oblique motion when splitting a single SATB note.
   These bars retain parallel motion because the antiphonal suspension
   character (repeated neighbor-tone figures) is musically more
   important than contrary motion between A1 and A2 at these points.
   Contrary motion is used between A1 and S2, and between A2 and T1
   at these bars, preserving the spirit of the rule across the texture.

3. Cross-pair voice crossing (bar 8, A1 below S2):
   S2 ends bar 8 on E4 (dotted half); A1 is on D4. S2=E4 > A1=D4:
   no crossing. (Earlier draft had A1=B4 which would have crossed;
   this was corrected to D4.)

4. T1 bar 13: G4 concert at range ceiling:
   As noted in the Range Log, T1 reaches G4 concert (the absolute
   stated maximum) at bar 13 beat 1-2 for a half-note duration.
   This is acceptable given: (a) it is the structural climax of the
   piece, (b) the note is approached by step from E4 in bar 12,
   (c) it is resolved by step downward (E4 on beat 3-4 of bar 13),
   (d) professional tenors routinely sustain G4 at climax.
