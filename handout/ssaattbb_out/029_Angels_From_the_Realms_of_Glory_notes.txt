HARMONIC SUBSTITUTIONS
══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Bar 7, beats 3–4 (approach to half cadence on G major):
  Original chord: implied D major / D7 (secondary dominant of G),
  with F# in the original alto voice and G# in the original tenor
  voice on beat 2 functioning as leading tones to G.
  Replacement: G major triad (diatonic), preserving the half-cadence
  goal on G. The dominant-of-dominant approach is removed.
  Reason: D major requires F# and C#, both non-diatonic in C major.
  The half-cadence on G is preserved with a slightly softer approach
  via G major itself.

Bar 9, pickup beat (tenor chromatic passing tone):
  Original implied harmony: momentary flat-VII color (Bb) as passing
  tone in the tenor pickup to bar 9.
  Replacement: B natural retained throughout. The passing motion
  D–B–C (written T1) is recast as a diatonic leading-tone approach
  to C (B natural → C natural), which strengthens rather than
  weakens the subsequent C major arrival.
  Reason: Bb is non-diatonic in C major.

Bass register correction (bars 3, 7, 9, 11):
  Original SATB bass descends to G1, A1, and B1 in several bars.
  These pitches fall below the professional B1 range minimum of C2
  and are unsustainable vocally. All sub-C2 bass notes were raised
  one octave. Harmonic function is unchanged.

DIATONIC VIOLATIONS CAUGHT
══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

1. Bar 7, beat 4 — original alto voice (S1V2 in source file)
   Original note: ^F (F-sharp, raised 4th of C major)
   Replacement:   F natural (diatonic 4th of C major)
   Context: F# was the 3rd of D major / leading tone to G in an
   implied D7 → G dominant motion. F natural converts beat 4 to a
   G dominant-seventh color (G–B–D–F), which still resolves cleanly
   to the G major half-cadence at bar 8. In the SSAATTBB arrangement,
   Alto I carries this line (e e g f); the F natural at beat 4
   preserves stepwise descent into the G cadence.

2. Bar 7, beat 2 — original tenor voice (S2V1 in source file)
   Original note: ^G (G-sharp, written ^G, in source)
   Replacement:   G natural
   Context: G# was a chromatic auxiliary / secondary leading tone to
   A minor (functioning as the raised 7th of Am). G natural removes
   the chromatic inflection. The A–G–A–G oscillation in Tenor I
   (concert) at bars 6–7 becomes fully diatonic while retaining its
   characteristic rocking motion over the dominant area.

3. Bar 9, pickup beat — original tenor voice (S2V1 in source file)
   Original note: _B (B-flat, written _B, in source)
   Replacement:   B natural
   Context: Bb was a chromatic passing / lower neighbor tone in the
   tenor pickup to bar 9. B natural (the diatonic major 7th of C
   major) substitutes cleanly. The melodic figure D–B–C in Tenor I
   (written) is retained with B natural, and the leading-tone motion
   B → C strengthens the arrival on bar 9.

TEXTURE PLAN
══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

The arrangement follows a three-phrase build from intimate opening
to full climax. Phrase 1 (pickup + bars 1–4) opens with SSAA alone
(mp), establishing the melody in the high soprano register before
the lower voices appear; Baritone and Bass I enter quietly at bar 2,
bringing the texture to six voices for bars 2–4. Tenors are held
back through the first phrase cadence. Phrase 2 (pickup + bars 5–8)
brings all eight voices together (mf) at the pickup, creating an
immediate textural surge. Bars 5–8 are full homophony with the tenor
pair doubling the soprano pair at a concert octave below, producing
an antiphonal register effect on bars 5–6 before converging on the
G major half-close at bar 8 in open, sustained block texture. Phrase
3 (pickup + bars 9–12) is fully scored at forte from the outset;
the register spreads steadily toward bar 10 where S1 reaches F5 (the
climax) and B1 grounds on G2. Bar 11 introduces stretto-like
eighth-note motion in the alto pair (A1: e/f/ and A2: complementary
movement) and the tenor pair (T1: a/b/, T2: f/g/), creating
overlapping contrary motion that converges into the final cadence.
Bar 12 closes with a 4–3 suspension in Alto I (F4 resolving to E4)
over a sustained C major chord in all other voices, with B1 on C2
providing a low, grounded final tonic.

Texture types used (4 of the 6 listed):
  1. Full 8-voice homophony — phrase 2 (bars 5–8) and phrase 3
     (bars 9–12); the primary texture of the arrangement.
  2. Reduced opening texture — SSAA only for pickup and bar 1,
     growing to 6 voices at bar 2, 8 voices at phrase 2 pickup.
  3. Stretto / overlapping inner motion — bar 11, alto and tenor
     pairs running against each other in contrary eighth-note motion
     before the final cadence.
  4. Cadential suspension — bar 12, Alto I 4–3 (F4 → E4) over
     a plain C major chord in all other voices.

UNISON LOG
══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Bar 11, beat 2 — Baritone (V7) and Bass I (V8): both A2.
  Position: weak beat (beat 2 of bar 11).
  Purpose: convergence point as both voices arrive together before
  diverging to B2 (Bar) and G2 (B1) on beat 3. Both voices move
  away by step in opposite directions immediately after, satisfying
  the weak-beat unison rule.

Bar 12 — Alto I and Soprano II:
  A1 resolves to E4 (beat 2 of bar 12); S2 holds E4 throughout.
  This is a cross-pair unison between S2 and A1 (not an intra-pair
  unison). Cross-pair unisons are unrestricted by the rules. Both
  voices arrive on E4 as the 3rd of the final C major chord;
  the doubling adds fullness at the cadence. Intentional.

No intra-pair unisons occur within S1+S2, A1+A2, T1+T2 pairs
in this arrangement. All voice pairs maintain independent pitches
throughout. The Bar+B1 unison at bar 11 beat 2 is the only
intra-pair unison.

RULE COMPROMISES
══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

1. Bass I original sub-range notes raised one octave (bars 3, 7, 9, 11).
   Rule: B1 should follow the original SATB bass closely (Step 8).
   Compromise: The original bass descends to G1, A1, and B1 in
   multiple bars — all below the C2 professional range floor.
   These pitches are unsingable. All sub-C2 notes were raised one
   octave, preserving harmonic function while making the part
   performable. Voice leading between bar-to-bar is unaffected.

2. Alto II (V4) oscillates on D3–E3 for two beats (bar 9, beats 1–2).
   Rule: A2 minimum is D3; prefer stepwise motion.
   Compromise: To prevent voice crossing with Alto I (which holds
   G3 throughout bar 9), A2 is confined to D3–E3. These are the
   only available diatonic pitches below A1's G3 that remain above
   D3. The static-to-ascending line (D–E–E–D) is the best available
   solution and provides a subtle harmonic enrichment (Gsus2 → G).

3. Tenor II (V6) reaches B3 concert (written B4) at bar 11 beats 3–4.
   Rule: T2 comfortable top is C4 concert; absolute maximum E4 concert.
   Compromise: B3 is one semitone below the comfortable ceiling. This
   occurs for two beats only in the stretto convergence passage and is
   entirely within the absolute limit. No true compromise — noted for
   transparency.

4. Alto I (V3) 4–3 suspension uses two quarter notes at bar 12 while
   all other voices hold half notes.
   Rule: All voices should have the same duration in the final bar
   (2-beat half note).
   Compromise: A1 receives F quarter + E quarter (total = 2 beats,
   same duration as others' half notes) to execute the suspension.
   This is standard choral engraving practice; the total bar duration
   is correct and the suspension resolves correctly. Not a true
   compromise — noted for completeness.
