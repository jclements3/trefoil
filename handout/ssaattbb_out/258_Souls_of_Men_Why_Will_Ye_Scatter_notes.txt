TEXTURE PLAN
══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
The arrangement opens with the first A section (bars 1–4) in full
8-voice homophony at a moderate dynamic (mp), establishing the
chorale character. The A repeat (bars 5–8) introduces contrary
motion between the recomposed A1/A2 and T1/T2 pairs, creating
a sense of melodic independence within the homophonic texture
(quasi-polyphonic homophony). The B section (bars 9–12) expands
to full textural intensity: S1 carries the chromatic G# figure
alone while inner voices diverge into stepwise contrary motion,
creating an antiphonal SSAA vs. TTBB effect on alternating beats;
the climax falls on bar 11 beat 3 (d = D5 in S1, with B rising
in S2 and A1 A2 expanding outward). The final A (bars 13–16)
returns to chorale homophony at full dynamic (f), closing with
all 8 voices converging to the tonic. Texture types used:
(1) Full 8-voice homophony (all A sections and final A),
(2) Contrary-motion inner voices providing polyphonic interest
    within homophony (A repeat and B section inner pairs),
(3) Antiphonal colouring in the B section (SSAA choir carries
    chromatic S1 colouring against diatonic TTBB sustain),
(4) Stepwise independent melodic lines in all inner voices
    throughout (A1 A2 T1 T2 each have genuine melodic shape).

RULE COMPROMISES
══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
1. Bar 3, T1/T2, beat 2 (and repeated in equivalent bars):
   T1 and T2 share a unison on F# (written F) on a weak beat.
   This was accepted because (a) both voices move away by contrary
   step immediately, (b) the F# functions as a passing note through
   which both tenor lines happen to pass simultaneously, and (c)
   the brief unison actually strengthens the diatonic harmony on
   that beat (a V7 passing tone). Per the instructions, a unison on
   a weak beat is acceptable when both voices resolve by step.

2. Bar 2, S2, beat 1–2: S2 sings F#4 E4 F#4 while S1 sings A4 G4 A4.
   The interval S1–S2 is consistently a third (A–F#, G–E, A–F#),
   which is exactly the preferred interval. No compromise needed;
   noted for reference.

3. Inner voice recomposition (bars 1–16, A1 A2 T1 T2):
   Because the original SATB source has only two inner voices (alto
   and tenor) and they contain diatonic violations, the A1 A2 T1 T2
   voices were substantially recomposed rather than mechanically
   split from the originals. The recomposed lines were designed to
   imply the original harmonies while maintaining smooth stepwise
   motion, contrary motion between pairs, and strict diatonic
   constraint. This is a deliberate compositional choice, not a
   voice-leading rule compromise.

4. Parallel motion check — bars 1/5/13: S1 and S2 move in parallel
   thirds throughout the A section (by design of the splitting
   strategy). Parallel thirds are not a voice-leading violation
   (only parallel fifths and octaves are forbidden). No compromise.

5. Bar 12, B1: F2 on beat 1. This is the lowest note in the
   arrangement and falls at a cadence point (end of B section),
   not a climax. It is within professional range but slightly below
   the "comfortable" F3 guideline for B1 (range C2–A3, comfortable
   top F3 — F2 is at the comfortable low end of C2–A3). The
   original SATB bass has F,, = F2 at this point, and it was
   retained as written to preserve the harmonic bass function.
   No concern.
