HARMONIC SUBSTITUTIONS
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
The original harmony is largely diatonic in G major. Chromatic notes
appear as secondary dominants or raised leading tones in inner voices;
these are addressed under "Diatonic Violations" below. The underlying
harmonic progression was preserved in all bars — no chord substitutions
were required because the chromatic color was entirely in the melody or
inner voice ornaments, not in the structural bass.

Bar 9 (bridge pickup): Original tenor had ^G, (G#2) implying a V/vi
(D major functioning as dominant of E minor). Replaced with G natural,
keeping the sonority as a plain Em chord. The bass A,, supports this
as a vi–iv progression rather than a secondary dominant.

TEXTURE PLAN
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
The arrangement opens with SSAA choir alone on verse 1 (bars 1–4),
with TTBB entering softly on an open G–D–B pedal tone, creating an
intimate, slightly antique sound. At the verse repeat (bars 5–8) all
eight voices engage in a full homophonic texture with the SSAA carrying
the melody and TTBB providing rhythmically coordinated harmonic support.
The bridge (bars 9–12) is antiphonal: SSAA delivers the ascending
sequential phrases while TTBB echoes with parallel motion a sixth below,
before both choirs unite for the refrain. The refrain (bars 13–end)
builds through full 8-voice homophony to a climax on bars 17–18 where
S1 reaches e' (E5) over a spread voicing extending down to B1 on E2,
achieving maximum ensemble spread. The final three bars use a stretto-
like descent in all voices to a unison cadence on E (tonic minor) with
B1 settling to E2. Texture types used: SSAA/TTBB divided choir (verses
1-2), antiphonal exchange (bridge), full 8-voice homophony (refrain),
and climactic spread voicing at bars 17-18.

RULE COMPROMISES
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
1. TTBB verse-1 pedal (bars 1–4): T1 and T2 hold sustained pitches
   (d and B, written = D3 and B2 sounding) for the full four bars.
   This sacrifices melodic independence for T1 and T2 in the opening
   in order to fulfil the instruction to open with reduced texture
   (4–6 voices — SSAA carries melody, TTBB provides harmonic foundation
   without competing melodic content). Both voices have independent
   pitches (fifth apart) and are not mere filler — the open fifth creates
   a deliberate archaic/modal color. Full melodic independence for T1/T2
   is established at bar 5 onward.

2. Bar 9 (bridge), A2: The line descends to A,2 (A3) which is the lowest
   note for A2 in this arrangement (range D3–B4). The descent is in
   stepwise motion and resolves upward on the next phrase. No concern.

3. Bar 11–12, T2: Sustained repeated-pitch writing (E E F G in bridge)
   was chosen for harmonic clarity during the antiphonal section where
   TTBB provides the harmonic bed beneath the melody. The line is
   singable and has directional shape despite limited intervallic variety.

4. S2 in verse 1 begins on C4 (a third below S1's E4) per the
   arrangement instruction "harmonizes a third below S1." The interval
   opens to a sixth (C4 vs G4) in bar 3, providing line variety. No
   voice crossing occurs at any point.

5. Parallel motion between S1 and S2 in several bars is intentional
   per the "harmonize a third below" instruction. To compensate, A1
   and T1 move in contrary or oblique motion at those same points,
   satisfying the overall voice leading requirement at the ensemble level.
