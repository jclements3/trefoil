HARMONIC SUBSTITUTIONS
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
The original SATB harmonization is largely diatonic in D major.
The chromatic elements are:

Measures 1, 5, 9, 13 — S1 melody uses =c (C natural, bar 7 scale
  degree): this is the modal flat-7, characteristic of Greensleeves'
  Dorian/Mixolydian color. It appears only in V1 (Soprano I), which is
  unrestricted. No harmonic substitution required; the harp simply
  sustains its diatonic D-major strings while S1 sings C natural,
  creating the intentional coloristic friction of lever harp writing.

Measures 3, 7, 11, 15 — original S2V1 (tenor) and S2V2 (bass) imply
  an E-dominant chord (E major, with G#) as a secondary dominant of
  Am. The bass had =C (C natural) implying a C major chord or an
  E7/C bass. The original S1V2 (alto) had ^D (D#) here, implying a
  German augmented-6th resolving to A major.

  Substitution: Replace the augmented-6th / secondary dominant
  with a diatonic E minor chord (Em = E-G-B), which is scale degree ii
  in D major and functions well as a pre-dominant moving to A.
  - Original chord: E major / Aug6 (E–G#–B, ^D in alto)
  - Replacement:    E minor (E–G–B) diatonic substitute
  - Reason: G# and D# are non-diatonic; Em preserves the dominant
    preparation function and the modal character of Greensleeves

Measure 4, 8, 12 — half cadences: original had B1 on B,, (B1 below
  the staff). Raised to D, (D2) in B1 for range compliance, giving
  Bm/D first inversion. Baritone takes B, (B2) for root-position feel
  in the upper bass.

DIATONIC VIOLATIONS CAUGHT
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
All inner voices (V2–V8) in the original SATB had the following
chromatic notes that were replaced:

1. Original S1V2 (now split into S2): ^D (D#) at bars 4, 8, 12, 16
   - Replacement: D (D natural) then B, or E (nearest diatonic neighbor)
   - Context: the raised 4th was part of an Aug6 chord resolving to A
   - Diatonic substitute: S2 stays on D or moves to E, implying Em→A

2. Original S2V2 (now B1): =C (C natural) at bars 3, 7, 11, 15
   - Replacement: B, (B2) — nearest diatonic neighbor below
   - Context: C natural was the bass of a passing C-major chord
   - Diatonic substitute: B (scale degree 6) under an Em harmony;
     the Em bass note B, also resolves naturally to A

3. Original S2V2 (now B1): B,, (B1) at bars 4, 8, 12 (half cadences)
   - Replacement: D, (D2) — raised an octave plus a third
   - Reason: B1 (concert) is below the stated B1 voice minimum (C2);
     D2 provides a Bm/D first-inversion bass, preserving Bm harmony

4. All inner voice D# (^D) ornaments: replaced with E or D throughout
   (see substitution notes above). No augmented melodic intervals
   were introduced by any of these substitutions.

TEXTURE PLAN
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
The arrangement opens with antiphonal texture: SSAA leads the first
phrase with TTBB providing quiet harmonic support, establishing the
modal-D color of Greensleeves before the full ensemble locks together
at phrase 2. Phrase 2 builds to an 8-voice homophonic cadence at m.8
with a 4-3 suspension in S2 and A1 voices. Phrase 3 shifts to
call-and-response between S1 (carrying the melody) and B1 (mirroring
the bass line), while the inner six voices provide a sustained harmonic
cushion, reducing texture to a chamber feel. Phrase 4 returns to full
8-voice texture for the climax (S1 reaching D5, the soprano high point),
with imitative stretto between SSAA and TTBB at m.15, and a final
4-3 suspension resolving to E major at the concluding cadence.

Texture types used:
  1. Antiphonal SSAA vs TTBB (phrase 1)
  2. Full 8-voice homophony (phrases 2 and 4)
  3. Call-and-response S1/B1 with inner-voice sustain (phrase 3)
  4. Stretto entries at final cadence (m.15-16)
  5. Suspension writing at all cadence points (mm. 4, 8, 12, 16)

UNISON LOG
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
Phrase 1, m.4 (half cadence):
  S1 + S2 share F#4 on beat 1 (strong beat), both on D3/2 approach
  to the Bm cadence. Purpose: emphasis and blend at the half cadence.
  Both voices move away by step (S1 to B3, S2 to B3) immediately.

Phrase 1, m.4 (beat 3):
  S1 + S2 share B3. Purpose: cadential unison on the final syllable,
  both voices arriving at the tonic of Bm. Effective at phrase ending.

Phrase 2, m.8 (final bar):
  S1 + S2 share E4 (E3/2 E3/2). Purpose: unison at the perfect
  authentic cadence in E minor (the secondary tonal center). This
  strong-beat unison is the structural goal of phrase 2; acceptable
  as it is deliberate and at a structural arrival.
  T1 + T2 also share A2 concert (A,3/2 A,3/2 written). Same
  cadential purpose.

Phrase 3, m.12 (pickup to phrase 4):
  S1 + S2 share D4 on the pickup beat (weak). Both voices immediately
  diverge (S1 rises to d/D5, S2 takes F#4). Acceptable per rule: weak
  beat unison where both voices move away by step immediately after.

Phrase 4, m.16 (final bar):
  S1 + S2 share E4 (E3/2 E). Final unison at the close; deliberate.
  A1 + A2 share A3/C4 (approximately — see bar). Bar + B1 share
  the E2 final. All final-bar unisons are structural and intentional.

RULE COMPROMISES
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
1. B1 voice: D2 (D,) used at bars 4, 8, 12 for the Bm half cadence.
   Stated minimum is C2; D2 is 2 semitones below. Reason: the Bm
   tonal center requires a low bass note; D, (D2) is the nearest
   diatonic note within range that preserves the Bm/D first-inversion
   harmony. A professional bass singer on a Bm piece can comfortably
   reach D2 in a supported choral context.

2. A2 voice: E3 (E,) used at the phrase 1 opening pickup. This is
   one step above the stated D3 minimum; no actual violation occurs.
   Noted for completeness: the low register should be approached
   gradually and singers should use chest support.

3. Parallel motion at m.7-8 (Bar and B1): both voices move in
   similar motion from A2/B2 to C3/E2. The interval widens from
   a third to a tenth, so this is not a parallel fifth or octave.
   Noted as a near-parallel that should be monitored in rehearsal.

4. Phrase 2 final bar (m.8): S1 and S2 both hold E4 (E3/2 E3/2),
   creating a prolonged unison across the bar. This is structurally
   motivated (the Esus cadence) and is the most prominent use of
   unison in the arrangement. Used once, at the structural midpoint.

5. The Greensleeves Dorian modal color (=c, C natural in S1) creates
   an explicit cross-relation with the harp's diatonic C# string.
   This is intentional and characteristic of lever harp writing,
   as specified in the prompt. The harp player must NOT re-tune for
   this note; the dissonance is the intended color.
