ARRANGER NOTES
033 — Far, Far Away On Judea's Plains
John M. MacFarlane, 1869 / SSAATTBB arr. for 8-voice choir and lever harp
===============================================================================

TRANSPOSITION
─────────────
Original key:   F major (1 flat: Bb)
Chosen key:     F major (1 flat: Bb)
Semitones shifted: 0 (no transposition required)
Reason: F major is fully valid for the lever harp in Eb storage tuning.
  Lever positions required: raise the Bb lever only (one lever).
  All other levers remain down (Eb storage). The harp plays F G A Bb C D E
  with no mid-piece lever changes.
  Nearest invalid keys in either direction are Ab (3 semitones down, too
  many flats) and B (6 semitones up, too many sharps) — neither was needed.


HARMONIC SUBSTITUTIONS
──────────────────────
No chromatic inner-voice harmonies requiring reharmonization were present in
the original SATB. The hymn is harmonically conservative and stays within
F major throughout. No secondary dominants or tonicizations appear in any
inner voice. No substitutions were necessary.


DIATONIC VIOLATIONS CAUGHT
──────────────────────────
No accidentals appear in the original SATB inner voices (S1V2 alto and S2V1
tenor). All original pitches are diatonic to F major. The new inner voices
A2, T2, and Baritone were composed entirely diatonically. No replacements
were needed.

Note: S1 (Soprano I) sings no accidentals in this hymn, so the "S1 free
chromatic" allowance was not exercised. All 8 voices are diatonic to F major
throughout.


TEXTURE PLAN
────────────
The arrangement opens with an SSAA trio (S1, S2, A1) for measure 1, growing
to an SSAA quartet by measure 2 as Alto II enters. This reduced opening (3–4
voices) establishes the melody before the full ensemble fills in. Tenor I and
Tenor II enter at measure 5, building to 6 voices with antiphonal contrast
between upper (SSAA) and lower (TTBB) choirs in measures 7–8. Measures 9–10
bring all 8 voices together in full homophony with staccato block chords,
driving toward the climax. Measures 11–12 introduce stretto: all voice pairs
echo the dotted-quarter figure in staggered entries (S1/T1 in canon at the
octave, S2/T2 harmonizing a third below each, A1/A2 in contrary stretto
motion), with the bass running a chromatic-adjacent scale below while the
Baritone ascends and then descends to a half-close. The final chord settles
on F major with B1 on the root (F2) in low register.

Texture types used:
  1. Solo-line trio (M1) — S1 S2 A1, TTBB silent
  2. Full SSAA quartet (M2–M4) — TTBB still silent
  3. Antiphonal SSAA vs TTBB (M7–M8) — both choirs present but traded
  4. Full 8-voice homophony (M9–M10) — staccato block-chord texture
  5. Stretto imitation between soprano and tenor pairs (M11–M12)


UNISON LOG
──────────
M7, T1+T2 — beat 4 approach: T1 written G4/F4, T2 written D4/E4; not unison.
M8, Bar+B1 — beats 3–4: both settle on C3 (unison). Strong half-beat
  cadential unison; both voices move away by step from the preceding A3/G3
  and G3/F3 figures. Justified as pair grounding at the half-cadence.
M10, Bar+B1 — beat 4: Bar arrives on F3 half-note, B1 on F3 half-note
  (unison into cadence). Strong-beat cadential unison, immediately concluded
  by the phrase break. Acceptable.
M12, S2+T2 — same pitch class D throughout final bar (D4 written S2, D3
  sounding T2). Cross-choir octave, not a within-pair unison; permitted
  freely.
No consecutive within-pair unisons on consecutive beats occur.


RANGE LOG
─────────
M10, A1 (V:3): reaches c (C5) — top of the A1 comfortable range (max D5).
  This falls at the climax approach (M10 precedes the final section) and is
  intentional; the A1 is at its peak here.
M10, T1 (V:5): written c (C5), sounding C4 — within comfortable T1 range
  (max G4 sounding). No concern.
M11, S1 (V:1): reaches c (C5) repeated — within S1 comfortable range (A5
  is the extreme; C5 is moderate). No concern.
M12, B1 (V:8): reaches F,, (F2) — within B1 range (C2–A3; F2 is one step
  above the minimum C2). This is the final root arrival, placed at climax
  descent. Intentional for resonance.
M12, Bar (V:7): descends to A,, (A2) — within Bar range (E2–C4). Low but
  comfortable; provides open bass spacing at the final cadence.
M1, A1 (V:3): opens at A, (A3) — within the A1 range (F3–D5). Deliberate
  low entry for warmth in the trio texture before the full SSAA adds weight.


RULE COMPROMISES
────────────────
1. M11: S2 and T2 carry identical written rhythm and pitch-class content
   (both A→G→A→G / F→E→D). This creates an octave doubling between S2 (A4
   sounding) and T2 (A3 sounding) in the stretto measures. This cross-choir
   octave is between non-adjacent voices (A1, A2, and T1 all intervene in the
   score order S2–A1–A2–T1–T2), so it does not violate the strict
   "adjacent-voices" parallel-octave rule. The doubling is intentional for
   the stretto climax and creates the characteristic "full choir" blend at
   the coda.

2. M7, B1: the original SATB bass line ends beat 4 on Bb3/A3 (B,/A,/). In
   this arrangement the Bb3 was lowered to G3 (G,/) to avoid crossing with
   the Baritone's A3 on the same beat. The resulting B1 line G,/A,/ is
   smoother and stays within range; the Bb sonority is covered by T2 and
   A2 simultaneously.

3. M12, A2 (V:4): the final bar has A2 ending on A, (A3) half-note while A1
   ends on C4. This places A2 a minor third below A1, which is a narrower
   spacing than the preferred third/sixth. The close spacing is intentional
   at the final cadence to compact the alto sound into a tight F major triad
   (A1=C4, A2=A3, then Soprano II covers F4 and E4 above).
