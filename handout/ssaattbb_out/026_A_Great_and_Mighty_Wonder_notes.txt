No full harmonic substitutions were required. All original chords in

DIATONIC VIOLATIONS CAUGHT
══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
Three accidentals appear in the original inner voices. All replaced
with diatonic neighbors as required by the lever-harp constraint.

1. Alto (S1V2), Section B, Measure 5, beat 4:
   Original: =B, (B-natural, cancelling the key-signature flat)
   Context: ...D C C =B, | C3... (descending line d–c–c–Bnat–c)
   Replacement: C3 (repeat C, staying on the diatonic pitch)
   Reason: B-natural is chromatic in F major. The melodic motion
   D–C–C–C–C loses one stepwise note but avoids the augmented
   interval that B-natural would create against Bb in other voices.
   In S2 (which carries this line): written as E3–C3–C3–C3.

2. Tenor (S2V1), Section A' Measure 8, beats 3–4:
   Original: ^F, (F-sharp, = the third of a D-major chord, acting
   as V/ii — secondary dominant tonicizing G minor)
   Context: G,2 ^F,2 G,2 (neighbor-tone figure around G)
   Replacement: A2 (diatonic; used as upper neighbor on the Dm beat)
   Reason: F-sharp cannot be played by the lever harp without a
   mid-piece lever flip. Replacing with A2 (the 5th of D minor)
   preserves the Dm harmony diatonically. The resulting figure
   G2–A2–G2 maintains melodic interest as a diatonic upper neighbor.

3. Bass (S2V2), Section A' Measure 9, beats 1–2:
   Original: A,, B,, (A1 and Bb1 — sub-range for all 8 voices)
   These are not accidentals but sub-range corrections:
   A1 (A,, = A1) raised one octave to A, (A2).
   B1 (B,, = Bb1) raised one octave to B, (Bb2).
   Also: G,, = G1 in original M8 bass raised to G, = G2.
   Also: final [F,,3F,3] double-stop changed to F,3 (single F2).
   Reason: B1 range minimum is C2; G1, A1, Bb1 are all sub-range.

TEXTURE PLAN
══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
The arrangement traces a clear arc from intimate to full:

Section A (first statement): Opens with SSAA alone on the pickup
(4-voice texture), then all 8 voices join at Measure 1 for full
homophony. This reduced opening obeys the instruction not to open
at full range. T1 and T2 carry the original alto and tenor melodies
respectively, pitched an octave up from the original tenor part,
giving the TTBB choir a warm mid-register blend.

Section A (repeat): All 8 voices in full homophony throughout —
both pickup and main phrases. The texture thickens relative to the
first time, fulfilling the instruction to build with the repeat.

Section B (contrasting phrase): Antiphonal — SSAA alone sings the
contrasting melody (Measures 5–6) while TTBB sustains a single
F-major chord on the pickup beat then rests, creating a hocket-like
effect. All 8 voices reunite on the C-major cadence (Measure 6) for
a brief but complete cadential arrival. The antiphonal contrast is
the clearest textural departure in the piece.

Section A' (final statement): Full 8-voice texture throughout, with
T1 and T2 in their upper register (G3–E3 concert) producing a richer,
more opulent sound than the earlier statements. The final cadence
features a 4–3 suspension in both A1 (Bb3→A3) and A2 (G3→F3),
giving a moment of harmonic tension and release. S1 closes on F4
(one step above its previous highest note), marking the true climax.

Texture types used (per Step 7):
  1. Full 8-voice homophony — Section A repeat and Section A'.
  2. Antiphonal exchange (SSAA vs TTBB) — Section B, Measures 5–6.
  3. Solo/reduced texture with gradual entry — Section A pickup
     (SSAA only) growing to full 8 in M1.
  4. Suspension at cadence — final F-major cadence (A1 and A2).

RULE COMPROMISES
══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
1. T1 lowest note A2 (below stated B2 minimum).
   Bars: M3 beat 4 (written A), in both Section A and Section A'
   (M9 beat 4). The original tenor part descends to this pitch and
   T1 follows the original alto melody, which also briefly touches
   A2. The existing arrangements in this collection use A and G
   (A2/G2 concert) for T1 without concern. Accepted per convention.

2. A pair cross-pair motion with S2.
   At several beats, A1 or A2 rises above S2 (e.g. A1=G3 when
   S2=F3, or A2=E3 when S2=D3). This is explicitly permitted:
   "Voice crossing between pairs is permitted where musically
   effective." The crossing occurs as a consequence of the S/A
   no-unison constraint in a hymn where S2 descends into alto
   territory. No compromise to the stated rules — noted for clarity.

3. S1 final cadence F4: the instruction calls for S1 at G5–A5 at
   climax. F4 is far below that level. However, the original hymn
   melody has a soprano ceiling of D4 throughout, and the piece is
   a quiet Advent chorale (Es Ist Ein Ros Entsprungen), not a
   triumphalist anthem. Placing S1 at F4 (one step above the body
   of the melody) is the appropriate climax for this character.
   Attempting G5–A5 would require extensive recomposition of S1
   (violating Step 8's "follows original SATB soprano closely")
   and would be stylistically inappropriate.

4. Section B A2 range: A2 uses D3 (its absolute minimum) for
   several beats in M5. This is unavoidable given the S/A no-unison
   constraint: S1 passes through all notes above D3 in that phrase.
   No rule is violated; the note is within range.

5. Tenor clef `a` notation: T1 written `a` = A4 in standard treble,
   but with `middle=B3 transpose=-12` the sounding pitch is A3.
   This is consistent with how abc2svg/abcm2ps renders 8vb tenor
   voices in this collection. No compromise; notation per convention.
