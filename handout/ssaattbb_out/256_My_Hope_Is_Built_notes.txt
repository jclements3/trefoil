ARRANGER NOTES
==============
My Hope Is Built (The Solid Rock)
Edward Mote / William B. Bradbury
SSAATTBB expansion from Open Hymnal Project SATB source
Arrangement date: 2026

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
TRANSPOSITION
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Original key:   C major (0 accidentals)
Chosen key:     C major (0 accidentals) — no transposition required
Semitones:      0 (no change)
Direction:      N/A

Lever positions required for C major on Eb storage tuning harp:
  All levers raised: Ab→Eb→Bb→F→C→G→D
  All 7 levers engaged — fully raised to concert C major.
  This is the maximum lever position (all strings sharpened by one

No range problems arose that would have required choosing a different

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
HARMONIC SUBSTITUTIONS
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The original SATB uses several secondary-dominant tonicizations and
chromatic alterations in the inner voices. Each was replaced as
follows to maintain diatonic consistency for all non-S1 voices:

Measure 7 (original bass ^C, ... ^D,):
  Original: C#3 C3 D3 D#3 — a chromatic bass line implying V/vi
  Replacement: C3 C3 D3 D3 — the bass reverts to diatonic I-V motion
  Reason: C# and D# are not in C-major diatonic set; the parallel
  diatonic version (C D) maintains the V pedal function without
  secondary-dominant coloring. The harp cannot support C# or D#
  without lever flips. S1's F# in m7 already provides sufficient
  leading-tone color above the diatonic bass.

Measure 8 (original bass ^F,):
  Original: E3 F#3 G3 A3 — chromatic passing tone F#3
  Replacement: E3 F3 G3 A3 — diatonic stepwise bass ascent
  Reason: F# is non-diatonic; F natural preserves the ascending
  motion and outlines I-IV-V-vi without chromatic color. The
  stepwise ascent is equally effective harmonically.

Measure 12 (original bass ^F,,):
  Original: C3 F#2 G2 G2 — chromatic #IV leading into V
  Replacement: C3 F2 G2 G2 — diatonic IV root motion to V
  Reason: F#2 would be a sharp-side accidental requiring a lever
  flip. F natural (subdominant root) leading into G (dominant) is
  a fully functional ii→V or IV→V half-cadential motion and
  preserves the original harmonic intent at a diatonic level.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
DIATONIC VIOLATIONS CAUGHT (inner voices)
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The following original SATB inner-voice accidentals were replaced
with diatonic neighbors. S1 is unrestricted; these apply to S2 A1
A2 T1 T2 Bar B1 only.

Voice assignments: original S1V2 material informs S2; original S2V1
informs T1; original S2V2 informs Bar/B1 direction.

Measure 4, original alto (S1V2), beat 2: F#4 → F4
  The original alto G3/2 ^F/ G D/E/ has F# on beat 2.
  Replacement: S2 m4 uses B4 (following S1 a 3rd below) and avoids
  the F# entirely by reharmonizing through diatonic chord tones.
  The measure implies I-V motion; F natural as the 7th of a G
  dominant seventh would require lever flip — F natural used instead,
  which at that register functions as IV color against the V bass.

Measure 7, original alto (S1V2), beat 4: F#4 → G4
  S2 m7 beat 4 uses G4 (3rd below S1's B4), diatonic ✓.
  The original F# (part of a V/vi tonicization) is replaced by G4,
  which continues the V-of-I dominant function without the raised
  leading tone to the relative minor.

Measure 8, original alto (S1V2), beats 2 and 4: D#4 → D4; F#4 → F4
  S2 m8: beat 2 D4 (diatonic, below S1 F#4), beat 4 F4 (diatonic).
  D# (raised 2nd in A minor tonicization) replaced by D natural;
  F# (raised 4th) replaced by F natural. Both are common tones in
  C major and preserve the inner-voice melodic line's character.

Measure 9, original alto (S1V2), beat 2: D#4 → D4
  S2 m9 beat 2: D4 diatonic. Same reasoning as m8 beat 2.

Measure 10, original alto (S1V2), beats 1-2: Bb3 → B3
  The original alto has _B, (Bb3) — a flat-6 inflection suggesting
  Aeolian or modal mixture on the I chord. In C major diatonic,
  B natural is the major 7th. Replacement: B3 (natural).
  This shifts the color from modal to diatonic major, which is
  appropriate for the harp-supported texture.

Measure 7, original bass (S2V2), beats 1 and 4: C#3 → C3; D#3 → D3
  See Harmonic Substitutions above.

Measure 8, original bass (S2V2), beat 2: F#3 → F3
  See Harmonic Substitutions above.

Measure 12, original bass (S2V2), beat 2: F#2 → F2

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
TEXTURE PLAN
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The arrangement opens with a 6-voice reduced texture on the pickup
(S1, S2, A1, T1, Bar, B1; A2 and T2 tacet), creating a warm but
transparent entrance before all 8 voices arrive together on measure
2. Measures 2–4 are full 8-voice homophony outlining the first
phrase, with the soprano climbing to C5 at m4 for the first phrase
peak. Measures 5–6 introduce antiphonal contrast: the TTBB pair
carries the harmonic foundation with slightly more independence while
SSAA completes the chord tones above. Measure 8 functions as a
call-and-response between the outer voices (S1 and the bass pair)
while inner voices sustain harmonic support through the chromatic
area. Measures 10–12 build steadily through ascending inner-voice
lines to a stretto-flavored approach to the final cadence. Measure
13 resolves in full 8-voice I-chord homophony with B1 on C3 (root),
Bar on E3 (3rd), T1/T2 on E3/C3 (concert), and S1+S2 meeting on a
cadential unison C4. Texture types employed: (1) full 8-voice
homophony, (2) reduced opening texture, (3) antiphonal SSAA/TTBB
exchange, (4) S1+B1 call/response with sustained inner voices, and
(5) stretto-flavored ascending lines at the cadential approach.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
RULE COMPROMISES
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
1. m3 beat 1, S1/T1 concert in contrary octave approach:
   S1=A4, T1 concert=A3 — an octave at the downbeat of m3,
   approached by contrary motion (S1 from G4 below, T1 from B3
   above). Strict Palestrina style forbids even contrary-motion
   octaves between outer voices at strong beats; however, in
   8-part choral writing of the hymn tradition this is standard
   practice. The contrary motion and the full 6-voice harmonic
   content around them mitigate any hollow effect. No concern for
   professional ensemble.

2. m6 Bar/T2 approach:
   T2 (E3 concert) and Bar (E3) hold in cross-choir unison for the
   full measure. This was a deliberate choice to create a pedal
   drone effect at the V7→I resolution, contrasting with the moving
   soprano line. Cross-choir unisons are explicitly permitted by
   the arranging rules.

3. m9 B1 octave substitution (B2 for original B3):
   Original bass had B3 on beat 1 of m9, which exceeds B1's maximum
   A3. The substitution to B2 creates a bass leap of a 7th (A3→B2)
   from m8 beat 4 to m9 beat 1. This leap exceeds the preferred
   stepwise motion guideline but is resolved immediately by ascending
   stepwise motion (B2→E3). The downward 7th is acceptable in bass
   voices at cadential arrivals and is preferable to placing B1
   out of range.

4. m4 T1 beat 1, contrary motion into P8 with B1:
   T1 concert E4 / S1 C5 = M6 ✓ (fixed). However T1 concert E4
   and A1 E4 are now in octave unison across choirs (A1=E4 treble
   = E4 concert; T1 concert E4). This cross-choir octave doubling
   at the phrase climax is intentional for fullness and falls at
   the strongest moment of phrase 1. Cross-choir octave doublings
   are not prohibited by the arranging rules.
