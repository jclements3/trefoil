Phrase 2, bar 1 (original hymn bar 6), beat 3:
  Original chord: F# major (F#–A#–C#) implied by tenor ^A, (A#) — a
    secondary dominant of B minor (V/ii).
  Replacement: D major sonority (D–F#–A). Inner voices move to A
    (diatonic) instead of A#. The moment retains its energy as a
    passing harmony between the dominant pedal and the ensuing D chord.
  Reason: A# is non-diatonic in D major; the harp cannot provide it.
    B minor (diatonic ii chord) or plain D major are the nearest
    diatonic substitutes; D major was chosen to preserve brightness.

    dominant-seventh flavor (A–C#–E–G) rather than the chromatic A

TEXTURE PLAN
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Phrase 1 opens with the SSAA quartet alone (4 voices), with the baritone
and bass entering quietly at bar 3 to anchor the tonic cadence — a
total of 6 voices by the phrase close. Phrase 2 brings all 8 voices in
full homophonic block-chord texture, giving the second couplet of text
its affirmative weight. Phrase 3 employs antiphonal writing (SSAA choir
in bars 10–11 answered by TTBB entering at the midpoint), converging
to a climactic full-8-voice moment on the dotted-rhythm bar (bar 12)
where S1 reaches D5 and B1 descends to D2 for maximum ensemble spread.
Phrase 4 returns to full 8-voice homophony with a quasi-stretto effect:
T1 and T2 reiterate the soprano's stepwise descent in the bar before the
final cadence, and the final D major chord distributes root and fifth
across 6 of the 8 voices for a resonant close.

Texture types used:
  1. Full 8-voice homophony (phrases 2, 4, and the climax bar of phrase 3)
  2. SSAA choir with bass doubling entering mid-phrase (phrase 1)
  3. Antiphonal exchange between SSAA and TTBB (phrase 3, bars 10–11)
  4. Quasi-stretto between soprano and tenor at the final cadence

RULE COMPROMISES
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────
1. A2 passing below minimum range (bar 2, beat 4):
   Bar number: phrase 1, bar 2 (hymn bar 2), beat 4
   Voice: A2 (V:4)
   Note: C, (C3) — 1 semitone below A2 minimum D3
   Reason: the descending scale line A,–G,–F,–E,–D,–C, in A2 provides
   genuine melodic interest and contrary motion against the ascending A1
   line. The single C3 passing tone on a weak fourth beat is inaudible as
   a range violation in context and resolves immediately upward at the
   start of bar 3. This is the only range compromise in the arrangement.
   Alternative: replace C, with D, (D3, minimum) and break the smooth
   scale descent — the current solution is preferred for melodic integrity.

2. Parallel thirds T1+T2 in phrase 2:
   Bar numbers: phrase 2, bars 1–4 (hymn bars 6–9)
   Voices: T1 (V:5) and T2 (V:6)
   Issue: T1 and T2 move predominantly in parallel thirds throughout
   phrase 2, producing a "doubled thirds" texture rather than independent
   lines. This is a deliberate textural choice to give the TTBB choir a
   warm, unified tone in its first full entry. Phrase 3 restores
   independence with the antiphonal and climbing-scale treatment.

3. Bar voice (V:7) close to bottom of range at phrase 4 cadence:
   Bar: A,, (A2) at final cadence. This is above the E2 minimum but
   below the comfortable top range typically associated with a closing
   gesture. The choice creates an open fifth (A2 + D2 in B1) in the
   bass register, supporting a resonant D major close. Acceptable.
