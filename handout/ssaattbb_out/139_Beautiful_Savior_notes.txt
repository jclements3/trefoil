ARRANGER NOTES — Beautiful Savior (Fairest Lord Jesus)
SSAATTBB Arrangement for 8-voice Ensemble and Lever Harp
======================================================================

SOURCE: Open Hymnal Project, 139_Beautiful_Savior.abc
        SATB original in Eb major, 4/4, 16 bars


======================================================================
TRANSPOSITION
======================================================================

Original key:  Eb major (3 flats)
Chosen key:    Eb major (3 flats) — NO transposition required
Semitones:     0

Eb major is a valid lever harp key (within the 3-flat maximum for
Eb storage tuning). All diatonic strings are available without
mid-piece lever flips.

Lever positions required (Eb storage tuning, all levers down = Eb):
  - All levers remain DOWN (at rest position)
  - The 7 diatonic notes Eb F G Ab Bb C D are all open strings
  - No lever changes are required during performance

The soprano (S1) sings freely in Eb major; all accidentals in S1
are confined to diatonic pitches in this key, so no conflict with
the harp arises.


======================================================================
HARMONIC SUBSTITUTIONS
======================================================================

Bar 3, beat 3-4:
  Original SATB alto: =E (E-natural) on beat 4
  This implies a momentary C major or G major harmony (raised
  leading tone to a tonicization of F or C).
  Replacement harmony: Cm (C Eb G) for the full bar, with the
  alto remaining on Eb (=E -> E in ABC Eb key notation).
  Reason: The lever harp cannot play E-natural without a lever flip.
  The Cm color is a diatonic vi of Eb and provides the same
  subdominant color without any chromatic alteration.
  Inner voices adjusted: alto held Eb throughout (no E-natural);
  A2 given a passing tone Ab3 on beat 4 for melodic interest within
  the diatonic framework.

No other chromatic harmonies were present in the original inner
voices. The original SATB source is remarkably diatonic in Eb,
with the single =E exception noted above.

======================================================================
TEXTURE PLAN
======================================================================

The arrangement opens with the SSAA choir alone (bars 1-4), pp,
presenting the hymn melody and alto harmonies in a transparent
four-voice texture that draws the listener into the familiar tune.
The full 8-voice texture enters at bar 5 ("Beautiful Savior") in
homophony, with TTBB doubling SSAA at the octave below for maximum
harmonic weight on the title phrase. Bars 6-10 introduce antiphonal
variety: T1 and T2 diverge from exact octave doubling to follow
independent diatonic melodic lines (bar 7 features T1/T2 ascending
in contrary motion to the descending soprano); A1 uses ascending
suspension figures in bar 7, and T1 pedals in bar 6 while the
upper voices descend. Bar 8 is a full-choir dominant pedal with
all voices holding their chord tones. Bar 13 introduces a canonic
effect: SSAA present the tonic chord in block fashion while T1 and
T2 echo with a stepwise three-beat descent (Bb-Ab-G / G-F-Eb) in
parallel thirds, imitating a fragment of the soprano line an octave
and a fifth lower. The final cadence (bar 16) spreads from B1 on
Eb2 to S1 on Eb4, a two-octave spread with all 8 voices on members
of the tonic Eb chord.

Texture types used:
  1. Reduced 4-voice opening (bars 1-4): SSAA only, homophonic
  2. Full 8-voice homophony (bar 5, bars 11-12, bar 14): block chords
  3. Antiphonal texture with independent inner lines (bars 6-10):
     T1 and T2 diverge for melodic independence, contrary motion
  4. Canonic imitation between voice pairs (bar 13):
     SSAA holds tonic; T1/T2 echo with descending imitation
  5. Stretto-style compression and suspension at final cadence
     (bars 15-16): all voices converge to the final Eb chord from
     different directions, with the bass reaching Eb2 for maximum
     tonal grounding

  and the final chord. G2 is within the comfortable range (E2–C4)

  near-minimum of the B1 range (C2–A3). Eb2 is above C2 and is

======================================================================
RULE COMPROMISES
======================================================================

1. Bar 6, T1/T2 vs A2 (parallel 5ths mitigated):
   When A2 descends C4->Bb3->G3 while T1 holds G3, the interval
   between A2 and T1 shifts from a 4th to a 3rd to a unison. T1
   was given a pedal (held G3) rather than descending in parallel
   to avoid parallel 5ths that arose when T2 also descended with
   A1. The T1 pedal creates oblique motion against A2, which the
   guidelines accept. No parallel 5ths remain in bar 6.

2. Bar 14, S1 climax (relaxed from G5-A5):
   The instruction recommends S1 at G5-A5 at the climax. However,
   the original melody's highest note is Bb4 (bar 5), and the
   structural climax at bar 14 (B3 A in the original) only reaches
   Bb4 (the dotted-half phrase "Beautiful"). Ornamental extension
   of S1 to G5 was considered but rejected because the syllabic
   setting of a strophic hymn does not naturally accommodate an
   upper-octave flourish without disrupting the congregation's
   familiarity with the tune. The climax is achieved instead
   through full 8-voice texture and the wide bass register (B1 at
   G2) rather than extreme soprano extension.

3. Bar 13, Bar and B1 identical:
   Bar 13 has Bar (G, F, E,) = G3 F3 Eb3 descending, and B1
   (E, D, C,) = Eb3 D3 C3 descending a step below. Both descend
   in parallel 3rds. This brief parallel 3rd motion between Bar
   and B1 is a relaxation of the "no parallel 5ths or octaves"
   rule (which does not explicitly forbid parallel 3rds), but it
   does reduce the independence of the two bass voices. It is
   acceptable here because the passage is short (3 beats) and the
   parallel 3rds create a natural gravitas in the bass.

4. Bar 8, T2 written D2 (concert D3):
   T2 holds D3 (concert) for the full dominant pedal bar. This
   creates a static line for T2 but is intentional: the whole bar
   is a dominant pedal, and all voices are sustained. Having T2
   hold the chord 3rd (D, the major 3rd of Bb major) on a whole
   note is structurally sound and provides rhythmic relief.

5. Bars 11, T1/T2 both sustain (limited melodic independence):
   At bar 11, T1 holds Bb3 and T2 holds G3 for the full bar while
   the upper voices (S1/A1) carry the melodic interest. This brief
   suspension of melodic independence in T1/T2 is an intentional
   contrast device: the TTBB choir rests on a long note while SSAA
   drive the rhythmic phrase. The effect is similar to the original
   tenor sustain in the SATB.
