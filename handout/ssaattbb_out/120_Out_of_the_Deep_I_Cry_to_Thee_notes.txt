HARMONIC SUBSTITUTIONS
──────────────────────
Phrase 2, Bar 10 of original (S1V1 line "A F !fermata!E F"):
  Original harmony: A minor moving to E major (V of A minor) implied
  by alto ^C4 ^C4 and bass ^C3 D3 A2 A2.
  This creates a secondary dominant tonicization (V/vi in C major).
  Substitution: The beat with ^C (raised leading tone to D) is
  replaced by the diatonic substitute ii chord (D minor) — inner
  voices stay on C/D/E/A rather than raised C#. The E in S1 is
  retained as the melody note (S1 may sing any note freely).
  Replacement chord: D minor / A minor complex instead of E major.

Phrase 2, Bar 10 of original (bass ^C3, alto ^C4):
  Original note: C# (raised 7th, secondary leading tone)
  Replacement: C natural in all inner voices (diatonic neighbor down
  from C#). This preserves stepwise motion while eliminating the
  accidental from harp-doubled voices.

Phrase 3, Bar 12 of original (tenor ^G3):
  Original: G# in tenor (chromatic passing tone between G and A,
  implying an E major / A minor tonicization in the cadential phrase).
  Substitution: G natural (diatonic neighbor down) in T1 and T2.
  The ascending stepwise motion G→A in the tenor is preserved.
  Replacement chord: The implied V/vi is relaxed to a plain vi or I6
  reading, consistent with the surrounding C major diatonic field.

  Original: D# in bass (chromatic passing tone)

TEXTURE PLAN
────────────
The arrangement opens phrase 1 (first time) in a reduced antiphonal
texture: SSAA voices carry the melody while TTBB provide a quiet
harmonic foundation, creating a sense of emerging from depths (6
functional voices, TTBB at reduced dynamic). The phrase 1 repeat
brings all 8 voices together in full homophony, building intensity
through doubled harmonic mass. Phrase 2 (new melodic material on "A c
B G") shifts to call-and-response: S1 declaims the rising line while
the inner voices (A1, A2, T1, T2) answer in contrary motion pairs,
and the bass pair (Bar, B1) provides a stepwise walking bass. The
final phrase 3 employs stretto entries across the voice pairs at the
penultimate bar, converging on a tiered cadence where S1 reaches D5
(a bright climax note), the tenor pair sustains the dominant pedal,
and B1 grounds on G2 then C3 at the final cadence. Texture types
used: (1) antiphonal SSAA vs. TTBB, (2) full 8-voice homophony,
(3) call-and-response between S1 and inner voices, (4) stretto at
the final cadence.

RULE COMPROMISES
────────────────
Phrase 2, Bar 11 (3-beat bar before phrase 3 pickup):
  V:2 (S2) ends on F4, V:3 (A1) ends on E4. The interval is a
  minor second (E–F). This close voicing was accepted to preserve
  stepwise motion in both voices as they approach the phrase 3 entry.
  A1 moves E4→D4 in phrase 3, resolving the dissonance immediately.
  Rule relaxed: prefer thirds/sixths between paired voices (Step 6,
  rule 5). The resolution in the next beat justifies the passing clash.

Phrase 1, Bar 2 (fermata bar): V:5 (T1) written G and V:6 (T2)
  written E at the fermata beat — these produce sounds G3 and E3.
  Bar (V:7) simultaneously sounds G3. This creates a T1/Bar unison
  at G3 (cross-choir). Per the rules, cross-choir unisons are freely
  permitted and do not count against either choir's limit. Noted for
  transparency.

Voice leading between Bar (V:7) and B1 (V:8) in phrase 2 bar 2:
  Bar moves A2→G2→A2, B1 moves C3→D3→A2→A2. At beat 3 of that bar,
  Bar=G2 and B1=A2 — B1 is momentarily above Bar. This constitutes
  a brief crossing within the bass pair. It was retained because both
  voices move by step and the crossing lasts only one beat, resolving
  immediately. The original harmonic texture (bass walk supporting
  the cadential E harmony) takes priority here.
  Rule relaxed: no voice crossing within a pair (Step 6, rule 2).
  Duration: 1 beat; resolved by contrary motion on beat 4.
