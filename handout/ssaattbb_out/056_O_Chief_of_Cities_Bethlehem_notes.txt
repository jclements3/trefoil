  Beat 3 of bar 9 (third beat of "(B,, A,,) ^G,," in the source)
  Original note: G#2 (written ^G,,) — chromatic passing tone ascending

TEXTURE PLAN
------------
The arrangement uses three distinct textural zones across its 16 bars
(pickup + 5 + 5 + 6):

1. PHRASE 1 (pickup through bar 5) — UPPER CHOIR ONLY (SSAA, 4 voices):
   The TTBB choir is tacet. The upper choir presents the hymn tune and its
   harmonization in an open, transparent sound. This allows the melody to
   be heard clearly and reserves the full ensemble for the climax.
   Texture type: reduced/antiphonal (SSAA alone vs. anticipated TTBB entry).

2. PHRASE 2 (bars 6–10) — FULL 8 VOICES, homophonic:
   The TTBB choir enters at bar 6 as the melody climbs to its peak (D5 in
   S1 at bars 7 and 9). All voices move in coordinated rhythm with
   independent inner-voice motion in A1/A2/T1/T2. Bar 7 and bar 9 are
   the textural and dynamic climax: S1 on D5, B1 on D3/G2.
   Texture type: full 8-voice homophony with inner independence.

3. PHRASE 3 (bars 11–16) — FULL 8 VOICES, call-and-response into cadence:
   S1 leads with a descending line while lower voices provide sustained
   harmonic support with contrary inner-voice motion. Bars 15–16 function
   as the final cadence with all voices converging on a root-position
   D major chord. Bar/B1 share a unison D3 at the final for grounded
   resolution.
   Texture types used: call-and-response (S1 melody over sustained lower
   voices), stretto-like convergence at bar 16.

Texture types employed: upper-choir antiphony, full 8-voice homophony,
call-and-response between outer voices, final cadential convergence.

RULE COMPROMISES
----------------
1. Parallel thirds sustained across barlines (bars 7–8, 8–9, S1/S2):
   S1 and S2 move in strict parallel thirds throughout the climax phrase
   (bars 6–10). This is a deliberate choice to create a seamless soprano
   duet at the high point of the piece. Traditional counterpoint prefers
   contrary motion, but for the lever harp accompaniment context and the
   hymn style, parallel thirds here reinforce blend rather than obscure
   harmonic function. The inner voices (A1, A2, T1, T2) provide
   sufficient contrary and oblique motion to offset the parallel surface.

2. A2 and T1 cross-choir unison at final cadence (bar 16, D3):
   A2 sounds D3 and T1 (concert) sounds D3 simultaneously. This is
   explicitly permitted under the cross-choir unison rule and is
   intentional for textural weight at the cadence.

3. Bar/B1 unison at final cadence (bar 16, D3):
   The Baritone and Bass I share D3 at the final. The rules allow
   Bar+B1 unisons at cadences for a grounded bass sound; this is
   the primary recommended use case.

4. T1 bottom note B2 at bars 9 and 15:
   B2 is the stated minimum of the T1 professional range. Appearing
   at the climax (bar 9) and pre-cadence (bar 15), these are musically
   justified moments. A professional tenor ensemble should handle B2
   without difficulty; it is noted here for conductor awareness.
