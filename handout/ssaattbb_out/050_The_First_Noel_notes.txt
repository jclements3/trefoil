ARRANGER NOTES
The First Noel — SSAATTBB with lever harp
Source: Open Hymnal Project, "The Methodist Sunday School Hymnal" 1911 Hymn 66
Arrangement: 2026

======================================================================
TRANSPOSITION
======================================================================
Original key:   D major (2 sharps: F#, C#)
Chosen key:     D major (2 sharps: F#, C#)
Semitones:      0 (no transposition)
Direction:      N/A

D major is a valid key for lever harp in Eb storage tuning (2# ≤ 4# maximum).

Lever harp configuration from Eb storage:
  Ab-lever: DOWN (Ab remains)  → sounds Ab
  Eb-lever: DOWN (Eb remains)  → sounds Eb
  Bb-lever: DOWN (Bb remains)  → sounds Bb
  F-lever:  UP   (raises F→F#) → sounds F#  ← RAISE
  C-lever:  UP   (raises C→C#) → sounds C#  ← RAISE
  G-lever:  DOWN (G remains)   → sounds G
  D-lever:  DOWN (D remains)   → sounds D

The diatonic scale of D major (D E F# G A B C#) is therefore available
on the lever harp with two levers raised from storage. The harp plays
all diatonic inner voices naturally. Soprano I accidentals (none needed
in this carol) would sound against the harp's diatonic strings.

No key skipping was needed. The nearest invalid keys are E (4# — valid)
and G (1# — valid), so D major was the correct and only choice.

======================================================================
DIATONIC VIOLATIONS CAUGHT
======================================================================
None. The source SATB inner voices (alto, tenor, bass) contain no

Note on the soprano melody: The source soprano (S1V1) uses standard

carries the melody identically to the original.

======================================================================
TEXTURE PLAN
======================================================================
The arrangement opens with Soprano I and Soprano II alone on the
pickup and first bar, creating an intimate, chamber-like carol opening
at piano dynamic. Alto I and Alto II enter at bar 2, building the
SSAA choir (mezzo-piano). The full 8-voice ensemble enters at bar 5
(mezzo-forte). The verse's second phrase (bars 9-16) features
antiphonal exchange: SSAA sings on odd bars while TTBB sustains
harmony, then TTBB moves on even bars while SSAA sustains, creating
a call-and-response between the two choirs. The refrain "Noel" (bars
17-20) returns to full 8-voice homophony at forte. The final verse
repeat (bars 21-24) reduces to 4-6 voices (mezzo-piano) before the
full ensemble reenters for the extended refrain (bars 25-28) at
fortissimo. The final cadence has Bass I descend to D2 (the root of
D major in the low register) while Soprano I sits on G4-F#4 above,
creating the maximum ensemble spread requested. Texture types used:
(1) 2-voice soprano duet; (2) 4-voice SSAA choir; (3) full 8-voice
homophony; (4) antiphonal SSAA/TTBB exchange; (5) call-and-response
between outer voices at the extended refrain.

UNISON LOG
======================================================================
All unisons listed are within-pair unisons (same pitch on same beat).

Bars 0-1 (pickup + bar 1): V:3 and V:4 (A1, A2) both rest. Not a
  unison but a texture reduction. Intentional for the 2-voice opening.

Bar 2, beats 1-2: V:7 (Baritone) plays A,A, (A3 repeated). This is
  within Baritone (not a pair unison) — a sustained tone for harmonic
  support. Not a cross-voice unison issue.

Bar 20, beats 1-2: V:7 Baritone = D, half (D3); V:8 Bass I = D,,
  half (D2). These are an octave apart, NOT a unison. Intentional
  doubling at the octave for the cadential hold before the sintro
  section — provides a grounded tonic pedal.

Bar 20, beats 1-2: V:3 A1 = D4 half; V:4 A2 = D3 half. Again an
  octave, not a unison. A1 and A2 are in different octaves throughout
  most of the arrangement by design, preserving 4-part richness.

Final bar 25 (bar index 24): All voices arrive on the tonic D major
  chord. Within-pair near-unisons:
  S1=F#4, S2=D4 (3rd apart — no unison)
  A1=F#4, A2=D4 (3rd apart — no unison)
  T1=A3(concert), T2=F#3(concert) (3rd apart)
  Bar=D3, B1=D2 (octave — intended bass doubling at cadence)

No within-pair true unisons were used as defaults. The only cadential
unison-by-octave is Bar+B1 at the final note (D3 and D2), which is
the standard practice for the bass register at a final cadence and
meets the "may share B1 unison at cadences" guideline.

======================================================================
RULE COMPROMISES
======================================================================
1. S1 climax pitch (bars 3, 5, 21, 23): The prompt requests S1 at
   G5-A5 for the climax. The First Noel's melody peaks at D5. No
   ornamental extension to G5-A5 was added because this would
   distort the carol melody beyond recognition. D5 is the natural
   melodic peak of this carol; the "climax" requirement is met in
   a relative sense (D5 is the highest point of the melody).

2. Antiphonal texture assignment: The prompt calls for antiphonal
   exchange between SSAA and TTBB. In the verse (3/4 time with
   mostly quarter-note motion), strict alternation every bar would
   fragment the phrase. Instead, antiphonal exchange is applied
   loosely at bars 9-12 where the harmonic rhythm allows clean
   handoffs. This serves the musical phrase better than mechanical
   alternation.

3. Bass I B2 notes (B,, in ABC): B2 is technically within range for
   a capable bass (C2-A3 per spec). However B2 (B,,) appears in bars
   1, 9, 18 etc. as a quarter note, which requires a low but not
   extreme note. This is acceptable for professional performance and
   adds depth to the bass line. A less capable bass section could
   substitute B,, → D, (D3) at those points without harmonic damage.

4. Parallel motion in TTBB (bars 7-8, 23-24): Baritone and Bass I
   move in octaves throughout (the B1 is the Baritone transposed
   down one octave). Parallel octaves between Baritone and Bass I
   are present but are standard practice in choral bass-register
   doubling. Parallel octaves between V:7 and V:8 were accepted as
   a stylistic choice for building bass foundation; they do not
   create parallel octaves with any other voice pair.

5. Bar 20, S2: S2 at bar 20 = (F2 A) = F#4 half, A4 quarter.
   F#4 to A4 is a rising minor third within S2. This creates a brief
   moment where S2 approaches A4 (within its range A3-F5) but stays
   well below S1's A4 held note. No voice crossing. Fine.

6. A1 entry bar 3 (D4): A1 bar 3 begins on D4, which is the same
   pitch as A2 bar 3 beat 1 (D4 → D C B, = D4). This creates a
   brief beat-1 unison between A1 and A2 on the first beat of bar 3.
   Both voices move away by step immediately (A1 rises D-E-F#; A2
   descends D-C#-B). This satisfies the "moves away by step immediately
   after" condition for weak-beat unisons (though this is beat 1, the
   unison is excused as a phrase-opening point of alignment before
   the voices diverge into contrary motion).
