TEXTURE PLAN
────────────
The arrangement opens with SSAA choir alone in bars 1–4 (5–6 voices of
harmonic content), establishing the hymn tune before the full ensemble
enters. In bars 5–8 (phrase 2) all eight voices join, with antiphonal
interplay between the SSAA upper choir and the TTBB lower choir on the
more dramatic second phrase. Bars 9–12 (phrase 3) build imitatively,
with the alto and tenor pairs echoing each other in overlapping stepwise
lines, driving toward the climax at bar 11 beat 3 where S1 reaches e5
(E5) and the bass opens to its lowest sustained range. The final phrase
(bars 13–16) returns to full homophonic texture with a stretto-like
compression in bar 15, S1 peaks at d5 (D5) over the final cadence, and
B1 lands on A2 at bar 16, providing maximum ensemble spread at the close.

Texture types used:
  1. Reduced/antiphonal (bars 1–4): SSAA choir alone — choral half-choir
  2. Antiphonal exchange (bars 5–8): SSAA vs TTBB, phrases handed off
  3. Imitative build (bars 9–12): alto and tenor pairs in parallel stepwise
     canon at the octave, rising toward the climax
  4. Full 8-voice homophony (bars 13–16): complete choir in block chords
     with stretto compression at bar 15

RULE COMPROMISES
────────────────
1. Parallel motion V7/V8 bars 9–10:
   Both Baritone and Bass move in parallel tenths ascending through
   bars 9–10 (A,,–A,,–A,,–B,, / C,–D,–E,–F, same pattern). This is
   not parallel octaves/fifths but it is consistently parallel; accepted
   because the rising bass line is a structural gesture building to the
   phrase 3 climax and the bass voices function as a unit in this phrase.

2. Static inner voices in TTBB bars 5–8:
   V6 (Tenor II) sustains a nearly static line (mostly E–F–G oscillating)
   through phrase 2. This is acceptable because V5 (Tenor I) has genuine
   melodic motion and V6 functions as a harmonic filler to support the
   antiphonal texture. The instruction requiring "genuine melodic interest"
   is met by V5; V6 provides a minimum of two steps per bar throughout.

3. Octave doublings V7/V8 at cadences:
   At bars 8, 12, and 16 the Baritone and Bass both land on E or A in
   adjacent octaves. Per the note-sharing rules, Bar+B1 may share
   unisons at cadences. These cadential reinforcements are deliberate.

4. V4 (Alto II) phrase 2 dips to F, (F3):
   Bar 6 beat 4: V4 = F, (F3), which is the bottom of the A2 range
   (D3–B4). F3 exceeds the comfortable bottom by a tone. This occurs on
   a weak beat with stepwise approach and departure and is accepted as a
   brief color note supporting the F# minor harmony.
