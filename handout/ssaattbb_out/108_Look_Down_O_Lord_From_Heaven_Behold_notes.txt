ARRANGER NOTES
Look Down, O Lord, From Heaven Behold
(Es ist das Heil uns Kommen Her)
SSAATTBB Arrangement

══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
TRANSPOSITION
══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Original key:    D major (2 sharps)
Chosen key:      D major (2 sharps) — no transposition required
Semitones:       0
Direction:       n/a

Lever harp setup (Eb storage tuning, levers raised):
  Eb → E (raise lever on all E strings)
  Bb → B (raise lever on all B strings)
  Levers for Ab, F, C, G, D remain DOWN (those are Eb-storage flats
  that are not needed — but wait: in Eb storage, all levers down = Eb
  major = Eb Bb Ab Db Gb Cb Fb. To play D major (D E F# G A B C#)
  from Eb storage, the following levers must be raised:
    Ab  → A  (raise)
    Eb  → E  (raise)
    Bb  → B  (raise)
    Fb  → F  (raise — giving F natural, then sharp by lever = F#)
    Cb  → C  (raise — giving C natural, then sharp = C#)
    Gb  → G  (raise)
    Db  → D  (raise)

  In practice, ALL seven levers are raised from Eb storage to reach
  D major — equivalent to a full lever reset. This is standard
  practice for harp in D major.

No transposition was required; D major (2 sharps) is well within the
valid range of ≤ 4 sharps.

HARMONIC SUBSTITUTIONS
══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

No harmonic substitutions were required. The original hymn is
predominantly diatonic in D major. The chromatic tones appear only
in the soprano (V1) and in the original inner voices, which were
replaced by diatonic neighbors (see Diatonic Violations below).

The secondary dominant implied by the original tenor's G# (^G,) in
Section A bar 3 (V/V tonicizing A major) is reharmonized in all
inner voices to a plain dominant A major chord using only diatonic
scale degrees. The characteristic sound of the D–A motion is
preserved. S1 retains the ^G (G#4) as an unrestricted accidental,
giving the soprano the raised leading tone against the harp's diatonic
A major harmony — an intentional lever-harp color.

══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
DIATONIC VIOLATIONS CAUGHT
══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

In K:D, the diatonic scale is D E F# G A B C#. The following
accidentals appeared in the original SATB inner voices and were
replaced:

1. Original S1V2 (alto), Section A bar 2, beat 3:
   Note: =C (C natural, written "D D/=C/ B," in source)
   Replacement: C (C# — diatonic leading tone in K:D, written just "C")
   Reason: C natural is non-diatonic in D major. The nearest diatonic
   neighbor preserving voice leading is C# (one semitone higher),
   which also strengthens the resolution to B.

2. Original S2V1 (tenor), Section A bar 4, beat 2:
   Note: ^G, (G#, written "(A, ^G,) A," in source)
   Replacement: G (G natural — diatonic 4th scale degree)
   Reason: G# is non-diatonic in D major (it would imply D/A or A
   major secondary dominant). All inner voices hold G natural,
   preserving the A major dominant quality diatonically. S1 retains
   ^G freely.

3. Original S2V2 (bass), Section A bar 2, beat 2 (pickup group):
   Note: =C, (C natural, written "B,,/=C,/" in source)
   Replacement: C (C# — diatonic in K:D)
   Reason: C natural is non-diatonic. B1 voice moves B–C# rather
   than B–C♮, maintaining smooth stepwise motion while staying
   diatonic. The original passing tone function is preserved.

4. Original S2V2 (bass), Section A bar 3, beat 4:
   Note: ^C, (C#, written "E, ^C," in source)
   This is DIATONIC in K:D (C# is the seventh scale degree).
   No replacement needed — retained as-is in V8 as plain "C".

5. Original S2V1 (tenor), Section B bar 2, beat 3:
   Note: ^G, (G#, written "A, ^G, A, C" in source)
   Replacement: G (G natural — diatonic)
   Reason: Same as violation 2. V5 (T1) sings G# only if it
   follows S1's line; T1 in Section B was given an independent
   diatonic line that avoids this entirely.

TEXTURE PLAN
══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

The arrangement opens with Section A sung by the upper choir (SSAA,
voices 1–4) alone — a four-voice antiphonal texture that establishes
the chorale character with restraint. On the written repeat of
Section A, the lower choir (TTBB, voices 5–8) enters in full,
producing an antiphonal expansion to all eight voices; this repeat
functions as the first textural crescendo. Section B is set in
continuous full eight-voice homophony with increasing harmonic density
as the melody ascends to its peak (S1 on D5, T1 on D4 concert),
building through the penultimate phrase to the final cadence, where
all voices converge with a 4-3 suspension in T1 and a stepwise
descent in all bass voices to close on the tonic in all registers.

Textures used:
  1. Four-voice SSAA antiphonal opening (Section A, first time)
  2. Full eight-voice homophony (Section A repeat onward)
  3. Antiphonal exchange (SSAA alone vs. full 8-voice texture)
  4. Canonic/imitative motion between S1 and T1 in Section B
     bars 1–3 (T1 follows S1 at the octave, same melodic line)
  5. Call and response between soprano pair and bass pair at
     final cadence, with alto/tenor providing sustained inner harmony

══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
RULE COMPROMISES
══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

1. Cross-choir register overlap (T2 / Baritone), Section B bars 1-3:
   T2 (V6) reaches A3 concert on beats where Baritone (V7) is at
   G3 or F3 — T2 momentarily sits above Bar. This is permitted by
   rule 3 (voice crossing between pairs is permitted where musically
   effective) and does not violate the within-pair ordering rules.
   The overlap creates a rich central band in the ensemble and is
   intentional.

2. S1 climax pitch (D5 rather than G5–A5):
   The instructions prefer S1 at G5–A5 for the climax. However,
   the original chorale melody peaks at D5 and cannot be raised
   without departing from the source tune. S1 therefore does not
   reach the extreme high range. The vertical spread at the Section
   B climax (S1 D5 / B1 D2) still spans more than two octaves and
   provides strong dynamic contrast.

3. Opening texture:
   The instructions specify "open with reduced texture (4-6 voices)."
   This arrangement opens with exactly 4 voices (SSAA), which meets
   the minimum of the guideline. The full 8-voice texture enters
   at the repeat of Section A rather than in Section B, giving a
   longer antiphonal build than a single-phrase introduction would
   provide. This was judged more effective for a chorale setting
   with a short, repeated first period.

4. Bass original notes below C2:
   As noted in the Range Log, the original SATB bass descends to
   A1 and B1 — below the professional B1 minimum of C2. These notes
   were raised by one octave, creating a slightly narrower bass
   register than the original. No other rule was compromised as a
   result; all voice leading is preserved at the new octave.
