SSAATTBB Arrangement for 8-voice Ensemble and Lever Harp
=========================================================

TRANSPOSITION
-------------
Original key: A major (3 sharps: F#, C#, G#)
Chosen key:   A major (unchanged — 3 sharps is within the 4-sharp maximum)
Semitones shifted: 0 (no transposition)
Direction: none required

Lever harp configuration for A major (Eb storage tuning):
  Raise levers on: F (→ F#), C (→ C#), G (→ G#)
  All three levers raised before performance; no mid-piece lever flips required.

Reason for key choice: A major is a valid key (3 sharps ≤ 4-sharp limit), all
8 voices sit comfortably within professional ranges at this pitch, and no
transposition was needed.


HARMONIC SUBSTITUTIONS
-----------------------
No secondary-dominant or tonicization chords required reharmonization.
The original harmony is solidly in A major throughout. All ^D (D#) and
^E (E#) occurrences in inner voices were ornamental/passing chromatic notes
in the original SATB arrangement; they were replaced with diatonic neighbors
(see Diatonic Violations below) without requiring chord substitution.

The original harmony in bars 6-8 (section B) implies a brief V/V color
through the raised leading tone D# (^D) in the alto; in this arrangement
the harmony is reinterpreted as a diatonic dominant preparation (E major =
V of A) using D natural in all supporting voices, which preserves the
cadential momentum while remaining fully diatonic. No chord root changes
were made.


DIATONIC VIOLATIONS CAUGHT
----------------------------
Every instance where an original inner-voice accidental was replaced:

Bar 6  (section B, bar 2):
  Voice: S1V2 (original alto, now split to A1/A2)
  Original: C/^D/ — the ^D (D#) was a chromatic passing tone toward E
  Replacement: C/D/ — D natural (diatonic scale degree 4 of A major)
  Reason: D# is not in the A major scale; D natural preserves stepwise motion

Bar 7  (section B, bar 3):
  Voice: S1V2 (original alto)
  Original: G/F/ E E (last note group has E ^D)
  Replacement: E2 → D used in the inner-voice line
  Reason: ^D removed; D natural placed as passing tone

Bar 7  (section B, bar 3):
  Voice: S2V2 (original bass)
  Original: ^D, B,, C, B,, — ^D, (D#2) was a secondary leading tone
  Replacement: D, B,, C, B,, — D natural (diatonic)
  Reason: D# not in A major; D natural retains the bass motion

Bar 8  (section B, bar 4):
  Voice: S1V2 (original alto)
  Original: E ^D E2 — ^D chromatic passing tone
  Replacement: E D C2 distributed across A1/A2 — D natural replaces D#
  Reason: D# not diatonic; D natural resolves smoothly to the tonic

Bar 20 (section E, bar 4):
  Voice: S1V2 (original alto)
  Original: E ^D E2 — same ^D chromatic passing tone at internal cadence
  Replacement: A1/A2 use E and C (tonic chord tones) at the cadence
  Reason: D# removed; voices resolve to tonic with clean thirds

Bar 22 (section F, bar 2):
  Voice: S1V2 (original alto)
  Original: G G F ^D — ^D chromatic passing tone approaching E
  Replacement: G E D E — D natural used as lower neighbor
  Reason: D# not diatonic; D natural maintains the descending line

Bar 23 (section F, bar 3):
  Voice: S1V2 (original alto)
  Original: E3/2 ^D/ E E — ^D chromatic anticipation
  Replacement: A,3/2 C/ B, A, (A1) / E,3/2 A,/ E, E, (A2) — D natural
  Reason: ^D replaced by diatonic neighbor; smooth contrary motion preserved

Bar 24 (section F, bar 4):
  Voice: S1V2 (original alto)
  Original: E ^D B,2 — ^D passing chromatic
  Replacement: B, A, A,2 — D natural used in A1; E, D A,2 in A2
  Reason: D# removed; smooth descent to cadence

Bar 28 (section G, bar 4):
  Voice: S1V2 (original alto)
  Original: F ^E F2 — ^E (E# = F natural) chromatic passing tone
  Replacement: D2 area — F# (diatonic) used; E# replaced by nearest
    diatonic neighbor F# (scale degree 6)
  Reason: E# = F natural is not in A major; F# is the nearest diatonic
    note (one semitone up from E#, which is in the key)

Note: =D (D natural) appearing in the original tenor S2V1 bar 9 is NOT
a violation — D natural IS diatonic in A major (scale degree 4). The
natural sign in the original ABC was redundant (D has no sharp/flat in
the A major key signature). It was retained as D.


TEXTURE PLAN
------------
The arrangement opens with a reduced 4-voice texture (SSAA, bars 1-2),
allowing the upper choir to establish the melodic material before the full
ensemble enters in bar 3. The piece builds through antiphonal exchanges
(SSAA vs TTBB, sections B–C), reaches a first climax at bar 15 with the
!sintro! phrase in full 8-voice homophony, then employs call-and-response
between the outer voices S1 and B1 (section E, bars 17-20) with inner
voices providing harmonic cushion. Section F introduces canonic imitation
with T1 echoing the soprano material at the lower octave. The grand finale
(section H) brings all 8 voices together in full homophony, culminating at
bar 31 with S1 on E5 and B1 on A2 for maximum ensemble spread, followed
by a stretto-inflected final cadence in bar 32.

Textures used:
  1. Reduced opening (4-6 voices): bars 1-2, section A
  2. Full 8-voice homophony: bars 3-4, section D (bars 13-16), section G,
     section H
  3. Antiphonal exchange (SSAA / TTBB): sections B and C (bars 5-12)
  4. Call and response between outer voices (S1 and B1) with inner
     harmonic support: section E (bars 17-20)
  5. Canonic imitation (T1 echoes S1 at the lower octave): section F
     (bars 21-24)


UNISON LOG
----------
Unisons within voice pairs are used sparingly and only at structurally
meaningful moments:

Bar 4   (section A, bar 4): T1/T2 on E (written) — phrase ending,
        strong beat 1, both move away by step in bar 5. Purpose: cadential
        blend at first phrase close.

Bar 8   (section B, bar 4): T1/T2 on E (written) on beats 3-4 — cadence,
        both voices converge for emphasis then diverge in bar 9.

Bar 12  (section C, bar 4): B1 and Bar both on E, (strong beat 3-4) —
        cadential grounding; T1/T2 converge on E (written) simultaneously.
        Use is deliberate for the half-close.

Bar 16  (section D bar 4): B1 and Bar on A,, — final cadence root,
        strong beats 3-4. Purpose: grounded tonic arrival.

Bar 32  (final bar): Bar and B1 both on A,, — tonic root doubling at the
        final cadence. Strong beat 3-4. Purpose: maximum bass solidity for
        the closing chord.

Cross-choir unisons (A2 / T1) at the register break occur freely
throughout and are not logged individually per the arranging brief.

  primary high note, appearing at phrase peaks and the climax. E5 is
  within comfortable soprano range (top A5 reserved for extreme climax;
  E5 is well within the comfortable zone). No concern.

A1 (Alto I):

Bar (Baritone):

  F2 is one step above minimum, acceptable at a structural moment.
  (This matches the original bass line movement; kept for authenticity.)

  Bar 27: A,, (A2) — low but within range.

B1 (Bass I):
  Bar 1: A,, (A2) — C2 is the minimum; A2 is four semitones above.
  Acceptable and gives a grounded opening bass.

  Bars 23-24: F,, (F2) and G,, (G2) — mid-low register, within range.

  Bar 31: C, (C3) — the B1 does not descend to C2 at the climax because

  fatigued, but a professional should sustain A2 comfortably for a final
  chord. This is within specification.

No voice exceeds its stated upper range at any point.


RULE COMPROMISES
----------------
Bar 27 (section G, bar 3): The baritone line moves A,, – D, – A,, – B,,
which involves a leap of a fourth (A to D) and return leap (D to A) and
then a step (A to B). The D,–A,, leap of a fifth downward is accepted
because it follows the original bass line arpeggiation and the bass voice
enters a chord tone each time. Strictly the rule prefers stepwise motion
but bass arpeggiation is conventional at this harmonic rhythm (I – IV –
I – V). No stepwise alternative preserves the bass function here.

Bar 9 (section C, bar 1): The bass V:8 has E,3/2 E,/ E, B,, — a drop of
a fourth from E, to B,,. This mirrors the original bass and is acceptable
as a bass leap to the dominant pedal; both notes are strong chord tones.

Section D, bar 14, V:8: the line A,, F, E,3/2 D,/ is taken directly from
the original source bass (S2V2). The F, is F#2 (diatonic). The descent
A,,–F,–E,–D, spans a seventh; this is a large melodic span but divided
across the bar in dotted rhythm. It is idiomatic bass writing and not
considered a voice leading violation since each note is a chord tone.

No parallel fifths or octaves were found between adjacent voices in spot-
checking. Voice crossings between non-paired voices (e.g. A2 below T1)
occur freely as intended at the register break and are not violations per
the arranging brief.
