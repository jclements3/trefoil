RULE COMPROMISES

1. S2–A1 voice crossing, bar 3 beats 2–4:
   As S1 descends to B3 in bar 3, S2 (third below) drops to D4–C4–B3–D4. Meanwhile
   A1 moves from D4 down through B3–A3–F#3 in a contrary-motion descent. On beats 2
   and 3, S2 (C4, then B3) briefly falls below or equals A1 (B3, A3), creating a
   voice-cross between the S and A pairs. This is permitted by the rules ("voice
   crossing between pairs is permitted where musically effective") and here reflects
   the deep descent of the soprano line. On beat 4, S2 rises to D4 to re-establish
   proper register above A1 (F#3). The crossing lasts only two beats and resolves
   cleanly; no S–A unisons occur.

2. B1 bar 16 range (G1):
   The original bass voice descends to G1 (G,,) at the final pedal. This is below
   the stated B1 professional range floor of C2. The note is retained as written for
   source fidelity; a conductor note is appended in the Range Log above recommending
   substitution to G2 if the singer cannot produce G1 reliably.

3. Baritone bar 16 independence from B1:
   Rule 8 (Splitting Strategy) states Baritone harmonizes a third above B1. On bar 16
   beat 3–4, B1 = G1, which would put Baritone at B1 — also below range. Instead,
   Baritone holds D2 (doubling the root of the dominant in a different octave), which
   is diatonic, within range, and reinforces the final G major cadence with a clear
   low fifth D2–G1 between the two bass voices rather than a unison-third cluster.

4. Reduced texture opening (bars 1 TTBB rests):
   The prompt's dynamic-shape guidance calls for opening with 4–6 voices. Bars 1 has
   TTBB on rest (4 voices), bar 2 adds TTBB (8 voices). This gives a gentle 4→8-voice
   bloom rather than the full ensemble from the downbeat, respecting the "do not open
   at full range" rule. The half-bar of silence in TTBB on bar 1 is intentional
   texture management, not a voice-leading compromise.
