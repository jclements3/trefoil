RULE COMPROMISES
─────────────────
1. Parallel motion in thirds throughout (intentional):
   S1 and S2 move in parallel diatonic thirds for much of the piece,
   including the passing eighth notes in bars 2 and 6. The instruction
   sanctions parallel thirds as a preferred interval between voice pairs,
   and this parallel motion is intentional color, not a default solution.

2. Bar voice (V7), bar 2, beat 1: leap of a major sixth (D3→A3):
   The rule states leaps of a sixth or more must resolve stepwise in the
   opposite direction. Resolution: A3→G3 (stepwise down). Rule satisfied.
   This leap was necessary because B1 entered on Bb2 (very low), requiring
   Bar to stay comfortably above; the sixth provided the only clean option
   that avoided T-voice unisons.

3. T2 voice (V6), bar 4: held on G3 (sounding) all four beats:
   Voice leading rule 6 prefers stepwise motion; an entirely static voice
   for one bar is a compromise. The C major half-cadence required the
   fifth of the chord in the TTBB choir; T2 holding G3 provided the
   clearest harmonic support while T1 moved with interest (C4-Bb3-C4-Bb3).
   The static T2 is brief (one bar) and immediately resumes motion in
   bar 5.

4. Bar voice (V7), bar 5, beats 1-3: held on G3 for three consecutive
   beats:
   Same rationale as item 3. The Bar line at this point was constrained
   by the need to stay above B1 (which descends to F2) and avoid all
   T-voice sounding pitches. G3 was the only consistently available
   pitch meeting all constraints; the descent to E3 on beat 4 provides
   resolution and prepares the bar 6 entry on C3.

5. A1 voice (V3), bar 1: held on F4 for all four beats:
   The original alto holds F4 for bar 1 (the original S1V2 line is
   F G F F). In the 8-voice reduction, A1 was kept static on F4 for
   bar 1 to maintain the original alto's character and avoid unisons
   with S2 (which passes through G4). The static F4 in A1 combined with
   A2's contrary motion (C4-E4-D4-C4) provides implied voice leading
   interest within the pair even when A1 is stationary.
