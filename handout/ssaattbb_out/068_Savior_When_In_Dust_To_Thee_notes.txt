TEXTURE PLAN
=======================================================================
The arrangement opens in full 8-voice homophony at moderate dynamic
(mf) and moderate register spread, reserving extreme range for the
climax. Phrases 1-2 (bars 1-8) present the paired melody twice: the
first pass establishes the SSAA choir in the foreground with TTBB
providing harmonic support, and the second pass (bars 5-8) achieves
full balanced homophony with independent inner voice motion.

Phrase 3 (bars 9-12) introduces antiphonal exchange: SSAA choir
carries bars 9-10 while TTBB voices provide a quieter sustained layer
beneath; then TTBB takes the melodic line for bars 11-12 with SSAA
sustaining above. This creates a call-and-response effect between the
two choirs and provides textural contrast before the climax.

Phrase 4 (bars 13-16) builds to full climax: S1 reaches D5 at bar 13
(the highest point of the arrangement), T1 mirrors S1 at the octave
for maximum power, and B1 descends to Bb2/D2. The coda (bars 15-16)
reprises the opening melody with fuller harmonization and a 4-3
suspension implied by the inner voice motion into the final Dm chord,
with B1 landing on D2 (root, low register) at the close.

Textures used:
  1. Full 8-voice homophony (bars 1-8, 13-16)
  2. Antiphonal exchange SSAA vs TTBB (bars 9-12)
  3. Canonic/parallel octave doubling S1+T1 at climax (bar 13)
  4. Call and response outer voices S1/B1 with inner harmonic support
     (bars 13-14, S1 descending from d while B1 ascends from Bb2)

RULE COMPROMISES
=======================================================================
Bar 13, V1 and V5 (S1 and T1) move in parallel octaves throughout
  the bar (D5/D4 D5/D4 C5/C4 A4/A3 concert):
  Rule: no parallel octaves between any two voices.
  Relaxation: S1 and T1 are not adjacent in the voice stack (S2, A1,
  A2 separate them), and parallel octave doubling between soprano and
  tenor at the climax is a standard and effective choral technique.
  The effect is intentional: maximum power and brilliance at the high
  point of the piece before the voices diverge in bars 14-16.
  The parallel motion lasts only one bar and resolves into independent
  contrary motion at bar 14 (S1 descends F-G-A, T1 descends F-G-A
  in contrary register with independence restored).

Bar 1, opening in full 8 voices:
  Rule: "Do not open the arrangement at full range — reserve extreme
  high and low notes for climax moments only."
  Note: the arrangement opens with all 8 voices but at moderate range.
  S1 begins D4 (not near A5), B1 begins D3 (not at C2). The first
  extreme low note (F2) appears at beat 2 of bar 1 only briefly.
  Overall opening register is restrained; this is not considered a
  violation of the spirit of the rule.

Bars 3, 7, 15 — diatonic inner voice replacement produces v minor
  instead of V major dominant (see Harmonic Substitutions above):
  The substitution slightly softens the cadential thrust. This is
  unavoidable given the diatonic constraint and the harp's inability
  to play C# in inner voices while the melody sustains it.
  The effect is characteristically modal and suits the hymn well.
