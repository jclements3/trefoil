ARRANGER NOTES
088 Lo, God To Heav'n Ascendeth
SSAATTBB arrangement — Von Gott will ich Nicht Lassen, F major
============================================================

TRANSPOSITION
-------------
Original key: F major (1 flat — Bb).
Chosen key: F major (1 flat). No transposition required.
F major is valid for lever harp in Eb storage tuning: raise levers on
Bb, Eb, and Ab strings. Since the piece uses only diatonic F major
(F G A Bb C D E), the required lever positions are:
  Bb string: lever UP (raised from Eb storage to Bb)
  Eb string: lever UP (raised from Eb storage to Eb — but Eb is not
             needed in F major, so this lever remains DOWN, giving Eb.
             Since Eb does not appear, no conflict arises.)
  More precisely: harp in Eb storage raises levers for Bb, F, C, G, D
  to reach F major. Exact lever map:
    Ab -> Eb -> Bb -> F -> C -> G -> D
    All levers raised through Bb give Bb major (2 flats).
    Raise one more (F lever) gives F major (1 flat). [Lever on F string
    raises Eb->F but that is the F lever, not the Bb lever.]
  Practical lever setting for F major from Eb storage:
    Raise levers: Bb, Eb, F, C, G, D (all except Ab)
    This gives the F major scale on all strings. ✓
  S1 carries no accidentals in this hymn, so the harp plays pure
  diatonic F major throughout.

HARMONIC SUBSTITUTIONS
-----------------------
Bar 10 (phrase 3, first A): The original SATB alto has ^C (C#4),
implying an A major chord (A-C#-E) — a secondary dominant (V of ii/vi)
or a modal tonicization of d minor.
  Original chord: A major (A-C#-E)
  Replacement: A minor (A-C-E), diatonic vi chord in F major.
  Reason: C# is not in the F major diatonic set; the lever harp cannot
  play C# without a lever flip. A minor (vi) is the nearest diatonic
  substitute by root — same bass note, same soprano E4, only the
  third changes from C# to C natural. The color is slightly softer
  but the voice leading is preserved: the S1 soprano E4 moves to A4
  on beat 4 identically in both versions.
  All inner voices (S2, A1, A2, T1, T2, Bar, B1) use C natural.
  S1 also uses E natural (which was already in the original soprano
  and requires no change).

DIATONIC VIOLATIONS CAUGHT
---------------------------
Bar 10, Alto (original S1V2): ^C4 (C#4) replaced with C4 (C natural).
  This was the only accidental in the original SATB inner voices.
  Replacement: diatonic C natural (the minor third of A minor / the
  fifth of F major). Voice leading is smooth: the alto line descends
  by step E-D at the bar boundary rather than C#-D, which is equally
  natural and avoids an augmented second.

No other accidentals appeared in the original SATB inner or bass voices.
The B-flat key signature is handled automatically by K:F in ABC; all
written B notes are B-flat as required.

TEXTURE PLAN
------------
Phrase 1 (pickup + bars 1-4, repeated melody): SSAA choir alone,
4-voice texture, piano dynamic. Opens softly to establish the chorale
character without overwhelming the listener. The TTBB voices are silent.

Phrase 2 (pickup + bars 5-8, same melody): Full 8 voices enter. TTBB
joins with the SSAA, creating a sudden bloom to mezzo-forte. The tenor
and bass lines enter on the pickup beat, achieving a clean antiphonal
effect: the audience hears the melody twice — first from the women,
then answered and reinforced by the full double choir.

Phrase 3 (bars 9-12): Full 8 voices, forte, with expanded harmonic
rhythm. The A1/A2 pair ascends stepwise during the sustained A minor
chord (bar 10), providing contrary motion against the descending T1/T2
pair. The half-bar ending (bar 12, 3.5 beats) is preserved from the
original and creates a brief breath before the final phrase.

Phrase 4 (bars 13-16, with sintro/eintro markings from original): Full
8 voices, climax. The Bar/B1 pair executes an ascending bass line
(G-A-Bb-C) in bar 15, driving to the final F major cadence in bar 16.
S1 reaches B-flat 4 (its phrase peak) in bar 13, and the S1/B1 spread
at the cadence spans F4 to F2 — two octaves. The final bar has all
voices settling on the F major triad with B1 on F2 (root, low register).

Texture types used:
  1. Reduced SSAA homophony (phrase 1) — opening with 4 voices
  2. Full 8-voice homophony (phrases 2-4) — block choral texture
  3. Antiphonal SSAA/TTBB contrast (phrases 1 vs. 2)
  4. Call and response between soprano (S1) and bass (B1) at the
     register extremes, with inner voices providing harmonic support
     (phrase 4, bars 13-16)

  cadence on F4 and D4 respectively — not a unison; S2 was revised
  to D4 to avoid the original unison. No within-pair unisons in SSAA
  phrase 1.

  V:6 (T2) = F (concert F3) — no unison. Bar (V:7) = A2, B1 (V:8) =

  notes (C and A) for 3 beats — sustained inner voices, no unison
  within either pair.

Phrase 4, bar 15 (beats 3-4): V:5 (T1) = G (concert G2) and V:6
  (T2) = G (concert G2) — unison at beats 3-4. Both voices are at

  is at the phrase ending (strong-beat cadential approach) and is
  immediately followed by the diverging final bar (T1 A3, T2 F3).
  Purpose: grounded bass-tenor blend at the cadential approach.
  Pair: T1+T2. Beat position: beats 3-4 (relatively strong/weak).

No S1+S2 unisons occur. No A1+A2 unisons occur. No Bar+B1 unisons occur.
No prohibited cross-choir S-A unisons occur.

RULE COMPROMISES
----------------
1. T1+T2 unison, phrase 4 bar 15 beats 3-4 (see Unison Log).
   Both voices land on concert G2. This was unavoidable given that T1
   follows the original tenor (G2 at this point) and T2 cannot go below
   G2 (its range minimum). The unison resolves immediately: T1 moves to
   A3 and T2 to F3 in the final bar (diverging by a third). The phrase
   ending context (cadential approach) makes this acceptable.

2. T2 bar 15 monotone (G2-G2-G2-G2): four repeated notes on G2.
   This is melodically inert. The cause is the same range constraint:
   T1 moves B-flat2-A2-G2-G2 and T2 cannot go below G2. An acceptable
   workaround would be to allow T2 to cross above T1 briefly (to B-flat2
   on beat 1 and A2 on beat 2), but that would create a voice crossing
   within the pair. The monotone G2 provides a pedal-point effect at
   the cadential approach, which is musically defensible.

3. Bass line octave adjustment (global): as noted in Range Log V:8,
   the original SATB bass was written for organ pedal and reaches Bb1
   and F1. All notes below C2 were raised an octave for B1. This
   changes the root-position bass profile in phrase 1/2 bar 1 from
   Bb1-A1-Bb1-F1 to Bb2-A2-Bb2-F2, and the Baritone from Db3-C3-Db3-A2
   to D3-C3-D3-A2. The harmonic function is preserved; the bass simply
   sounds less cavernous than the organ original, appropriate for a
   choral performance.

4. Phrase 3 bar 12 (3.5-beat half-bar): A1 and A2 enter this bar
   shorter than S1/S2 (2.5 beats vs 3.5 beats in the original voices).
   Fixed in revision: A1 has E2 D (half + quarter) and A2 has C2 Bb
   (half + quarter) before the final eighth rest, matching the 3.5-beat
   count of S1 and S2. ✓ (no remaining compromise)
