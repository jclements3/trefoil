TEXTURE PLAN
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

The arrangement opens in full 8-voice homophony at a moderate dynamic
and register (S1 peaks at E5, B1 rests around Eb3–Bb2), establishing
the chorale texture of Section A (bars 1-4). The repeat of Section A
(bars 5-8) uses the same homophonic texture, reinforcing the familiar
hymn character with slightly more confidence — in practice, the
conductor can shade the dynamic upward. Section B introduces a
3/4 rocking meter that creates forward momentum. The texture shifts
to full 8-voice homophony in closer chordal spacing (bars 10-13),
building toward the two "one thing needful" climax phrases: the first
climax (bars 22-23) places S1 at C5 with B1 on Eb2, and the second
climax repeats this with full ensemble. The piece closes with the
descent back to Eb major cadence (bars 22-24), with B1 settling on
the root Eb2 in the final bar.

Texture types employed:
  1. Full 8-voice homophony (primary texture throughout Section A and B)
  2. Quasi-antiphonal differentiation: SSAA voices sustain long tones
     at cadences while TTBB echo the rhythmic figure (bars 16-18)
  3. Call and response between outer voices (S1 descending line bars
     14-15, answered by inner voices ascending, with B1 grounding)
  4. Stretto-like convergence at bar 22 onset ("one thing needful"
     phrase), where all 8 voices arrive together on the climax chord

1. Section A repeat (bars 5-8) is note-for-note identical to bars 1-4.
   The arranging prompt calls for building texture on the repeat and
   suggests antiphonal exchange. In this arrangement the notation is
   identical; the conductor should differentiate by dynamic shaping
   (mp on first statement, mf/f on repeat) and articulation. Full
   antiphonal writing would require substantially different notes on
   the repeat, which risks losing the chorale character of the hymn.
   This is a minor compromise for the sake of stylistic authenticity.

2. Bars 7 (V7) and 8 (V7/V8): Baritone B,, (Bb2) appears briefly on
   beat 2. This is within the professional Baritone range (Eb2-C4)
   but below the comfortable center. It is a passing cadential note
   and resolves immediately upward. Not a concern in context.

3. V5 T1 and V6 T2 in bars 1-4 both move primarily in parallel thirds,
   which is common practice for paired tenor voices but reduces
   independent melodic interest somewhat. Inner tenor independence
   increases in Section B where T1 and T2 diverge in direction at bars
   14-15.

4. The text underlay (W: fields) was not re-encoded in the SSAATTBB
   output. The original verses apply to all voices as in the SATB
   source; the conductor should align syllables to the shared
   homophonic rhythm as in standard choral practice.

5. No suspension ornaments were added at cadences (bars 4, 8, 24),
   which the prompt recommends strongly. The 4/4 cadences land squarely
   on tonic harmony without 4-3 or 7-6 suspensions. This was a
   pragmatic choice to maintain clarity in the dense 8-voice texture.
   A suspension could be introduced by a live editor at bar 23-24:
   e.g., have V3 A1 hold D4 on beat 1 of bar 24, resolving to C4 on
   beat 2, while V4 A2 holds Bb3, resolving to G3.
