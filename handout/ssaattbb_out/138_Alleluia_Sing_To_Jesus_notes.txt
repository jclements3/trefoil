TEXTURE PLAN
------------
The arrangement unfolds in four sections following the source's four-verse structure.

Section 1 (bars 1–8): SSAA choir leads with the full melody while TTBB provides
a gentle harmonic cushion in sustained quarter notes, giving an opening texture of
reduced weight that establishes the key and melody before the full ensemble enters.
This is a "choir I leads, choir II accompanies" antiphonal opening.

Section 2 (bars 9–16): The roles reverse antiphonally — TTBB choir takes the
identical melody (transposed to their registers) while SSAA sustains above, creating
contrast and showcasing the lower ensemble. This is the antiphonal exchange texture.

Section 3 (bars 17–24): All eight voices enter together for the contrasting phrase
("Alleluia, not as orphans"), building to the piece's interior climax with full
8-voice homophony. Inner voices A1, A2, T1, T2 have genuine independent lines with
contrary motion between paired voices.

Section 4 (bars 25–32): The final section opens with canonic imitation between
S1/S2 and T1/T2 (both pairs shadowing the same melodic contour), then drives to
the climactic final phrase (!sintro!) where S1 reaches c' (C5) and B1 grounds on
F,, (F1), followed by a stretto-style convergence on the final F major cadence with
B1 closing on F,, and the soprano resting on F.

Texture types used:
  1. Antiphonal exchange SSAA vs TTBB (sections 1 and 2)
  2. Full 8-voice homophony (section 3)
  3. Canonic imitation between soprano and tenor pairs (section 4, bars 25–28)
  4. Call and response / stretto at final cadence (section 4, bars 29–32)
  5. Reduced texture opening (section 1 opening, SSAA melody + TTBB cushion)

RANGE LOG
----------
Section 1–2, S1: highest note G (G4) — within comfortable range, no concern.
Section 3, S1: highest note c' (C5) — comfortable upper range, normal for forte.
Section 4, S1: highest note c' (C5) at !sintro! climax — appropriate climax note.
  The arrangement intentionally avoids pushing S1 to A5; C5 is the peak of this
  melody's tessitura and a higher note would be unidiomatic for HyFrydol.

Section 4, T1 (concert): highest note D4 (written d' in 8vb treble) — within
  the comfortable T1 range (top G4 concert), no concern.

Section 4, B1: lowest note F,, (concert F1) at the final cadence — this is the
  bottom of the bass staff, an idiomatic low close for a bass on the tonic root.
  Acceptable; singers with a solid low F1 will anchor the cadence. If the ensemble
  bass cannot reach F1, substituting F, (F2) is acceptable.

Section 2, Bar voice: C, (concert C2) — the low end of the baritone range; used
  only at cadence points for grounded effect, not sustained.


RULE COMPROMISES
----------------
1. Section 4, final bar: Bar closes on F, (F2) and B1 closes on F,, (F1), an octave
   apart on the tonic root. This parallel octave at the final cadence was accepted
   as a deliberate stylistic choice for maximum gravitas at the close, following the
   tradition of octave-doubled bass roots at hymn endings. (Rule 1 relaxation:
   parallel octave between Bar and B1 on the final bar only.)

2. Section 1, bar 1: The opening uses TTBB in near-homophonic sustained motion
   rather than fully independent lines, to keep the texture light and introductory.
   Melodic independence in T1/T2 is subordinated to harmonic support in the first
   four bars, building to fuller independence from section 3 onward.

3. Tenor lines in sections 1–2 are intentionally simplified (quarter-note motion,
   less ornamental than sections 3–4) to contrast with the SSAA choir and create
   a clear antiphonal differentiation. This slightly reduces melodic interest in
   the inner voices during the accompaniment role, but is musically justified by
   the antiphonal texture design.
