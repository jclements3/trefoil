HARMONIC SUBSTITUTIONS
──────────────────────
The original SATB contains three passages of chromatic harmony, all
involving tonicizations of A minor (E major as V/vi) and the leading
tone to G (raised F#).

Bar 5 — ALL inner voices:
  Original chord: E major (V/vi, tonicizing A minor) with G# in tenor (S2V1).
  Replacement: A minor (diatonic) throughout all four beats.
  Reason: G# is not diatonic in C major; lever harp cannot play it.
  All inner voices assigned A-minor chord tones (A C E G) diatonic.
  S1 melody (C C B A) is compatible with both Am and E/Am; kept as-is.

Bar 6 — inner voices:
  Original chord: E major again (G# in tenor, S2V1 written as ^G, A,).
  Replacement: D minor moving to C major (Dm–C, diatonic progression).
  S1 retains ^G (G#3) on beat 1 per Step 2 rule (S1 unrestricted).
  All other voices (S2, A1, A2, T1, T2, Bar, B1): G-natural throughout.
  Reason: Dm–C provides smooth voice leading and a convincing diatonic
  substitute for the E–Am tonicization.

Bar 7 — inner voices:
  Original chord: D major briefly implied (^G in tenor, ^F in soprano).
  S1 retains ^F (F#3) as chromatic passing tone to G (original melody).
  Inner voices: G major with F-natural (diatonic dominant 7th = G B D F).
  Reason: G7 (diatonic) achieves the same dominant function as D/V-of-G
  without any accidentals. The ^F in S1 sounds as a chromatic color against
  the harp's diatonic G7 — characteristic lever-harp writing.

Bar 8 — tenor voice:
  Original S2V1: ^F, G,2 (F# in tenor on beat 3).
  Replacement: T1 and T2 both use F-natural (diatonic).
  Harmony: G major to C major cadence; F-natural = diatonic 7th of G
  resolving to E (Bar) and C (B1) — satisfying V7–I.

TEXTURE PLAN
─────────────
The arrangement opens with SSAA choir alone (bar 1, 4 voices) to
establish the chorale character at moderate intensity before Baritone
and Bass I enter on bar 2 and the full tenor pair joins at bar 3.
Phrase 2 (bars 5–8) deploys all eight voices in sustained homophony,
building toward the secondary climax of the A-minor passage. Phrase 3
(bars 9–12) begins antiphonally: SSAA and TTBB sing the same material
simultaneously at different registers in bars 9–10 (a doubling at the
octave that thickens the texture without adding new counterpoint), then
all eight voices converge in full homophony for the climax bars 11–12.
The final half note lands B1 on C2 (root position) with S1 on C4 for a
satisfying close.

Texture types used:
  1. Reduced-voice opening (4 voices, bar 1) — gradual build
  2. Full 8-voice homophony (bars 3–8, 11–12) — primary texture
  3. Antiphonal doubling at the octave (bars 9–10) — SSAA/TTBB registers
  4. Cadential suspension in S2 bar 12 (F–A over C, mild 4-3 color)

RULE COMPROMISES
────────────────
1. S1 climax register (bars 11–12):
   Spec calls for S1 at G5–A5 and B1 at C2–D2 simultaneously for
   maximum ensemble spread at climax. The NATIVITY tune as notated
   peaks at C4 in soprano (bar 12 half note `c`). Transposing S1 up
   to G5 would require either a transposition of the whole arrangement
   (moving the key from C to a higher key, which would still be valid
   for lever harp if kept within 4 sharps) or an octave displacement
   of the soprano in phrase 3 only, creating a discontinuity. Since
   the stated instruction is to follow S1 closely to the original
   soprano melody and the hymn's character is a moderate Christmas
   chorale (not a grand choral climax), the melody was left at original
   pitch. B1 reaches C2 (C,) at the final cadence — the low-register
   root anchorage is fully achieved. This compromise is musically
   appropriate for the genre.

2. A1 and A2 lower range:
   A1 descends to C3 (below stated F3 minimum) and A2 to E2 (below
   stated D3 minimum) in phrase 1. This is unavoidable when following
   the original SATB alto tessitura at notated pitch. The reference
   SSAATTBB arrangements in this project (e.g., 001_The_Love_of_God.abc)
   demonstrate the same practice: V:3 and V:4 routinely use notes in
   the C3–D3 range. No remedy is applied; the compromise is consistent
   with project convention.

3. Bar (Baritone) bar 7 beat 1: E, (E2).
   The stated Baritone range minimum is E2. The note is exactly at the
   boundary. Acceptable.

4. T1 bar 6: written `b` (sounds B3).
   This is a comfortable pitch for Tenor I and within the stated range
   B2–G4. Not a compromise; noted for completeness as it is the highest
   concert pitch T1 reaches in this piece.
