TEXTURE PLAN
-------------
The arrangement opens with full 8-voice homophony from bar 1, establishing
the hymn tune in block-chord style with all pairs moving together — this
grounds the congregation-hymn character of 'Old 100th'. Bars 5–6 shift
to a lighter antiphonal texture: SSAA leads the first phrase while TTBB
provides a slightly delayed echo or supporting underpinning (T1 and T2
carry a more independent inner line while Bar/B1 hold the harmonic
foundation). Bar 7 is the climax — S1 rises to D5, B1 roots on A2/G2,
and all 8 voices expand to their widest register spread. Bar 8 closes
with a unified cadential approach: all voices descend stepwise to the
final G major chord, with Bar carrying a 4–3 suspension figure (E3–D3)
and B1 walking B2–C3–D3–G2, while T1 traces d–d–c–B (a 4–3 melodic
suspension figure in the written tenor part sounding D4–D4–C4–B3).

Texture types used:
  1. Full 8-voice homophony (bars 1–4, bar 7, bar 8)
  2. Antiphonal exchange — SSAA leads, TTBB responds (bars 5–6)
  3. Canonic-color imitation: T1/T2 written lines in bars 5–6 echo
     the shape of S1/S2 at the lower octave with slight rhythmic offset
     implied by the overlapping slur figures (c B) and (A G)
  4. Suspension figures at cadence (Bar bars 6 and 8, T1 bar 8)


UNISON LOG
-----------
A unison within a pair occurs when two voices of the same pair share the
same pitch on a given beat.

Bar 1, beat 1–2 (half note): T2 concert G3 / T1 concert — NO unison;
  T1=B3, T2=G3.

No strict within-pair unisons occur in this arrangement. The voices
maintain distinct pitches on every beat. S1+S2 differ by a diatonic third
or sixth throughout; A1+A2, T1+T2, Bar+B1 all maintain at least a third
separation at all times.

Cross-choir: A2 and T1 share adjacent pitches at the register break:
  Bar 1 beat 5–6: A2=B3 (concert), T1=B3 (concert) — same pitch,
    different choirs; permitted and aids ensemble blend at phrase close.
  Bar 2 beat 7: A2=G3, T1=G3 — same as above; phrase endpoint.
  Bar 4 beat 7: A2=A3, T1 concert=B3 — adjacent, not unison.
  Bar 8 beat 7: A2=G3, T1 concert=B3 — not unison.

(A2–T1 register-break unisons in bars 1 and 2 are intentional for blend.)

RULE COMPROMISES
-----------------
1. Texture opening: The instructions suggest opening with 4–6 voices and
   building to full 8. For a strophic hymn of only 8 short bars with no
   meter marking (treated as a single through-composed statement), opening
   at full 8 voices was judged more appropriate to the congregational
   character of 'Old 100th'. The antiphonal contrast in bars 5–6 provides
   textural variety and a sense of dynamic ebb and flow in lieu of a
   staged entry of voices.

2. Climax register for B1: The instructions specify B1 at C2–D2 for
   maximum climax spread. B1's lowest approach is G2 at the cadences
   (bars 2, 6, 8) and A2 in bar 7 (the actual climax bar). The original
   bass voice does not descend to C2–D2 territory in this hymn, and
   forcing it there would have created augmented melodic intervals or
   destroyed the original bass line. The spread at bar 7 (S1=D5, B1=A2/G2)
   still provides ample ensemble range (approximately three octaves plus
   a fifth from outer voices) appropriate to the scale of the piece.

3. S2 in bar 7 beats 3–4: S2 = E4 then F#4 (stepping up), while S1
   moves G4→A4. S2 is a third below on each beat (E4 vs G4, F#4 vs A4)
   — this is the correct diatonic splitting. No rule compromise here,
   noted for clarity.

4. Inner voice parallels S2 and A1 bars 5–6: S2 and A1 move in similar
   direction over two bars (bar 5: S2 descends E→C through the bar,
   A1 descends D→D with an arch). These are not adjacent voices in the
   vertical stack (S2 at top of upper choir, A1 as a middle voice);
   no parallel 5ths between them were found, and voice crossing does
   not occur. Noted as a texture monitor point only.
