TEXTURE PLAN
------------
The arrangement opens with a reduced texture (pickup and bar 1) where
the soprano and bass pairs carry the melody and bass line, with the
alto and tenor pairs entering more quietly as harmonic support — roughly
6 functional voices rather than all 8 at full weight. The texture builds
through the verse (bars 1–12) using antiphonal coloring: SSAA present
the melody in the upper register while TTBB echo the harmony below in
parallel but independent lines. The refrain (bars 13–18) moves to full
8-voice homophony at the climactic phrase "Bold I approach th'eternal
throne," with S1 reaching up to f' and B1 anchored on low F,. The final
cadence (bars 19–24 / intro repeat) features stepwise voice leading in
all parts converging to a grounded root-position F cadence, with the
fermata bar (bar 17 of the original, "Bold I approach") serving as the
dynamic peak before the hymn-tune conclusion.

Texture types employed:
  1. Full 8-voice homophony — refrain (bars 13–18)
  2. Antiphonal exchange (SSAA vs TTBB) — verse bars 1–6
  3. Call and response outer voices (S1 and B1) — bars 7–12
  4. Sustained harmonic pads beneath melody — bars 19–24 intro repeat

RULE COMPROMISES
----------------
Voice leading Rule 6 (leaps of a sixth or more resolve stepwise
opposite): In bar 8, S2 leaps from F (bar 7 final beat) up to B
(b') — a diminished fifth / tritone — then resolves stepwise down
to A. This is a tritone leap, not a sixth, so technically within
the stepwise-preference guideline, but it is the most angular
motion in the arrangement. It was retained because it creates
genuine melodic interest in S2 at the climactic phrase and the
resolution is correct (down by step to A). If a more conservative
line is preferred, S2 could hold A through bar 8 beat 1 instead.

No other rule compromises. Parallel fifths and octaves were
avoided throughout by keeping A1/A2 and T1/T2 in contrary or
oblique motion relative to their paired outer voices whenever
the harmony tightened.
