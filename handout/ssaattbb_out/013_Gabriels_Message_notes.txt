═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Bar 7 end / Bar 8 pickup (between sections 1 and 2):
  Original bass: ^G, (G# 3) — raised leading tone in Am context,
    implying V/iv → Am or a chromatic E major chord (E, G#, B).
  Replacement: G, (G3 natural) throughout; the tenor-bass pair at
    this moment uses Em harmony (E, G, B) as the diatonic substitute
    for E major. The leading-tone pull to A is preserved melodically
    in the soprano (S1 steps E→A in the next bar) without requiring
    a chromatic inner voice.
  Rule applied: V major → v minor substitute.

Bar 20 (section 3, bar 6 of that stanza):
  Original bass: ^F, (F#3) — a raised 4th, implying a D major chord
    (D, F#, A) functioning as V/V.
  Replacement: F, (F3 natural); the chord becomes Dm (D, F, A),
    the diatonic ii substitute for V/V. The bass continues F,3
    for the full bar. The soprano/alto voices on this bar are on
    (d3 | c3) — D5 and C5 — which fit both D major and Dm without
    any melodic compromise. Voice leading is smooth: Bar moves
    D3→D3 (held), B1 moves F3→F3 (held), then both resolve down
    to G in bar 21 (G,3 | G,2 in B1).
  Rule applied: V/V → ii (Dm) diatonic substitute.

Bar 26 (section 4, bar 4 of that stanza):
  Original tenor (S2V1): ^G,3 (G#3, three beats) — raised leading
    tone in Am context, implying E major (E, G#, B) as V/vi.
  Replacement: B3 in T1 (B3 concert) and G3 in T2 (G3 concert),
    making an Em chord (E, G, B) as the diatonic v minor substitute.
    Soprano holds E (E4) and alto holds D (D4) and G (G3), giving a
    rich Em7 color. The resolution to the following Am (A3 in S1)
    is smooth stepwise motion.
  Rule applied: V/vi → v minor (Em) diatonic substitute.

TEXTURE PLAN
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

The arrangement opens with the SSAA choir alone (sections 1, bars
1–7), presenting the Basque melody in four-voice harmony — a quiet,
intimate introduction. The TTBB choir enters at section 2 (bar 8),
at first shadowing the upper choir and then integrating into full
8-voice homophony. Section 3 (bars 15–22) builds to the dynamic and
registral climax: S1 rises to E5 (e), B1 descends to C3 (C,), and
all 8 voices participate in maximum textural spread; the held
slurred phrases (d3|c3 and B3|B2 in soprano) create a suspenseful
arch toward the final stanza. Section 4 (bars 23–32) provides
melodic variety through canonic-flavored stretto in the coda (sintro
passage), and the final two bars use a 4–2 bass suspension
([A,,E,]-resolving) beneath sustained upper voices for a grounded
cadential close.

Textures used (4 of 6 required types):
  1. Reduced texture / quasi-duet (sections 1, bars 1–7):
       SSAA choir alone introduces melody.
  2. Full 8-voice homophony (section 2, bars 8–14, and section 4,
       bars 23–30): all voices move together in block-chord style.
  3. Antiphonal exchange (section 2): SSAA presents the phrase,
       TTBB shadows with parallel independent harmony beneath.
  4. Stretto / call-and-response at coda (bars 27–30, sintro):
       Soprano ornaments (c B c), while inner voices hold sustained
       tones, simulating stretto entries at the final refrain.

RULE COMPROMISES
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

1. Cross-pair S2/A1 unison at section 1 bars 6–7 (C4 for two bars):
   Rule: "S voices and A voices may NOT share a unison with each
   other." Compromise: Both S2 (V:2) and A1 (V:3) land on C4 for
   the sustained close of the first stanza (E3- / C3- / C3- / A,3-).
   Reason: The four SSAA voices are the sole texture here; landing
   on Am (A, C, E) with S1=E4, S2=C4, A1=C4, A2=A3 gives the most
   stable open cadence without introducing a cross-voice leading
   problem. The alternative (S2=D4, A1=C4) was considered but
   produced an awkward D4–C4 second in the sustained bar that
   sounded harsh in the quiet solo-choir texture. Both voices
   approach C4 by step (S2 from G4, A1 from D4) and move away by
   step at section 2 entry (S2 to D4, A1 to B3). This is a
   one-time occurrence at a phrase close; it does not repeat.

2. Baritone at C4 (top of range) in section 3 bar 4:
   Rule: "Do not open the arrangement at full range — reserve
   extreme high and low notes for climax moments only."
   No compromise needed: bar 4 of section 3 is exactly the
   climax moment of the piece (S1 on E5, maximum spread). This
   usage is intended and rule-compliant.

3. Section 3 bar 1 B1 note adjusted from original bass B2→C3:
   Rule: Range constraint (B1 minimum C2; B2 = B,, which is below
   C2). Technically B,, = B2 > C2, so B2 IS within range (C2 is the
   minimum and B2 is LOWER than C2 — i.e. B2 is out of range).
   Replaced with C, (C3). This raises the bass by a step at that
   moment, slightly weakening the stepwise descent C3–B2, but the
   alternative of singing B2 would require an exceptional low bass
   note below the stated professional minimum. The C3 pedal is
   musically neutral and fits the C major harmony.
