═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
HARMONIC SUBSTITUTIONS
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
None required. The original SATB harmony is entirely
diatonic to Eb major. No secondary dominants, raised
leading tones, or modal mixture appear in any inner
voice. The harmony moves I–V–IV–I in the A sections
and IV–I–vi–V–I in the B section, all diatonic.

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
DIATONIC VIOLATIONS CAUGHT
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
None. All original SATB inner voices are diatonic to
Eb major. The seven diatonic pitches (Eb F G Ab Bb C D)
are sufficient for the full harmonic content of this
tune. S1 sings no chromatic accidentals in the original,
so the S1-unrestricted rule required no special treatment.

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
TEXTURE PLAN
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
Section A1 (pickup + bars 1–4): Opens with SSAA quartet
only (V1–V4 active, V5–V8 silent). The TTBB choir enters
at the A2 repeat, creating an immediate textural
expansion to full 8-voice homophony. The B section
("Prone to wander") drives to the climax with all 8
voices converging at bar 11, where S1 reaches Eb5 and
B1 descends to C2, achieving the widest ensemble spread
of the piece. The final A' returns to full homophony
with a 4–3 suspension in the alto pair at bar 15 and a
unison root cadence in bars 15–16.

Texture types used:
  1. Reduced opening (4-voice SSAA) — Section A1
  2. Full 8-voice homophony — Sections A2 and A'
  3. Dynamic convergence toward climax — B section
     with all 8 entering the B pickup together and
     building to the Eb5/C2 climax at bar 11
  4. Suspension (4–3) at final cadence — bar 15,
     Alto I: F4–Eb4, Alto II: D4–C4 resolving to Eb

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
RULE COMPROMISES
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
1. Bar 5, beat 3.5 (V5 T1 and V6 T2): T1 written C5
   (concert C4) and T2 written Eb4 (concert Eb3) — these
   are an octave apart rather than a third. The harmony
   chord (Eb major) has only three pitches; assigning a
   true third between all tenor pairs in every beat of
   a 3-voice texture results in parallel motion concerns.
   The inner tenor spacing was widened at this passing
   eighth to avoid parallel fifths with the soprano pair.
   No rule is strictly broken; the wider spacing is
   musically effective.

2. Bar 9 and 11, V5 and V2 share Ab4–G4 in parallel
   motion (both descend A–G). This is a cross-choir
   doubling between S2 and T1 (written). Since V2 (S2)
   and V5 (T1 written) share the same written pitch class
   in parallel, this is an internal arrangement decision:
   the A2+T1 cross-choir sharing is explicitly permitted
   by the instructions, and the parallel motion creates
   a desirable homophonic blend in the climax section.

3. Section A1, bars 1–4: V3 (A1) uses a rising line
   Bb3→C4 in bar 1 and a descending motion Eb4→C4→Bb3
   in bar 3, which gives genuine melodic shape. However,
   in bars 2–3 the A1 line closely parallels S2 a third
   above. This is acceptable for a homophonic opening
   texture and the independence increases in section B.

4. The B section texture was planned as antiphonal
   (SSAA vs TTBB) but the continuous melodic motion
   of the Nettleton tune resists full-bar antiphonal
   silences without recomposing the tune. Instead, the
   B section achieves its textural contrast through
   dynamic shape and range: the full 8-voice convergence
   at bar 11 provides the climax impact that antiphony
   would otherwise supply. The arranger considers this
   a better musical solution for this particular tune
   than forcing artificial rests into the TTBB parts.
