HARMONIC SUBSTITUTIONS
══════════════════════════════════════════════
The original SATB harmony is essentially functional diatonic in C major
throughout, with secondary-dominant inflections creating the accidentals
listed below. Because all accidentals are confined to inner voices and the
melody S1 is permitted any chromaticism, no full chord substitution was
needed. The harmonic rhythm and chord roots were preserved exactly.

  Bar 2  (phrase 1 bar 2): Tenor has ^G, (G#3) implying a V/vi approach
          to A minor. The G# is a chromatic passing tone under an A minor
          chord. In all other voices (S2 A1 A2 T2 Bar B1) the chord remains
          a plain A minor triad (A C E) with G natural in T2. No chord root
          or quality change required; only the chromatic passing tone in T1
          was replaced (see Diatonic Violations).

  Bar 6  (phrase 2 bar 2): Same situation as bar 2; same treatment.

  Bar 9  (phrase 3 bar 1, alto): Original alto has D/^C/ D — C# functions
          as a chromatic passing tone under a D chord implying V/ii. The
          D minor chord substitute rule (V/ii → ii) is already satisfied
          because the harmony is already ii (D–F–A). C# replaced with C
          natural in S2/A1/A2 (see Diatonic Violations). The bass holds D,
          and the diatonic D minor voicing is clean.

  Bar 10 (phrase 3 bar 2): Tenor has _B, (Bb3) implying a Bb major chord
          (VII or mixture). Original bass goes G,,–Bb,,–A,,–G,,, suggesting
          a passing Bb major. Replaced with a diatonic B natural throughout
          all non-S1 voices, treating the chord as v (G minor: G B D) →
          which still supports the stepwise bass motion G–B–A–G as a
          passing sequence. This is a V→v (G major → G minor) substitution
          absorbed into passing motion; the ear accepts G minor here.

  Bar 13 (phrase 4 bar 2, alto): Original alto has D/^C/ D again (same as
          bar 9). Same substitution: C natural in S2/A1/A2/T1/T2.

  Bar 16 (phrase 4 bar 4, alto): Original alto has D ^C → passing tone
          under a final approach to D. C natural in S2/A1/A2.

  Bar 16 (phrase 4, bass): Original bass has _B,, (Bb2). Same Bb substitution
          as bar 10; B natural used, treating the chord as G minor passing.

TEXTURE PLAN
══════════════════════════════════════════════
The arrangement opens with reduced texture: all 8 voices are present from
the pickup, but TTBB is kept soft and supporting so the SSAA choir carries
the first phrase statement (pp/mp contrast). At phrase 2 (bar 5) the full
8-voice homophony arrives at a forte dynamic, matching the strophic repeat
with added weight. Phrase 3 (bar 9) introduces antiphonal exchange: SSAA
leads the ascending lines (bars 9–11) while TTBB answers and supports,
reversing roles on the descending figures. Phrase 4 builds to full
8-voice homophony at climax (S1 reaches e/E5, B1 descends to D,,/D1),
then narrows to a 4-3 suspension at the penultimate bar before a quiet
final close.

Texture types employed:
  1. Reduced-texture homophony — phrase 1 (SSAA foregrounded).
  2. Full 8-voice homophony — phrase 2 and phrase 4.
  3. Antiphonal SSAA vs TTBB exchange — phrase 3.
  4. Suspension at cadence — bar 15 (4-3 in all pairs simultaneously).

RULE COMPROMISES
══════════════════════════════════════════════
1. Bar/B1 unison throughout phrases 1 and 2 (bars 1–7):
   Rule: avoid consecutive-beat unisons within a pair.
   Compromise: Bar and B1 move in complete unison for these bars to provide
   a grounded bass foundation at the soft opening dynamic and to avoid
   creating too many independent lines simultaneously on the first hearing
   of the tune. Differentiation begins at phrase 3.

2. T1/T2 and Bar/B1 near-parallel motion in bars 5–7:
   Rule: no parallel fifths or octaves between adjacent voices.
   The tenor pair and bass pair occasionally move in parallel tenths within
   their respective registers. No parallel fifths or octaves exist between
   any two adjacent staves; the tenors and basses are separated by a tenth
   or more and move in parallel thirds/tenths, not fifths/octaves. No
   violation; logged for transparency.

3. G,,, in V7 bar 10 (Baritone G1):
   Rule: Baritone comfortable range is E2–C4.
   G1 is below the nominal comfortable bottom (E2). This is a passing bass
   note on a weak beat in a low-tessitura phrase. The note arises from
   strict adherence to the original bass line. A professional choral
   baritone can manage G1 briefly; if the singer cannot produce it cleanly,
   raise to G,, (G2) and allow Bar and B1 to diverge by an octave at that
   beat (B1 takes the low G1, Bar sings G2). Flagged as a concern.
