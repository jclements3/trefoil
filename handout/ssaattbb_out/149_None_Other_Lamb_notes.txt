Bar 7 — Bass voice (original S2V2: ^A,2 B,2 =A,2):
  Original chord: Beat 1-2 — Augmented 6th / French Aug 6th
    (A# in bass with C# in alto and tenor above = chromatic

  Replacement: A2 sustained throughout the bar (dominant pedal).
  Reasoning: A# (= Bb) is not available on the lever harp in D major
    without a mid-piece lever flip. Replacing with sustained A2
    creates a V pedal beneath the inner-voice motion (C#→D, B→B),
    which preserves the harmonic tension and resolution character
    while remaining fully diatonic. The bar still functions as a
    prolonged dominant preparation into bar 8.

No other bars required harmonic reharmonization; all remaining
original chords are diatonic to D major.

TEXTURE PLAN
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
The arrangement opens with a reduced 4-voice SSAA texture for the
pickup bar, building to 6 voices (SSAA + Tenors) in bars 2-2, then
expanding to the full 8-voice ensemble from bar 3 onward. The climax
arrives in bar 4 with the soprano's descending run from D5 through
the characteristic G#4 accidental and all 8 voices at full spread.
Bar 5 uses the SSAA choir to carry the melody while the lower voices
provide a sustained harmonic foundation, creating an antiphonal
effect between choirs. Bar 6 achieves full 8-voice homophony with
active passing motion in all inner voices. Bar 7 broadens into a
sustained chord with inner-voice neighbor motion (the "breathing"
bar before the intro). Bar 8 introduces mild canonic exchange: the
soprano pair presents the ascending E–F–G# line while the tenor pair
follows in parallel thirds beneath, and the alto and bass pairs
provide rhythmic and harmonic grounding. Bar 9 closes with a simple
rooted D major chord, all voices converging on root-position harmony.

Textures used:
  (1) Reduced SSAA opening (bar 1 — 4 voices)
  (2) Gradual SSAATT build (bar 2 — 6 voices)
  (3) Full 8-voice homophony (bars 3, 6, 9)
  (4) SSAA melody over sustained TTBB foundation (bar 5 — antiphonal)
  (5) Sustained chord with inner-voice motion (bar 7)
  (6) Mild canonic imitation between soprano and tenor pairs (bar 8)

RULE COMPROMISES
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
1. Bar 6, Alto II (V:4) ends on G, = G3.
   This places A2 below the sounding range of T1 (which has A3
   on the same beat), creating a momentary cross-pair voice
   crossing between A2 and T1. Per the arranging rules, voice
   crossing between pairs is permitted where musically effective.
   G3 here is a passing stepwise descent (A3→B3→A3→G3) providing
   contrary motion against the soprano's ascending then stepwise
   line. The effect is a natural opening of the inner-voice spread
   at the cadential approach. No rule violation; noted for
   conductor awareness.

2. Bar 5, Baritone (V:7) descends to C, = C#3.
   C#3 is within the Baritone range (E2–C4 professional). The
   figure F#3→E3→D3→C#3 is a deliberate stepwise descending line
   providing contrary motion against the ascending soprano. No
   rule violation; included as a melodic interest choice. Lighter
   baritones may find the sustained C#3 in a lower-medium dynamic
   slightly effortful — the director may raise this line by a
   third (using A3→G3→F#3→E3) if ensemble balance requires it.

3. Bar 4, Alto II (V:4): D3/2 C/ — D4(1.5) C#4(0.5).
   Written `D3/2 C/` = D4 dotted quarter, C#4 eighth note. This
   rhythmic cell mirrors the soprano's characteristic dotted figure,
   providing imitative texture. No rule violation; noted for clarity
   of intent.

4. The pickup bar (bar 1) is only 3 beats in a 3/2 bar. TTBB voices
   are tacet for the pickup to achieve the reduced opening texture
   as specified. This is a deliberate textural choice, not a
   compromise. All 4 absent voices have a full-bar rest (z3 in
   the pickup measure).
