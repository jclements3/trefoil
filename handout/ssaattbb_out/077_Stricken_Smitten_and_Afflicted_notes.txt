HARMONIC SUBSTITUTIONS
──────────────────────
The original harmony is entirely diatonic in Bb major with one recurring
chromatic element: the raised 6th degree (F#, written ^F) used as a
secondary leading tone. This appears in the soprano (S1V1) and tenor
(S2V1) lines of the original SATB.

No full chord substitutions were required. The chromatic F# in the tenor
was simply replaced by diatonic F (see Diatonic Violations below). All
underlying harmonies remain identical:

  Verses 1-2:
    Pickup: Bb major (I)
    Bar 1:  G minor (vi) → Eb major (IV) → Bb/D (I6) → Cm (ii)
    Bar 2:  Bb (I) → F7 (V7) → Eb (IV) → Bb (I)
    Bar 3:  G minor (vi) → IV → I → V approach
    Bar 4:  Bb (I) — tonic close

  Verse 3 (climax, higher range):
    Same harmonic rhythm; bass moves to D minor cadence (iii)
    on bar 4 = A4 in S1, D2 in B1 (half cadence / iii chord)

  Verse 4 (final):
    Same as verses 1-2; tonic close G,,=G2 in B1.

No secondary dominants, no tonicizations, no modal mixture survived
into the inner voices. The color of the ^F in S1 is retained
deliberately as a chromatic passing tone against the harp's diatonic
accompaniment.


DIATONIC VIOLATIONS CAUGHT
───────────────────────────
The following original inner-voice accidentals were replaced with the
nearest diatonic neighbor:

1. Original S2V1 (Tenor), Bar 3, beat 5: ^F, (F#3 in original ABC)
   Voice: T1 (V5 in arrangement)
   Original note: F# (raised 6th, secondary leading tone to G)
   Replacement: F natural (diatonic 5th degree of Bb)
   Reason: F# is non-diatonic in Bb major; lever harp cannot play F# without
   a mid-piece lever flip while the Ab lever is already adjusted.
   Voice leading preserved: F→G stepwise motion retained.
   This substitution occurs in all four verses (same music repeated).

2. Original S2V2 (Bass), verse 1-2, pickup beat 2: ^F, (F#3)
   Voice: Not carried to B1 directly; original pickup has G,, ^F,, in bass.
   In this arrangement, B1 pickup is either resting (verse 1) or on G2 D2
   (verse 2), both diatonic. The F# pickup in the original bass was replaced
   by D2 (diatonic preparation of the tonic) in verse 2, and omitted
   (rest) in verse 1. Voice leading: preserved by arriving on G2 (tonic
   pitch of the Gm chord) without chromatic approach.

3. Original S2V2, bar 4 of final section: =F (Fb? or natural F sign)
   The original ABC shows =F, in S2V2 for the last verse only, beat 5.
   In key Bb, F is already natural; =F is redundant (a cautionary accidental)
   and equals diatonic F. No substitution needed — F is fully diatonic.
   In the arrangement, B1 bar 3 beat 5 has F2 (=F,,) which is correct.


TEXTURE PLAN
────────────
The arrangement opens with reduced texture (verse 1: SSAA on pickup with
Bar present; T1 T2 B1 enter on bar 1 beat 1) and gradually expands to
full 8-voice writing in verse 2. Verse 3 is the climactic verse: all
8 voices in close homophony, S1 reaching E5 (the arrangement's peak),
and B1 anchored at G2-D2 for maximum ensemble spread. Verse 4 employs
a call-and-response texture, with S1 presenting the melody in dialogue
with the lower voices, and the inner voices providing sustained harmonic
support in bars 1-2 before the full texture reassembles at bar 3.

Texture types used:
  1. Antiphonal (SSAA vs TTBB): verse 1 pickup — SSAA alone, TTBB enters
     mid-section; the two choirs exchange leadership.
  2. Full 8-voice homophony: verse 2 (full texture from pickup), verse 3
     (climax, maximum dynamic and range).
  3. Call and response between outer voices (S1 and B1): verse 4, bars 1-2;
     inner voices provide sustained harmonic cushion beneath.
  4. Stretto-like density at final cadence (verse 4, bars 3-4): all voices
     converge to closing I chord with contrary chromatic motion in S1
     (^F retained) against diatonic stepwise descent in all other voices.

RULE COMPROMISES
────────────────
1. Bar/B1 parallel motion (Verse 1, Bar 3, beats 1-2):
   Bar = E3 sustained; B1 = E2 sustained.
   Both voices hold the same pitch class (E = Eb in Bb major) in octaves
   for two beats. This is a sustained parallel octave between Bar and B1
   on the tonic-third degree of a Cm chord. It is accepted here because
   (a) both notes are held (not moving), so there is no motion to be
   "parallel," and (b) the octave reinforces the harmonic color of the
   Cm sonority. By strict reckoning, the moment of arrival on beats 1-2
   coming from F3/F2 to E3/E2 constitutes a similar-motion octave. This
   was retained for sonority; a revision could give Bar a passing C3 on
   beat 3 to break the texture.

2. T2 voice (V6) enters below B, (B2 written = B2 sounding... wait,
   sounding = written - 12, so written B, = B3 sounds B2) which is the
   T2 floor (G2-E4). B2 is above G2, so this is within range. No compromise.

3. Verse 3, B1 (V8), bar 4: note D2 (D,,) — within range C2-A3 ✓.
   No compromise.

4. Verse 1, B1 (V8) rests on pickup and does not enter until bar 1.
   This is a deliberate texture choice (antiphonal opening), not a rule
   compromise. The tacet on the pickup is intentional for dramatic effect.

5. S2 (V2), Verse 3, bar 4: F4 sustained (F4 for 4 beats on cadential
   tonic chord). This note is F4, the 5th of the Bb tonic chord, held
   while S1 has A4 (the 7th/6th degree) and the lower voices close to
   D-F-A (iii chord in bar 4 of verse 3 = intermediate cadence). The
   sustained F4 in S2 creates a mild suspension against the D minor
   harmony and resolves by voice connection into verse 4. This is
   intentional and creates effective color.
