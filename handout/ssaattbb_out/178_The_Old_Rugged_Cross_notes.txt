HARMONIC SUBSTITUTIONS
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
The inner-voice chromatic moments in the original SATB occur in two
contexts:

1. Bars 1 & 5 — Original S1V2 (now inner voices) had D3/2 ^C/ C D2.
   The ^C (C# / raised 6th of Eb leading to D) implies a secondary
   dominant tonicization of D minor (V/ii passing motion).
   Substitution: ^C replaced by D (the note it was approaching);
   inner voices hold or approach D by step, preserving the resolution
   without the chromatic passing tone.
   Result: Bar 1 and bar 5 inner voices move D–D–E–D (diatonic).
   Harmonic effect: The brief tonicization is removed; the harmony
   reads as Bb I throughout, which is authentic for this gospel style.

2. Bars 2 & 6 — Original S1V2 had =E (E natural) against Eb key.
   Context: moving from G minor toward Bb/F; the E♮ in the original
   is a chromatic passing tone (raised 3rd of C, implying a brief C
   major sonority, i.e. V/vi passing motion).
   Substitution: =E replaced by E (Eb, diatonic), holding the third
   of the C minor / Bb harmony. The S1 melody retains =E freely as
   color against the harp and inner voices.
   Inner voices: A1–A2–T1–T2 all remain on Eb (diatonic).

3. Chorus bar 10 (original bar 10) — Original S1V2 had (D ^C E D2).
   The ^C again implies V/ii or raised-6th ornament.
   Substitution: ^C replaced by C (diatonic). Tuplet rewritten as
   (D C E D) — smooth chromatic-free passing motion.
   Harmonic context: Bb major tonic still implied. ✓

4. Chorus bar 10 — Original S2V1 had (F, =E, G, F,2).
   The =E, (E natural) in the bass implies a passing V/IV or
   chromatic bassline embellishment.
   Substitution: =E, replaced by F, (diatonic), giving (F, F, G, F,2).
   Baritone/Bass keep the F pedal through this bar.
   Harmonic effect: Minor color removed; movement reads as plain
   F–F–G–F (neighbor tone figure on F), entirely diatonic. ✓

TEXTURE PLAN
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
The arrangement opens with a moderately full texture (6 voices in pickup,
all 8 from bar 1) but with TTBB in a simpler, more sustained role while
SSAA carries the melody — creating an antiphonal effect within a homophonic
framework. The chorus (bars 9–16) brings in full 8-voice homophony for
maximum breadth and impact, with the climactic bars 14–16 pushing S1 to
e'' (Eb5) and d'' before resolving, while B1 grounds the final cadence
on Bb1–Bb2. The final bar achieves maximum registral spread (S1 on Bb5
written B, B1 on Bb1 written B,,), the widest possible ensemble sound.

Texture types employed:
1. ANTIPHONAL STRATIFICATION (verse, bars 1–8): SSAA carries moving
   melody; TTBB provides sustained quarter-note harmonic support, creating
   a quasi-antiphonal balance between the two choirs.
2. FULL 8-VOICE HOMOPHONY (chorus, bars 9–16): All voices move together
   in block chords with rhythmic unison, building to the climax.
3. CALL AND RESPONSE — outer voices (bars 7–8): S1 carries the descending
   F7 line (A3/2 G/ F e d c) while B1 holds the F pedal, with inner voices
   providing harmonic filling — brief moment of outer-voice call and response
   before the chorus entrance.
4. STRETTO / OVERLAPPING ENTRIES at the final cadence (bar 16): inner voices
   in contrary motion with S1 and B1 creating a converging final cadence
   effect.

RULE COMPROMISES
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
1. B1 low Bb (B,,) in bars 1, 2, 5, 6, 17.
   Rule: B1 range C2–A3.
   Compromise: Bb1 (B,,) is one semitone below the stated low.
   Reason: The original SATB bass firmly establishes Bb1 as the pedal root
   throughout the verse. Omitting it or raising it would destroy the
   characteristic gospel-hymn bass resonance. A professional bass voice
   in this genre is expected to reach Bb1. If a specific ensemble cannot
   manage it, the conductor may substitute Bb2 (B,) throughout.

2. Bar 12 and 14: T1 and T2 (V:5, V:6) share the same pitch in some beats
   (both on G, or both on D). This is a pair unison on inner beats but they
   move away immediately by step in subsequent beats, which is within the
   unison usage guidance (weak-beat unison, both voices move away by step).
   Kept for harmonic clarity on the Bb chord.

3. Bar 8: parallel motion between S2 (descending thirds) and A1 (descending
   with similar contour). Near-parallel motion in thirds is acceptable and
   desired (it creates a smooth SSAA choir descent into the chorus). No true
   parallel fifths or octaves occur between any two adjacent voices.

4. Bar 15: inner voices (V:3–V:6) use repeated-note sustained figures
   (e.g., C C C C2 in A1) rather than independent melodic lines during the
   held Eb cadential measure. This prioritizes harmonic stability at the
   climax over strict melodic independence. The !sintro! marker is retained
   from the original to indicate this is a performance direction (intro repeat
   marking), not a fermata.

5. Bar 14: A1 (V:3) and A2 (V:4) both on B (Bb4) — unison within the alto
   pair on beat 1 (strong beat, phrase climax). Both voices move away by step
   in bars 15–16. This is a deliberate unison for emphasis at the climactic
   bar. Per rules, S and A voices must not share unison with each other —
   this is an A1/A2 pair unison which is explicitly permitted.
