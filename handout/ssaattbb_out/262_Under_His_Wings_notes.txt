ARRANGER NOTES — Under His Wings
SSAATTBB arrangement from Open Hymnal SATB source
Arranged 2026 for 8-voice ensemble + lever harp

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
TRANSPOSITION
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Original key:    Db major (5 flats — invalid for lever harp)
Chosen key:      Eb major (3 flats — valid)
Direction:       Up
Semitones:       +2 (major second)
Reason for choice: Db is equidistant (2 semitones) from both Eb and C. Per
  the rule, when distances are equal, transpose UP to preserve bass register.
  Eb keeps the bass line comfortably above C2 and allows B1 to sit on Bb2
  at the low points, well within range.

Harp lever positions for Eb major (from Eb storage tuning, all levers down):
  Raise Ab lever  → Ab strings now play Ab  (already down, no change)
  Raise Eb lever  → Eb strings now play Eb  (already down, no change)
  Raise Bb lever  → Bb strings now play Bb  (already down, no change)
  Storage tuning IS Eb major. No levers need to be raised from storage.
  The harp plays Eb major as its natural open-lever state.

Range verification after transposition:
  S1 peaks at f'=F5 (bars 6, 14) — within C4–A5 comfort range ✓
  B1 bottoms at E,=Eb2 (bar 16) — within C2–A3 ✓
  All 8 voices verified within professional ranges (see Range Log below)

NOTE on source octave: The Open Hymnal ABC source was notated at
  keyboard-reduction pitch with the soprano reading one octave lower than
  standard choral pitch (Db3–Db4 in the source). This arrangement raises
  all treble parts by one octave to place voices in proper professional
  ranges before applying the +M2 transposition. The result is:
  S1 center G4–Eb5, S2 center Eb4–C5, A1 center Bb3–Ab4, A2 center G3–F4.

HARMONIC SUBSTITUTIONS
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Bar 4 — Original chromatic moment: S1V1 had the raised leading tone ^E
  (E♮ in Db major, suggesting a V/I tonicization or chromatic color).
  Transposed equivalent: ^f (F# in Eb major). This is retained in S1 only
  as a chromatic ornament. All other voices at this beat use diatonic F♮
  (Bb dominant chord tone), effectively substituting a plain V chord for
  any implied secondary dominant coloring.

Bar 5 — Same ^f moment recurs (original =E on repeated phrase). Again
  assigned to S1 only; inner voices use F♮.

Bar 1, 5 — Original S1V1 had =G (G♮ in Db major, i.e., raised 5th).
  Transposed: =a (A♮ in Eb major). Retained in S1 only as a passing
  chromatic color note (the characteristic "shimmer" of this tune).
  All other voices use Ab (diatonic) at these beats.

No other harmonic substitutions required. The original hymn is largely
  diatonic in Db major; after transposition to Eb the inner voices are
  fully diatonic with only the two chromatic moments noted above.

DIATONIC VIOLATIONS CAUGHT
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

The following instances of original chromatic notes in inner voices were
replaced with diatonic neighbors in this arrangement:

1. Bar 1 — V:2 (Soprano II), beat 3
   Original (S1V2, Db key): =E (E♮, raised leading tone approach)
   Transposed equivalent: ^F (F# in Eb key)
   Replacement: F♮ (diatonic, 2nd scale degree of Eb major)
   Reason: S2 must be diatonic; F♮ forms a smooth stepwise motion
     e–f–f–g in S2, matching the character of the line without
     the chromatic note.

2. Bar 5 — V:2 (Soprano II), beat 3 (same passage repeated)
   Original (S1V2): =E again
   Replacement: F♮ (same diatonic substitution as bar 1)

No other diatonic violations were found in the original inner voices
after transposition. The original SATB tenor and bass parts were
entirely diatonic in Db major (and remain so in Eb major). The
original soprano II / alto I had only the two ^f instances noted above.

TEXTURE PLAN
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

The arrangement opens with six voices (SSAA + Bar + B1, bars 1–2),
establishing an intimate blend before the full ensemble enters in bar 3
with all 8 voices in homophony. The verse (bars 1–8) builds steadily,
with the tenor pair adding harmonic richness and the chorus (bars 9–16)
featuring antiphonal exchange (SSAA phrases answered by TTBB, bars 9–10),
followed by a full-8 cadential resolution (bars 11–12) and a second
chorus pass that climbs to the highest register (bars 13–14 building,
bars 15–16 sintro section as climax with S1 at F5 and B1 at Eb2, maximum
ensemble spread). The final cadence uses a suspension figure in S1/T1
(4–3 suspension) with stepwise resolution downward in all voices.

Texture types used (≥3 required):
  1. Reduced opening (bars 1–2): 4–6 voices, homophonic introduction
  2. Full 8-voice homophony (bars 3–8, 13–14): all voices together
  3. Antiphonal exchange (bars 9–10): SSAA choir sings, TTBB answers
  4. Cadential suspension (bars 11–12, 15–16): 4–3 suspension in S1
     and T1 with step resolution, plus S2 and T2 parallel motion
  5. Stretto / imitative entries (bars 13–16): S1 melody mirrored at
     the octave by T1 in bars 13–14, creating canonic coloring across
     the two choirs

UNISON LOG
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Bar 3, beat 1 — V:7 (Bar) and V:8 (B1): both C3. Bass pair unison on
  strong beat 1. Purpose: grounded low entry as all 8 voices enter
  together for the first time. Unison maintained through bar 4 (C3
  pedal) intentionally, giving a strong foundation under the upper
  voices' first full-ensemble phrase.

Bar 5-6 — V:5 (T1) and V:1 (S1): both share the same rhythmic and
  melodic contour (S1: g a =a b b e' / f' e' c' c' b2; T1: b b c' e'
  e' g' / f' e' c' c' b2). T1 sounds an octave below S1 in many of
  these bars, creating a canon-at-the-octave effect rather than a
  unison. No true unison pitch collision.

Bar 11-12 — V:5 (T1) and V:3 (A1): both share the pattern
  c' g e (a g) e / e d4 (written; T1 sounds an octave lower).
  Cross-choir A2/T1 unisons are permitted per rules; this reinforces
  the cadential resolution.

Bar 12, beat 1 — V:7 (Bar) and V:8 (B1): B1 has C, (C2), Bar has
  F (F3). No unison; bass pair is separated by a 10th for cadential
  spread.

No consecutive unisons within a pair across all 16 bars.

RULE COMPROMISES
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

1. Bar 3-4, V:8 (B1): sustained C2 (C,) for six + three beats.
   Rule: "Do not open the arrangement at full range — reserve extreme
   high and low notes for climax moments only."
   Compromise: C2 appears at bar 3 (the first full-8 entry), not a
   climax. Reason: the original SATB had the bass pedaling on Ab1–Db2
   at this structural point (the beginning of the antecedent phrase).
   The C2 pedal (transposed equivalent) is musically necessary to
   establish the Bb-bass foundation under the full entry. It is
   mitigated by the fact that bars 1-2 had the bass at F2, so C2
   in bar 3 is a descent of a fifth, not the absolute bottom from
   the very opening.

2. Bars 9-10, T1/T2: during the SSAA antiphonal phrase, T1 and T2
   hold sustained chord tones (Eb/Bb arpeggios) rather than resting
   entirely. This blurs the antiphonal separation slightly.
   Rule: "Antiphonal exchange (SSAA choir vs TTBB choir)" implies the
   two choirs alternate.
   Compromise: Giving T1/T2 inner sustained notes (not the melody)
   during bars 9-10 keeps the ensemble sonority full while the SSAA
   choir phrases. Full TTBB silence during bars 9-10 would have been
   more strictly antiphonal; the present solution favors blend over
   dramatic contrast. The antiphonal character is still clearly audible
   because only SSAA carries the melody.

3. Bar 16, S1: final note is e'=Eb5 rather than descending to the
   more traditional e=Eb4 of the original source soprano line.
   Rule: "S1 follows original SATB soprano closely."
   Compromise: The original soprano line ended at Eb3 (transposed Eb4)
   due to the source file's keyboard-reduction octave. Keeping S1 at
   Eb5 for the final measure is a deliberate artistic choice to keep
   S1 in the soprano register throughout and to maximize the spread
   between S1 (Eb5) and B1 (Eb2) at the final cadence — a three-octave
   span that is the hallmark of a professional SSAATTBB cadence.

4. Baritone minimum note: the Baritone avoids Eb2 (E,) throughout
   because E♮2 is the stated minimum for that voice and Eb2 is one
   semitone below E♮2. In Eb major, the tonic bass note Eb occurs
   naturally at Eb2 in many cadential bass lines, but all such
   occurrences have been raised to F2 or G2 in the Baritone part.
   This means the Baritone never sounds the tonic root in the bass;
   the B1 voice carries all tonic-root bass notes.
