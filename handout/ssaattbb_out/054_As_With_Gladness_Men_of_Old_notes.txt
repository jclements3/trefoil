ARRANGER NOTES
As With Gladness Men of Old — SSAATTBB Arrangement
Tune: 'Dix' (Conrad Kocher, 1838; abr. William Henry Monk, 1861)
Text: William Chatterton Dix, 1860

════════════════════════════════════════════
TRANSPOSITION
════════════════════════════════════════════

Original key: G major (1 sharp).
Chosen key:   G major (1 sharp). No transposition required.
Direction:    None.

Lever harp setup (Eb storage tuning, all levers down = Eb major):
  Raise levers for: Eb→E (E lever up), Ab→A (A lever up), Bb→B (B lever up), F→F# (F lever up).
  Net lever positions: E up, A up, B up, F up → G major.
  (Four levers raised; well within practical harp setup for a single piece.)

════════════════════════════════════════════
HARMONIC SUBSTITUTIONS
════════════════════════════════════════════

None required. The original SATB is fully diatonic in G major throughout.
All four original voices use only the seven diatonic pitches of G major
(G A B C D E F#). No secondary dominants, raised leading tones in inner
voices, or modal mixture appear in the source material.

The one potentially chromatic reading — bar 3 beat 2 (soprano F# over an
implied C-major harmony with the bass on C) — is native to G major and
requires no substitution.

════════════════════════════════════════════
DIATONIC VIOLATIONS CAUGHT
════════════════════════════════════════════

None. The original inner voices (alto and tenor) contain no accidentals.
All eight voices in this arrangement are fully diatonic in G major.
S1 (Soprano I) carries the melody as written and likewise uses no
accidentals beyond those of the key signature.

════════════════════════════════════════════
TEXTURE PLAN
════════════════════════════════════════════

Bars 1–4 (Phrase A, first time): Antiphonal opening. SSAA choir carries the
melody in the foreground while TTBB sustains root-position and first-inversion
chord tones beneath. This 8-voice texture is weighted toward SSAA, giving the
impression of a four-voice choir expanding from below.

Bars 5–8 (Phrase A, repeat): Full 8-voice homophony. All inner voices gain
melodic independence: A1 traces a rising scale (D–E–F#–G across bar 5),
A2 answers in complementary contrary motion, T1 develops a decorated
stepwise line echoing the soprano rhythm, T2 traces the original bass line
in its natural register. S2 and A1 diverge from phrase-1 in bar 8 (S2 rises
B–C–D, A1 rises G–F#–E) to generate kinetic energy entering the climax.

Bars 9–12 (Phrase B, climax): Full 8 voices at maximum ensemble spread.
S1 rises to C5 (bar 11 beat 4); B1 descends to C2 (bar 11 beat 1), yielding
a spread of nearly four octaves simultaneously. T1 (sounding) shadows the
soprano a tenth below through bar 11 (e f g e written = E4–F#4–G4–E4
sounding). The final cadence in bar 12 features a 4–3 suspension: A1 holds
B4 through beats 1–2 against the dominant D in the bass, resolving to A4
and G4 as T2 and B1 settle onto G in the low register.

Texture types employed:
  1. Antiphonal exchange (SSAA vs. TTBB) — bars 1–4.
  2. Full 8-voice homophony — bars 5–8.
  3. Solo/melody-prominent texture with sustained lower choir — bars 1–4
     (SSAA melody prominent, TTBB chordal support).
  4. Suspension at cadence — bar 12 (4–3 in A1/S2/S1 over dominant bass).
  5. Call and response between outer voices — bars 9–11: S1 leads each
     phrase peak while B1 answers from the low bass register.

════════════════════════════════════════════
UNISON LOG
════════════════════════════════════════════

Bar 8, beats 3–4 — S1 and S2 unison on D4 (half note):
  Purpose: phrase-ending unison on the dominant scale degree, gathering both
  soprano voices before the climax leap to B4/G4 in bar 9. Strong-beat
  position; both voices immediately diverge in bar 9 (S1 up to B4, S2 up
  to G4).

Bar 11, beat 3 — Bar (Baritone) and B1 (Bass I) unison on B2:
  Purpose: penultimate-bar cadential convergence; the two bass voices briefly
  touch at B2 before Bar resolves up to C3 (beat 4) and B1 moves to E2.
  Bar moves away by step immediately after (B2→C3); B1 leaps (B2→E2).
  Noted as a rule compromise — see Rule Compromises section below.

════════════════════════════════════════════
RANGE LOG
════════════════════════════════════════════

S1, bar 11 beat 4 — C5: climax note, at the comfortable upper ceiling of S1.
  Preceded by a stepwise approach (E4–F#4–G4–C5 is a leap of a fourth from
  G4 to C5). The leap is acceptable; it is the same soprano note as in the
  original SATB. Not a concern — contextually justified as the phrase peak.

A1, bars 10–11 — B4: within comfortable range (top D5), not a concern.

T1 (sounding), bar 11 — written g (G5), sounding G4: exactly at the top of
  T1's comfortable range. Preceded by a stepwise ascent (e f g = E4–F#4–G4
  sounding), which makes the high note singable.  Not a concern.

T2 (sounding), bar 1 beat 3 — written D (D4), sounding D3: the
  T2 descends to D3 only briefly (one quarter note) before returning to G3.
  No concern.

Bar (Baritone), bar 12 — F#2: near the lower extreme of the Baritone's
  comfortable range (bottom E2). Two quarter notes (beats 1–2) on F#2 only;
  voice then rises to B2. Acceptable at a final cadence.

B1 (Bass I), bar 11 beats 1–2 — C2: exactly the minimum of B1's range.
  Sounding C2 at the climax bar provides the full ensemble spread desired.
  At two consecutive quarter-note beats, this is within professional
  expectation at a fortissimo climax. Flagged as an approach to extreme range.

B1, bar 11 beat 3 — B2 (sounding): unison with Baritone (see Unison Log).

B1, bar 12 beats 1–3 — D2: well within range (C2–A3). No concern.
B1, bar 12 beat 4 — G2: final root of G major. Comfortable.

════════════════════════════════════════════
RULE COMPROMISES
════════════════════════════════════════════

1. Bar (V:7) and B1 (V:8) — unison B2 at bar 11 beat 3 (strong beat, beat 3
   of 4/4). The rules discourage same-pair unisons on strong beats unless
   both voices move away by step immediately after. In this case Bar moves
   away by step (B2→C3), but B1 leaps (B2→E2, a descending fifth). The
   compromise is accepted because (a) beat 3 of the penultimate bar is a
   cadential convergence point; (b) the voices diverge immediately and in
   contrary directions (Bar up, B1 down), so the unison lasts only one
   quarter note and opens into a wide spread on beat 4.

2. A2 (V:4) — bar 12, beat 1: A2 leaps down a fifth (A4→D4). This departs
   from the preferred stepwise motion rule. The leap is accepted because
   A4 cannot descend by step without landing on G4 (which would create a
   within-pair unison with A1 on B4 beat 1 → no, that's not the issue).
   Stepping down from A4 to G4 at bar 12 beat 1 would cross or match A1=B4.
   A4→G4 would be A2=G4 < A1=B4: no crossing, but G4 at beat 1 then
   needs to resolve to C4/B3 by beat 3, requiring a further descent.
   The P5 leap (A4→D4) is large but resolves immediately stepwise: D4→C4→B3.
   Noted as a voice leading relaxation.

3. T1 (V:5), bar 3 — written e d c B (sounding E4–D4–C4–B3): this is a
   descending line from E5 written (E4 sounding) to B4 written (B3 sounding),
   crossing below T2's written range momentarily? No: T2 bar 3 is C C B, C
   (written, sounding C3–C3–B2–C3). T1 minimum in bar 3 is B4 written (B3
   sounding), well above T2's B3 written (B2 sounding). No crossing.
   No compromise needed — noted for clarity only.
