TEXTURE PLAN
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
The arrangement opens with a 4-voice SSAA texture on the 2-beat pickup
(bar 1), suggesting a gentle, intimate beginning before the full ensemble
enters. The tenor pair (T1/T2) joins in bar 2 for a 6-voice texture,
and the baritone and bass complete the full 8-voice choir in bar 3,
where they remain through the final cadence. The middle section (bars 5-6)
employs antiphonal exchange, with the SSAA choir presenting melodic
material while TTBB provides rhythmic and harmonic motion beneath, then
the roles briefly reverse. Bar 7 brings the full 8-voice ensemble into
stretto-like convergence for the climax, with all voices moving in
coordinated eighth-note motion toward the final C major cadence.

Texture types employed:
  1. Reduced-voice opening (bars 1-2): SSAA alone, then +T1/T2 (rule:
     open with 4-6 voices, build to full 8).
  2. Full 8-voice homophony (bars 3-4, bar 8): all voices together in
     block-chord style with rhythmic variety in inner voices.
  3. Antiphonal exchange (bar 6): SSAA and TTBB trade phrases; the
     upper choir carries the running eighth-note melody while the lower
     choir sustains longer values, then momentum shifts.
  4. Stretto convergence (bar 7): all eight voices move in parallel
     eighth-note motion toward the cadence, creating density and urgency
     before the quiet resolution of bar 8.
  5. Call and response (bars 5-6): S1 leads the melodic phrase while
     S2 and the alto pair provide harmonic thirds beneath; TTBB answers
     with the same rhythmic figure a beat later in bar 6.

RULE COMPROMISES
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
1. Bar 6, A2 final eighth (D3): This note sits at the absolute minimum
   of the A2 range (D3). It is a passing note on a weak beat moving
   toward C4 in bar 7 and resolves immediately. If the ensemble's low
   altos cannot produce a confident D3, substitute E3 (keeping the
   stepwise motion) or F3 (a slightly larger skip but still within
   range). Rule 3 (no voice crossing within pair) is observed: A1 is
   at F3 on the same beat, so A1 > A2 is maintained throughout.

2. Bar 7, S2 beat 1 (A3): Strict voice leading would place S2 a third
   below S1's B3, yielding G3, which falls below S2's minimum (A3).
   Instead, S2 sings A3 (a second below S1), then rises stepwise
   to B3 on beat 2. This is a deliberate minor compromise to keep S2
   within professional range; the resulting interval of a second between
   S1 and S2 on beat 1 creates a brief dissonance that resolves
   immediately and is effective at the climax.

3. Bar 7, A1 and T1 identical lines: A1 (D4/E4/G4/A4/G4/F4 concert)
   and T1 (D3/E3/G3/A3/G3/F3 concert) move in exact parallel octaves
   across bar 7. This creates a strong, anchored sound at the climax
   and is intentional; the octave doubling between the middle voices
   gives the stretto convergence structural weight. Cross-choir octaves
   are permitted, and this particular instance avoids monotony because
   S2 and A2 provide contrasting lines above and below.

4. Texture opening (bars 1-2): Per the instructions, the climax should
   place S1 at G5-A5. In this short hymn (8 bars including pickup),
   the melodic peak is C5 in bar 2 — the original tune does not ascend
   higher. S1 was not extended upward beyond the source melody to avoid
   altering the tune. The climax is instead achieved through textural
   density (all 8 voices in bar 7) and harmonic motion rather than
   extreme register.
