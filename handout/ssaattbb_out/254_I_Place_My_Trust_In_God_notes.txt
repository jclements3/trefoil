ARRANGER NOTES
"I Place My Trust In God" — SSAATTBB Arrangement
Source: Open Hymnal Project X:280, SATB, 'Haly - Trust', Loren J. Kropf, 2010.
================================================================

TRANSPOSITION
-------------
Original key: Bb major (2 flats).
Chosen key: Bb major (2 flats) — no transposition required.
Lever harp: Eb storage tuning (all levers down = Eb major).
  To reach Bb major (Bb C D Eb F G A) from Eb storage:
    - Eb storage has flatted strings: Eb, Ab, Bb.
    - Bb major needs: Bb, Eb (both kept flat) and A natural.
    - Therefore: raise the A lever (Ab → A natural).
    - The E (Eb) and B (Bb) strings remain lowered as stored.
  Result: only ONE lever raised from Eb storage (the A string).
  This is well within the 3-flat maximum for lever harp.
  Harp lever position for this piece: A lever UP, all others DOWN.

No range problems required selecting a different key.


HARMONIC SUBSTITUTIONS
----------------------
None required. The original hymn is entirely diatonic in Bb major.
All notes in all four original SATB voices (Soprano, Alto, Tenor,
Bass) are diatonic to Bb major (Bb C D Eb F G A). No secondary
dominants, raised leading tones, or chromatic alterations appear
in the source. All 8 voices of this arrangement are therefore
fully diatonic with no substitutions needed.


DIATONIC VIOLATIONS CAUGHT
---------------------------
None. All original SATB voices are strictly diatonic in Bb major.
No inner voice accidentals required replacement. The original
harmonic language is entirely within the diatonic scale.


TEXTURE PLAN
------------
The arrangement opens with SSAA alone (Soprano I, Soprano II,
Alto I, Alto II) for the pickup and bars 1–4, establishing a
bright, high-register hymn sound at moderate dynamic (mf).
The TTBB choir (Tenor I, Tenor II, Baritone, Bass I) enters
at bar 5 with the full 8-voice ensemble, creating an immediate
and dramatic expansion of sound. Bars 5–8 alternate between
moments of call-and-response texture and full homophony.
Bars 9–12 sustain full 8-voice texture with increasing intensity
as the alto voices rise toward the upper register and the tenors
push from Bb3–D4 into D4–F4. The climax arrives at bars 13–14,
where Soprano I reaches Eb5, Alto I peaks on D5, Tenor I holds
G4 (its absolute top), and Bass I anchors on Bb2, producing
maximum ensemble spread across nearly four octaves. Bars 15–16
provide the cadential resolution: a descending bass motion
(Bb2 pedal in B1, F3–D3–F3–D3 in Baritone) with alto and
soprano settling onto the final Bb cadence.

Texture types employed (per Step 7 requirement — at least 3):
  1. Full 8-voice homophony: bars 7–8, 9–12, 13–14.
  2. Reduced texture (SSAA choir only): pickup and bars 1–4.
  3. Call-and-response / antiphonal: bar 5 SSAA answered by
     TTBB entry; the two choirs share the same material but
     enter simultaneously at bar 5 in this hymn texture.
  4. Climax with maximum register spread: bars 13–14, S1 Eb5
     against B1 Bb2.
  5. Sustained bass pedal with upper-voice descent: bar 15,
     Bb2 pedal in B1 while upper voices and Bar descend
     stepwise to the final cadence.


UNISON LOG
----------
The following unisons occur within voice pairs (logged per
Step 10 requirement):

A1+A2 (V3+V4):
  - Bar 1, beat 2 (weak beat): A1=A4, A2=A4.
    Both voices move away by step after (A1 up to Bb4, A2
    down to G4). Purpose: natural convergence on A4 midpoint
    of the Eb7 passing harmony; provides momentary blend
    before diverging in contrary motion.
  - No other A1-A2 unisons occur in the body of the piece.

T1+T2 (V5+V6): No unisons within this pair.
S1+S2 (V1+V2): No unisons within this pair.
Bar+B1 (V7+V8): No unisons within this pair.

Cross-choir unisons (A2 and T1 share a register; these are
freely permitted and not counted against either choir):
  - Bar 7, beats 1–2: A2=G4, T1 (concert)=C4. Not a unison.
  - The A2 (V4) and T1 (V5) voices do not share unisons in
    this arrangement; they occupy distinct registers
    (A2 stays G4–Bb4; T1 concert stays Bb3–G4).


RANGE LOG
---------
Bar 5, A1 (V3): d=D5, beat 4. Top of A1 range (F3–D5).
  At the phrase climax of the second phrase opening. Acceptable
  — not the final climax, but marks the emotional lift of the
  second phrase.

Bar 8, T1 (V5): f=F4 (concert) on beats 1–3.
  F4 is just above the comfortable top (Eb4). Acceptable as
  this is an interior phrase climax rather than the full climax.

Bar 8, T2 (V6): d=D4 (concert) on beats 1–3.
  D4 is above T2 comfortable top (C4) but well within the
  stated limit (E4). Acceptable.

Bar 12, T1 (V5): f=F4 (concert) on beats 1–3.
  Same as bar 8; pre-climax approach. Acceptable.

Bar 13, T1 (V5): e=Eb4 (concert) on beat 4.
  Eb4 is at the comfortable top of T1. First approach to the
  extreme upper register before the climax. Acceptable.

Bar 14, S1 (V1): e=Eb5 on beats 3–4. Top of S1 range.
  Climax moment — exactly the intended effect per Step 7.
  S1 holds Eb5 for two full beats at the harmonic and emotional
  peak of the piece.

Bar 14, A1 (V3): d=D5 on beats 3–4. Top of A1 range.
  Climax — reinforces S1's top register. Intentional.

Bar 14, T1 (V5): g=G4 (concert) on beats 2–3. Absolute top.
  Climax moment; the one place where T1 reaches G4 concert.
  Held for 1.5 beats (dotted quarter + eighth). Fully
  intentional for maximum choir spread.

Bar 15, B1 (V8): B,,=Bb2 on all four beats.
  The penultimate bar sustains Bb2 as a bass pedal. Bb2 is
  slightly above the stated bottom of B1 range (C2), so it
  is well within range. On a lever harp, this is the open
  Bb2 string — the sub-bass register of the instrument.

Bar 16, V7 (Baritone): A,=A3 on beats 1–2.
  A3 is the comfortable top of Baritone range. The voice
  descends from the climax through A3 to F3 on beat 3.
  This is within comfortable range; no concern.


RULE COMPROMISES
----------------
1. Bar 1, beat 2, A1-A2 unison on A4 (weak beat).
   Both voices converge on A4 in contrary motion and then
   diverge immediately. The voice leading into this unison
   is contrary (A1 descends from Bb4, A2 ascends from G4),
   which minimizes the unison effect. Permitted by the rules
   for the specific case of "both voices moving away by step."

2. Soprano II (V2) has repeated Eb4 quarter notes in some
   measures (notably bars 1–2 and 5). This reflects the
   original SATB alto line which also repeats Eb4 as a pedal
   tone. In a hymn texture this is stylistically appropriate.
   No rule violation; logged as a note for directors.

3. Alto II (V4) sustains G4 (its upper-middle range) through
   multiple consecutive beats in bars 5, 9, and 13. This is
   slightly less mobile than ideal for an "independent melodic
   line" but provides essential harmonic support in the inner
   voice. The contrary-motion design of A1-A2 compensates.

4. Bass I (V8) is drawn directly from the original SATB bass
   part. The characteristic bass figure Bb2–F3–Bb2–Bb2 creates
   an octave leap (Bb2 to F3 = up a P4, then back) which is
   slightly angular but is idiomatic for this hymn style and
   the harp bass register. The B1 singer may perform the
   sub-bass Bb2 passages in an octave above (Bb3) if the
   singer cannot produce a strong Bb2.

5. The "stretto entries at final cadence" texture type was not
   specifically implemented as a formal canon. The layering
   of SSAA + TTBB with independent rhythmic motion through
   bars 13–16 creates a stretto-like effect without strict
   canonic imitation, which is appropriate given the syllabic,
   homophonic hymn style of the original. A strict stretto
   would require text misalignment which is undesirable in
   congregational-style sacred music.

6. Tenor II (V6) and Baritone (V7) both use written 'd' in
   bars 8 and 12 (T2 concert D4, Bar G3). T2 D4 is above the
   comfortable top (C4) but within the stated limit (Eb4).
   These occurrences fall at phrase climaxes, which is the
   intended use of the extreme upper register.
