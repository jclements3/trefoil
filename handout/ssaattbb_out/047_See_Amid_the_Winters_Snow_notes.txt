No full harmonic reharmonizations were necessary. The original hymn is
essentially diatonic in G major. The only chromatic moments are the raised
C# (^C) used as a chromatic passing tone / temporary leading tone toward D in
the inner voices (see Diatonic Violations below). The harmony at those points
remains a first-inversion D major chord (V of G); replacing ^C with C natural
(the diatonic 7th of the scale, or the 3rd of an implied Am chord) turns it
into a smoother diatonic passing motion through IV6–V or ii–V. No chord root
or function changes.

All three ^C (C#) instances in the original SATB inner voices were replaced:

1. Bar 4, original Tenor (S2V1 in source): D ^C D2
   → Replaced with D C D2 (C natural, diatonic scale degree 4).
   In the SSAATTBB arrangement, T1 (V:5) at bar 4 uses A G F E — a
   descending stepwise line that avoids the chromatic note entirely
   while outlining the dominant harmony. T2 is silent at this point.

2. Bar 13, original Alto (S1V2 in source): B,3/2 B,/ ^C D
   → Replaced in A1 (V:3) with D E D C — a stepwise arch figure
   using only diatonic scale degrees. The C# is eliminated; the line
   still moves toward D (scale degree 5) through stepwise motion.

3. Bar 14, original Alto (S1V2 in source): D ^C D2
   → Replaced in A1 (V:3) with B, C B, A, — a neighbor-note figure
   on B and C natural, descending to A, which provides a smoother
   diatonic line while implying the same dominant preparation.
   A2 (V:4) similarly uses G, A, G,2 (all diatonic).

TEXTURE PLAN
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
The arrangement opens with a reduced 6-voice texture (S1 S2 A1 A2 T1 B1,
bars 1–4), creating an intimate pastoral character appropriate to the
Christmas carol's gentle imagery. Bars 5–8 expand to full 8-voice homophony
for a richer second strophe. Bars 9–12 introduce antiphonal exchange: the
SSAA choir alone answers bars 9–10 (upper voices, lighter texture), then the
TTBB choir alone answers bars 11–12 (lower voices, darker color), the two
half-choirs in dialogue. Bars 13–16 reunite all 8 voices for the climactic
final strophe, with S1 ascending to D5 (bar 15, d) and B1 on G2 at the
cadence (bar 16), producing broad ensemble spread. The final cadence (bar 16)
settles with B1 on the root G2 and sustained parallel thirds throughout.

Texture types used:
  - Reduced 6-voice texture (bars 1–4): opening build
  - Full 8-voice homophony (bars 5–8): second phrase full statement
  - Antiphonal SSAA vs TTBB exchange (bars 9–12)
  - Full 8-voice homophony with climax (bars 13–16)

1. Bar 15, S2 and A1 voice crossing (between pairs, not within):
   S2 has G4 on beat 2 (G3/2 B/ G c, second note G), while A1 has A4
   (G A B c, beat 2 A). A1 briefly crosses above S2 on beat 2.
   Cross-pair voice crossing is explicitly permitted by the rules.
   The effect is a brief convergence that immediately resolves as S1
   rises to D5 and A1 continues ascending, creating a satisfying
   contrary-motion opening of the texture at the climax phrase.

2. Bar 8, T1+T2 share D (concert D3) on the final beat:
   T1 written c B A G ends on G (concert G3); T2 written D2 ends on D
   (concert D3). These differ — no actual unison occurs. (Log entry
   above is corrected: no unison on beat 4 of bar 8.)

3. Bars 9–12, antiphonal rests create brief silences in half the choir:
   This is an intentional texture choice, not a rule compromise. The
   silence of TTBB in bars 9–10 and SSAA in bars 11–12 is a structural
   antiphonal device. No voice leading rule is violated; the resuming
   voices begin fresh phrases.

4. A2 range: bar 9–10, A2 descends to E, (E3) and C (C4). The descending
   figure E, F, G, F, / E D C2 touches C4 on beat 4 of bar 10. C4 is
   well within A2's range (D3–B4). No issue; noted for completeness.
