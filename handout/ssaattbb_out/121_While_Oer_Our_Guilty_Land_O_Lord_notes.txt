While O'er Our Guilty Land, O Lord
'Federal Street' — SSAATTBB expansion
============================================================

TRANSPOSITION:
- Original key: F major (1 flat)
- Chosen key: F major (1 flat) — no transposition required
- Semitones shifted: 0
- Lever harp (Eb storage tuning) requires raising levers for F major:
    Ab → Eb → Bb → F → C → G → D
    Levers raised: F, C, G, D (4 levers up from Eb storage)
    This is within valid range (max 4 sharps / 3 flats).
- Note: F major requires raising the Bb string (lever up) to B natural.
  The harp plays Bb diatonically throughout. =B natural in bar 7
  (melody S1 only) cannot be supported by the harp without a lever
  flip and is treated as a pure vocal color supported by the voice
  alone, consistent with the diatonic constraint rules.

HARMONIC SUBSTITUTIONS:
- Bar 7: Original harmony implied a secondary dominant / chromatic
  approach (S1 sings =B natural = raised 4th, creating a tritone
  against the key). No inner voice substitution was required because
  all inner voices (S2 through B1) were kept on diatonic pitches
  forming a D minor triad (D–F–A), which is the nearest diatonic
  substitute for the chromatic color and resolves naturally to C major
  (V) in bar 8. The =B natural is confined to S1 per the diatonic
  constraint rules.
- No other chromatic harmonies appear in the original SATB.

DIATONIC VIOLATIONS CAUGHT:
- Bar 7: S1V1 sings =B natural (B natural). In the original, S1V2
  (alto) had D4 and S2V1 (tenor) had G below — both diatonic. No
  violations in inner voices of the original. The arrangement
  preserves this: =B appears only in V:1 (S1).
- All original inner voice notes (S1V2, S2V1, S2V2) were diatonic
  in F major throughout, so no inner voice accidental replacements
  were necessary.

TEXTURE PLAN:
The arrangement opens in full homophony (bars 1–4) presenting the
hymn tune in all 8 voices to establish the sound. Bars 5–6 shift to
a more transparent texture with the SSAA choir carrying melodic
motion while TTBB provides sustained harmonic support (antiphonal
lean). Bars 7–8 return to full homophony with chromatic color
concentrated in S1. The extended middle section (bars 9–11) uses
call-and-response between outer voices (S1/B1) with inner voices
providing stepwise passing motion, achieving genuine melodic
independence in A1, A2, T1, T2. Bar 12 builds to a half-cadence on
G minor with the full ensemble sustained for maximum resonance. The
climax (bars 13–14, sintro) expands to full 8-voice spread with S1
reaching D5 and B1 descending to Bb2–D2. The final cadence (bars
15–16, eintro) settles in full homophony with B1 on F2 (root, low)
and S1 on F4 for a broad, grounded close. Texture types used: (1)
full 8-voice homophony (bars 1–4, 12, 16), (2) antiphonal lean SSAA
vs TTBB (bars 5–6), (3) call-and-response outer voices with inner
voice passing motion (bars 9–11), (4) climax stretto build (bars
13–14).

RULE COMPROMISES:
- Bar 12, V:7+V:8 (Bar+B1): Unison G2 for the entire bar (4 beats).
  Rule states avoid unisons on consecutive beats within the same pair.
  This is a deliberate exception at the half-cadence to create maximum
  bass weight before the climax entrance at bar 13. The two voices
  diverge immediately in bar 13 (Bar=F,2 B, B,; B1=F,2 B, D,), so
  the unison functions as a point of convergence before the climax
  spread. This is the only multi-beat unison in the arrangement.
- Bar 11, V:7+V:8 (Bar+B1): Bar=E,2 G,2 (E2→G2), B1=C,2 C,2 (C2).
  This gives a C major open-fifth sonority. The Bar voice moves up
  a third (E2 to G2) while B1 holds. This satisfies the C major
  harmony (C–E–G) and keeps both voices independent. No rule
  compromise needed.
