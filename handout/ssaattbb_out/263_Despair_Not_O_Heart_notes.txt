ARRANGER NOTES — Despair Not, O Heart (SSAATTBB)
Source: OpenHymnal 263, "The Wartburg Hymnal" 1918
Arrangement for double choir (SSAATTBB) and lever harp

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
TRANSPOSITION
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
Original key:  F major (1 flat — Bb)
Chosen key:    F major (1 flat — Bb)
Transposition: None required.

F major is within the valid harp range (≤ 3 flats). No transposition needed.

Lever harp setup for F major (Eb storage tuning, levers raise strings):
  Raise the Bb lever on all octaves to produce Bb.
  All other levers remain down (Eb, Ab, Db, Gb, Cb = flat, producing:
    E-flat, A-flat, D-flat, G-flat, C-flat — these are not used)
  In practice for F major: only the Bb lever is raised on every octave.
  Available diatonic pitches: F G A Bb C D E.

HARMONIC SUBSTITUTIONS
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
Bar 9 (A F): The original tenor has a ^C (C#) suggesting a secondary
  dominant tonicization (A7/D or V/vi). Because C# is non-diatonic and
  must not appear in any inner voice, the harmony is treated as a
  plain vi chord (D minor: D F A) on beat 1 and IV (F major: F A C)
  on beat 2. Voice leading is preserved by stepwise contrary motion.
  Original: A major triad / V of d minor → Replacement: D minor / F major.

Bar 11 (F E/C/) — the original tenor uses =B natural (B-natural in
  F major = raised 4th / Lydian color, or passing toward the ^C in bar 9).
  Because B-natural is non-diatonic in F major, inner voices use Bb
  throughout. No reharmonization needed; the diatonic neighbor (Bb or A)
  preserves the descending stepwise line.

DIATONIC VIOLATIONS CAUGHT
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
All instances below are from the original SATB inner voices. The S1
(soprano melody) is unrestricted; the original soprano has no accidentals.

1. Bar 9, Tenor (S2V1): original note ^C (C#)
   Replaced with: C (natural, diatonic 5th of F major)
   Reason: C# is non-diatonic in F major. The line descends A–C–B,–A,
   giving smooth stepwise motion implying D minor / V of d. Replaced
   with A–C–B,–A, (all diatonic), preserving contour.

2. Bar 11 / approach to bar 12, Tenor (S2V1): original note =B, (B natural)
   Replaced with: B, (Bb, diatonic 4th of F major)
   Reason: B-natural is not in the F major scale. The descending tenor
   line G,–G,/E,/ continues smoothly to F, without the B natural.
   In T1 the line reads A,–G,–E,–F, (diatonic throughout).

3. Bar 11 approach, Alto (S1V2): original note =B, (B natural)
   Replaced with: B, (Bb) in all inner voices.
   Reason: same as above. S1V2 in the source reads =B,2 at bar 12.
   All inner voices use Bb (diatonic); only S1 is permitted accidentals,
   and the soprano melody does not use B natural at this point.

TEXTURE PLAN
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
The piece opens with a restrained 8-voice homophony at a moderate
dynamic (mp), all voices moving together in block-chord style through
the first phrase (bars 1–8), establishing the solemn, hymn-like character.
The middle section (bars 9–12) thins slightly through registral spacing —
SSAA and TTBB move in parallel within their choirs, creating an antiphonal
layering effect where the upper choir (SSAA) carries the melodic interest
while the lower choir (TTBB) provides sustained harmonic grounding.
The refrain (bars 13–17, marked !sintro!/!eintro!) returns to full 8-voice
homophony with the widest registral spread for the climax (S1 at c5,
B1 at F,,), closing with a broad suspension on the penultimate bar (S2
D–E–F, Alto C–D) before all voices converge on the final F major chord.

Texture types used:
  1. Full 8-voice homophony — bars 1–8 and 13–17 (block chords, principal texture)
  2. Stratified antiphonal layering — bars 9–12 (SSAA vs. TTBB registral separation)
  3. Suspension at final cadence — bar 16 (S2, A1, T1, Bar each sustain a 4–3
     or 7–6 suspension figure into the final bar 17 chord)

UNISON LOG
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
Unisons are used sparingly. No within-pair unisons on consecutive beats.

Bar 4 (G2): S1=G, S2=E — no unison. A1=C, A2=B, — no unison.
  T1=C, T2=G, — no unison. Bar=E,, B1=C, — no unison.
  (No unisons in this bar; all 8 voices distinct at the phrase cadence.)

Bar 8 (G2): Same distribution as bar 4; S2 and A2 are both on their
  respective 3rds below — no intra-pair unison. T1 and T2 share B,2 —
  T1+T2 unison on strong beat (beat 1, held whole bar), purpose: phrase
  cadence blend and textural anchor for the lower choir after the
  ascending melodic line.

Bar 16 (G2 → suspension): S2 and A1 move through tied suspensions
  rather than unisons. No within-pair unisons.

Bar 17 (final F): B1 on F,, — at final cadence, B1 and Bar both on
  F, (Bar=F,, B1=F,,) — Bar+B1 unison on strong beat (final), purpose:
  grounded root-position close on the tonic, deliberate blend at cadence.
  This is an intentional structural unison.

RULE COMPROMISES
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
1. Bar 17 (final bar), B1 F,,: B1 descends to F1 (below comfortable
   C2). Justified as a deliberate final cadential low root for maximum
   resonance. May be raised to F2 at conductor's discretion.

2. Bars 1–17 general: Because this is a short through-composed strophic
   hymn (17 bars including pickup), the texture variety goal (≥3 types)
   is met with homophony, antiphonal stratification, and cadential
   suspension, but full canonic imitation and stretto were not employed.
   The brevity of the piece (2/4 meter, quarter-note pulse) does not
   afford sufficient space for a canonic entry without distorting the
   hymn character. The antiphonal stratification in bars 9–12 provides
   the required contrast.

3. Bar 12 (D2): The original bass reaches B,,,2 (Bb1). In this
   arrangement B1 is written B,,,2 — this is below standard bass range
   (C2). Replaced in the arrangement with B,,2 (Bb2) for B1, keeping
   Bar at B,,2 as well, sounding a unison at the phrase cadence. If the
   source bass note B,, is essential, a solo bass may take it; otherwise
   the written Bb2 is preferred.

4. Voice leading in bars 2–3: the outer voices (S1 and B1) move in
   similar motion at "B G/G/" / "B,, B,,/C,/" — technically similar
   motion to an octave (S1 Bb, B1 Bb,,). This is not parallel octaves
   (they do not move from the same interval to an octave simultaneously
   by similar motion from a different interval) but is a borderline
   similar-motion approach. Accepted for the hymn-style texture.
