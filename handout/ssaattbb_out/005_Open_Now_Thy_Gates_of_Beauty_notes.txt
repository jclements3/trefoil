HARMONIC SUBSTITUTIONS
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
One chromatic harmony appears in the original SATB:

Bar 10 (phrase 3, bar 2), tenor voice (S2V1 in original):
  Original tenor: G, G, ^F,2 — G3, G3, F#3 (half note)
  The F# (^F,) creates a V/V (D major) secondary dominant resolving
  toward the Gm area.

  Substitution: F# replaced with F-natural (F3) throughout all
  inner voices in the diatonic choir.

  Original chord sequence at bar 10 beats 3-4: D major (D, F#, A)
  Replacement chord: D minor (D, F-natural, A) — i.e., iv/v or simply
  Dm in Bb context. This preserves the bass D pedal and the descending
  melodic momentum while remaining fully diatonic. The A natural (in T1
  and T2 at beats 3-4 of bar 10) still provides the characteristic
  leading-tone color against the Bb tonal center.

  Effect: The Dm chord at bar 10 beats 3-4 creates a soft plagal-minor
  color (Gm → Dm) rather than the original's secondary dominant sharpness.
  This is tonally smooth and well-suited to the harp's diatonic constraint.

S1 (Soprano I) may sing accidentals freely and is not subject to the
diatonic constraint. S1 sings the original soprano exactly including
any chromatic passing tones (none appear in this melody — Bb major
is the natural key and all soprano notes are diatonic).

TEXTURE PLAN
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
The arrangement opens with SSAA alone (bars 1-4), giving the chorale
a light, feminine texture for the first statement of the hymn. The TTBB
choir enters in bar 5, expanding to full 8-voice homophony for the
repeated phrase (bars 5-8), achieving a dynamic swell on the repeat.
Phrase 3 (bars 9-12) sustains the full 8-voice tutti through the final
cadence, with a Bar-B1 pair unison on the penultimate bass note (D3)
and a rich Bb major voicing (F, D, Bb, Bb) at the final cadence.

Textures used:
  1. SSAA duet texture (bars 1-4): upper choir alone, 4-voice
  2. Full 8-voice homophony (bars 5-8): tutti entry on the repeat
  3. Full 8-voice climax texture (bars 9-12): sustained tutti with
     pair unisons at cadence points for harmonic grounding
  4. Call-and-response implied by SSAA-only opening vs. TTBB entry:
     the upper choir states the theme; the full choir confirms it.

RULE COMPROMISES
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
1. S1 Bb3 below C4 minimum (bars 1, 4, 5, 8, beat positions noted above):
   The original SATB soprano descends to Bb3 throughout this chorale,
   particularly on the characteristic pickup figure (beat 1) and at
   the phrase-ending half notes. Forcing S1 to C4 at these moments
   would require departure from the original melody, violating the
   S1-follows-original-soprano rule. The lower bound is treated as a
   comfort guideline rather than an absolute limit for this melody.

2. A1 voice crosses above S2 at bar 3/7 beat 3 (S2=Bb3, A1=D4):
   At bar 3 beat 3, S2 descends to Bb3 (returning from the G4 peak)
   while A1 sustains D4. This momentary crossing between pairs (not
   within a pair) is explicitly permitted by the rules and creates a
   full Bb major voicing (F4, Bb3, D4, G3) at an Eb→Bb→Gm harmonic
   transition. No voice crossing within any pair occurs.

3. T1-T2 unison at bar 5 beat 1 (both D3c written):
   The TTBB choir's first entrance requires the tenor pair to announce
   their arrival with weight. A T1-T2 unison on D3c is used for the
   dotted-quarter opening beat, after which the voices immediately
   diverge (T1→F3c at beat 1.5, T2→D3c continuing). This satisfies
   the "occasionally" allowance for T1-T2 unisons.

4. Bar=B1=D3 unison at bar 10 beats 3-4 (half note):
   The diatonic Dm substitution chord requires D in the bass register.
   The baritone and bass naturally converge on D3 at this cadential
   half note. This is a permitted Bar-B1 pair unison used for emphasis
   at a harmonic arrival.

5. Bar=B1=Eb3 unison at bar 11 beats 1-2:
   Eb3 is the bass note of the Eb major chord opening the penultimate
   phrase. The baritone arrives on Eb3 together with Bass I as a
   weight-enhancing pair unison on the strong beat. Permitted
   occasionally.

6. T2 voice leading in phrase 3 involves occasional 4th and 5th leaps:
   The constraint of avoiding T-B unisons with a dense bass register
   forces T2 into non-stepwise motion at several points (e.g.,
   bar 10 beat 1 to beat 3: F3c→A3c, a minor 3rd; bar 11 beat 2
   to beat 3: D3c→G3c, a 4th). All leaps are perfect intervals or
   minor thirds; no augmented intervals appear in T2. The 5th leaps
   (bar 9 beat ~2 to beat 3: G3c→D3c, a 4th; bar 11 beat 4 D3c to
   bar 12 beat 1 F3c, a minor 3rd) resolve by step or 3rd in the
   following beat.

7. S2 harmonic color at bar 12 final cadence (G F G2):
   S2 ends on G4 (half note) rather than Bb4 (the natural third of
   Bb major). This gives the cadence a Bb6 color (Bb, D, F, G across
   the choir) — a warm, open sonority appropriate for a chorale ending.
   The G4 is diatonic and within S2 range. Bb4 was avoided to prevent
   an S2-S1 unison (S1=Bb4 at the cadence).
