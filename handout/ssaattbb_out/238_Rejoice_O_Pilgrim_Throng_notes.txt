TRANSPOSITION
================================================================

Original key: F major (1 flat: Bb)
Chosen key: F major (unchanged)
Semitones shifted: 0 (no transposition required)

Lever harp (Eb storage tuning): raise the Bb lever only.
  String Bb is raised to B-natural... wait: in Eb storage, all levers
  down = Eb major (Eb Ab Bb Cb Db Eb Fb). Raising levers sharpens in
  order: Ab → Eb → Bb → F → C → G → D.
  For F major (F G A Bb C D E), we need: F# raised from Fb = no;
  we need F natural (raise F lever), C natural (raise C lever),
  G natural (raise G lever), D natural (raise D lever), E natural
  (raise E lever from Eb), A natural (raise A lever from Ab), and
  Bb (raise Bb lever from Bb... Bb is already in Eb tuning, so Bb
  lever stays DOWN).

  Correction — in Eb storage (levers down = Eb major = Eb Fb Gb Ab Bb Cb Db):
  To reach F major (F G A Bb C D E) from Eb storage:
    Raise Ab → A (lever 1)
    Raise Eb → E (lever 2)
    Raise Bb → stays as Bb — already correct (lever stays down)
    Raise Fb → F (lever 3)
    Raise Cb → C (lever 4)
    Raise Gb → G (lever 5)
    Raise Db → D (lever 6)
    Bb lever: DOWN (Bb is diatonic in F major, storage provides Bb ✓)

  So for F major from Eb storage: 6 levers raised (Ab, Eb, Fb, Cb, Gb, Db);
  Bb lever stays down. This is a standard "1-flat" harp setting.

  No key was skipped; F major is valid and singers stay within professional
  ranges in this key without transposition.

HARMONIC SUBSTITUTIONS
================================================================

Bar 3 (phrase 1, m.3): Original soprano had B-natural (=B) creating a
  momentary raised-4th or #IV color over F major (Lydian inflection or
  approach to C major). This accidental is retained in S1 only (S1 is
  unrestricted). All other voices remain on F major diatonic tones (A, C, F, D).
  No harmonic reharmonization needed — the clash is intentional color.

Bar 5 pickup (phrase 2): Original SATB alto entered on Eb4 (b7, modal-
  mixture flavor approaching the mediant phrase). In the original this
  supported a brief tonicization of Bb major (IV). Since non-S1 voices
  must remain diatonic, the Eb was replaced with E-natural (E4) throughout.
  The resulting harmony on the pickup beat is F major (I) rather than a
  Bb-color approach, which slightly weakens the modal color but preserves
  the harp constraint without disrupting voice leading. No full chord
  substitution was needed; S1's freedom compensates for the color.

Bar 7-8 (phrase 2, mm.7-8): Original bass contained sub-range passing
  tones (G1, Bb1, A1 = G,,, B,,, A,, in bass-clef ABC). These were
  written for a keyboard instrument and lie well below the B1 vocal range
  (C2 minimum). All sub-C2 bass notes were raised an octave:
    G,, → G,  (G1 → G2)
    B,, → B,  (Bb1 → Bb2)
    A,, → A,  (A1 → A2)
  The passing-tone motion is preserved; only the octave is changed.

================================================================
TEXTURE PLAN
================================================================

The arrangement opens with a reduced 4-voice texture (SSAA choir alone on
the pickup and first few beats), establishing the melody intimately before
the TTBB choir joins at measure 1. Phrase 1 (mm.1-4) uses call-and-response
between the two choirs: SSAA carries the harmonic color while TTBB provides
a steady rhythmic foundation. Phrase 2 (mm.5-8) builds to full 8-voice
texture with antiphonal exchange (SSAA leads mm.5-6, TTBB answers mm.6-7)
before the two choirs merge into homophony at the m.7-8 cadence. Phrase 3
(the intro/refrain, mm.9-12) is the climax: full 8-voice sustained homophony
with S1 holding C5 for two measures, a stretto-like ascending bass ostinato
(B1 enters one beat late and ascends C-D-E-F beneath sustained upper voices),
and a final cadence with step-descending inner voices implying a 4-3 suspension
effect (S2: G4→F4, A1: E4→D4→C4).

Texture types used:
  1. Reduced-to-full build: SSAA alone (pickup) → 8 voices (m.1) [Phrase 1]
  2. Antiphonal exchange: SSAA vs TTBB [Phrase 2, mm.5-7]
  3. Full 8-voice homophony: sustained block chords [Phrase 3, mm.9-10]
  4. Stretto bass entry (B1 delayed by one beat in m.9, ascending under
     sustained upper voices) [Phrase 3, mm.9-10]
  5. Call-and-response within phrases (outer voices S1+B1 frame the harmony,
     inner voices provide melodic support) [throughout]

UNISON LOG
================================================================

Phrase 3, S1+S2 at final cadence (m.12): both on F4 (dotted half).
  Beat position: strong (beat 1 of final measure, phrase ending).
  Purpose: deliberate blend for tonal closure; the two sopranos
  merge at the phrase's resolution. Both voices have been moving
  in contrary motion through m.11 (S1 descending G-F-A-G, S2
  descending G-F-E-F) and the unison F is the natural meeting point.
  Both voices arrive on F from above (S1 from G4, S2 from E4), so
  the unison is approached by step in contrary motion ✓.

Phrase 2, m.7, Bar+B1 on G2 (beat 3):
  Both Bar (V:7 = G2 = G,) and B1 (V:8 = G2 = G,) coincide briefly
  on beat 3 of m.7 (weak beat, part of passing motion).
  The harmony at that point is G minor (G Bb D) so both voices on G2
  provides a solid double-bass root before the cadence.
  Bar moves G2→F2 (down), B1 moves G2→A2 (up) — both depart by step
  in contrary directions immediately after ✓ (rule satisfied).

Phrase 3, T1+T2 on F3 (mm.9-10):
  Both tenors sustain F3 concert (written F4 in V:5, F4 in V:6) through
  the climax measures. This is a deliberate blend at the register center
  to support the sustained soprano C5. The unison is released when the
  tenors diverge in m.11 (T1: G3→F3→G3→F3; T2: G3→F3→F3→E3).
  Used for emphasis and tonal weight at the climax ✓.

================================================================
RULE COMPROMISES
================================================================

1. Parallel motion S1/T1, phrase 2 m.5:
   S1 descends D5→C5 (2nd), T1 descends Bb3→A3 (2nd) simultaneously —
   parallel 10ths. This is not prohibited (only 5ths/8ths are forbidden),
   but noted for transparency. The parallel motion reinforces the unison
   feel of the full-choir arrival. Not a rule violation.

2. Baritone below E2 in source avoided by redesign:
   The original SATB bass had many notes below E2 (minimum for Bar).
   Bar was recomposed as an independent line harmonizing a third or
   fourth above B1, staying entirely within E2–G2. This required
   departing from the original bass melody in phrase 1 (Bar was given
   E-E-F-E-G motion rather than following the keyboard bass directly).
   No rule compromise — this is correct procedure.

3. Tenor voices in phrase 1 (mm.1-4) do not follow the original SATB
   tenor (which lay in a keyboard range of F2–G2 extending to E2).
   The SSAATTBB tenor lines were recomposed entirely in the B2–G4
   (T1) and G2–E4 (T2) ranges for vocal feasibility. The harmonic
   content (F major chord tones) is preserved; the melodic contour
   differs from the original. This is standard procedure for expansion
   from keyboard-range SATB to vocal SSAATTBB.

4. T1+T2 sustained unison F3 in mm.9-10 (phrase 3 climax):
   Two beats of unison are used in the climax section for tonal weight.
   While "avoid unisons on consecutive beats within the same pair" is
   the guideline, the sustained climax context (supporting a held S1 C5)
   justifies an exception. The unison is released by step in m.11.
   Logged as a deliberate compromise for texture.
