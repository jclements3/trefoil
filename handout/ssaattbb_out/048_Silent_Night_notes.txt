ARRANGER NOTES — Silent Night (048)
SSAATTBB Expansion for Double Choir and Lever Harp
====================================================

TRANSPOSITION
-------------
Original key: Bb major (2 flats)
Chosen key:   Bb major (2 flats) — no transposition required.

Bb major is valid for lever harp in Eb storage tuning (3 flats or 4 sharps maximum;
Bb = 2 flats, within range).

Lever positions required on harp (from Eb storage, all levers down = Eb major):
  Raise lever on Ab strings → A natural  (providing the major 7th of Bb major)
  Raise lever on Db strings → D natural  (providing the major 3rd of Bb major)
  All other levers remain down:
    Eb, Bb, F, G, C stay at their Eb-tuning pitches,
    yielding the full Bb major scale: Bb C D Eb F G A.

No range problems required consideration of a different key.

HARMONIC SUBSTITUTIONS
----------------------
None required. The original SATB hymn setting is entirely diatonic in Bb major.
Every chord is built from scale degrees of Bb (Bb C D Eb F G A):
  Bars 1–2, 4, 6, 8, 10, 12: Bb major (I)
  Bar 3, 9:                    F dominant 7th (V7) — F A C Eb, all diatonic in Bb
  Bars 5, 7:                   Eb major (IV) — Eb G Bb, all diatonic in Bb
  Bar 11:                      Bb/F with passing motion through Eb and C (diatonic)

No secondary dominants, chromatic alterations, or tonicizations appear in the source.
No harmonic substitutions were necessary.

DIATONIC VIOLATIONS CAUGHT
---------------------------
None. All four original SATB voices are completely diatonic in Bb major throughout.
No inner-voice accidentals required replacement. The `E` written in the original
(in K:Bb) always represents Eb (flattened by the key signature), which is the
diatonic 4th degree of Bb major. The `A` written in the original represents A
natural (not affected by the Bb key signature), which is the diatonic major 7th.
Both are fully diatonic. No violations to report.

TEXTURE PLAN
------------
The arrangement opens with all eight voices present but TTBB in a subordinate,
sustained role beneath the characteristic SSAA figuration of bars 1–2. The
antiphonal texture emerges at bars 5–6, where SSAA carries the Eb (IV) phrase
in bar 5 with TTBB following in echo on the returning Bb (I) gesture of bar 6.
Bars 7–8 begin the full 8-voice homophonic texture as the two choirs combine in
parallel motion on the Eb–Bb phrase pair. The climax at bars 9–10 deploys full
8-voice writing, with S1 ascending to D5 (d) and B1 holding Bb2 (B,), achieving
the maximum ensemble spread specified. The final cadence (bars 11–12) uses a
descending stepwise chromatic contraction in all voices, converging onto the Bb
tonic chord.

Texture types employed:
  1. Full 8-voice homophony (bars 7–12)
  2. Antiphonal exchange SSAA vs TTBB (bars 5–6 and bars 7–8)
  3. Call and response between outer voices S1 and B1 with inner voices as
     harmonic support (implied throughout bars 1–4)
  4. Sustained chord with melodic figuration above (bars 1–2: TTBB sustain,
     SSAA carry the melody)

UNISON LOG
----------
Bar 12, S1+S2, beat 1 (strong): Both voices arrive on Bb3 (B,) in unison at
  the final tonic cadence. Purpose: deliberate blend and repose at close;
  neither voice crosses the other in the approach. Both approach by step
  (S1 from C4, S2 from A3), move in contrary/oblique motion into the unison,
  and both hold for three beats. Per the rules, this cadential unison on the
  strong beat is fully appropriate.

No other intra-pair unisons occur. Cross-choir unison A2/T1 appears in
  Bar 3: A2=C4 and T1=C4 (first note). Permitted by the cross-choir rule.
  Bar 9: A2=C4 and T1=C4 (first note). Same cross-choir alignment.
  Bar 12: A2=D3 and T2=Bb2 — no cross-choir unison here.

RANGE LOG
----------
S1, bar 10, beat 3: d (D5) — climax note. Within the specified A5 ceiling but
  approaching the comfortable top of G5. D5 is moderate and appropriate; the
  piece does not push to G5 or A5 because the gentle lullaby character of Silent
  Night does not call for an exposed climax scream. D5 is the highest note in
  the original melody.

A1, bar 12: F,3 (F3) — exactly at the comfortable minimum (F3). This is a quiet
  final resolution; singers in this register will manage without strain given the
  slow tempo (♩=60) and gentle dynamic context.

A2, bar 12: D,3 (D3) — exactly at the comfortable minimum (D3). Same context as
  A1 bar 12 above.

T1, bar 10: F,3 (F3, sounding) — within the comfortable T1 range (B2–G4). The
  approach to this note from D3 is a rising third, resolving comfortably.

T2, bars 5, 7, 11: G,,2 (G2, sounding) — the bottom of the T2 range. These
  brief passing touches at G2 occur on weak subdivisions or passing beats and
  are idiomatic for professional tenors.

Bar (Baritone), bar 11: A,,2 (A2, sounding) — the baritone descends to A2 on
  the passing quavers of the cadential bar. E2 is the official bottom of the
  range; A2 sits comfortably above that. No concern.

B1, bar 10: B,2 (Bb2, sounding) — within B1 comfortable range (C2–F3). Not a
  concern.

B1, bar 11: F,,2 (F2, sounding) — the lowest note in the arrangement. Within B1
  range (C2–A3 listed, C2 minimum). F2 is above C2 minimum ✓. This matches
  the original bass exactly.

RULE COMPROMISES
-----------------
None required beyond the following deliberate choices:

1. Bar 3, T1: Tenor I doubles A2 at C4 (cross-choir unison). Permitted explicitly
   by the cross-choir rule (A2 and T1 may freely share a pitch at the register break).

2. Bar 9, T1: Same cross-choir unison A2=T1=C4 on beat 1. Same justification.

3. Bar 12, S1=S2=Bb3: Intra-pair unison at the final cadence. Used for deliberate
   blend and repose; both voices approach by step; the unison falls on the strong
   beat of the final bar; no consecutive unison issues (the prior beats in bar 11
   have S1 on C4 and S2 on A3 — separated by a third).

4. A1 bar 5 and 7: A1 moves in parallel thirds with S1 (both follow the descending
   G–Bb–A–G figure). This produces a parallel third relationship between S1 and A1,
   which is musically natural and desirable for blending the soprano and alto pairs
   on the arch of the Eb phrase. Rule 1 prohibits parallel fifths and octaves, not
   parallel thirds; this is fully compliant.

5. T5 bar 11, final note: the original plan for A2 in the tenor was adjusted from
   A2 (below T1 minimum B2) to C3, keeping T1 within its professional sounding
   range throughout.
