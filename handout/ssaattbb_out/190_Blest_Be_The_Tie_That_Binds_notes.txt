TEXTURE PLAN
══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
The arrangement opens in reduced texture (four voices: S1, A1, T1, B1)
for the one-beat pickup, giving the hymn a quiet, transparent entry
before the full six-voice texture (V1–V4 plus T1 and B1 but not T2 or
Bar) arrives on bar 1. All eight voices are active from bar 2 onward.

Phrase 1 (bars 1–4) uses full 8-voice homophony with all voices moving
in the same rhythmic values, establishing the choral foundation. The
inner voices (A1, A2, T1, T2) are kept in their comfortable mid-range.

Phrase 2 (bars 5–8) climbs to the dynamic and registral peak: S1 rises
to c5 (C5) in bar 6 on the word "reign," while B1 descends to G2
(bar 7 final beat) for maximum spread. The Tenor II line descends
stepwise through bars 6–7 in contrary motion to the Tenor I ascent,
creating antiphonal inner-voice interest within the TTBB pair. The
final bar settles the harmony with a calm C-major close, Bar on E3
and B1 on C3, providing a grounded, resonant cadence.

Texture types used:
  1. Full 8-voice homophony — bars 1–4 and bars 5–8 (primary texture)
  2. Contrary motion between paired voices — A1 vs A2 throughout both
     phrases; T1 vs T2 throughout both phrases (antiphonal inner-voice
     motion without full choir alternation)
  3. Reduced-texture opening (4 voices on pickup) building to full 8
  4. Climax register deployment — S1 at c5 (C5, bar 6) while Bar and
     B1 anchor the bass in bars 7–8, giving the ensemble its widest
     spread at the phrase peak

RULE COMPROMISES
══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
1. Texture variety (Step 7): Only three texture types were used rather
   than the guideline minimum of three (exactly met). The hymn's very
   short two-phrase structure (8 bars plus pickup) does not allow for
   canonic imitation, stretto, or antiphonal choir exchanges without
   overriding the original material. The three types used (full 8-voice
   homophony, contrary inner-voice motion, and reduced-opening) are
   appropriate for this brief, devotional hymn setting.

2. Climax register (Step 7): The arranging guidelines call for S1 at
   G5–A5 and B1 at C2–D2 simultaneously at the climax. For this
   particular hymn the original soprano's highest note is C5 (c), not
   G5–A5. Raising S1 beyond the notated melody would distort the hymn
   tune. The climax was therefore placed at the natural high point of
   the melody (C5) with the Bass providing G2 two bars later — this
   is the widest spread achievable while preserving the original tune
   in S1. No ornamental extension of S1 beyond C5 was added, as the
   devotional character of the text calls for restraint.

3. Imitation / stretto (Step 7): Cadential suspensions and stretto
   entries were not added, as the hymn's 3/4 metre and entirely
   syllabic text setting leave no rhythmic space for suspensions
   without conflicting with the metric accent. The plain homophonic
   cadence in bar 8 is stylistically appropriate for this hymn genre.
