\vspace{2mm}
\noindent{\fontsize{11}{13}\selectfont\raggedright%
\textbf{How to read.} Rows are voicing shapes (generic intervals bottom-up: \osf{2}=2nd, \osf{3}=3rd, \osf{4}=4th). Columns are diatonic degrees \osf{1}--\osf{7}. A cell is the Roman-numeral chord that results (case = major/minor; suffix = quality). A \emph{figure} concatenates column-degree + row-intervals (\osf{5333} = \Ro{V}\osf{7}). Superscripts \osf{1}/\osf{2}/\osf{3} mark inversion (bass chord tone). \nd{} = non-diatonic. \textbf{To voice a chord} (\Ro{G}\osf{7} in \Ro{C}): express as Roman (\Ro{V}\osf{7}), find the cell, stack the row intervals from the bass note.\par}
